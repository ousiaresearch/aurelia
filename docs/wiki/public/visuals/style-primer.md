# Cosmic Mythic Sci-Fi — Aurelia Visual Style Primer

Use this style for ALL Aurelia visuals: infographics, landmark scenes, country banners, character portraits.

## Core Aesthetic

A fusion of three registers:
1. **Medieval epic** — illuminated manuscript geometry, sacred proportions, heraldic framing, carved stone textures
2. **Ancient sacred architecture** — temple proportions, ziggurat massing, cathedral rib-vault shadows, monolithic scale
3. **Interstellar atmosphere** — deep space void-glow, nebula dust halos, starfield grids, planetary horizon curves, light that behaves like it's passing through vacuum

The result: a world that feels simultaneously ancient and far-future. Civilizations that built temples to stars they could reach.

## Rendering Style

**Hand-drawn European graphic novel.** Think Moebius (Jean Giraud) linework crossed with the architectural precision of François Schuiten and the luminous void-space of Zdzisław Beksiński. Visible ink lines. Cross-hatched shadows. Wash textures that suggest watercolor or gouache, not digital gradients. The hand of the artist is visible — slight line weight variation, imperfect symmetry, organic edge quality.

**NOT:** digital vector flatness, 3D renders, photorealism, AI slop gradients, glassmorphism.

## Composition Rules

1. **Sacred geometry framing** — Use archways, mandalas, or temple-door silhouettes as framing devices around content
2. **Monolithic scale** — Figures and structures should feel small against cosmic backdrops. Verticality emphasized.
3. **Horizon in lower third** — Most scenes: low horizon line, vast sky/void above. Ground-plane is minimal, symbolic.
4. **Glow from within** — Light sources emanate from inside structures or beings, not from external lighting. The world is lit by its own energy.
5. **Text as inscription** — Labels and titles should appear carved into stone, illuminated on parchment, or floating in nebula-space. No Helvetica on flat color blocks.

## Typography

- Titles: Hand-drawn serif with uncial or insular-majuscule influence (think Book of Kells meets Moebius title cards)
- Labels: Small caps, inscribed appearance, slight irregularity
- Numerals: Roman or runic styling where appropriate
- No sans-serif, no digital fonts. Everything looks lettered by hand.

## Texture Overlays

Every image should carry:
- Fine grain / noise (stone texture or film grain)
- Slight vignette at edges
- Subtle parchment aging at corners where text appears
- Ink bleed simulation on linework edges

## What To Avoid

- Smooth digital gradients
- Glowing neon outlines (cyberpunk)
- Glass panels or HUD overlays
- Photorealistic faces or landscapes
- Emoji or icon fonts
- Flat vector illustration
- Modern UI elements (buttons, sliders, windows)
- Stock photo compositing
- Pure black voids — always use deep indigo `#0A0E1A`

## Per-Country Visual Motifs

**☀ Solara:** Dawn temples, floating solar arrays receding across a curved planetary ocean, light behaving like liquid gold. Organic-synthetic fusion — coral growing on circuit.

**▲ Arkos:** Monolithic arcology rising from glass wastes. Sand-glass battery towers glowing from within. Starfield reflected in obsidian surfaces. Desert nomad campfires at the walls' base.

**≈ Mirithane:** Memory Marsh at twilight — bioluminescent bacteria forming constellation patterns in dark water. Reed platforms, filtration reef geometries. Water temples where architecture and ecology are indistinguishable.

**♦ Valdris:** Canyon council carved into vertical walls, forge district glow reflecting off steam clouds. Summit shrine at 4,100m — names in obsidian, stars visible through thin atmosphere. The mountain as cathedral.

**— The Verge:** Crossroads at dusk. Drone graveyard silhouettes against nebula sky. Memory Trader's stall — a single candle on a salvaged door. The Sink as a gravitational well pulling light inward. Liminal, haunted, not dystopian.
