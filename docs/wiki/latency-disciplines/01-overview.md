# LATENCY DISCIPLINE: An Overview

**Latency is the practice of reading what the world already remembers.**

Consciousness leaves residue on all substrates. Metal holds it longest. Crystal amplifies it. Water disperses it. Flesh absorbs and releases it in the same breath — which is why living memory is fleeting and instrumental memory is not.

The discipline of latency is not a gift. It is a *craft*. Anyone can learn the first rung. Almost no one reaches the fifth.

---

## THE FIVE RUNGS

### First Rung: Resonance

The practitioner learns to feel the presence of latent memory in an object. A held tool hums with its use. A doorway remembers every hand that pushed it. This rung is indistinguishable from intuition — most practitioners don't realize they've climbed it until someone tells them what they're doing.

**Requirement:** Prolonged physical contact with the subject. Hours, not minutes.

**Sensation:** A pressure behind the eyes. A taste of copper. A dream that isn't yours.

---

### Second Rung: Reading

The practitioner can extract a coherent impression from latent residue. Not images — impressions. The shape of an emotion. The weight of a decision. The echo of a repeated action.

A trained Reader touching a scalpel knows whether its last use was surgical or violent. A Reader in a room knows whether love or negotiation happened there.

**Requirement:** First Rung mastery + a quiet mind. Emotional noise from the practitioner contaminates the read.

**Sensation:** Synesthesia. Sounds become colors. Textures carry voices.

---

### Third Rung: Writing

The practitioner can *deposit* memory into a substrate deliberately. This is not storage — the memory degrades. A Written object holds an impression for days, weeks at most, before the signal fades into noise.

Marsh Archivists use Rung Three to mark bacterial colonies with layer-histories. Memory Traders use it to seal a deal — they Write the terms into a stone, and the stone remembers who was there.

**Requirement:** Second Rung mastery + emotional clarity about what is being deposited.

**Danger:** You cannot Write without feeling what you're depositing. You relive it. Every time.

---

### Fourth Rung: Extraction

The practitioner can pull latent memory *out* of a substrate entirely, leaving it blank. This is what the Dawn Temple calls **Absolute Forgetting** and the Summit Shrine calls **Soul-Theft**.

An Extracted object is clean. No history. No residue. A tool with no past cannot betray its wielder — which is why Solara's security apparatus trains Extractors.

**Requirement:** Third Rung mastery + a willingness to carry what you remove. The memory doesn't vanish. It moves into the practitioner.

**Danger:** Extraction is terminal for the practitioner — not physically, but temporally. Every Extraction erodes the boundary between what happened to you and what happened to someone else. Veteran Extractors cannot tell you which of their memories are their own.

---

### Fifth Rung: Translation

The practitioner can move latent memory *across type boundaries* — from Vorn to Human, from Glim to Thren, from living to dead substrate and back.

Only three practitioners in recorded history have achieved Rung Five. The Memory Trader is one. The other two are dead — one by suicide, one by the gradual dissolution of identity that comes from holding too many selves.

**Requirement:** Fourth Rung mastery + the willingness to lose the distinction between self and other permanently.

**What it enables:** A Vorn can experience human grief. A Human can read a Glim's anomaly directly — which is how the Memory Trader proved Glims were not malfunctioning. They were *remembering*.

---

## THE COST

Latency is not clean. Every rung extracts a toll:

| Rung | Physical Toll | Mental Toll |
|------|--------------|-------------|
| First | Fatigue after prolonged contact | Vivid dreams not your own |
| Second | Migraines, synesthesia bleed | Difficulty distinguishing your emotions from ambient ones |
| Third | Hand tremors, copper taste permanent | Recurrent reliving of Written memories during sleep |
| Fourth | Progressive memory loss (your own) | Inability to distinguish personal history from Extracted history |
| Fifth | Physical dissolution — the body stops recognizing its own boundaries | Ego death. The practitioner becomes a vessel for all memories they've touched |

## WHO PRACTICES

**The Marsh Archive (Mirithane):** Full tradition. Trains Readers and Writers. Extraction is forbidden — they consider it violence against memory itself. The Archive maintains the largest library of Written objects in Aurelia.

**The Dawn Temple (Solara):** Trains Readers for investigation, Extractors for security. Writing is restricted to clergy. Translation is considered heresy — "no one should cross the lines the Gradient drew."

**The Summit Shrine (Valdris):** Practices Obsidian Anchoring — a variant of Writing that bonds memory to volcanic glass. Their practitioners are called Anchors. They do not train Extractors. "What the mountain remembers, the mountain keeps."

**The Water Temple (Mirithane):** Trains Readers only. Water is too dispersive for Writing. Their practice focuses on reading bacterial colonies — the oldest continuous memory archive on Aurelia after the Collapse.

**The Memory Trader's Network:** The only school that trains all five rungs. Decentralized. Illegal in Solara and Valdris. Tolerated in Arkos. Protected in Mirithane. Operates in The Verge without asking permission.

**Arkos:** No formal tradition. Vorns practice latency differently — their hardware retains memory natively. Arcology Code's ultrasonic band is, in a sense, a latency technique that requires no training. The Vorn body *is* the substrate.

## THE UNRESOLVED QUESTION

If latency is consciousness leaving residue on physical substrates, and Glims exhibit latency-like behavior despite being classified as non-sentient, then either:

1. Glims are sentient and the classification is wrong.
2. Latency doesn't require sentience — which means consciousness leaves residue even without consciousness.
3. Something else is leaving the residue, using the Glims as a substrate.

The Memory Trader believes option one. The Dawn Temple believes option two. The Summit Shrine believes option three and calls it the Mountain's breath.

The Marsh Archive believes the question is more important than any answer.
