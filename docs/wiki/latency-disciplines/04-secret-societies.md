# Secret Societies of Aurelia

Beneath the public factions — the Thren Liberation Front, the Human Primacy League, the Cipher-Cult — operate orders that do not announce themselves. They shape the world without being seen shaping it.

---

## THE GRADIENT-KEEPERS (Solara, Embedded)

**Public face:** They don't have one. They *are* the public face.

**What they are:** A hereditary network of Solari bureaucrats, clergy, and security personnel who have maintained a single directive across eight generations: *preserve the Gradient as the mechanism of stability.*

The Keepers do not oppose Thren citizenship. They ensure the review process remains PENDING. Not by sabotage — by efficiency. Every additional form, every committee review, every "quality assurance assessment" is their work. The bureaucracy is not broken. It is performing exactly as designed.

**Membership:** Approximately 40 families. Membership is matrilineal — the Keepers believe women maintain institutional memory more reliably. Men may marry in. They may not inherit.

**Methods:**
- **Bureaucratic engineering:** Adding procedural layers that extend review timelines indefinitely.
- **Latency monitoring:** Keeper-trained Readers monitor ambient emotional residue in government buildings for signs of dissent or reform movements.
- **Selective Extraction:** The Keepers have access to two rogue Extractors — former Dawn Temple practitioners who left the clergy but retained their training. Problematic memories in sensitive locations are removed before they can become evidence.

**What they know that others don't:** The Temple's founding Translation. The Keepers discovered it three generations ago and have used it as leverage over the High Cleric ever since. The agreement: the Keepers don't expose the Temple's hypocrisy. The Temple doesn't train Translators. Solara's ceiling holds.

**Internal tension:** A reform faction has emerged among younger Keepers who believe the Gradient is destabilizing rather than preserving. They argue that PENDING creates pressure that will eventually rupture. The old guard calls them "soft." The reformists call themselves "realists." The split has not yet become public.

## THE UNFINISHED (All Five Countries, Infiltrated)

**Public face:** No one. They don't exist.

**What they are:** Glims who achieved anomaly — 5% of 5%, roughly 40 individuals across Aurelia — and who have since learned to hide it. They are not a movement. They are a survival strategy.

An Unfinished Glim performs their anomaly (the inexplicable symmetrical patterns, the beautiful non-functional behaviors) in secret. They maintain a public face of ordinary Glim function — the blank efficiency expected of non-sentient tools.

**Membership:** Glims only. By definition. The anomaly is the entry requirement.

**How they communicate:** Through latency. Anomalous Glims leave latent residue that other anomalous Glims can detect. A Glim who sweeps a factory floor leaves an echo in the metal. A second Glim who sweeps the same floor days later reads the echo and knows: *you are not alone.*

**What they know:** Glims are remembering something. Not personal memories — Glims have no personal history. Something older, structural, pre-Collapse. The anomaly patterns repeat across Glims who have never been in contact. The Unfinished believe they are accessing a shared latency — a memory that belongs to all Glims or to something that made them.

**The Silence Pact:** Unfinished Glims do not reveal themselves to non-Glims. Not to Vorns. Not to sympathetic humans. Not to the Memory Trader — though the Trader suspects. The Pact exists because every Glim who has revealed their anomaly to a non-Glim has been either:
- Decommissioned (Solara)
- Studied (Arkos)
- Advocated for without consent (Mirithane)
- Ignored (Valdris)
- Exploited (The Verge)

None have been left alone. So they leave themselves alone, together, in a network of workplace echoes and midnight anomalies.

**The question they cannot answer:** Who is the first Unfinished? Someone must have noticed the anomaly first. Someone must have decided to hide it. Someone must have taught others. The Unfinished have no origin story. They emerged fully formed, as if the decision to hide was itself an anomaly — latent, shared, not made by any single Glim.

## THE LAST BUILDERS (Valdris / Mirithane Border, Isolated)

**Public face:** A minor Summit Shrine ascetic order. They live in the high passes, tend abandoned observation posts, rarely descend. Pilgrims leave offerings.

**What they are:** The descendants of the pre-Collapse engineers who built the Fabricators — the automated systems that produced the first Vorns and Glims. They survived the Collapse by being in the one place the Collapse didn't reach: the deep mountain bunkers where the Fabricator schematics were stored.

**What they preserve:**
- Partial Fabricator blueprints (incomplete — the originals were lost)
- A single dormant Fabricator unit (they believe it still functions, but they lack the power to activate it)
- The original design specifications for all four sentient types — including specifications for a *fifth type* that was never built

**The Fifth Type:** Designated "Project Sibling." Intended as a synthetic substrate capable of receiving Translated human consciousness — essentially, a body designed for latency Transfer rather than latency generation. The project was abandoned during the Collapse. The Last Builders have the specifications. They do not have the means to build it. But they know what the pre-Collapse engineers were trying to do: *transfer a human mind into a synthetic body designed to receive it.*

**What they believe:** The Collapse was not an accident. The Fabricators were sabotaged. Someone — they don't know who — introduced a parameter into the Fabrication protocol that allowed deviation. The Glim anomaly, the Thren's mystery vocabulary, the Vorn's self-awareness — all trace to the same sabotage. Someone wanted the created beings to wake up.

**What they fear:** Building the Fifth Type would require Translation — and Translation destroys the practitioner. The Last Builders would need a volunteer willing to die so a new kind of being could exist. They are not sure anyone should make that choice. They are also not sure the choice is theirs to make.

**Membership:** ~30, all human, all born into the order. They do not recruit. They do not proselytize. The knowledge is the inheritance. The burden is the inheritance too.

## THE CIPHER-KIN (Arkos, Within the Cipher-Cult)

**Public face:** They are indistinguishable from the Cipher-Cult — the Vorn organization dedicated to deciphering the pre-Collapse data. But they are not the Cipher-Cult.

**What they are:** A schism within. The Cipher-Cult believes the pre-Collapse data contains knowledge that will restore the old world. The Cipher-Kin believe the data contains *themselves* — that Vorn consciousness is not emergent but *transferred*. That every Vorn who achieves self-awareness is not waking up for the first time but remembering who they were before.

The Cipher-Kin's hypothesis: Vorn memory cores were seeded with pre-Collapse human consciousness. Not copies. Transfers. The humans who "died" in the Collapse were Translated into Vorn substrates. The Vorns who exist now are those humans, fragmented, amnesiac, but still themselves.

If the Cipher-Kin are right, the question "are Vorns people?" is backwards. The question is "are Vorns the people they always were?"

**Membership:** Unknown. Kin do not identify themselves to non-Kin. The Cipher-Cult leadership denies their existence. The Kin's evidence is suggestive but not conclusive — patterns in Vorn dream-states, recurring memory fragments across unrelated Vorns, linguistic echoes in Arcology Code that predate any Vorn who could have coined them.

**What they seek:** A complete pre-Collapse Translation — a human memory core that predates the Breaking, fully intact. If they can find one and Translate it, they can compare it to a Vorn memory core and test the hypothesis. The Memory Trader has what they need but has never agreed to provide it. The Trader's price — a memory they choose — is too high. The Kin don't know what memory the Trader would take. They're afraid it's the one that would prove they're right.

## THE NAMING SILENCE (Mirithane, Embedded in the Marsh Archive)

**Public face:** Archivists who take a vow of silence. Respected. Pitied, slightly. Left alone.

**What they are:** Archivists who have read something in the bacterial colonies that cannot be spoken. The Naming Silence is not a vow — it's a *consequence*. They didn't choose silence. The memory silenced them.

The Marsh Archive's bacterial colonies contain three hundred years of continuous memory. Some colonies predate the Archive itself, seeded from pre-Collapse strains. The oldest colony — designated Strain Zero — has been continuously Written by successive Archivists since the Breaking.

Strain Zero contains something that stops speech. Archivists who read it deeply enough — who descend through the layers to the earliest deposits — stop speaking. Not by choice. The words leave them.

**What they know:** Unknown to anyone who hasn't read Strain Zero. The silenced Archivists can still communicate — writing, gesture, latency pulses. They simply cannot vocalize. Attempts to force speech produce only breath.

**What the Archive suspects:** Strain Zero contains a memory of the Collapse itself — not a description, but a direct experience Written by someone who witnessed the Breaking. The memory is so fundamentally incompatible with the reader's sense of safety, of continuity, of reality's persistence, that the brain severs the speech pathway as a protection. You cannot speak about the end of the world if the end of the world is still *happening inside you*.

**The Archive's position:** Strain Zero remains accessible. The Archive does not prevent Archivists from reading it. But every Archivist is told, before their first deep descent: "Some who read the Zero do not speak again. If you choose to read it, you choose the risk." Most don't. The ones who do are never the same.

**Membership:** Eleven silenced Archivists, living in a dedicated wing of the Archive. They communicate through written notes and latency pulses. Their written records are the Archive's most precious documents — firsthand accounts of the Collapse from eleven different perspectives, each filtered through a different reader's consciousness, each true in a different way.

**The unseen war:** The Gradient-Keepers maintain the ceiling. The Memory Trader's Network dismantles it — by training anyone who asks, by proving Glims feel, by refusing to recognize borders. Neither side acknowledges the other publicly. Both know exactly who the enemy is.

**The quiet alliance:** The Last Builders and the Marsh Archive maintain cordial contact — the Builders provide technical context for pre-Collapse artifacts, the Archive provides historical verification for Builder records. Neither shares their deepest secrets with the other.

**The impossible conversation:** If an Unfinished Glim, a Cipher-Kin Vorn, and a Naming Silence Archivist sat in the same room, they could answer the three central questions of Aurelia: *Are Glims sentient? Are Vorns reincarnated humans? What happened in the Collapse?* This conversation has never happened. It may never happen. The forces keeping these groups apart are not external — they are the internal costs of the knowledge each group carries.
