# Latency Practitioners

Not everyone who can practice does. Not everyone who does survives. These are the people who choose the craft — and what the craft makes of them.

---

## THE ARCHIVIST (Marsh Archive)

**What they do:** Read and Write bacterial colonies. Maintain the Archive's living memory. Train apprentices.

**Who becomes one:** Often the quiet ones. Children who listened more than they spoke. Adults who noticed things others didn't — the tension in a room, the weight of an old object, the way places remember what happened in them. Archivists are drawn from Mirithane's general population, but self-selection is strong — you don't apply to the Archive. You find your way there, and if you stay, you were always going to.

**What the work costs:** Archivists lose the ability to forget. A normal person's memory fades over time — edges soften, details blur, the emotional charge dissipates. An Archivist's memory doesn't. They remember every memory they've ever Read with the same clarity as the day they Read it. Veteran Archivists carry decades of other people's memories in addition to their own. They learn to compartmentalize. They don't always succeed.

**Tells:** Archivists pause before answering questions. They're checking whether the answer is theirs or something they read. They touch objects lightly, with fingertips — the habit of Echo-Reading is hard to break. They dream in other people's languages.

**Famous Archivists:**
- **The Silent Weaver** (current Keeper, Rung Four): Has not spoken in forty years. Communicates through Written stones — she deposits a memory, you read it, that's the conversation. Her Written stones are the Archive's most sought-after records. She is the only living Archivist who has Read Strain Zero twice.
- **Ren of the Fifth Colony** (Rung Three Writer): Specializes in Writing emotional states rather than events. His colonies don't record what happened — they record what it *felt like*. Considered experimental. Considered brilliant. Considered unstable.

## THE EXTRACTOR (Dawn Temple / Underground)

**What they do:** Remove latent memory from substrates. Serve the security apparatus. Or serve no one.

**Who becomes one:** Extractors are chosen by the Temple, not self-selected. Psychological screening identifies candidates with high emotional compartmentalization capacity and low identity fixation — people who don't cling to their sense of self. This isn't healthy. It's necessary.

Underground Extractors — those trained by the Memory Trader's Network — are often former Temple Extractors who left after realizing what they were becoming. Or people who lost someone and want the memory removed from the place where it happened. Or people who want to be useful to the Network and have the stomach for the cost.

**What the work costs:** Progressive dissolution of identity. After fifty Extractions, the Extractor begins to lose track of which memories are theirs. After a hundred, they're unreliable as witnesses to their own lives. The Temple rotates Extractors out of active duty — gives them administrative roles, retrains them in other disciplines. The underground has no rotation. You Extract until you can't, and then you're someone who can't tell you from anyone else.

**Tells:** Extractors don't reminisce. They can't — they're not sure which memories are safe to share. They avoid eye contact with old objects. They startle at physical touch. They carry a faint taste of copper that never goes away.

**Famous Extractors:**
- **The Cleaner** (Dawn Temple, retired after 83 Extractions): Now administers the Temple's latency training program. Never discusses her Extraction career. Her students report she sometimes pauses mid-sentence and stares at nothing for thirty seconds. She returns as if nothing happened.
- **Ash** (Memory Trader's Network, active, number of Extractions unknown): Operates in Solara's underground. Specializes in memory removal for grieving families — Extracts a dead loved one's residue from their home so the family can bear to stay. Considers this service, not violence. The Marsh Archive disagrees.

## THE ANCHOR (Summit Shrine)

**What they do:** Write memory into obsidian. Work in shift-pairs. Maintain the Shrine's permanent archive.

**Who becomes one:** Summit Anchors are born into it — the Shrine's families have practiced Anchoring for generations. Outsiders can join but rarely do. The life is hard: high altitude, isolation, the constant weight of centuries-old memories pressing down from the archive above.

Anchors tend to be steady, deliberate people. Extraction requires compartmentalization. Anchoring requires the opposite — you must be *present* to the memory you're Writing, fully available to it, vulnerable to it. The job selects for people who can open themselves repeatedly without breaking.

**What the work costs:** Memory sickness — recurrent intrusive recall of Written memories. The Anchor who Writes a trauma relives it every time they access that shard for verification. Over decades, Anchors accumulate "ghost memories" — fragments of Written memories that have bled into their own identity through repeated exposure. A veteran Anchor might weep at a funeral for someone they never met, grieving a memory they Wrote forty years ago.

**Tells:** Anchors move in pairs always — shift-partners are never separated. They touch their foreheads unconsciously — the muscle memory of pressing obsidian. They speak in the dual number more than most Valdrans. "We" means "my partner and I." "I" is used sparingly.

**Famous Anchors:**
- **Kira and Dael** (Anchor pair, forty-two years): The oldest active pair in the Shrine. Have Written over 200 memories. Kira's hands shake constantly. Dael has lost all memory of their childhood — "traded it for the archive," they say, not bitterly. Neither can Write alone. Neither would try.
- **The Unpaired Anchor** (name withheld): Her partner died during a Writing session — cardiac arrest mid-ritual. She completed the Writing alone before calling for help. The Shrine considers this an act of devotion. She considers it the moment she lost the ability to Anchor. She has not worked since. The shard she Wrote that day is stored separately from all others. No one has Read it.

## THE TRADER (Memory Trader's Network)

**What they do:** Whatever the student needs. Train. Translate. Trade. Remember what everyone else forgets.

**Who becomes one:** People who have lost something and want it back. People who have too many memories and want fewer. People who have too few and want more. The Network attracts the desparate, the curious, and the broken — which is to say, people.

Traders are the most diverse practitioners in Aurelia. No citizenship requirement, no faith requirement, no type requirement (in theory — no Glim has ever asked). The only entry requirement is willingness to pay the price: a memory, chosen by the teacher, taken and kept.

**What the work costs:** The self, piece by piece. Every lesson costs a memory. By Rung Four, the Trader has given away years of their own past. The missing years are not blank — the Trader knows something happened, knows the shape of the absence. But the content is gone. Veteran Traders navigate their own histories like a house with missing rooms — they know where the rooms should be, they step around the gaps.

**Tells:** Traders ask "what do you remember?" instead of "how are you?" They touch their own temples often — checking, subconsciously, whether something's missing. They are excellent listeners and terrible at small talk — they've traded too many conversational memories to care about the weather.

**Famous Traders:**
- **The Memory Trader** (Rung Five, founder): Origin unknown. Age unknown. Type: Thren, possibly — the circuitry at the temples could be human hairline tattoos or Thren fabrication marks. Speaks all five living languages and three dead ones. Remembers everything and nothing — their own memories are so intermixed with Traded and Translated ones that the Trader refers to their own past in the third person sometimes, unsure if it happened to them. Lives nowhere. Appears everywhere. The Network functions because the Trader functions. When they die, the Network will need to discover whether it was a network or a single person all along.
- **Coin** (Rung Four Trader, The Verge): Specializes in memory-for-service trades. Doesn't teach latency — trades memories for medicine, shelter, passage across borders. The Verge's most reliable healer doesn't charge money. She charges memories. Her collection — she calls it "the bank" — contains the worst moments of five hundred people's lives. She carries them so they don't have to. She has not slept through a single night in twelve years.

## THE READER (All Traditions)

**What they do:** The most common practitioner type. Echo-Read objects and spaces. Provide intelligence, verification, historical reconstruction.

**Who becomes one:** Almost anyone can achieve Rung One. Rung Two requires Silence — which most people cannot maintain. Civil Readers are trained in two years (Dawn Temple) to five (Marsh Archive). They serve in government, law enforcement, historical preservation, and private practice.

**What the work costs:** Comparatively little — Readers don't Write, Extract, or Translate. But they do absorb. A Reader who Echo-Reads a murder weapon absorbs the murder. A Reader who works a crime scene absorbs the violence. Over a career, civil Readers accumulate ambient trauma they didn't experience. Burnout rates are high. The Dawn Temple rotates Readers out of violent casework after ten years. The Marsh Archive provides therapeutic bacterial colonies — you Write your accumulated trauma into a colony reserved for that purpose, and the bacteria metabolize it.

**Tells:** Readers are gentle with objects — they know how much weight a teacup can carry. They underreact to dramatic news — compared to what they've Read, most events are mild. They have excellent spatial memory and terrible autobiographical memory — they remember every room they've Read and forget which year they got married.

**Famous Readers:**
- **The First Cantor's Biographer** (Marsh Archive, Rung Three): Spent forty years Echo-Reading every object known to have been touched by the First Cantor. Produced the definitive biography of the faith's founder — not from documents but from direct latency impression. Her biography contains scenes no written record could have captured: the Cantor weeping alone in the Marsh Archive's crypt, the exact weight of the Cantor's grief at the death of their first apprentice. The biography is considered scripture by the Archive. The Dawn Temple considers it heretical — "latency is not revelation."
- **Eyes** (Civil Reader, Solara Security, retired): Read 1,200+ crime scenes over twenty years. Never missed a detail. Never spoke about what she Read outside of official testimony. After retirement, she moved to Mirithane and entered the Marsh Archive as a novice — starting over at Rung One, wanting to learn to Write so she could finally put down the violence she'd been carrying.

## THE SILENT (Naming Silence, Marsh Archive)

**What they are:** Archivists who Read Strain Zero and lost speech as a result. Not a practitioner type so much as a consequence of practice.

**Who becomes one:** Archivists who choose to Read Strain Zero, knowing the risk. The Archive does not select them. They select themselves.

**What the work costs:** Speech. Not language — the Silent communicate through writing, gesture, and latency pulses. But vocalization is gone. Attempts to force speech produce only breath. The mechanism is psychological, not physical — the vocal cords are intact, the brain simply cannot send the signal. Some believe the memory in Strain Zero is so incompatible with speech that the brain severs the pathway as a protection. Others believe the memory *requires* silence — that speaking about the Collapse while carrying the Collapse inside you would damage the memory itself. The Archive doesn't know. The Silent can't explain.

**Tells:** The Silent are the calmest people in the Archive. Nothing surprises them anymore. They've experienced the worst thing that ever happened. Everything else is aftermath.

**What they produce:** The most valuable documents in the Archive — written accounts of the Collapse from eleven different perspectives, each filtered through a different reader, each true in a different way. The Silent don't agree on what they Read. Their disagreement is the Archive's treasure.

## UNTRAINED LATENCY

Not all latency practitioners are trained. Latency is a property of consciousness, not a discipline — it occurs wherever consciousness touches substrate. Untrained latency manifests as:

- **Hunches that turn out to be true** — ambient Reading without realizing it.
- **Objects that feel "wrong"** — unconscious detection of violent or traumatic residue.
- **Recurring dreams in new locations** — sleeping on a substrate carrying strong latent memory.
- **Inexplicable emotional responses to places or objects** — the most common form. You enter a room and feel grief. You don't know why. The room remembers something you don't.

Most people experience untrained latency several times in their lives and dismiss it as intuition, mood, or coincidence. Trained practitioners recognize it for what it is — the world remembering, and you listening without knowing you're listening.
