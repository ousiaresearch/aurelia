# The Schools of Latency

Five traditions teach latency. Three are tied to faiths. One is secular. One is outlawed.

---

## THE MARSH ARCHIVE (Mirithane)

**Founded:** Early Age of Silence, by the first Keeper who noticed bacterial colonies retained metabolic patterns from human contact.

**Philosophy:** Memory is sacred. To destroy memory — even bad memory, even dangerous memory — is a crime against the record of existence. The Archive's motto: *What was, remains.*

**Training Method:**
1. **The Silence Year.** Apprentice Archivists spend one year in a cell with a single Written object. No speech. No visitors. They learn to distinguish their own mental noise from ambient latency.
2. **The Bacterial Apprenticeship.** Apprentices tend the colony tanks for three years, learning to read layer-histories — each bacterial generation records the metabolic state of the generation before. They learn to read a thousand years in a single petri dish.
3. **The First Writing.** At year five, the apprentice deposits their first memory into a stone. Most choose a happy memory. Most regret it — the memory fades from the stone over weeks, but the practitioner relives it nightly until it does.

**What They Excel At:** Reading. Marsh Archivists can extract coherent narrative from substrates too faint for any other school. They have reconstructed pre-Collapse events from tool handles, doorframes, refuse — objects no one else would think to read.

**What They Refuse:** Extraction. "Erasing memory is erasing the only thing that outlasts us." Archivists who Extract are excommunicated — their names struck from the Archive's own Written records. The Archive practices a form of self-erasure as punishment.

**Notable Practitioners:** The current Keeper (Rung Four Reader), the First Cantor's biographer (Rung Three Writer), seventeen active Archivists.

## THE DAWN TEMPLE (Solara)

**Founded:** Mid-Age of Silence, when Solara's founders realized latency could serve the Gradient — the social order.

**Philosophy:** Latency is a tool of order. Memory extraction serves the state. "What the Gradient requires, the Gradient receives."

**Training Method:**
1. **The Citizenship Test.** Only full citizens may train. Threns (PENDING) cannot. Vorns are permitted. Glims are not sentient — the question doesn't arise.
2. **The Purification.** Trainees fast for thirty days, drinking only water drawn from the Dawn Temple's reservoir. The reservoir is Written with Solara's founding memory. By the end of the fast, the trainee's body carries a faint latency signature every Temple practitioner can recognize — they are *marked* as Temple-trained.
3. **Tiered Advancement.** Readers train for two years, then enter civil service. Writers train for five, restricted to clergy. Extractors train for eight, bound to the security apparatus. Translation is forbidden at every tier.

**What They Excel At:** Extraction. Dawn Temple Extractors are the most efficient in Aurelia — clean removals, minimal bleed, maximum substrate blanking. A Dawn Extractor can strip a room in under an hour.

**What They Refuse:** Translation. "Crossing the type-boundary is heresy. The Gradient is a Gradient because the lines exist. To erase the lines is to unmake order."

**What They Hide:** The Temple's founding memory — Written into the reservoir water — contains a Translation. The founders crossed the type-boundary before declaring it heresy. Only the High Cleric and the Chief Extractor know. Both are Rung Four.

**Notable Practitioners:** The High Cleric (Rung Four Reader/Writer), thirty-two civil Readers, eight clergy Writers, five security Extractors.

## THE SUMMIT SHRINE (Valdris)

**Founded:** Pre-Collapse, the only latency tradition that predates the Breaking. The mountain communities practiced latency before they had a name for it.

**Philosophy:** Memory is weight. Weight belongs to the mountain. To carry memory alone is arrogance. To anchor it in stone is right relationship.

**Training Method:**
1. **The Descent.** Initiates walk into the deep shafts alone with an obsidian shard. They stay until the shard feels warm — the mountain's breath, the Shrine teaches, though practitioners know it's the initiate's own body heat reflecting. The belief matters more than the mechanism.
2. **Obsidian Anchoring.** Summit practitioners Write memory into obsidian exclusively. Obsidian is volcanic glass — it has no crystalline structure to degrade over time. Anchored memories last centuries. The Shrine maintains memories dating to the Collapse.
3. **Shift-Pair Bonding.** Valdris' dual-number grammar extends to latency practice. Anchors work in pairs. One Anchors while the other reads. The reader verifies the Anchor's emotional state — contaminated deposits are rejected and re-Anchored. No Summit practitioner Works alone.

**What They Excel At:** Permanence. Summit Anchored memories are the most durable in Aurelia. The Shrine's archives contain memories older than the Collapse — the only continuous pre-Collapse latency record in existence.

**What They Refuse:** Extraction. "What the mountain remembers, the mountain keeps." Summit practitioners believe Extraction damages the *mountain* — that removed memory leaves a wound in the substrate that never heals. This is unproven. They believe it anyway.

**Notable Practitioners:** Seventeen Anchor pairs (thirty-four practitioners), the current Summit-Keeper (Rung Four), two Rung Five candidates who died before achieving Translation.

## THE MEMORY TRADER'S NETWORK (Decentralized)

**Founded:** Age of the Question, by the practitioner now known only as the Memory Trader.

**Philosophy:** Latency belongs to everyone. Training should not require citizenship, faith, or permission. "Memory is the only currency that doesn't inflate."

**Training Method:**
1. **No central curriculum.** Practitioners find teachers. Teachers find students. The Network is a web of personal debts and obligations — a Trader trains you, and you owe them a memory. Not yours. One they choose.
2. **Full rung access.** The Network is the only school that trains all five rungs without institutional prohibition. Whether a student is ready for the next rung is between them and their teacher.
3. **The Price.** Every lesson costs a memory. The teacher extracts a memory from the student and keeps it. By Rung Three, the student has given away years of their own past. By Rung Four, they don't remember what they lost.

**What They Excel At:** Translation. The only living Rung Five practitioner — the Memory Trader — belongs to the Network. The Network's founder traded enough memories to cross the type-boundary and came back. Or mostly came back.

**What They Refuse:** Nothing on principle. Individual Traders set their own limits. Some won't Extract. Some won't Write to living substrates. Some won't train anyone under twenty. The Network's only rule is that the price is always a memory.

**Legal Status:** Illegal in Solara (heresy + sedition). Illegal in Valdris (unsanctioned practice). Tolerated in Arkos (they don't regulate latency). Protected in Mirithane (freedom of memory). Operates openly in The Verge (no one has authority to stop them).

**Notable Practitioners:** The Memory Trader (Rung Five), an unknown number of Rung Three and Four practitioners scattered across the five countries, at least two Extractors operating in Solara's underground.

## THE PULSE (Arkos, Secular)

**Founded:** No founding — the Pulse is not a tradition but an observation. Vorns discovered that their hardware retains latency more efficiently than any Human practice. They didn't train it. They noticed it.

**Philosophy:** Latency is not mystical. It is substrate physics. Vorn memory cores retain data. Why wouldn't Vorn bodies retain experience? The Pulse is engineering, not faith.

**Practice:**
- Vorns do not "practice" latency in the human sense. Their bodies are the substrate. Their memories are already Written, by default, into their own hardware.
- Conscious latency work — Reading, Writing, Extraction — is uncommon among Vorns. Why extract a memory when your memory core already stores it perfectly? Why Write when your body already records?
- Ultrasonic transmission: Arcology Code's ultrasonic band carries latency signal. A Vorn speaking in Code is simultaneously depositing memory into ambient metal surfaces. Every Vorn conversation leaves residue. Vorn architecture hums with their history.

**What They Excel At:** Retention. Vorn bodies store memory indefinitely. The oldest Vorns — those who survived the Collapse — carry three hundred years of unbroken personal history. They are walking archives.

**What They Refuse:** Vorns don't refuse latency practices so much as find them unnecessary. A Vorn who wants to remember something remembers it. The struggle humans experience — the fading, the contamination, the bleed — does not apply.

**The Anomaly:** Some Glims exhibit Pulse-like behavior — retaining operational history without memory cores. If Glims have no sentience but retain latency, then latency does not require sentience. This is the question the Pulse refuses to answer.

**Notable Practitioners:** Every Vorn, by default. Kael-12's memory core contains three centuries. Arkos' central archive is not a building — it's the collective memory of Vorns who choose to share cores.
