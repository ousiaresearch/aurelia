# Latency Techniques

The rungs describe capacity. The techniques describe application. A Rung Three Writer knows *how* to write — the techniques are *what* they write and *why*.

---

## ECHO-READING (All Rung Two+)

The foundational technique: physical contact with a substrate, quiet mind, receptive state. The practitioner lets ambient latency resolve into impression.

**Procedure:**
1. Hold the object in both hands. Stillness. No gloves — skin contact matters for organic substrates, less for metal and crystal.
2. Empty the mind. Marsh Archivists call this "the Silence Year." Dawn practitioners call it "the Purification." The principle is identical: your own emotional noise contaminates the read.
3. Wait for the first impression. It will not be visual — synesthetic, bodily, emotional. A weight in the chest. A taste. A color behind the eyelids.
4. Hold the impression without naming it. Naming fixes the read — introduces the reader's interpretation. Good Echo-Readers learn to suspend naming until the impression resolves on its own.

**Variants:**
- **Ambient Reading:** No object. The practitioner reads the room. Less precise but useful for detecting recent emotional residue — violence, grief, joy, decision.
- **Depth Reading:** Sustained contact over hours or days. The practitioner descends through "layers" of latency — most recent first, then older, then oldest. Depth Readers can reach pre-Collapse memories in old enough substrates.
- **Cross-Reading:** Reading a substrate that was in contact with another substrate that was in contact with a person. Two degrees of separation. Less reliable. Used by Marsh Archivists for objects too fragile to handle directly.

## ANCHORING (Summit Shrine Writing variant)

Writing memory into obsidian. Obsidian is preferred for its amorphous structure — no crystal lattice to degrade, no grain to scatter the signal.

**Procedure:**
1. The Anchor holds the obsidian shard in their dominant hand. The shift-partner holds their off-hand.
2. The Anchor recalls the memory to be Written with full sensory detail — not a summary, but the event itself, relived. Heart rate rises. Skin temperature changes. The partner monitors.
3. At peak recall, the Anchor presses the shard to their forehead. The memory transfers.
4. The partner reads the shard immediately to verify: Is the memory whole? Is it contaminated with the Anchor's present emotional state? If contaminated, the deposit is rejected. The shard is discarded. The Anchor tries again.

**Duration:** A single Anchoring takes twenty minutes to two hours depending on the complexity of the memory.

**Cost:** The Anchor relives the memory during Writing and cannot Write a memory they cannot relive. Traumatic memories produce traumatic Writing sessions. Summit Anchors have high rates of what the Shrine calls "memory sickness" — recurrent intrusive recall of memories they've Written multiple times.

**Permanence:** Summit Anchored memories last centuries. The oldest Anchored memory in the Shrine's archives is dated to approximately 40 years pre-Collapse — a Valdris elder's memory of the first Fabricator's arrival.

## BACTERIAL WRITING (Marsh Archive technique)

Writing memory into living bacterial colonies. The only latency technique that uses a living substrate — and the only one where the memory *evolves* over time.

**Procedure:**
1. The Archivist cultivates a colony from the Archive's master strain — a pre-Collapse bacteria preserved through the Breaking.
2. The Archivist enters the Water Temple's immersion chamber: waist-deep water at body temperature, colony tank suspended before them.
3. Hands in the water, touching the colony surface. The Archivist recalls the memory. The bacteria absorb it metabolically — their growth patterns shift to encode the memory's emotional signature.
4. Over weeks, the bacteria reproduce. Each generation carries the Written memory plus its own metabolic response to it. After a hundred generations, the colony is no longer a record of the original memory. It's a record of *what the memory meant* to a hundred generations of bacteria.

**Duration:** Initial Writing: one hour. Full colony maturation: three to six months.

**Cost:** The Archivist must remain emotionally available to the memory throughout the Writing. Suppression contaminates the colony. The Archivist cannot protect themselves from what they're Writing.

**What It Produces:** A living record that changes over time. Marsh Archivists consider this a feature — the Archive is not a library of static memories but a living ecosystem of how memories are *experienced*. A three-hundred-year-old colony contains not just the original memory but three hundred years of bacterial response to it.

## EXTRACTION (Dawn Temple / Memory Trader's Network)

The removal of latent memory from a substrate. The substrate is left blank. The memory moves into the practitioner.

**Procedure (Dawn Temple method):**
1. The Extractor enters the space alone. All other personnel evacuate — ambient emotional noise complicates the extraction.
2. Full physical contact with the primary substrate. If a room, the Extractor lies on the floor, arms spread. If an object, both hands.
3. The Extractor invokes the memory they intend to remove — a specific event, a specific person, a specific period. Precision matters. Unfocused extraction risks removing adjacent memories or, in extreme cases, the entire latency history of the substrate.
4. The memory flows into the Extractor. This is not a metaphor — practitioners report a physical sensation of heat, pressure, or weight settling into their body.
5. The substrate is tested: a Reader confirms the target memory is gone. Adjacent memories should remain intact.

**Duration:** 30 minutes to 4 hours depending on substrate age and memory depth.

**Cost (immediate):** The Extractor now carries a memory that is not theirs. It will surface in dreams, in idle moments, in emotional responses to situations the Extractor has never experienced.

**Cost (cumulative):** After approximately fifty Extractions, the practitioner begins to lose the ability to distinguish personal memory from Extracted memory. After a hundred, most cannot tell you which events in their life actually happened to them. Veteran Dawn Extractors are rotated out of active duty after eighty Extractions — not for their health, but because an Extractor who cannot distinguish truth from Extraction is unreliable as a witness.

**Illegal variant:** Forced Extraction from a living subject. The Marsh Archive considers this a capital crime. The Dawn Temple authorizes it for state security but does not publicly acknowledge the practice. The Memory Trader's Network refuses to teach it — "trading is consent. Theft is not trading."

## TRANSLATION (Memory Trader's Network only)

Moving memory across type boundaries. The only technique that requires Rung Five.

**Procedure:** Unknown in full. The Memory Trader has never documented the method. What is known:

1. Translation requires the practitioner to hold both the source memory and the target substrate's *empty space* — the latency vacuum that will receive it.
2. The practitioner cannot act as a passive conduit. They must *understand* the memory from both perspectives simultaneously — human and Vorn, Vorn and Glim, living and dead.
3. During Translation, the practitioner's own identity becomes permeable. The boundary between "the memory I'm translating" and "the memory I am" collapses.

**What the Memory Trader has said:** "You don't move the memory. You become the bridge, and the memory walks across you. Some of it stays. You stop counting which parts."

**Known Translations:**
- The Glim Testimony: the Trader Translated a Glim's experience into human-readable impression. The resulting record — shown to the Marsh Archive — was the first evidence that Glims experience something latency can detect.
- The Vorn Requiem: a dying Vorn's final memory core was Translated into a human volunteer who then Wrote it into obsidian. The memory is stored at the Summit Shrine. The volunteer did not survive the process — not physically, but psychologically. She stopped responding to her own name within a week.
- Rumored: the Trader has Translated a human memory into a Glim. No verification exists.

**Cost:** Ego death — temporary during the Translation, cumulative over a career. The Memory Trader is no longer certain which of their memories are originals, which are Translations they've carried, and which belong to people who never existed but emerged from the accumulated residue of too many Translations.

## THE PULSE-READ (Arkos, Vorn-native)

Not a taught technique — an inherent capability. Vorns read latency through their hardware directly. No training required, but refinement is possible.

**Variant — Core-Sharing:** Two Vorns temporarily bridge memory cores. Each experiences the other's latency history as their own. Used in Arkos for dispute resolution — a Vorn accused of wrongdoing opens their core to the accuser. The accuser experiences the memory of the event. There is no testimony, only direct experience. Arkos' legal system is built on Core-Sharing. The system works because lying is impossible. The system breaks when the accuser experiences something they cannot reconcile.

**Variant — The Pulse Silence:** Vorns can suppress their latency output — the ultrasonic signal that writes ambient memory into surrounding metal. Most don't. Suppressing the Pulse is considered antisocial in Arkos. A Vorn who mutes their Pulse is hiding something. Human practitioners cannot detect the Pulse at all — the signal is ultrasonic. Other Vorns always know.

## SILENCE (All traditions, Rung One prerequisite)

The technique that precedes all others: the ability to distinguish one's own mental noise from ambient latency. Without Silence, a practitioner reads their own emotions and believes they are reading the world.

**Training:** Varies by school. The Marsh Archive's year of isolation. The Dawn Temple's thirty-day fast. The Summit Shrine's deep shaft descent. All pursue the same goal: the practitioner must learn what *their own mind* sounds like before they can hear anything else.

**Failure mode:** An un-Silenced practitioner contaminates every read, every Write, every Extraction with their own emotional state. A Dawn Extractor who hasn't achieved Silence doesn't remove memories — they overwrite them with their own.
