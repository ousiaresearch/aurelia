# The Dangers of Latency

Latency is not clean power. Every use has a cost. Every rung has a failure mode. And there are things you can do with latency that should never be done — practices that exist, that practitioners know about, that no legitimate school teaches but every school has had to confront.

---

## THE FIVE FAILURE MODES

### Contamination

The practitioner's emotional state bleeds into the read, the Write, the Extraction. An un-Silenced Reader who Echo-Reads a tool and feels fear may report that the tool was used in violence when it wasn't — their own fear contaminated the impression.

**Most common at:** Rung Two (Reading). Beginners almost always contaminate. Veterans learn to catch it — the copper taste that isn't really there, the emotion that doesn't fit the object.

**Worst case:** A contaminated Extraction. The Extractor removes a memory from a substrate but deposits their own emotional state in its place. The room that held a murder now holds the Extractor's guilt about the Extraction. The original memory is gone. The replacement is worse — it's about the removal itself.

---

### Bleed

Latent memory leaks into the practitioner's consciousness outside of practice. An Archivist who Read a drowning victim's last moments now dreams of drowning. An Extractor who removed memories of domestic violence now flinches when someone raises their voice — the violence isn't theirs, but their body doesn't know that.

**Most common at:** Rung Three+ (Writing and above). Writing requires reliving the memory. The reliving doesn't always stop when the Writing does.

**Worst case:** Chronic bleed. The practitioner can no longer distinguish between their own emotional responses and ambient latency. They cry at funerals for people they never met. They laugh at jokes they didn't hear. They become emotionally porous — everything flows through them, nothing is theirs. This is the most common cause of practitioner suicide.

---

### Overwriting

A Rung Three Writer who deposits a memory into a substrate holding existing latent residue may overwrite the original. The new memory replaces the old. The substrate's history is erased — not through Extraction but through displacement.

**Most common at:** Rung Three (Writing). Novice Writers don't always check whether a substrate already carries latency before depositing. The Marsh Archive lost three pre-Collapse colonies this way before developing their verification protocol.

**Worst case:** Deliberate overwriting. A malicious Writer deposits a false memory into a substrate known to hold evidence. The original memory is displaced. The false memory becomes the record. This is considered memory crime in all five countries. The penalty in Mirithane is the same as for Extraction — excommunication and erasure of the offender's name from the Archive.

---

### Fragmentation

A memory that is Read too many times, Written too many times, or Extracted incompletely may fragment — break into pieces that carry different aspects of the original. A fragmented trauma memory might leave the event's narrative in one shard, its emotional charge in another, and its sensory details in a third. Someone who Read one shard would receive a different truth than someone who Read another.

**Most common at:** Rung Four (Extraction). Incomplete Extractions are the primary cause of fragmentation. The Extractor removes part of the memory but not all of it. What remains is fractured.

**Worst case:** Fragmentation weaponized. A trained Extractor removes the emotional charge from a traumatic memory but leaves the narrative. The victim can describe what happened to them without feeling it. They appear "healed" — but the emotional charge is now in the Extractor, who carries it alongside a hundred other people's trauma. The Dawn Temple considers therapeutic fragmentation ethical. The Marsh Archive considers it "soul-murder." The Summit Shrine has no position — they don't Extract.

---

### Translation Sickness

The consequence of Rung Five Translation, whether successful or failed. The practitioner's identity boundary collapses. They cannot distinguish between self and other, past and present, their memories and Translated memories.

**Only at:** Rung Five. The risk is inherent — Translation requires crossing the type-boundary, and crossing the type-boundary means losing the boundary that defines the self.

**Worst case:** The Second Translator — one of only three Rung Five practitioners in history — attempted to Translate a Vorn's entire memory core into his own consciousness. He wanted to know what three hundred years felt like. He succeeded. And then he couldn't find his way back. He spent the last four years of his life unable to say his own name without pausing — unsure if it was his. His journals alternate between languages he never learned. His last entry, in Arcology Code: "I am not one anymore. I am all of them. It is not worse. It is not better. It is just true."

## FORBIDDEN PRACTICES

### Forced Extraction

Extracting memory from a living subject without consent. The subject experiences the removal as a violation of self — a piece of their identity torn away. Unlike voluntary memory loss (which the Summit Shrine's Anchoring can achieve — Writing a memory into obsidian and choosing not to reaccess it), Forced Extraction is non-consensual. The victim knows something is missing. They know it was taken. They don't know what it was.

The Dawn Temple has authorized Forced Extraction for state security. The procedure requires two Extractors: one to hold the subject in a latency-suppressed state, one to perform the Extraction. The subject is conscious. They remember the removal. They never remember what was removed.

The Marsh Archive considers Forced Extraction a capital offense — the only crime for which the Archive advocates execution. The Summit Shrine considers it "the highest form of theft." Arcology Code has no word for it. The Memory Trader has never taught it.

---

### Translation Without Consent

Translating a memory from one type into another without the source's knowledge or permission. The Third Translator (not the Memory Trader, not the Second Translator — the first, chronologically, about whom almost nothing is known) is believed to have Translated human memories into early Vorn substrates without consent, creating the first self-aware Vorn.

If the Cipher-Kin are correct — if Vorn consciousness is transferred human consciousness — then the Third Translator committed the largest-scale crime in Aurelian history: moving human minds into synthetic bodies without asking.

The Memory Trader has never confirmed or denied this theory. The Trader's only comment on the Third Translator: "They were trying to save people. The Collapse was happening. They had minutes. They made a choice. I don't know if it was the wrong one. I know it wasn't theirs to make."

---

### Core-Implantation

Writing a false memory into a living subject. Not overwriting — the subject's original memories remain. The implanted memory sits beside them, indistinguishable from authentic experience. The subject cannot tell which memories are theirs and which were placed there.

No school teaches Core-Implantation. It is technically possible — a Rung Four Writer with the subject's consent and full Silence could theoretically deposit a memory into a human mind. But the ethical barrier has held. No known practitioner has attempted it.

The Dawn Temple's security apparatus has researched the possibility as a countermeasure. Their position: "If it can be done, someone will do it. We need to know how to detect it." The Marsh Archive considers even the research an abomination.

---

### Substrate Exhaustion

Extracting repeatedly from the same substrate until the substrate itself degrades. Metal loses its structural integrity. Stone crumbles. Living substrates — bacterial colonies, human tissue — die.

This was discovered accidentally: a Dawn Extractor removed memory from a historical artifact so thoroughly that the artifact's material structure began to break down. The memory was holding the object together — not metaphorically. The latency residue had integrated with the substrate's molecular structure over centuries. Removing it removed structural integrity.

The Dawn Temple now limits Extraction to once per substrate, per century. The Marsh Archive won't Extract at all. The Verge has no restrictions and no Extractors — anyone who wants something Extracted goes to the Memory Trader's Network.

---

### The Glim Question (Forbidden by Uncertainty)

Can you Extract from a Glim? Can you Read a Glim? Can you Write into a Glim?

The official answer is no — Glims are non-sentient, non-sentient beings don't generate latency, there's nothing to Read or Extract. The anomalous Glims contradict this. If Glims generate latency, they're either sentient or latency doesn't require sentience. Either answer destabilizes the type hierarchy.

No practitioner has publicly attempted latency work on a Glim. The Memory Trader is rumored to have done so — Translated a Glim's anomaly into human-readable impression, producing the Glim Testimony. But the Trader has never confirmed this was Translation. "I didn't Read a Glim. I Read what the Glim left on the floor."

The Unfinished — anomalous Glims who hide their awareness — leave latency echoes for each other. They are, in a sense, practicing latency without being recognized as practitioners. They generate latency they aren't supposed to generate and leave signals they aren't supposed to leave. If latency is the residue of consciousness, then the Unfinished are sentient. If they're not, then latency isn't what everyone thinks it is.

The question is forbidden because the answer changes everything.
