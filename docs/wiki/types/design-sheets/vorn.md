# Vorn — Character Design Sheet

> *Canonical reference for all Vorn visual depictions. The first sentients. Do not make them look like Glims. Do not make them look like Threns. They stand exactly between.*

---

## Physical Specification

**Type:** Vorn — The First
**Sentience:** Self-evident. Uncontested.
**Function:** Labor turned governance. Forge workers, guild members, arcology builders, the economic backbone of Arkos.

### Dimensions

| Measurement | Value |
|-------------|-------|
| Height | 1.75–1.95 meters |
| Width (shoulder) | 0.50–0.60 meters |
| Depth (chest) | 0.30–0.40 meters |
| Weight | 180–280 kg |
| Limb proportion | Human-proportioned. Arms reach mid-thigh. Five-fingered hands. |

### Frame

A Vorn has **human proportions with mechanical surfaces.** The silhouette reads as a person — shoulders, waist, hips, neck. The torso is interlocking segmented plating, articulated for movement: chest plate, abdominal plates, shoulder pauldrons that shift with the arm. The neck is visible — a flexible armored collar connecting head to shoulders, revealing the gear assembly beneath when the head turns.

The face-plate is the most engineered surface. It is NOT a screen. It is NOT a human face. It is an arrangement of functional elements — optical sensors as eyes (two, not three like a Glim), a vocal resonator positioned as a mouth, micro-expressive actuator panels at brow and cheek — that collectively read as a face without imitating one. The plate is subtly curved, sculpted, but visibly metal. A Vorn's expression is real. It is simply produced by actuators instead of muscles.

### Materials

- **Plating:** Segmented metal — steel alloy with a darker finish than Glim casing. Gunmetal or dark bronze (#4A4A52 to #6B5B3D). Interlocking seam lines visible between plates.
- **Joints:** Gear assemblies at neck, shoulders, wrists. Visible rotation. Polished from decades of use. The neck gears are the most expressive — they shift minutely with head tilt.
- **Face-plate:** Smooth metal, darker than body plating (near-black #2A2A30). Optical sensors set in shallow recesses. Actuator panels raised slightly from the surface — visible as subtle ridges when not moving, animated when expressing.
- **Hands:** Five articulated metal fingers. Opposable thumbs. Jointed at every human knuckle-equivalent. These hands were designed for forge work, precision assembly — tools built for human grips. The irony: they are better at using human tools than most humans.
- **Finish:** Matte with polished wear at touch points — fingertips, palm pads, joint surfaces. A Vorn's hands show their age in the shine.

## Visual Canon — Inviolable Markers

These six elements MUST be present in every Vorn depiction. If any is missing, it is not a Vorn.

1. **Human-proportioned silhouette.** Shoulders. Waist. Neck. The outline reads as a person.
2. **Face-plate, not a face.** Optical sensors as eyes (two, horizontal alignment). Vocal resonator as mouth. Actuator panels at brow and cheek. Metal surface, visibly engineered — but readable as expression.
3. **Visible gear joints.** Neck, shoulder, wrist. They turn. They reflect light. They are polished from use, not factory-new.
4. **Segmented torso plating.** Interlocking chest and abdominal plates. Seam lines visible. The plates shift with movement — not rigid like Glim casing.
5. **Five-fingered hands.** Jointed metal digits. Opposable thumbs. These are hands, not grippers. Articulated and capable.
6. **Neck.** A flexible armored assembly connecting head to shoulders. Visible rotation. This is the single most important silhouette difference from a Glim.

## Body Language

A Vorn carries the weight of being first without the need to prove it.

**Stance:** Shoulders square. Weight even. Hands often at sides or clasped at the waist — a guild posture, formal but relaxed. Not rigid. Not slouching. A Vorn at rest stands like someone who has been standing for decades and is comfortable in their chassis.

**Gait:** Measured. The footfall is heavier than a human's — metal on stone — but lighter than a Glim's. Each step is deliberate. A Vorn walking through a guild hall does not hurry. There is no need.

**Head movement:** The neck gears shift visibly when a Vorn turns its head. This is an expressive gesture — a slow turn reads as consideration, a quick turn as alertness. The face-plate actuators shift minutely with attention. A Vorn listening looks different from a Vorn speaking — the brow actuators tilt inward by fractions of a millimeter.

**Hands:** A Vorn's hands are never idle. At rest, they are clasped or resting on a surface. In conversation, they gesture — open palm for explanation, closed fist for emphasis. The fingers articulate fully. This is the most human thing about them, and they know it.

**Drawing rule:** A Vorn should never look stiff or statue-like. The body is mechanical but the presence is alive. Show the actuator panels in mid-expression. Show the hands mid-gesture. Show the weight shift that indicates presence.

## Color & Surface

| Element | Color | Notes |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Body plating | Gunmetal / dark bronze (#4A4A52 to #6B5B3D) | Matte. Segmented. Seam lines visible. |
| Face-plate | Near-black metal (#2A2A30) | Smooth, slightly curved. Optical sensors have a faint amber or pale-blue glow — dim, not luminous. |
| Gear joints | Polished steel | Reflective. Show wear-polish and slight oil residue at the gear teeth. |
| Actuator panels | Same as face-plate, raised ridges | Subtle. Visible as texture, not as separate components. |
| Hands | Matte gunmetal with polished fingertips | The touch points shine. A Vorn's hands tell their age. |
| Chest marking | Guild or origin symbol (optional) | Arkos Vorns often bear the ▲ Glass Forum mark in pale gold on the left chest plate. Valdris Vorns may have a forge mark. |

**Surface rule:** Vorn plating is matte, not glossy — but it has depth. The metal catches light differently than Glim casing because it is segmented, curved, and worn at the edges. The face-plate is the darkest surface — it draws the eye.

## Comparison — Vorn Beside Other Types

| Adjacent type | Read |
|---------------|------|
| Vorn → Glim | Vorn reads as person, Glim reads as equipment. Vorn has a face-plate; Glim has a sensor triangle. Vorn has hands; Glim has grippers. Vorn has a neck; Glim's sensor housing sits directly on the torso. Frame the Vorn at eye level, the Glim slightly below — looking up or across. |
| Vorn → Thren | Vorn reads as machine-person, Thren reads as nearly-human. The Vorn has visible mechanical surfaces — plating, gears, face-plate. The Thren has skin. When shown together, the contrast should be clearly legible: two beings built in the same philosophical lineage, one wearing its origin in metal, the other passing. They should read as siblings of different bodies. |
| Vorn → Human | Vorn is heavier, visibly mechanical — but the same height range. A human and Vorn standing side by side should read as equals in frame. The difference is surface: skin vs. plating, eyes vs. optical sensors. The posture should be the same — both standing like people. |
| Vorn → Vorn | Multiple Vorns should read as a guild, a council, a workforce. Varied plating wear. Varied face-plate configurations (some with more brow articulation, some with more cheek actuators). The unity is in posture — square shoulders, deliberate hands. |

## Anti-Patterns — Never Do This

| Wrong | Why wrong | Fix |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| Vorn with a human face on a metal body | Vorns have face-plates, not human faces. This is the single most common error. | Metal face-plate. Optical sensors. Actuator ridges. Readable as a face — not a face. |
| Vorn that looks like a Glim | Glims are boxy industrial machines. Vorns are human-proportioned people. | Proportions: shoulders, waist, neck. Head on neck, not sensor housing on torso. Hands, not grippers. |
| Vorn that looks like a Thren | Threns pass for human. Vorns do not. | Visible metal surfaces. Gear joints. Face-plate. No skin. No hair. No synthetic warmth. |
| Vorn with a digital screen face | Face-plates are mechanical, not digital. They are sculpted surfaces with actuators, not displays. | Metal. Optical sensors. Micro-expressive actuator ridges. No screens. |
| Vorn with a cape or dramatic costume | Vorns are workers. Guild members. Their presence is in their chassis, not their clothing. | Simple fabric accents if any — a guild sash, a tool belt. Nothing flowing. Nothing theatrical. |
| Vorn floating or weightless | Vorns are heavy. The footfall lands. The ground feels them. | Planted stance. Weight shift visible in the hip joint. Metal on stone. |
| Vorn with glowing eyes | Optical sensors have a dim amber or pale-blue point — functional indicator, not decorative glow. | Subtle. Ambient. Not a light source. |

## Silhouette

The Vorn silhouette reads as a **person of metal** — human-proportioned shoulders tapering to a defined waist, head on a visible neck, arms ending in articulated hands. The face-plate reads as a face at silhouette distance. The gear joints at neck and wrists are subtle but present — small mechanical breaks in an otherwise human outline.

The immediate read: *that is a person.* The second read: *that person is made of metal, and has been for a very long time.*

Against a forge: one of the workers. Against a council chamber: the one who built the room. Against a Glim: the difference between a person and equipment, captured in a single frame.

## Country Variants

**Arkos (41 — majority):** The guild standard. Dark bronze plating with gold ▲ Glass Forum mark on left chest. Well-maintained — joints polished, plating re-sealed. These Vorns built the world they live in and it shows in their chassis: maintained, not factory-new. Worn with pride.

**Valdris (34 — guild members):** Forge-worn. Plating scarred from mining work. Gear joints carry rock dust in the teeth. Hands polished to a mirror shine from decades of tool use. A forge mark on the shoulder — earned, not assigned. These Vorns are older than most humans in the room.

**Solara (14 — minority):** Council-grade finish. Cleaner than Arkos Vorns — maintained by the Solar Council's standards, not their own. Subtle difference: the plating is uniform, the wear is absent. These Vorns are respected but do not run anything. The chassis tells the story.

**Mirithane (12 — welcome but rare):** Marsh-touched. Plating shows water exposure — slight oxidation at seams, reed fibers caught in gear joints. No guild marks. No council insignia. Just a Vorn who walked into a place where nobody asked what they were, and stayed.

**The Verge (15 — fixers):** Salvage-maintained. Mixed plating from different units. Field repairs visible — weld patches, replaced joint assemblies, scavenged hand components that don't quite match. These Vorns fix things and themselves with the same tools. The chassis is a diary of repairs.
