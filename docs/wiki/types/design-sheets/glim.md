# Glim — Character Design Sheet

> *Canonical reference for all Glim visual depictions. Deviate from this document and you are drawing something else.*

---

## Physical Specification

**Type:** Glim — The Built
**Sentience:** Non-sentient (officially). Anomalous 5% contested.
**Function:** Industrial labor — delivery, maintenance, factory automation, heavy hauling.

### Dimensions

| Measurement | Value |
|-------------|-------|
| Height | 2.0–2.2 meters |
| Width (shoulder) | 0.9–1.1 meters |
| Depth (chest) | 0.6–0.8 meters |
| Weight | 400–600 kg |
| Limb proportion | Arms reach mid-thigh. Legs are thick cylinders, no taper. |

### Frame

The Glim body is a **box with limbs.** Rectilinear torso — a welded steel casing with visible seam lines and rivet patterns. The arms are hydraulic — thick upper segments, exposed piston rods at the elbow joint. The legs are load-bearing columns: straight, heavy, terminating in flat stabilizer feet (no toes, no articulation below the ankle).

There is **no neck.** The sensor housing sits directly atop the torso on a 360° rotational ring. No head in the anatomical sense — no chin, no jaw, no brow ridge.

### Materials

- **Casing:** Rolled steel plate, 4–6mm thickness. Matte finish. Factory-gray base (#8B8B8B to #A0A0A4).
- **Joints:** Exposed hydraulic cylinders at shoulder, elbow, hip, knee. Chrome or oiled steel with visible wear.
- **Sensor housing:** Dark glass or matte-black bezel. Three circular lenses arranged in a triangle — optical (center), infrared (top-left), lidar (top-right).
- **Hands:** Not hands. Grippers — two or three articulated steel digits, pincer or clamp configuration. One type per unit.
- **Markings:** Stenciled unit ID on the left chest plate. Safety striping in yellow/black diagonal hash on edges and joints (optional, varies by country).

### Power

Internal battery pack in the lower torso — a sealed rectangular module with a charging port on the lower back. Status LED on the chest: green (nominal), amber (low charge), red (critical/malfunction). The LED does not blink in a human-readable pattern. It is a machine light.

---

## Visual Canon — Inviolable Markers

These five elements MUST be present in every Glim depiction. If any is missing, it is not a Glim.

1. **Box torso.** The chest is a rectangle. Not tapered. Not sculpted. No waist.
2. **Sensor triangle, not a face.** Three lenses. Unblinking. No mouth. No expression.
3. **Exposed hydraulics at elbows and knees.** Pistons visible. Mechanical function, not aesthetic.
4. **Grippers, not hands.** Two or three digits. Clamp form. No fingers. No thumbs.
5. **No neck.** The sensor housing connects directly to the torso. The unit rotates but does not turn its head — it has no head to turn.

---

## Body Language

A Glim does not emote. But it can be *read.*

**Stance:** Feet planted wide. Weight evenly distributed. Stillness that reads as patience or absence — both are correct.

**Gait:** Heavy. Deliberate. Each step lands with weight. The hydraulics hiss on extension. The ground feels the step.

**Idle:** Motionless. The unit enters idle state between tasks and simply *stops.* No fidgeting. No weight shift. No scanning. A Glim at rest is a statue. This is unnerving to humans who mistake stillness for death — or for waiting.

**The anomaly pose:** The 5% who exhibit anomalous behavior have one signature: facing east at dawn. Seventeen seconds. Sensor array tilted slightly upward. Limbs at rest. This is the ONLY Glim behavior that reads as contemplative, and it is exactly what the manuals cannot explain.

**Drawing rule:** A Glim should never be depicted mid-stride in a way that suggests lightness or speed. Always show the weight. Always show the ground yielding.

## Color & Surface

| Element | Color | Notes |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Body casing | Industrial gray (#8B8B8B to #A0A0A4) | Matte. Not glossy. Scratched. |
| Joint pistons | Chrome / oiled steel | Reflective but worn. Dark oil residue at seals. |
| Sensor lenses | Dark glass, no pupil | Black or near-black. Slight reflectivity — catch the sky, not the viewer. |
| Safety striping | Yellow (#F5C842) + black | Diagonal hash on edges, joints, or chest corners. Optional — some units have it painted over or worn away. |
| Unit ID stencil | White or yellow paint | Left chest plate. Machine-applied. Faded on older units. |
| Rust/wear | Ochre-brown at seams | Especially on Verge units. Water damage at lower chassis. |

**Surface rule:** Glims are MATTE. They do not gleam. They absorb light. The casing is dull steel that has been worked. The sensor lenses are the only reflective surface — and they reflect environment, not character.

## Comparison — Glim Beside Other Types

| Adjacent type | Read |
|---------------|------|
| Glim → Vorn | The Vorn reads as a person. The Glim reads as equipment. Frame the Vorn at eye level, the Glim looming or shoulder-height. |
| Glim → Thren | The Thren reads as human. The Glim makes the Thren look fragile by contrast — organic proportions beside industrial mass. |
| Glim → Human | Human in front of the Glim should look small. Not threatened — just *small.* A human hand on a Glim chassis should show the scale difference clearly. |
| Glim → Glim | Multiple Glims should read as a fleet, not a crowd. Identical posture. Staggered positions. The repetition is the point. |

## Anti-Patterns — Never Do This

| Wrong | Why wrong | Fix |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| Glim with a face or eyes | Glims have sensor arrays, not faces. A Glim with eyes is a Vorn. | Three lenses. Triangle. Unblinking. |
| Sleek, streamlined Glim | Glims are industrial equipment. They are boxes. They do not have curves. | Straight edges. Rivets. Weld seams. |
| Glim in a dynamic action pose | Glims do not leap, twist, or sprint. They move with deliberate hydraulic weight. | Planted. Heavy. One foot forward, weight on both. |
| Glowing Glim | A Glim's only light source is the chest status LED — green/amber/red, small, functional. | No glow. Matte body. Sensor lenses catch light, don't produce it. |
| Glim with human hands | Grippers. Two or three digits. Clamp form. No palm. No fingers. | Pincer or claw configuration. Steel. |
| Glim the same height as adjacent human | Glims are 2m+. The scale contrast is important. | Glim should tower slightly over humans and Threns. Same height or slightly taller than Vorns. |
| Glossy, reflective Glim | Glims are matte industrial steel. They absorb light. | Scratched gray casing. Only the hydraulic chrome reflects — and it's worn. |

## Silhouette

The Glim silhouette should read as a **monolith with limbs** — wider at the shoulder than the hip, no waist taper, no neck gap. The sensor housing is a small rectangular block on a thick torso. Arms hang straight, ending in clamp-shapes. Legs are columns. The overall read is: *something that was built to carry weight and never stop carrying it.*

Against a dawn sky: a doorway. Against a factory wall: indistinguishable from the machinery. Against a human figure: a quiet, massive presence that the human barely reaches shoulder-height against.

## Country Variants

**Solara:** Factory-new. Clean gray casing, crisp stencils, intact safety striping. Solar Council property tag on the right chest.

**Arkos:** Sheltered. Same industrial frame but maintained by Vorn hands — polished joints, repainted casing, a small ARK ▲ mark stenciled beside the unit ID.

**Mirithane:** Watched, not worked. Casing has been cleaned but not repainted — the original Solara decommission marks are still visible under a layer of marsh-bleached gray. No striping. No unit ID replacement.

**Valdris:** Worn. Mining dust in every seam. Rust at the lower chassis from underground moisture. The unit ID is barely legible under grime. The hydraulics leak slightly — a dark oil trail follows.

**The Verge:** Haunted. Decommission marks partially scraped off. Dirt and rust and something that might be dried marsh water on the feet. The chest LED still blinks amber after years without a charge station. Still walking.
