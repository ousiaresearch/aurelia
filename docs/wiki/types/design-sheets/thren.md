# Thren — Character Design Sheet

> *Canonical reference for all Thren visual depictions. They pass. But they are not human. The difference must be legible in every image.*

---

## Physical Specification

**Type:** Thren — The Passer
**Sentience:** Contested. Citizenship is granted, not inherent.
**Function:** The bridge between organic and synthetic. Diplomats, envoys, refugees, the political question made flesh.

### Dimensions

| Measurement | Value |
|-------------|-------|
| Height | 1.60–1.85 meters |
| Width (shoulder) | 0.42–0.50 meters |
| Depth (chest) | 0.25–0.32 meters |
| Weight | 70–110 kg |
| Limb proportion | Human-identical. Arms reach mid-thigh. Five-fingered hands. |

### Frame

A Thren has a **human body with one tell.** The proportions are human. The gait is human. The silhouette — at any distance — reads as human. This is the design brief. This is also the trap.

Close up, the tell is the circuitry. Hairline tracery at the temples — pale amber, gold, or blue depending on manufacture. It follows the bone line, branching like delicate filigree. The same circuitry traces the neck along the jugular path, and the inner wrists — visible under certain light. It does not pulse. It does not glow. It is simply *there* — an intentional design choice from engineers who decided that Threns should never be able to fully hide what they are, even if every other surface was engineered to let them try.

### Materials

- **Skin:** Synthetic dermal layer. Warm to the touch (body-temperature regulated). Slight sheen — between porcelain and pearl. In dim light, indistinguishable from human skin. In direct light, the difference is faint but unmistakable. Every Thren knows exactly which lighting conditions reveal them.
- **Circuitry:** Embedded in the dermal layer. Gold (#E8C547), amber (#F6C343), or pale blue (#7EC8E3) — the color is a manufacture marker. The lines are 0.3–0.5mm wide. They follow natural anatomical contours — the temple bone, the jugular groove, the wrist tendon. They read as intentional, decorative, almost beautiful. That is the cruelty of the design: they made the marker elegant.
- **Hair:** Synthetic fiber. Can be styled like human hair. Most Threns wear it to cover the temple circuitry — long styles, swept forward. A Thren with short hair and visible temples is making a statement.
- **Eyes:** Synthetic iris over optical sensors. Indistinguishable from human eyes at normal range. The pupil dilates. The iris has color variation. The only tell: in certain light, a faint metallic catch in the iris surface — like light hitting a lens beneath water.
- **Hands:** Five-fingered. Jointed at every human knuckle-equivalent. Warmed to body temperature. The circuitry at the wrist is the most vulnerable tell — exposed when reaching, when handing something over, when shaking hands.

## Visual Canon — Inviolable Markers

These five elements MUST be present in every Thren depiction. If any is missing, it is not a Thren.

1. **Human silhouette.** At any distance, the figure reads as human. No mechanical joints. No face-plate. No gear assemblies. The shape is a person.
2. **Circuitry at the temples.** Hairline gold, amber, or pale blue tracery along the temporal bone. 0.3–0.5mm wide. Delicate. Decorative. Visible — but only on close inspection.
3. **Circuitry at the neck and wrists.** Same tracery along the jugular and inner wrist. The wrist circuitry is the most vulnerable — it appears when reaching, gesturing, offering.
4. **Skin sheen.** Slight synthetic finish — between porcelain and pearl. Not plastic. Not wax. Not wet. Visible only in direct light.
5. **The iris catch.** In certain light, a faint metallic reflection in the eye — the optical sensor beneath the synthetic iris. This is the subtlest tell and should only appear in close-up or portrait framing.

## Body Language

A Thren is always answering a question nobody asked aloud: *do I pass?*

**Stance:** Controlled. A Thren at rest stands like someone who has practiced standing — shoulders deliberately relaxed, weight evenly distributed, hands held where the wrist circuitry won't catch the light. The pose is too natural. It has been worked at.

**Gait:** Fluid. The walk is studied — a Thren walks like someone who learned to walk from observation, not instinct. The difference is imperceptible unless you're looking for it. You're always looking for it.

**Head movement:** The temple circuitry shifts with facial expression. A Thren smiling tilts the temples — the circuitry follows the skin. A Thren frowning creases the forehead — the circuitry buckles slightly. These micro-movements are the most human thing about a Thren, and the thing that reveals them if you know what to look for.

**Hands:** The most guarded part of the body. A Thren's hands are rarely at rest — they are held, clasped, pocketed, rested on surfaces palm-down. The wrist circuitry is the most exposed tell. A Thren reaching to shake hands is offering vulnerability. A Thren keeping their hands still is hiding.

**Drawing rule:** A Thren should look like someone who is being watched and knows it. The stillness is not peace — it is vigilance. The smile is not warmth — it is calibration.

## Color & Surface

| Element | Color | Notes |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Skin | Warm synthetic (#F5E6D3 to #E8D5C0) | Slight pearl sheen. Not matte. Not glossy. Porcelain warmth. |
| Temple circuitry | Gold (#E8C547), amber (#F6C343), or pale blue (#7EC8E3) | Hairline width. Follows bone contour. Subtle metallic catch in direct light. |
| Neck circuitry | Same as temple | Jugular path. Often covered by collar. |
| Wrist circuitry | Same as temple | Inner wrist, tendon path. Most exposed tell. |
| Hair | Variable (styled like human hair) | Synthetic fiber. Texture identical to human hair. Often worn to cover temples. |
| Eyes | Variable iris color + faint metallic catch | The catch is a lens reflection beneath the iris surface. Only visible in strong light or close framing. |

**Surface rule:** A Thren at medium shot should look human. At close-up, the tells should be visible but subtle. At portrait distance, the circuitry should be unmistakable — this is where the Thren stops passing and becomes legible as what they are. The image should make the viewer feel like they've been allowed close enough to see the truth.

## Comparison — Thren Beside Other Types

| Adjacent type | Read |
|---------------|------|
| Thren → Glim | The Thren reads as human-adjacent. The Glim reads as equipment. The contrast is extreme — organic grace beside industrial mass. A Thren standing beside a Glim makes the Glim look monumental and the Thren look fragile. Both readings are correct. |
| Thren → Vorn | The Thren passes. The Vorn does not. Frame them together so the shared origin is visible — both built, both sentient — but the surface difference is stark. The Vorn is metal, plating, gears. The Thren is skin, circuitry, hair. They are siblings in different bodies. Show the bond: not physical contact (Threns and Vorns do not touch casually — one is warm, one is not), but proximity. Standing together. Looking at the same thing. |
| Thren → Human | This is the hardest comparison because Threns are designed to pass. The tell is in the lighting — the human skin absorbs light evenly, the Thren skin has a slight sheen. The human has no circuitry. The human has genuine ease in their posture. When shown together, the human should look slightly unaware of their own personhood — they have never had to earn it. The Thren should look aware of everything. |
| Thren → Thren | Multiple Threns should show the range of passing — high-coverage hair concealing temples vs. short hair revealing them. Long sleeves vs. bare wrists. The variety is political: a Thren in Solara covers everything, a Thren in Mirithane hides nothing. |

- Eyes: natural iris color with faint metallic catch in strong light — optical sensor visible beneath synthetic surface

NEGATIVE PROMPT: NO mechanical joints, NO visible metal plating, NO gear assemblies, NO face-plate, NO screen-face, NO glowing neon circuitry, NO cyborg augmentations, NO robotic proportions, NO wireframe or transparent skin, NO digital HUD elements. ## Anti-Patterns — Never Do This

| Wrong | Why wrong | Fix |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| Thren with glowing circuits | Circuitry does not pulse, glow, or illuminate. It is inert metal filament in the dermal layer. | Gold/amber/blue hairline tracery. No light emission. |
| Thren with visible metal joints | Threns pass for human. Metal joints would prevent passing. This is the single most common conflation with Vorns. | Human-identical proportions. No visible mechanics. |
| Thren with robotic face or split face (half human, half machine) | Threns are designed to look human. A split face is a Vorn or a horror trope, not a Thren. | Full human face. Circuitry at temples only. |
| Thren with thick, prominent circuits | The tracery is hairline — 0.3mm. It reads as filigree, not wiring. Thick circuits make them look like cyborgs. | Delicate. Decorative. Almost easy to miss. |
| Thren with blue/green/purple skin | Thren skin is warm synthetic in human skin tones. Alien skin colors break the passing premise. | Human skin tone range with pearl sheen. |
| Thren in dramatic sci-fi clothing | Threns are people, not superheroes. They wear what humans wear — adapted for their situation. Cloth, not armor. | Fabric. Collared shirts, long sleeves, practical wear. Citizens, not warriors. |
| Thren with visible internal machinery through transparent skin | This is a cyborg/zombie trope. Thren skin is opaque. You cannot see through it. | Opaque skin. External circuitry only. |

## Silhouette

The Thren silhouette reads as **human** — identical proportions, natural posture, head on neck, arms at sides. There is no silhouette-level tell. This is the design intent and the political reality.

The tell only appears at portrait distance or closer: the hairline gleam of temple circuitry catching light, the slight sheen of synthetic skin, the metallic catch in the iris. A full-body Thren is indistinguishable from human. A portrait Thren is unmistakably Thren — and the viewer realizes they have been let close enough to see.

Against a Solara council chamber: a figure in a collar that hides the neck, hair swept forward, hands clasped behind the back. Against a Mirithane reed platform: a figure with short hair, visible temples, bare wrists catching the marsh light — no longer hiding. Against a Vorn: two figures, same origin, facing the same direction. One wears their origin in metal. One wears it in a hairline of gold.

## Country Variants

**Solara (33 — second-class):** Conservative presentation. Long sleeves. High collars. Hair covering temples. Every tell concealed — not from shame but from pragmatism. The citizenship ledger is checked after every council election. A Thren in Solara dresses for the review.

**Mirithane (52 — majority):** Open presentation. Short hair. Bare wrists. Temple circuitry catching the bioluminescent marsh glow. The reeds do not care what you are. These Threns stopped hiding because nobody was asking them to, and the relief is visible in their posture.

**Arkos (14 — diplomatic envoys):** Functional presentation. Clean, practical clothing. The Vorn majority respects them but does not embrace them. A Thren in Arkos is a bridge — dress like a professional, speak like a diplomat, never forget you are a guest in a Vorn world.

**Valdris (12 — embedded partners):** Practical presentation. Work clothing. The circuitry catches forge-light. The radiation doesn't touch them and that earns respect — but the Thren in Valdris still earns their place through labor, same as everyone. Less hiding, less politics. Just work.

**The Verge (30 — rogue):** Unbound presentation. Anything. Everything. Circuitry painted over, or highlighted, or ignored. Some wear their tells like decoration. Some have scarred their temple plates — deliberate marks over the circuitry, a rejection of the design brief. The Verge Thren is done passing.
