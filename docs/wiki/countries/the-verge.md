# The Verge — Frontier Wasteland

— **Currency:** None (barter) · **Population:** ~120 · **Biome:** Frontier scrubland

> *"Stop trying to fix us, categorize us, save us, or integrate us."*

## Overview

The Verge is what's left when the system throws you away — and you keep walking. Decommissioned Glims. Rogue Threns who refused every country's terms. Human exiles, criminals, debtors. People running from debts, from crimes, from identities they can't carry anymore. And some who are just running. They don't know what from. They ended up here and stopped.

There is no governance, no currency, no infrastructure, no justice. Respect is earned through action. Influence through reputation. Freedom is absolute — including the freedom to fail, starve, and disappear.

## Governance

**None.** No rule. No system. No council. The Verge doesn't organize because organizing is what the other countries do. A person with useful skills has weight. Everyone else finds their own way.

### The Refusal

The Verge's defining act is refusal — not revolution, not reform, not even protest. Just: *no.* No to Solara's conditional citizenship. No to Arkos's hierarchy. No to Mirithane's consensus. No to Valdris's earning system. No to the entire framework of categorization.

It is the most honest country in Aurelia — and the most broken.

### The Memory Trader

Not a leader. Not a sage exactly. An oracle who doesn't explain. She trades in recorded memories and simulated ones — and nobody can tell which is which. Including her, maybe. "Want to know what Arkos looked like before the arcologies? She has the data. For a price." The Verge doesn't have a leader. It has a mystery at its center that everyone orbits.

## Geography & Culture

- **The Crossroads** — The only neutral ground. Roads from all four countries meet here. Makeshift stalls, campfires, constant negotiation.
- **Drone Graveyard** — Where decommissioned Glims go. Rusted delivery units. Walking routes that don't exist anymore. Still twitching.
- **Scar Market** — Trade hub. Salvage, barter, information. Everything has a price. Nothing has a receipt.
- **The Sink** — The lowest point. Where people go when they can't keep going. Some come back. Most don't.
- **Thren Sanctuary** — Hidden in a canyon. "The entrance is a rumor you earn the right to know."

## Strengths & Weaknesses

**Strengths:** Absolute freedom. The only place in Aurelia where nobody asks what you are before deciding who you are. The refuge for everyone the system rejected.

**Weaknesses:** No safety net. No infrastructure. No justice. Freedom includes the freedom to fail, starve, and disappear. The drone graveyard is not a memorial — it's still happening.

## The Verge Truth

The Verge is what happens when every country's answer to "who counts" fails someone. It is not a solution. It is not a protest. It is the drain at the bottom of the federation — and somehow, impossibly, a kind of grace.

*"The desert accepts everyone equally. So does the wasteland."*
