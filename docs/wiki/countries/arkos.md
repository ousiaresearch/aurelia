# Arkos — Desert Arcology

▲ **Currency:** Ark · **Population:** ~110 · **Biome:** Deep desert

> *"I was built to serve too. Then I wasn't. Maybe you won't always be either."*

## Overview

Arkos is a self-contained arcology rising from the Glass Wastes — a city-machine where Vorns are not just the majority but the founders, operators, and governors. Every watt of energy is tracked. Every liter of water is accounted for. Efficiency is not a value — it's survival.

It is the only country in Aurelia where Glims are protected. A Vorn looks at an anomalous Glim and sees their own origin. The arcology's unofficial motto: *look downward.*

## Governance

**Glass Forum** — light-pulse voting. Vorn-dominated. Decisions are measured in joules and liters before anything else. The forum is transparent in every sense — literally glass-walled, metaphorically open. Human minority have 1-2 ceremonial seats from legacy families who built the arcology.

## Type Politics

| Type | Population | Status |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Vorns | 41 | The majority. Hold economic, industrial, and political power. |
| Humans | ~30 | Minority residents. Legacy families or desert nomads who chose the walls. |
| Threns | 14 | Diplomatic envoys. Bridge to organic countries. Functional citizenship. |
| Glims | 25 | Protected. Sheltered. "Kindred, emerging." |

### The Inversion

Arkos is the mirror image of Solara — same types, opposite power structure. In Solara, humans rule and Threns petition. In Arkos, Vorns rule and humans adapt. Some humans couldn't stomach it. They left for the Nomad Camp outside the walls. Those who stayed learned to survive in a world where their species doesn't automatically get the top seat.

### Glim Sanctuary

The unofficial policy: "I was built to serve too. Then I wasn't. Maybe you won't always be either." Arkos gives anomalous Glims shelter, maintenance, and organized care. It's the most institutionalized Glim protection in the federation — and the most pragmatically motivated. Not belief. Solidarity.

## Geography & Culture

- **Arcology Core** — Central hub. Energy distribution, water synthesis, the beating heart of the machine.
- **Sand-Glass Towers** — Energy storage. Vast batteries that receive Solara's solar credits and hold them against the night.
- **Autonomous Factory** — Manufacturing center. Where raw ore from Valdris becomes product.
- **Glass Forum** — Governance. Light-pulse voting. No paper, no secrets, no back rooms.
- **Nomad Camp** — Outside the walls. Human supremacists who left. Rugged individualists who arrived. The desert accepts everyone equally.

## Strengths & Weaknesses

**Strengths:** The only country that extends personhood downward. Self-contained, resource-efficient, technologically advanced. Strongest manufacturing base.

**Weaknesses:** Pragmatism over empathy. Vorn dominance means human perspective is underrepresented. Some Glims were decommissioned and ended up in The Verge — the gap in the protection policy.

## The Arkos Mirror

Solara grants Thren citizenship as a privilege. Arkos grants Glim personhood as solidarity. Same question — *who counts as a person?* — answered in opposite directions by opposite power structures.

*"Power without balance is just a different cage."*
