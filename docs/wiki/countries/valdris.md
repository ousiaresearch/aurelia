# Valdris — Highland Federation

♦ **Currency:** Kael · **Population:** ~120 · **Biome:** Highland mountain

> *"Prove yourself by the work you do. The mountain remembers everyone."*

## Overview

Valdris is the productive middle of the federation — a terraced canyon civilization built on mining, forging, and guild representation. The Canyon Council governs by craft: miners, forgeworkers, refiners, farmers each hold seats. Vorns who've worked the deep mines long enough earn guild membership and council seats. No one is born into power here. Everyone earns it.

It is also the country most divided at the dinner table. Some families see Vorns as community members. Others see machines that took their grandfather's job. Same mountain. Different memories.

## Governance

**Canyon Council** — guild-based representation. Seats held by craft: miners, forgeworkers, refiners, farmers. Vorns earn guild membership through decades of service. Council meets in a chamber carved into vertical canyon walls. Debate echoes across the chasm.

### Earned Trust

Valdris's philosophy: *trust is earned through labor.* It's the only system in Aurelia that doesn't discriminate by type — but it also doesn't guarantee anything by birth. A Vorn who works the deep mines for 30 years earns the same guild seat as a human who did the same. A Thren who can descend where radiation would kill a human earns respect even from skeptics. But a person who can't work — due to age, injury, or design — has no political existence.

### The Divided Families

The deepest fault line in Valdris runs through households. Long mining lineages are split. "They took our jobs" vs. "They're our neighbors." Same dinner table. Same mountain. Different memories of what was lost and what was gained. The forge is sacred. The mountain is sacred. Work is the answer to everything — except the question of whether work can heal a wound this old.

## Geography & Culture

- **Canyon Council Chamber** — Governance. Carved into vertical canyon walls. Terraces cascade down the rock face.
- **Forge District** — Industrial heart. Smelters, fab-rigs, rare-earth refineries. Where ore becomes circuit.
- **Deep Mines** — Where Threns earn their place. Radiation that kills humans is just warm to them.
- **Hot Springs** — Social hub. Where hierarchy dissolves in steam. Cross-type friendships form here that would never form in the council chamber.
- **Summit Shrine** — 4,100 meters up. Names carved into obsidian — the people the mountain took. Human, Thren, Vorn — the stone doesn't sort them.

## Strengths & Weaknesses

**Strengths:** Most stable governance. Genuine cross-type integration at the workplace level. The only country where the system's answer to "who belongs" has internal consistency. Strongest trade partnership with Arkos.

**Weaknesses:** The divide runs through families. Glims are invisible. The runoff pollutes Mirithane's water. "Earned trust" has no answer for people who can't earn.

## The Valdris Truth

Valdris is the pragmatic middle — not because it's moderate, but because it's the only country where both sides of the personhood question share a dinner table. The argument never ends. The mountain doesn't wait for it to.

*"Learn who came before you. The stone doesn't sort them."*
