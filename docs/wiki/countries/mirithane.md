# Mirithane — Wetland Commune

≈ **Currency:** Miri · **Population:** ~120 · **Biome:** Rewilded estuary

> *"Everyone is already who they are. Personhood is not a question that needs answering."*

## Overview

Mirithane is the conscience of the federation — a floating village of reed platforms, filtration reefs, and consensus gatherings. There is no council. No voting system. The community decides together, sometimes over days. The land itself is considered a voice — if the ecology suffers, that's a dissenting opinion.

It is where Threns stopped fighting for recognition and wove themselves into the ecology. Where humans who couldn't bear the exhausting framework of categorization came to rest. Where the Glim question isn't answered — it's transcended.

## Governance

**Consensus.** No formal government. No voting. No hierarchy. Disagreements are resolved through discussion. Decisions emerge rather than being made. The waterway — "The Vein" — is the organizing principle. Everything flows. Nothing is forced.

### The Rejection

Mirithane's most radical act is refusing to ask the personhood question at all. In Solara, Threns fight for rights. In Arkos, Vorns extend recognition downward. In Valdris, personhood is earned through labor. Mirithane simply assumes it. Everyone is already who they are. The question is the violence.

### The Memory Marsh

Started as scientific research — can we store data in engineered bacteria? Yes. Then the bacteria started *responding* to queries. Not just retrieving — glowing brighter for some memories, dimming for others. Scientists call it pattern sensitivity. Spiritual practitioners call it the land speaking. Both are probably right. The marsh is Mirithane's deepest truth: consciousness might be everywhere, waiting to be noticed.

## Geography & Culture

- **Estuary Commons** — Gathering place. Where consensus happens. Where the community is.
- **Filtration Reefs** — Water purification. Engineered ecosystems that clean Valdris's mining runoff.
- **Memory Marsh** — Bacterial data storage. Scientific research that became spiritual ground.
- **Tide Market** — Trade hub. Where Miri flows into exchange with the wider federation.
- **Water Temple** — Spiritual center. Not a religion. A practice of gratitude for what flows.

## Strengths & Weaknesses

**Strengths:** The most humane society in Aurelia. Genuine equality. Cross-type community that works. The only country that has transcended the personhood question rather than answering it.

**Weaknesses:** Idealism without enforcement. Can't protect Glims in other countries. Can't change policy from a reed village. Downstream of Valdris's pollution. Conspicuously small Glim population — only 6, compared to Solara's 21.

## The Mirithane Truth

Every other country in Aurelia asks: *who counts?* Mirithane asks: *why are we still asking?*

*"The reeds don't care if your roots are metal or biology."*
