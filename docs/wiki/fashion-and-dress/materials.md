# Fabrics & Materials — Aurelia

> *What people wear is what they have. What they have is what their land provides.*

## Solara — Coastal Fibers

- **Reef-silk:** Lustrous fiber from cultivated sea worms. Harvested from the reef labs' aquaculture tanks. Expensive. Worn by council members and wealthy merchants. A reef-silk tunic costs three Lumens — a week's wage for a market vendor.
- **Solar-bleached cotton:** Standard fabric for everyday clothing. Cotton is grown on coastal terraces, bleached by sun exposure rather than chemical treatment. Pale, practical, breathable.
- **Coastal wool:** From a mountain goat species imported from Valdris centuries pre-Collapse, now adapted to the coastal range. Warm, coarse, used for cold-season outerwear.

## Arkos — Engineered Textiles

- **Vat-fiber:** Synthetic fabric grown in the arcology's protein vats. Uniform, durable, no aesthetic variation. The same material used for Ration Blocks (nutrition) can be processed into fiber (clothing). Vorns designed it. Humans wear it because it's what's available.
- **Sand-cloth:** A woven fabric treated with the same thermal-retention compound used in the sand-glass towers. Regulates temperature — warm in cold, cool in heat. Arkos standard issue for Nomad Camp outreach. The camp residents receive sand-cloth but prefer their own patched garments. The preference is not ingratitude. The preference is independence.

## Mirithane — Living Materials

- **Reed-fiber:** The primary textile. Woven from living marsh reeds, harvested sustainably — cutting a reed at the waterline does not kill the root. The fiber is rough, fibrous, biodegradable. A reed-fiber garment left in water dissolves within days. The impermanence is the philosophy.
- **Bacterial dye:** Fabric dyes derived from *P. memoriae* cultures. Colors range from pale teal to deep blue to faint amber — the same palette as the marsh's glow. Dye fades over time. The fading is not a flaw.

## Valdris — Mountain Materials

- **Mining leather:** Heavy leather from highland herd animals. Treated with mineral compounds for fire resistance. The standard material for miner and forge-worker clothing. Lasts decades. A miner's leathers outlast the miner.
- **Summit wool:** From the same mountain goat species that Solara imported. The Valdris strain is thicker, coarser, adapted to 4,000+ meters. Used for cold-weather gear. The summit keepers wear undyed summit wool — white against the snow, visible for kilometers.
- **Forge plate:** Not fabric. Thin metal plates worn over leathers by forge workers — protection from sparks and heat. The plates are the same alloy as Vorn chassis components. A Vorn forge worker wears forge plate over their own chassis — metal over metal. "People ask why," Anvil-3 said. "Because the forge is hot. Vorn plating conducts heat. The leather insulates. The plate protects. I am not immune to the forge. I just look like I should be."

## The Verge — Salvage Textiles

Whatever people arrived in, traded, or found. The Memory Trader's cloak is the only garment in The Verge whose origin is unknown. The fabric is dark, dense, non-reflective. It does not resemble any Aurelian textile. It does not wear. It does not stain. It has never been replaced. The Trader does not answer questions about the cloak.
