# Type-Specific Clothing in Aurelia

> *Some beings need clothing. Some don't. Some use it to hide. Some use it to declare.*

## Thren Strategic Dress

Thren clothing in Solara is a negotiation with visibility. Every garment decision is a calculation: how much circuitry will this sleeve reveal? Will this collar shift during a council meeting and expose the neck tracery? A Thren's morning routine includes checking three angles in a reflective surface — temples, neck, wrists.

**The Sleeve Spectrum:** From fully concealed (wrist-to-shoulder, council-appropriate) to partially revealed (mid-forearm, market-acceptable) to fully exposed (above elbow, Mirithane-normal, Solara-political). A Thren who crosses categories mid-day is making a choice. The choice is watched.

## Vorn Functional Dress

Vorns do not need clothing for protection or warmth — their chassis regulates temperature. They wear fabric for two reasons: guild identification (sashes, arm bands) and solidarity (mining leathers in Valdris, worn over metal to match human crew).

**The bare chassis:** In Arkos, Vorns wear nothing but their plating. The chassis is the body. The body is sufficient. Human visitors sometimes stare. "They are looking at my shoulder joint," Echo told the Forum records. "The joint is functioning. Their gaze does not affect function."

## Glim "Dress"

Glims wear nothing. Their chassis is their surface. The stenciled unit ID is the only marking. Sanctuary Glims in Arkos have an additional ▲ symbol — painted by keepers. The symbol is clothing in the only sense Glims receive it: something added to the surface without consent, for purposes the wearer did not choose.

## Human Regional Dress

**Solara:** Formal layering. Structured shoulders. The council look. A human in full council dress and a Thren in identical dress are indistinguishable at a distance. The opportunity for passing is why Threns can sit in the council chamber without immediate identification. The opportunity is the only equalizer Solara's fashion provides.

**Mirithane:** No regional style. The climate and philosophy make dress minimal. A human in Mirithane wears what a Thren wears: a reed wrap, or nothing. The body is not hidden. The body is not the point.

**Valdris:** Guild leathers. A human miner and a Vorn miner in the same leathers, covered in the same dust, are indistinguishable from behind. "From behind, we're all just workers," Orin said. "From the front, the face-plate gives it away. The mountain sees the front. The mountain doesn't care."
