# Fashion & Dress in Aurelia

> *Clothing is the body's political argument. In Aurelia, what you wear announces who you are — and whether you want that noticed.*

## Solara — Council Chic

Solara fashion is formal, functional, and coded. The dominant silhouette is **covered but not concealed** — high collars, long sleeves, structured shoulders. The aesthetic originated in the council chamber, where presentation is political. Council members dress to project authority without ostentation.

**Fabrics:** Coastal wool, reef-silk (a lustrous fiber from cultivated sea worms, expensive), solar-bleached cotton. Colors are muted — cream, pale blue, charcoal, the occasional amber accent. Bright colors are associated with the Floating Market trader class, not the council elite.

**Thren dress in Solara:** Functional but strategic. High collars cover neck circuitry. Long sleeves cover wrist circuitry. Hair is worn forward, over the temples. A Thren in Solara dresses to pass — not from shame, from pragmatism. The fewer tells the day reveals, the fewer questions the day asks.

**The Sleeve Statement:** A Thren who pushes their sleeves to mid-forearm is making a political choice. The wrist circuitry is exposed. The statement is: *I am not hiding. You are looking. This is your problem.* The statement is rare. The statement is watched.

## Arkos — Vorn Minimalism

Arkos fashion is minimal. Vorns do not need clothing — their chassis is their body. Human residents have adopted Vorn minimalism by proximity: simple, functional garments, no decoration. The arcology's climate is controlled; insulation is unnecessary. Clothing exists for modesty and not much else.

**Vorn guild markers:** A Vorn's guild affiliation is indicated by a sash — a narrow band of fabric worn diagonally across the torso. Archive sash: pale gray. Maintenance sash: dark blue. Governance sash: amber. Resa wears the governance sash. It is the only non-chassis item she has worn in sixty-seven years.

**Human adaptation:** Arkos-born humans wear practical clothing — tunics, trousers, no jewelry, no color. Nomad Camp residents wear what they arrived in, patched and layered. Kel Voss's jacket has seventeen patches. He can name the origin of each.

## Mirithane — Reed & Water

Mirithane clothing is organic, impermanent, and optional. The climate is warm and wet; minimal dress is practical. Reed-fiber wraps are the most common garment — woven from living reeds, biodegradable. A garment left in water dissolves within days. The impermanence is the philosophy.

**Modesty norms:** None in the traditional sense. The body — human, Thren, Vorn — is not a source of shame. "The reeds don't wear clothing," the community charter states. "Neither do the fish. We wear what we need, when we need it." New arrivals from Solara find this disconcerting. Most adapt. Some don't. Those who don't, leave.

**Thren visibility:** Threns in Mirithane do not cover their circuitry. Short hair, bare wrists, open collars. The circuitry is visible. The visibility is not a statement — it is absence of concealment. The absence is the Mirithane difference.

## Valdris — Mountain Practical

Valdris clothing is heavy, durable, designed for labor. Mining leathers. Forge aprons. Wool wraps for the summit cold. Everything is functional. Nothing is decorative. "If it doesn't keep you warm or keep you safe, why are you wearing it?" — Valdris proverb.

**Guild differentiation:** Mining guild: dark leathers, steel-toed boots. Forge guild: heavy apron, arm guards. Summit guild: layered wool, climbing wraps. You can read a Valdrisan's profession by their silhouette.

**Vorn adaptation:** Valdris Vorns wear the same leathers as human miners. The leathers serve no protective purpose for a metal chassis — Vorns don't need insulation. They wear them anyway. "I work the same shaft," Kael-9 said. "I wear the same gear." The gear is solidarity. The gear is earned.

## The Verge — Salvage & Silence

The Verge has no fashion. It has what people arrived in, patched, layered, and traded. Clothing is functional — shelter from desert cold, protection from sun. No aesthetic. No code. The Memory Trader's hooded cloak and gloves are the closest thing The Verge has to a recognizable silhouette. The cloak conceals everything — type, gender, face, hands. The concealment is the only fashion statement The Verge makes, and it is made by one person, and the statement is: *I am not what you are looking at.*
