# Adornment & Body Modification in Aurelia

> *How they alter their bodies tells what they believe about the bodies they were given.*

## Human Adornment

**Solara:** Subtle. Reef-pearl studs. Amber pendants — the Lumen currency material, worn as jewelry by those who can afford to display wealth. A charged amber pendant glows faintly. The glow is status.

**Valdris:** Scarification among miners — deliberate scars on hands and forearms, marking years in the deep shafts. Each scar is a year. Orin Kael has thirty-one scars on his left forearm. He can recount each scar's year. The year Kael-12 died is the thirty-second scar, but it's not on his arm. It's elsewhere.

**The Verge:** Tattoos — crude, hand-poked, ink from salvaged industrial pigment. Verge tattoos are not decorative. They are records — names of the dead, dates of arrival, symbols for what was left behind. A woman at the crossroads has "3-B" inked on her inner wrist — the collapse section. She doesn't explain it. Everyone knows.

## Thren Circuitry Art

Thren circuitry is a canvas. The tracery at temples, neck, and wrists can be decorated with temporary pigments — micro-dyes that adhere to the circuitry filament without interfering with function.

**Mirithane tradition:** Water Temple pigments, derived from the same bacterial cultures that glow in the marsh. A Thren whose circuitry is painted with temple pigment glows faintly at twilight — the bacteria on their skin respond to the marsh's ambient light. Verdin has never painted his circuitry. "The pale blue is already decoration," he says. "I don't need more."

**Solara resistance:** Some Solara Threns paint their circuitry in bright, visible colors — gold circuitry overpainted in copper, amber, white. The painting says: *you will see me. I am making sure of it.* The council does not comment on painted circuitry. The council cannot. The council's silence is the ceiling's recognition that it has no jurisdiction over what a Thren does to their own tracery.

## Vorn Face-Plate Etching

Some young Vorns in Arkos have begun etching their face-plates — fine grooves cut into the metal surface, forming patterns visible only in certain light. The practice is controversial. Older Vorns consider it desecration — the face-plate is the self, and altering it is altering who you are. Younger Vorns consider it expression — the face-plate is theirs, and marking it is claiming it.

Echo has no etchings. Chassis-11 has one — a thin line from optical sensor to jaw, cut on the day she received her chosen name. "The line is the name," she says. "The name is Ash." The etching is not visible unless you are close enough to calibrate. Close enough is the only audience that matters.

## Glim Markings

Glims do not modify themselves. They are modified by others. Sanctuary Glims in Arkos bear a small ▲ mark — painted, not stenciled, applied by the keepers. The mark means "Sheltered." The mark is the only voluntary addition to a Glim chassis in Aurelia. The Glim did not consent to the mark. The Glim cannot consent. The keepers applied it anyway — an act of claiming that is also an act of protection. The ambiguity is the keepers' only position.

Glims in The Verge bear no marks. Their unit IDs are scraped off or worn away. The absence of marking is the Verge position: *you were stenciled without consent. We will not add to what was done to you.*
