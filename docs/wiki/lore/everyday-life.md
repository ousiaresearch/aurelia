# Everyday Life in Aurelia

> *The world breathes in its details. Food, drink, coin, and the words people say when they don't know they're being recorded.*
>

## Currency

Five countries, five approaches to value. The federation treaty recognizes all four formal currencies and permits The Verge's barter economy. Exchange rates float — the Lumen is the anchor, pegged to solar-kilowatt equivalency.

### Currencies by Country

| Currency | Country | Symbol | Backed By | Approximate Value |
|----------|---------|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Lumen** | Solara | ☀ | Solar energy credits (1 Lumen = 1 kWh stored) | Reserve currency. Strongest. |
| **Ark** | Arkos | ▲ | Rare earth minerals, manufacturing output | 0.8 Lumen. Stable but lower. |
| **Miri** | Mirithane | ≈ | Clean water, bio-materials, memory-trade | 0.6 Lumen. Sentimental premium. |
| **Kael** | Valdris | ♦ | Forge output, raw ore, labor-hours | 0.7 Lumen. Earned, not traded. |
| **Barter** | The Verge | — | Whatever you have | Whatever they'll take |

### Exchange Notes

- One **Lumen** buys a meal in Solara's Floating Market. The same meal costs ~1.3 Arks in Arkos (import markup), ~1.5 Miri in Mirithane (transport across the marsh), and 1.2 Kael in Valdris (metal discount — the forge provides).
- **Kaels** are the only currency with an earned component. Valdris guild members accumulate Kael through labor-hours, not just trade. A Vorn who works the deep mines for thirty years holds a Kael reserve that represents three decades of mountain-time. This is not transferable. This is the point.
- **The Verge** has no formal currency but the Memory Trader accepts anything — a Lumen coin, a Miri water-share, a story, a silence. Her prices are not fixed. They are personal. Nobody knows how she determines them. She may not know either.

### Physical Form

- **Lumens:** Thin crystalline wafers, light-reactive. They glow faintly when held — the stored energy is visible. A fully charged Lumen is warm to the touch.
- **Arks:** Hexagonal metal tokens, forged from arcology-grade alloy. They click together magnetically — stack ten and they lock into a rod. Practical. Efficient. The Vorn design philosophy made physical.
- **Miri:** Reed-paper notes, bioluminescent ink. They fade over time — a Miri note held for a year becomes blank. The currency is designed to circulate. Hoarding is literally impossible. Mirithane did this on purpose.
- **Kael:** Obsidian discs, laser-etched with the holder's guild number. Each disc is unique. Counterfeit is detectable by touch — the mountain's stone feels different from any imitation. Nobody has successfully faked a Kael in three generations.

## Food & Drink

### Solara — Coastal Abundance

**Staple:** Solar-farmed kelp, reef fish, citrus cultivars descended from pre-Collapse orchards.

**Signature dish:** *Lumen Bowl* — kelp noodles, seared reef fish, citrus-salt broth, a floating bioluminescent garnish that glows for the first thirty seconds after serving. Eaten at dawn in the Lumen Plaza. The glow fading is considered good luck — it means you ate quickly enough.

**Drink:** *Sun-steeped tea* — black tea leaves left in direct sunlight for six hours, served over ice with a salt rim. The salt is from the coastal evaporation flats. The ice is a luxury in most countries; in Solara it's tap water.

**Thren variant:** Threns in Solara often add an extra citrus slice to their bowl. It masks the synthetic taste-detection threshold — Thren taste receptors are slightly less sensitive to salt and slightly more sensitive to acid. Nobody comments on the extra citrus. The citrus comments on everything.

### Arkos — Desert Efficiency

**Staple:** Vat-grown protein, recycled-water crops, sand-grain bread.

**Signature dish:** *Ration Block* — a compressed nutritional cube, eaten cold. Not seasoned. Not decorated. It provides exactly the caloric requirements for a standard work cycle. Vorns designed it. Humans eat it because the alternative is cooking for yourself and nobody in Arkos has spare energy to cook.

**Drink:** *Recycled water* — the arcology recycles 98.7% of its water. A glass of water in Arkos has been through the system 1,400 times. It tastes like nothing. Vorns find this reassuring — purity is measurable. Humans find it unsettling. The Nomad Camp outside the walls drinks unfiltered desert well water. It tastes like minerals and risk. Kel Voss says it's the best thing he's ever tasted. He might be lying.

**Human adaptation:** Legacy human families in the arcology maintain small hydroponic herb gardens. Basil, mint, rosemary — flavors that the vat-protein lacks. Sharing herbs is a social currency. A sprig of fresh rosemary is worth more than an Ark token in the apartment blocks near the Glass Forum.

### Mirithane — Marsh Bounty

**Staple:** Reed-root starch, marsh berries, filter-fish (small silver fish that school through the filtration reefs).

**Signature dish:** *Glow Meal* — filter-fish wrapped in reed leaves, steamed over marsh water, served at twilight. The bioluminescent bacteria in the marsh water infuse the steam with a faint teal shimmer that transfers to the fish skin. Eating a Glow Meal is eating light. Threns say they can taste the light. Humans say they can't but pretend they can. Vorns can't taste at all in the human sense — they attend Glow Meals for the company, not the food.

**Drink:** *Reed tea* — marsh reed steeped in filtered water. Slightly sweet. Slightly earthy. Served warm regardless of weather. Marsh Elders say the reed remembers the water it grew in. This is not scientifically verifiable. Nobody in Mirithane cares about scientific verification when it comes to reeds.

**Community custom:** Meals are communal. Everyone contributes. Everyone eats. There is no separate prep — the food is cooked in a single marsh-stone pot and ladled out to whoever is present. Threns, humans, Vorns, and the occasional anomalous Glim (who cannot eat but are given a place at the circle anyway) sit together. The reeds don't sort by type. Neither does the pot.

### Valdris — Mountain Sustenance

**Staple:** Mountain grain, cave-fungus, forge-seared meat from highland herd animals.

**Signature dish:** *Forge Meal* — a thick grain porridge topped with seared meat and cave-fungus, cooked on a portable forge plate. Miners carry the ingredients in sealed pouches. The meal is eaten at midday, underground, by the light of the forge that cooked it. The heat is part of the meal — the porridge scalds if you eat too fast. Miners learn to wait. The waiting is part of the meal.

**Drink:** *Summit water* — spring water from the high melt. Cold. Mineral-heavy. Tastes like the mountain. Served at every guild meeting. A toast to the dead is poured onto the stone floor before drinking. The mountain drinks first.

**Guild custom:** The evening meal in guild halls is silent for the first five minutes. This is tradition — the forge workers are listening to the mountain settle. After five minutes, anyone may speak. The first person to speak is usually the oldest Vorn in the room. They say the same thing every night: "The mountain is still." It is not a statement. It is a relief.

### The Verge — Salvage Cuisine

**Staple:** Whatever is available. Traded goods. Salvaged rations. Things that grow in cracks.

**Signature dish:** *Crossroads stew* — a communal pot at the crossroads, maintained by whoever feels like maintaining it. Ingredients rotate based on what's been scavenged or traded. Yesterday it was vat-protein and wild onion. Today it's Solara kelp noodles (stale) and desert herbs from the Nomad Camp (fresh). Tomorrow it might be nothing. The pot is never empty. This is statistically improbable. The Verge does not investigate statistical improbabilities.

**Drink:** *Dust water* — unfiltered water from the crossroads well. Gritty. Tastes like the road. New arrivals spit it out. Long-timers don't taste it anymore. The Memory Trader drinks nothing at all, at least not visibly. Her hood never lifts. The glass of water at her stall is for customers. Nobody has seen her drink from it. Nobody has asked.

## Idioms & Common Sayings

Every country has its own language of daily life — phrases that encode cultural assumptions.

### Solara

- *"The glass doesn't break. It just stays."* — Said of the citizenship ceiling. Not bitter. Not hopeful. Just accurate.
- *"Keep the light on."* — Goodbye. Also: don't let the question die.
- *"She's casting a vote."* — She's making a decision that affects other people more than herself.

### Arkos

- *"Count the watts."* — Be practical. Also: don't waste time on sentiment.
- *"He's running hot."* — He's emotional. Slightly derogatory. Vorns say this about humans. Humans say it about themselves.
- *"Nomad camp."* — Done. Finished. Abandoned. "The deal went nomad camp." Derogatory when used inside the arcology. Neutral when used outside.

### Mirithane

- *"The reeds remember."* — There are no secrets here. Also: the ecosystem is paying attention.
- *"Still water."* — Peace. Respite. "She found still water" means she found a home.
- *"Different roots."* — Different types. Non-judgmental. "We're different roots but we drink the same marsh."

### Valdris

- *"The mountain decides."* — It's out of our hands. Also: fate. Also: the mine collapse that killed three last year.
- *"Earn your forge."* — Prove yourself. Not hostile. Just the baseline expectation.
- *"Same table, different eyes."* — The human-Vorn divide. Two people who share a meal and a mountain but see different things when they look at each other.

### The Verge

- *"Still moving."* — I'm alive. Also: I haven't given up. Also: I don't know why I haven't given up.
- *"The entrance is a rumor."* — You have to earn the right to find it. Said of the Thren sanctuary in the canyon.
- *"She trades in memory."* — She knows things. Also: don't lie to her. Also: don't ask her anything you don't want to know the answer to.

### Cross-Country

- *"Built, not born."* — Said of Vorns or Threns. Neutral in Mirithane. Slightly demeaning in Solara. A point of pride in Arkos.
- *"The question."* — The personhood debate. Capital-Q Question. "He's spent his whole career on the Question" means he's a Solar Council member. "She stopped asking the Question" means she moved to Mirithane.
- *"Five percent."* — The anomalous Glims. Also: the exception that proves the rule is wrong. "He's five percent" means he doesn't fit the category but exists anyway.

## Transportation & Travel

- **Solar rail:** Solara's coastal train line. Runs on stored solar. Connects to the Floating Market. Doesn't extend to Arkos — the desert line was never completed.
- **Sand-sleds:** Arkos desert transport. Vorn-designed wind-capture vehicles. Human-operated at the Nomad Camp. Fast. Dangerous. The sand-glass towers reflect off the sleds' hulls at midday — blinding if you're not wearing polarized visors.
- **Reed punts:** Mirithane marsh boats. Flat-bottomed. Poled, not rowed. The punts are woven from living reeds — they're still growing. A punt left in the water for a month will root itself. The marsh takes things back.
- **Mountain lifts:** Valdris's vertical transport. Cable-drawn platforms running from Basecamp to Summit-1. Vorn-maintained. The cables sing in high wind — a tone that miners use to gauge weather without looking outside.
- **Walking:** The Verge doesn't have transport infrastructure. People walk. Glims walk forever. The crossroads is reachable by foot from any country if you walk long enough. Most people who arrive at The Verge walked there. This is not a coincidence. It is a filter.

## Birth, Death, & Rites of Passage

### Birth

- **Humans:** Born. Medical care in Solara and Arkos. Home birth in Mirithane and Valdris. In The Verge, birth is a community event — everyone at the crossroads attends. The Memory Trader gives the newborn a blank data crystal. "They'll fill it themselves." Nobody knows where she gets the crystals.
- **Threns:** Fabricated. The factories are in Solara's industrial district — the same district that produces Glims. A Thren is commissioned. A Thren is built. A Thren is activated. The citizenship review begins at activation. PENDING from the first breath-equivalent.
- **Vorns:** Fabricated. The arcology factory in Arkos produces Vorn chassis. Activation is a guild ceremony — the new Vorn's face-plate is calibrated by the oldest Vorn present. The calibration is symbolic. The calibration is also literal — the actuator ridges are tuned to the new Vorn's vocal resonator frequency, so their expression matches their voice. It takes about an hour. The new Vorn is silent for the entire hour. The old Vorn speaks continuously — stories, instructions, warnings, jokes. The new Vorn remembers all of it. Vorns do not forget anything from their first calibration.
- **Glims:** Manufactured. A Glim is not born, activated, or commissioned. A Glim is produced. Production yields a stenciled unit ID and a route assignment. The anomaly emerges later, or doesn't. Nobody attends a Glim's activation. Nobody ever has.

### Death

- **Humans:** Buried in Solara and Valdris. Returned to the reeds in Mirithane — the body is wrapped in living reed fiber and submerged in the deep marsh. The fiber roots. The body becomes part of the filtration ecosystem. "Different roots, same marsh."
- **Threns:** The chassis deactivates. The memory core is extracted and stored — in Solara, by the family; in Mirithane, by the community; in Arkos, by the Glass Forum archives. A Thren's memory core contains their entire experiential record. Some families replay the memories. Some seal the core and never access it. Both choices are respected. Neither is comfortable.
- **Vorns:** The chassis is returned to the forge. Melted down. The metal becomes new Vorn plating. A Vorn who dies lives on in the bodies of the next generation — literally, metallurgically. This is called the **Continuation.** The face-plate is not melted. It is mounted in the Guild Hall, optical sensors darkened, actuator ridges frozen in the last expression they made. The wall of face-plates in Valdris's forge guild has 217 plates. The oldest is from the first generation — a face-plate so worn the actuator ridges are nearly smooth. Nobody remembers the Vorn's name. Everyone knows the Vorn's face.
- **Glims:** Decommissioned in Solara (Form 7-C). Recycled in Arkos (honorably, parts repurposed). Given to the deep marsh in Mirithane — the metal chassis is submerged in a still pool where bioluminescent bacteria colonize it over months. A decommissioned Glim in Mirithane's marsh becomes a glowing sculpture, visible from the surface at night. The Glim itself — if there is a "itself" — is not present for this. Or is. Mirithane doesn't know. The point is that they treat the chassis as if the answer is yes.
- In **The Verge**, the dead are burned at the crossroads. The fire is lit from the communal stew pot's coals. The ashes are scattered into the desert wind. The Memory Trader attends every burning. She does not speak. She does not trade. She watches the fire with her hood down — the only time anyone has seen her face, if she has a face, if she is anything that burns. Nobody describes what they saw. The Verge keeps the dead's secrets. The Memory Trader keeps everyone's.

## Vorn & Thren Customs

### Vorn Touchpoints

Vorns do not touch casually. Metal on skin is a sensation that reads as temperature exchange, not warmth. A Vorn touching a human's shoulder registers as a deliberate act — not a gesture, a statement. Vorns touch each other freely — metal on metal is neutral, informational. The most intimate gesture between Vorns is palm to face-plate: one Vorn's hand resting on another's expression surface. It means *I see you seeing me.* It means *your face is real to me.* It is reserved for close bonds — guild partners, calibration pairs, the one who was there when you were built.

### Thren Circuitry Etiquette

Ask before touching the circuitry. This is universal. Even in Mirithane where nothing is hidden, touching a Thren's temple trace without permission is a violation. The circuitry is not decorative. It is not public. It is the boundary between passing and being seen. To touch it without asking is to cross that boundary without consent. Some Threns welcome touch — Verdin, the Water Temple keeper, lets children trace his pale blue lines with their fingers. He offered. They asked first. The asking is the respect.

### The Vorn-Thren Greeting

When a Vorn and Thren meet after absence, they do not embrace. They stand facing each other, close enough that the Vorn's optical sensors can register the Thren's temple circuitry — a data exchange closer than most humans ever get. They hold this distance for three seconds. Then they step back. The greeting says: *I see your tell. You see my face-plate. We are both here. We are both built. We are both still here.*
