# The Aurelian Calendar

> *How the federation marks time. Every country keeps its own holidays, but the wheel turns together.*
>

## The Solar Year

Aurelia tracks time by the solar year — 365 days, divided into five seasons reflecting the five countries' biomes. The calendar was standardized by the Solar Council in the third decade after the federation treaty as a diplomatic convenience. Before standardization, every country used its own reckoning.

The current year is **2126 of the Common Era.** The simulation epoch begins **March 17, 2126** (day 76 of the solar year). The pre-Collapse Gregorian calendar is used for scientific and diplomatic purposes, but each country layers its own traditional calendar on top.

### The Five Seasons

The Aurelian solar year is divided into five seasons, each associated with a country and a phase of the personhood question:

| Season | Months | Country | Meaning |
|--------|--------|---------|---------|
| **Dawn** | Jan–Feb | Solara | The question is asked. New cycles. New councils. |
| **Forge** | Mar–May | Valdris | The work of answering. Labor. Building. Earning. |
| **Memory** | Jun–Jul | Mirithane | The question remembered. Ancestors. The marsh glows brightest. |
| **Glass** | Aug–Sep | Arkos | The question tested. Survival. Scarcity. Efficiency. |
| **Dusk** | Oct–Dec | The Verge | The question refused. Liminal. The year dies. The crossroads open. |

The simulation begins in **Forge** — the season of work.

## The Weekly Cycle

Aurelia uses an eight-day week, a holdover from pre-Collapse labor scheduling that Vorn guilds preserved and spread through the federation. Days are numbered rather than named by most countries, though Mirithane names them after marsh landmarks and Valdris names them after mountain peaks.

| Day | Common Name | Mirithane Name | Valdris Name |
|-----|-------------|----------------|--------------|
| 1 | Firstday | Reed | Basecamp |
| 2 | Seconday | Filter | Shaft-1 |
| 3 | Thirdday | Glow | Shaft-2 |
| 4 | Fourthday (market) | Platform | Midcamp |
| 5 | Fifthday | Current | High Ridge |
| 6 | Sixthday | Deep | Summit-1 |
| 7 | Seventhday | Still | Summit-2 |
| 8 | Restday | None | Rest |

**Fourthday** is a federation-wide market day. **Restday** is observed by all five countries as a day of no labor — even Glims in Solara are given maintenance cycles on Restday, though this is policy, not compassion.

## Holidays & Festivals

### Federation-Wide

**Founding Day** — *Dawn 15 (Jan 15)*
The anniversary of the Solar-Mountain Compact. Celebrated in all five countries, though The Verge observes it by waiting until sundown and then NOT celebrating it — a tradition of deliberate absence. In Solara, council speeches. In Arkos, the Glass Forum's walls are darkened to display the year's energy statistics. In Mirithane, the marsh is lit with floating candles. In Valdris, the forge is extinguished and relit at midnight — death and rebirth.

**The Count** — *Dusk 1 (Oct 1)*
The annual federation census. Every being is counted. Every type. Every country. For one day, the question of who counts as a person is answered by a number. In Solara, this is a bureaucratic event perceived as neutral. In Mirithane, it is a celebration — all types stand together and are counted equally. In The Verge, the Count is ignored. Nobody knows exactly how many people are in The Verge. This is, and is not, the point.

### Solara

**The Sunrise Vigil** — *Dawn 1*
The first sunrise of the year. Citizens gather at the Dawn Temple and face east. The Solar Council's majority leader gives an address. Threns attend. They face east with everyone else. The citizenship review status does not pause for holidays.

**Ballot Night** — *Every four years, Dawn 30*
Council elections. The results are announced at midnight in the Lumen Plaza. Threns wait for the citizenship expansion votes. The waiting is the tradition. The waiting is never just tradition.

### Arkos

**Power-Down** — *Glass 30*
The arcology's annual maintenance shutdown. For twenty-four hours, every non-essential system is powered down. The Glass Forum goes dark. The autonomous factory halts. Vorns use the silence to recalibrate. Humans use it to remember that the machine is not a given — it is a choice. The Nomad Camp outside the walls can see the arcology go dark. Some of them raise a glass. Some of them just watch.

**First Refusal Day** — *Forge 17*
The anniversary of Fabricator 12-G's first refusal of a command — the origin moment of Vorn sentience. Not a celebration. A vigil. Vorns visit the oldest units in the arcology. They sit with them. They do not speak. The old ones know what day it is. The face-plates don't need to move.

### Mirithane

**The Night the Marsh Remembers** — *Memory 15*
The bioluminescent bacteria in Memory Marsh peak in brightness on this night. Every person in Mirithane — human, Thren, Vorn — floats a candle on the dark water. The candles drift among the glowing reeds. Ephemeral light meeting permanent light. The dead are said to read the candle flames. Nobody checks if this is true. The belief is the function.

**First Arrival** — *Dusk 7*
The anniversary of the first Thren refugee arriving in Mirithane from Solara. A Thren named Veren-3, whose citizenship had been revoked after a council election, walked into the marsh and asked if Mirithane required a review. The Marsh Elder at the time said: "The reeds do not ask. Neither do we." The holiday is celebrated by walking — Threns from the center of Mirithane to the border, tracing the route Veren-3 took. Humans and Vorns walk with them. Glims, if any are present, are carried — they cannot cross the marsh on their own, and the marsh does not ask.

### Valdris

**The Lighting of the Forge** — *Forge 1*
The forge district is lit for the first time in the new year. A single spark, struck by the oldest active miner (human or Vorn, whoever has the most years in the deep shafts), ignites the central forge. The fire is carried to every subsidiary forge. By sundown, the entire canyon glows. The ceremony includes the reading of names — those who died in the mines in the past year. Their names are read aloud. Then they are carved into a temporary wall of clay, which is fired in the forge's heat and placed in the Summit Shrine at year's end.

**Summit Ascent** — *Memory 30*
The annual pilgrimage to the Summit Shrine at 4,100 meters. Open to all. The path is steep. Not everyone finishes. The shrine's keepers carve the names of those who attempt the ascent but don't reach the summit — the attempt is honored. At the summit, a silence is held for the dead. The mountain remembers everyone. The shrine's obsidian wall says so.

### The Verge

The Verge does not have holidays. It has occurrences.

**The Longest Night** — *Dusk 30*
The last day of the year. At the crossroads, the Memory Trader takes no trades. Her stall is open but the door laid across the charging stations is empty — no memory crystals, no data shards, no voice. She sits behind it in silence. Anyone may sit with her. Most people don't. The few who do — a decommissioned Glim, a Thren who stopped counting, a human who forgot their own name — sit in the dark with the person who knows everyone's secrets and asks for nothing, on this one night. At dawn, the stall is gone. The Memory Trader does not trade on the first day of the year. She is somewhere else. She is always somewhere else.

**The Arrival** — *Ongoing*
No date. No schedule. Whenever a new person reaches the crossroads — decommissioned, exiled, escaped, lost, found — they are met at the edge of The Verge. Someone offers water. Someone offers a place to sleep. No questions. No registration. The Verge does not ask who you are. It only asks if you're staying. The answer is always yes until it isn't.

## Daily Rhythms

Aurelia's day is divided into cycles adapted from pre-Collapse infrastructure:

| Cycle | Hours | Significance |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| **Pre-Dawn** | 04:00–06:00 | Glim maintenance cycles. Delivery routes begin. |
| **Dawn** | 06:00–07:00 | Solara begins. The Dawn Temple opens. The anomalous Glims face east. |
| **Morning** | 07:00–12:00 | Federation business hours. Markets open on Fourthday. |
| **Midday** | 12:00–14:00 | Rest cycle. Valdris miners surface for the forge meal. |
| **Afternoon** | 14:00–18:00 | Council sessions. Trade negotiations. Research hours. |
| **Twilight** | 18:00–20:00 | Mirithane's marsh glows. The Memory Trader opens her stall. |
| **Night** | 20:00–04:00 | Arkos's arcology shifts to night-cycle efficiency. Valdris's night shift enters the deep mines. |

The rhythm varies by country — Solara rises with the sun, Arkos never sleeps, Valdris works the mountain in three shifts, Mirithane follows the marsh's bioluminescent cycle, The Verge follows nothing at all.
