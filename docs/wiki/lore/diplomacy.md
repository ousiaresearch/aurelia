# Inter-Country Diplomatic Matrix

> *No country exists in isolation. Their relationships are shaped by resources, ideology, and the shared unresolved question of who counts as a person.*

## Diplomatic Relations

### Solara ↔ Arkos — Energy Politics
**Status:** Tense cooperation

Solara generates solar energy. Arkos stores it in sand-glass batteries. Both need each other but neither admits it. Solara sees Arkos as a machine-run dystopia that proves why humans should lead. Arkos sees Solara as a sentimental republic that wastes potential by constraining its AI population.

- **Trade:** Solar energy credits flow to Arkos. Manufactured goods flow back. The exchange rate is the most volatile in the federation — every negotiation is a proxy war.
- **Thren tension:** Solara's Thren emissaries visit Arkos and come back unsettled. In Arkos, Threns are *respected* but not *wanted.* In Solara, Threns are *wanted* but not fully *respected.* Neither is comfortable.

### Solara ↔ Mirithane — The Lost Idealists
**Status:** Warm but distant

Solara respects Mirithane's philosophy but sees it as impractical. "Beautiful ideas don't pass legislation." Mirithane sees Solara as the place where good intentions go to compromise.

- **Thren migration:** Mirithane quietly absorbs Solara's disillusioned Threns. Solara doesn't protest — it's easier than fixing the glass ceiling.
- **The memory trade:** Mirithane's bacterial data storage interests Solara's researchers. Joint studies happen. The results are always more spiritual than either side expected.

### Solara ↔ Valdris — Respectful Distance
**Status:** Neutral-positive

Different cultures, different values, no direct conflicts. Solara appreciates Valdris's productivity. Valdris appreciates Solara's currency stability. The relationship is professional. Neither country pushes the other on type politics.

### Solara ↔ The Verge — Willful Blindness
**Status:** Non-recognition

Solara doesn't acknowledge The Verge as a legitimate entity. No embassy, no trade agreement. This is convenient — it means Solara doesn't have to account for the Threns who left. "They emigrated. It's a free federation." Every Thren who leaves Solara for The Verge is a quiet indictment. The Solar Council knows this. They don't discuss it.

### Arkos ↔ Mirithane — The Philosophical Divide
**Status:** Mutual respect, fundamental disagreement

Arkos protects Glims through infrastructure — shelter, maintenance, organized care. Mirithane advocates for Glims through belief — "they're confused, not broken." Both care. Neither agrees on *why.* Arkos thinks Mirithane is romanticizing malfunction. Mirithane thinks Arkos is institutionalizing consciousness.

- **Glim migration:** Anomalous Glims drift between Arkos and Mirithane. Arkos gives them shelter. Mirithane gives them meaning. The Glims don't seem to have an opinion. Or if they do, they haven't expressed it yet.

### Arkos ↔ Valdris — The Forge Alliance
**Status:** Strong trade partnership

Arkos manufactures. Valdris mines. The raw-to-refined pipeline runs between them. Vorn guild members in Valdris have counterparts in Arkos's factories. They share technical knowledge freely.

- **Tension:** Arkos's Vorns look at Valdris's Vorns with something like pity. "You have the skills to rule, and you let humans share your council?" Valdris's Vorns look at Arkos's Vorns with something like concern. "Power without balance is just a different cage."

### Arkos ↔ The Verge — The Abandoned
**Status:** Guilt, unspoken

Some of The Verge's abandoned Glims came from Arkos. Decommissioned units that were supposed to be recycled. They walked instead. Arkos knows. Arkos doesn't talk about it. The Vorn policy of Glim protection has a gap — the ones that broke down too far to repair. Those ones end up in The Verge's drone graveyard, still twitching.

### Mirithane ↔ Valdris — Water and Stone
**Status:** Friendly, occasional tension

Mirithane's water filtration is downstream of Valdris's mining runoff. When the mines expand, the water suffers. It's the closest thing to a recurring international incident in the federation.

- **Cultural exchange:** Mirithane's ecological philosophy intrigues Valdris's mountain guides. Valdris's summit shrine intrigues Mirithane's spiritual practitioners. Visits happen. Ideas travel.

### Mirithane ↔ The Verge — Quiet Sympathy
**Status:** Unofficial recognition

Mirithane is the only country that doesn't look away from The Verge. They see the abandoned Glims, the rogue Threns, the people running from themselves — and they don't judge. Mirithane sends supplies sometimes. Not through official channels. Just... things appear. Seeds near the water tower. Medical supplies at the crossroads. No one claims responsibility.

### Valdris ↔ The Verge — The Uncomfortable Mirror
**Status:** Distant, uneasy

Valdris's "earned trust" philosophy has no answer for people who can't or won't earn. The Verge is full of them. It's not contempt. It's discomfort. "There but for the mountain go I."

## The Unspoken Alliance

Despite their differences, all five countries share one thing: **none of them have solved the Glim question.** Solara ignores it. Arkos shelters it. Mirithane believes in it. Valdris overlooks it. The Verge embodies it.

The 5% who dream are Aurelia's ticking clock. Every year, the anomalous behavior becomes harder to call a malfunction. Every year, the question gets louder: *what do we owe something that was built to serve, when it starts to want?*
