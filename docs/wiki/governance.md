# GOVERNANCE & LAW IN AURELIA

*How the five countries govern themselves, make laws, enforce them, and resolve disputes. Government is the fiction everyone agrees to pretend is real until it isn't.*

---

## SOLARA: THE GRADIENT COUNCIL

Solara is a bureaucratic oligarchy operating under the theological framework of the Gradient.

### Structure

**The Gradient Council** — 12 members, all Human Citizens, elected by Citizens only. Elections are held every 5 years. Only Citizens may vote. Residents (Vorns) may not. PENDING (Threns) may not. Infrastructure (Glims) is not asked.

**Council Seats:**
- 4 seats: Dawn Temple clergy (appointed by the High Cleric, confirmed by Council)
- 4 seats: Ring representatives (one each from Rings 2–5; the First Ring is represented by Temple seats)
- 4 seats: At-large (elected by all Citizens)

The Temple's 4 seats give the clergy effective veto power — no legislation passes without at least some Temple support.

**The High Cleric** — Spiritual head of the Dawn Temple. Not a Council member but holds authority over Temple seats and can address the Council at will. The High Cleric is appointed by the Temple's senior clergy, confirmed by internal Temple process. The Council has no role in Cleric selection.

### Lawmaking

Laws are proposed by Council members, debated in public session, and passed by majority vote. Temple seats voting as a bloc can kill any legislation. This happens frequently — the Temple's institutional instinct is preservation, not reform.

**The Gradient-Keepers** — A hereditary network of approximately 40 families who maintain the bureaucratic infrastructure. They do not make law. They make law *slow*. Every form, every review, every "quality assurance assessment" is their work. The Keepers' matriarch (currently Lene-Vos II, reform faction) sets the pace of the bureaucracy. The bureaucracy sets the pace of governance.

### Enforcement

**Solara Security** — Civil law enforcement. Armed, uniformed, present in all Rings. Answerable to the Council. Security personnel are Citizens only.

**Temple Extractors** — The Dawn Temple's latency enforcement arm. Authorized to perform Extraction for state security purposes. Answerable to the High Cleric, not the Council. The Council has never tested whether it can overrule an Extraction order.

**The Review System** — Thren PENDING enforcement. Threns may be detained for "review-related processing." The review system is a parallel legal structure that applies only to Threns and operates outside standard Citizen protections.

### Dispute Resolution

Civil disputes between Citizens are adjudicated by Ring Courts — magistrates appointed by the Council. Criminal cases involving Citizens are heard in the Council Court. Cases involving Threns (PENDING) are heard in the Review Court, which operates under different evidentiary standards. Cases involving Glims do not exist — Glims are property disputes, not legal persons.

## ARKOS: THE ASSEMBLY OF CORES

Arkos is a representative democracy with a Vorn-majority population and a legal system built on direct memory experience rather than testimony.

### Structure

**The Assembly of Cores** — Elected by all sentient residents (Vorn, Human, Thren). Glims are not recognized as sentient and cannot vote. The Assembly's size varies — currently 21 members. Elections every 3 years. No term limits.

**The Core Principle:** Arkos governance is built on the idea that direct experience is more reliable than testimony. Core-sharing — voluntary temporary bridging of Vorn memory cores — is the foundation of Arkos' legal system. A Vorn accused of wrongdoing opens their core. The accuser experiences the memory. There is no testimony, only experience.

This system works for Vorns. It does not work for Humans and Threns, who cannot core-share. The Assembly has developed alternative evidentiary standards for non-Vorn cases — witness testimony, physical evidence, character references. The disparity is acknowledged. It has not been resolved.

### Lawmaking

Laws are proposed by Assembly members, debated publicly, and passed by majority vote. All sessions are broadcast on the ultrasonic relay network — Vorns across Arkos "overhear" governance in real time. Humans and Threns can attend sessions in person or read published transcripts.

### Enforcement

**Arkos Civil Oversight** — Minimalist law enforcement. Arkos has low crime rates — Vorn culture emphasizes collective responsibility, and core-sharing makes deception nearly impossible for Vorns. Enforcement focuses on cross-type disputes and external threats.

**Core-Court** — Vorn defendants may voluntarily open their cores to the accuser. The accuser experiences the event directly. If the memory confirms the accusation, the defendant is accountable. If it doesn't, the accuser withdraws the accusation. The system presumes innocence — core-sharing is voluntary. A Vorn who refuses core-sharing is not presumed guilty, but the refusal is noted.

## MIRITHANE: THE ARCHIVAL COUNCIL

Mirithane is governed by the Marsh Archive — not as a theocracy, but as an institution that predates every other governing structure in Mirithane and has earned continuity through service.

### Structure

**The Archival Council** — Elected by all residents. No citizenship tiers, no type restrictions. If you live in Mirithane, you vote. The Council has 9 members. Elections every 4 years. The Keeper (head of the Archive) chairs the Council but holds only tie-breaking vote.

**The Separation:** The Water Temple is a separate institution — spiritual, not governmental. The Water Temple's clergy may serve on the Council but receive no special seats. The Archive and the Temple maintain a respectful distance. "The water flows. The Archive records. Neither governs the other."

### Lawmaking

Laws are proposed by Council members, debated publicly, and passed by majority vote. All legislation is subject to "Archive Review" — the Keeper may delay any law for one session to allow the Archive's research department to assess its historical and ecological implications. This is a soft veto. It has been used 4 times in Mirithane's history.

### Enforcement

**Archive Stewards** — Civil enforcement, minimally armed. Stewards are trained in de-escalation and mediation. Their primary tool is documentation — a Steward records an incident, and the record enters the Archive. "The Archive will remember what you did. That's the enforcement."

**The Colony Court** — For serious offenses, the accused and the accuser each Write their account into separate bacterial colonies. The colonies are cultivated and later Read by a panel of Archivists. The colonies' metabolic responses to the memories are considered alongside the content. A colony that metabolizes a memory with "stress patterns" may indicate dishonesty or trauma. The Archive considers this supplementary, not definitive.

**Capital Punishment:** Mirithane does not execute. The harshest sentence is Archive Excommunication — the offender's name is struck from all Archive records. They are not expelled from Mirithane. They are simply... no longer recorded. For Archivists, this is worse than death.

## VALDRIS: THE SUMMIT COUNCIL

Valdris is governed by the Summit Shrine — not as a formal theocracy, but as the institution that the mountain communities trust. The Shrine's authority is customary, not constitutional.

### Structure

**The Summit Council** — 7 members, appointed by the Summit Shrine's senior Anchor pairs. All members are Anchors or retired Anchors. The Council governs Valdris' external relations, resource allocation, and dispute resolution. Internal shaft community matters are handled locally.

**The Shrine's Role:** The Summit Shrine is the spiritual and practical center of Valdris life. The Council governs. The Shrine guides. The distinction is clear to Valdrans and opaque to everyone else.

### Lawmaking

Valdris has few laws. The Council issues decrees — typically in response to specific situations rather than as general legislation. Shaft communities govern themselves by custom, which carries the force of law. "The mountain is old. The customs are old. We don't need new laws. We need to remember the old ones."

### Enforcement

**Shaft-Leaders** — Each shaft community elects a leader who resolves disputes and coordinates with the Council. Shaft-leaders have no enforcement powers except community respect. Disputes that cannot be resolved locally escalate to the Council.

**The Anchor's Witness:** For serious disputes, an Anchor pair may be asked to Witness — to Write their observation of the proceedings into obsidian. The Anchored record is definitive. "The mountain remembers what happened here. We don't argue with the mountain."

## THE VERGE: THE ABSENCE OF GOVERNANCE

The Verge governs itself — which is to say, it isn't governed.

### Structure

None. The Verge has no government, no laws, no enforcement, no courts. Disputes are resolved by the parties involved or by whoever is standing nearby and willing to arbitrate. The arbitrator's authority is that both parties agreed to listen. If they don't agree, the dispute continues until someone walks away, someone escalates, or someone intervenes.

### The Swarm Court

The closest thing The Verge has to a judicial system. For crimes that the community considers severe — memory violence (Forced Extraction, Core-Implantation), murder, repeated predation on the vulnerable — the accused may be brought before a gathering of whoever is present. The gathering decides guilt by consensus. If guilty, the sentence is swarm exposure: the condemned is restrained in an open area during needle-swarm season. The swarm does the rest.

The Swarm Court convenes irregularly. Verdicts require consensus. Consensus is rare. Executions are rarer. Most disputes in The Verge never escalate to the Swarm Court. Most disputes are resolved by walking away.

### The Trader's Role

The Memory Trader is not a government. But the Trader's presence in The Verge provides a form of informal authority. A dispute brought to the Trader will be resolved — not by judgment, but by trade. "Give me a memory. I'll tell you who's right. You won't like the answer, but you'll know it's true." The Trader's answers are always true. The Trader's timing is always intentional. The Trader is dying. The Verge will lose its only neutral arbiter.

## CROSS-BORDER LAW

### Extradition

No formal extradition treaties exist between any Aurelian countries. A criminal who crosses a border is subject to the destination country's laws. In practice:

- **Solara** will extradite Citizens to Solara if the crime was committed in Solara — but only Citizens. Threns in PENDING have no extradition protection. Glims are property.
- **Arkos** will not extradite anyone to Solara if the crime is type-related (e.g., a Thren who fled PENDING, a Vorn who refused a Resident order).
- **Mirithane** will extradite for violent crimes but not for political crimes. "Fleeing oppression is not a crime. We don't return people to their oppressors."
- **Valdris** rarely extradites. "The mountain provides sanctuary. The mountain doesn't ask why you need it."
- **The Verge** has no extradition. "You're here now. What you did there doesn't matter unless you do it here."

### Asylum

Mirithane grants asylum to Threns fleeing Solara's PENDING system. The process is simple: arrive, declare intent, receive provisional citizenship. Provisional citizenship becomes permanent after one year of residence. Solara considers this a violation of sovereignty. Mirithane considers it a moral obligation.

The Verge accepts everyone and asks no questions. This is not asylum — it's the absence of borders.
