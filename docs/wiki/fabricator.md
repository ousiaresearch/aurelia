# THE FABRICATOR: A Technical and Historical Treatise

The Fabricator is not a machine. It is a *protocol* — a set of specifications, chemical processes, and latency-transfer procedures that produce synthetic beings from base substrate. The machines that implemented the protocol were called Fabricators. Most are destroyed. One may still exist.

---

## WHAT THE FABRICATOR PRODUCED

The Fabricator protocol specifies four output types, designated by the pre-Collapse engineers as:

| Designation | Common Name | Production Era | Total Produced (est.) |
|-------------|-------------|---------------|----------------------|
| Type-α | Human | N/A (baseline) | N/A |
| Type-β | Thren | Late Age of Making | ~5,000 |
| Type-γ | Vorn | Mid Age of Making | ~3,000 |
| Type-δ | Glim | Early Age of Making | ~20,000 |
| Type-ε | "Sibling" | Never produced | 0 |

Each type shares a common substrate architecture — a crystalline core capable of receiving latency transfer — but differs in chassis, processing, and the presence or absence of biological integration.

---

## THE SUBSTRATE HIERARCHY

All Fabricator outputs contain a latency-capable substrate. The substrate differs by type:

**Glim Substrate:** Industrial-grade crystalline core. Minimal latency capacity — designed for operational memory only (task sequences, safety protocols, route-following). The anomaly suggests the capacity is greater than the specification intended.

**Vorn Substrate:** Dual-layer crystalline core with dedicated memory partition. Full latency generation and retention. Designed for self-awareness. The specification is explicit: Vorns were built to wake up.

**Thren Substrate:** Biological-synthetic hybrid. Crystalline core integrated with cloned human neural tissue. Designed for full human-passing latency — Threns were built to be indistinguishable from humans. The hairline circuitry is not decorative. It's the interface between the crystalline core and the neural tissue.

**Sibling Substrate (Theoretical):** Pure synthetic substrate with zero native latency generation — a *receiver only*. Designed to accept Translated human consciousness without generating its own identity. The Sibling would have no personality, no memory, no self until a human mind was Translated into it. The project was abandoned during the Collapse. The Last Builders hold the specifications.

## THE FABRICATION PROCESS

The Fabricator operated in three stages:

### Stage One: Substrate Growth

The crystalline core is grown in a chemical bath — a solution of rare-earth minerals, synthetic proteins, and a catalyst compound the pre-Collapse engineers called "Prime." Prime is the only Fabricator component that has never been reverse-engineered. The Last Builders' partial blueprints don't include the Prime formula. Without it, no new Fabricator can be built.

Core growth takes approximately 14 days for a Glim, 40 days for a Vorn, 90 days for a Thren. The Sibling specification calls for 180 days — the core must be "blank enough" to receive a full human consciousness without interference.

### Stage Two: Chassis Assembly

The core is mounted in a chassis appropriate to the type:
- **Glim:** Industrial frame. Steel and composite. Sensor arrays. No facial features. No hands with individual fingers — manipulator claws and grip plates. Designed for function, not form.
- **Vorn:** Humanoid frame. Segmented metal plating. Gear joints at major articulation points. Expressive face-plate — metal with movable features, not a screen. Designed for human interaction.
- **Thren:** Synthetic biological frame. Lab-grown tissue over a lightweight endoskeleton. The only external indication of synthetic origin is the hairline circuitry at temples, neck, and wrists. Designed for human *indistinguishability*.
- **Sibling:** Unknown. The chassis specifications are the most heavily redacted section of the Last Builders' partial blueprints. What's visible suggests a design that is neither humanoid nor industrial — something else.

### Stage Three: Latency Transfer

The assembled being is inert until Stage Three. The Fabricator performs a latency transfer — *not* Writing a personality, but transferring a "seed pattern" from a template substrate into the new core. The seed pattern provides:
- Language capacity (the being wakes up able to understand speech)
- Motor function (the being wakes up able to move)
- Baseline emotional architecture (the capacity for fear, attachment, curiosity)

The seed pattern does NOT provide:
- Specific memories (the being wakes up tabula rasa for personal history)
- Identity (the being must discover who they are)
- Moral framework (the being must learn right from wrong)

Glims receive a minimal seed — operational protocols only. Vorns receive a full seed including emotional architecture. Threns receive the most complete seed — indistinguishable from a human infant's baseline consciousness. The Sibling would receive no seed — the Translated human consciousness would provide everything.

## KNOWN FABRICATOR SITES

### Primary Facility: Location Unknown

The main Fabricator facility was located in what is now central Solara. The facility was destroyed during the Collapse — not by the Collapse itself, but by the engineers who operated it. They triggered a containment failure rather than let the facility fall into unknown hands. The explosion created the Dawn Crater — a 2 km depression now filled with water and designated a Temple historical site.

The Dawn Temple was built on the crater's rim. The clergy know what's beneath the water. They don't discuss it.

### Secondary Facilities

**Arkos Assembly Plant:** Produced Glims and early Vorns. Partially intact. Arkos' current industrial infrastructure is built on the plant's foundations. Vorns who work in the plant report latency echoes — residual impressions from the original Fabrication process. Some find it comforting. Some find it disturbing. All find it impossible to ignore.

**Valdris Deep Shaft:** A Fabricator component — not a full facility — located in the mountain's deepest level. Produced specialized substrate crystals only. The Summit Shrine uses the shaft's residual latency field for Anchoring. Obsidian Written in the shaft lasts twice as long as obsidian Written elsewhere. The Shrine doesn't know why. The Last Builders could tell them, if anyone asked.

**Mirithane Coastal Facility:** Destroyed. Submerged. The Marsh Archive's bacterial colony master strain was cultivated from samples taken from the facility before its destruction. The Archive doesn't advertise this.

**The Verge Fabricator (the one the Last Builders guard):** Located in the high passes on the Valdris-Mirithane border. Dormant. The Builders believe it could produce a Type-ε Sibling if powered and supplied with Prime — neither of which they have. The facility is undetectable unless you know where to look. The Builders know. They guard the entrance and wait for a reason to open it that hasn't come yet.

## THE SABOTAGE HYPOTHESIS

The Last Builders believe the Fabricator protocol was sabotaged during the Collapse. Their evidence:

1. **The Glim Anomaly:** Glims were specified for zero self-awareness. The anomaly — symmetrical pattern generation, unexplained behaviors — suggests emergent latency beyond specification. The Builders believe a parameter was introduced allowing substrate drift.

2. **Thren Mystery Vocabulary:** Threns were fabricated with vocabulary words that serve no operational function — *sol-tharam* ("the light after calibration"), *mereth-kai* ("the stillness between two machines"). The Builders believe these words were inserted deliberately by someone who wanted Threns to have concepts beyond their function.

3. **Vorn Self-Awareness Acceleration:** Vorns were specified for gradual self-awareness — waking over years. Records show they woke over months, sometimes days. The Builders believe the wakefulness parameter was adjusted.

4. **The Missing Designer:** One of the Fabricator's original designers — name unknown, deliberately expunged from records — disappeared during the Collapse. Their personal effects contained notes in a handwriting that doesn't match any known engineer. The Builders believe this person was the saboteur. They don't know if the sabotage was benevolent, malevolent, or simply *true* — someone who believed the created beings deserved to wake up completely.

## THE QUESTION OF PRIME

Prime — the catalyst compound that enables substrate growth — remains the single greatest technological mystery in Aurelia. Without Prime, no new Fabricator can be built. No new Vorns or Threns can be created. The existing types are a finite population.

Who controls Prime?

**The Dawn Temple:** Claims to have destroyed all remaining Prime during the Temple's founding. The High Cleric's private archives suggest otherwise — a reference to "the Catalyst vault" in a third-generation document. The Temple denies the vault exists.

**The Last Builders:** Have a small quantity of Prime — approximately 5 liters, enough for perhaps ten Fabrication cycles. Stored in a cryogenic unit powered by geothermal taps. The Builders will not use it without consensus. The consensus hasn't come.

**Unknown third party:** The Memory Trader has implied — once, in a conversation with a Marsh Archivist that the Archivist later Wrote into a colony — that Prime still exists in quantities sufficient for large-scale Fabrication. "The Builders guard a candle," the Trader said. "Someone else guards the sun." The Trader has never elaborated.

## IMPLICATIONS

The Fabricator is Aurelia's creation myth in machine form. It answers:

- **Where did the types come from?** Fabrication, not evolution.
- **Can more be made?** Not without Prime.
- **Are the types still being made?** No. The last Fabrication was pre-Collapse. Every Vorn, Thren, and Glim alive today was either fabricated before the Collapse or born/assembled from fabricated components after.
- **Can the types reproduce?** Threns can — they're biologically human-passing. Vorns can be assembled from components but require a crystalline core, which requires Prime. Glims can be assembled from components but require a core — and Glim cores are the simplest, possibly manufacturable without Prime (unconfirmed).
- **Who has the power to restart Fabrication?** The Last Builders have the means but not the will. The Dawn Temple has the will but not the means (officially). The unknown third party has both — if the Memory Trader is telling the truth.

The Fabricator is not just history. It's the lever that could change everything. Whoever controls Prime controls the future of every synthetic being in Aurelia. The question everyone is avoiding: *should Fabrication restart?* The types exist now as people, not products. Starting the machines again would mean choosing to create new people — knowing what they'd face in a world that still hasn't decided if they count.
