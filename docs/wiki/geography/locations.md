# Locations & Landmarks — Aurelia

> *Every country has places that concentrate meaning. These are the sites where the question is asked most clearly.*

## Solara

**Dawn Temple:** Eastern coast, elevated on a limestone bluff. One room. Open eastern wall. Pale stone floor. The most sacred site in Solara. Also, paradoxically, the most political — the Cantor's silence on the Glim question is the only institutional position the temple takes.

**Lumen Plaza:** Central Solara. Open square surrounded by council chambers, administrative buildings, and the Cantor's residence. The public heart of the republic. Ballot Night occurs here. The citizenship review results are read from the council steps. Threns stand in the plaza and wait.

**Reef Labs:** Coastal shelf, partially submerged. The scientific pride of the federation. Coral symbiosis research. Cael-7's domain. The labs are also where Solara's most advanced latency research occurs — the material science of memory extraction was developed here.

**Floating Market:** A collection of permanently moored vessels and floating platforms off the coastal shelf. Trade hub. The primary economic interface between Solara and the rest of the federation. Thren vendors outnumber human vendors. The market is the most integrated space in Solara.

**Solar Farm Alpha:** Primary energy installation. Kilometers of floating solar arrays. Self-maintaining since pre-Collapse. The arrays feed the sand-glass towers in Arkos through an energy credit exchange system. The exchange rate is renegotiated annually. The negotiation is always tense.

## Arkos

**Glass Forum:** Central governance chamber. Glass walls, transparent ceiling. Light-pulse voting system. The Forum is the political, cultural, and symbolic heart of Arkos. Echo stands before it every morning. The Pulse is observed from it every midnight.

**Sand-Glass Towers:** Hexagonal storage structures rising the full height of the arcology. Contain energy-storage sand infused with thermal-retention compounds. Glow from within at night. The towers are the most photographed structures in Arkos, and the most sacred — Vorns consider them the arcology's circulatory system.

**The Glim Sanctuary:** Lower level, converted maintenance bay. Sixteen anomalous Glims. The keepers are volunteer Vorns. The sanctuary is not open to the public. The sanctuary is the only space in Arkos where the classification of Glims (Sheltered, not property, not persons) is tested daily — by the keepers who maintain them, and by the Glims who pause at dawn.

**Nomad Camp:** Outside the southern wall, clustered around a natural aquifer. Population fluctuates — humans who couldn't adapt to the arcology, desert wanderers who chose the camp, the occasional Thren passing through. The camp is not recognized as a residential district. The camp is also the only fully human space in Arkos.

## Mirithane

**Water Temple:** Floating platform at the edge of the deep marsh. No walls. Knee-deep water over the surface. The temple is the diagnostic, theological, and educational center of Mirithane. The Consensus Pool — the bacterial archive of community decisions — is located here.

**The Deep Marsh:** Central pool, deepest point in the marsh (approximately 4 meters). The submerged Glim chassis are here. The bioluminescent bacteria peak in brightness here. The Night the Marsh Remembers is celebrated here. The deep marsh is the closest Mirithane has to a sacred center.

**The Arrival Tapestry:** A living coral-and-reed tapestry at the eastern border, depicting the moment Veren-3 walked into Mirithane. Still growing after 140 years. The tapestry is the physical record of Mirithane's founding principle: the reeds don't ask.

**The Consensus Platform:** Central reed platform, largest in Mirithane. Community decisions are reached here. The platform has been rebuilt three times — expanded as the community grew. "The platform is temporary," the Elder says at the start of every consensus. "The consensus is not."

## Valdris

**The Forge District:** A chain of carved chambers descending the canyon wall. Forty-three active forges. The evening anvil cascade — forty-three strikes from highest to lowest — is the daily ritual of Valdris. The forge district is visible from 10 kilometers at night — a chain of orange pinpricks descending into darkness.

**Summit Shrine:** 4,100 meters. The obsidian wall. 847 names carved by hand. The blank slab waiting for the wall to fill. The shrine is the closest Valdris has to a temple. The shrine does not worship. The shrine remembers.

**Section 3-B:** 800 meters underground. The collapse site. The beam Kael-12 held. The brass plaque: KAEL-12. 2083-2123. HELD. The section is an active mining zone — the ore still needs to be cut. The monument and the workplace are the same space. Valdris does not separate memory from labor.

**Midcamp:** 2,200 meters. Transition point between the base and the summit. Contains the trauma station, the mid-shift barracks, and the temporary clay wall where failed Summit Ascent names are recorded. The clay is fired and carried to the summit at year's end.

## The Verge

**The Crossroads:** The center. A flat expanse of packed earth. The charging station where stories are told. The stew pot. The Glim that stands motionless, chest LED blinking amber. The crossroads are not a location in the geographic sense — they are where you stop walking.

**The Memory Trader's Stall:** A salvaged door on two rusted charging stations. East side of the crossroads. Opens at dusk. Closes at dawn — or doesn't. The Memory Wall — the outer surface of the door — is covered in objects left and taken. The stall is the closest thing The Verge has to an institution.

**The Drone Graveyard:** West of the crossroads. A field of decommissioned Glim parts — chassis, gripper arms, sensor arrays — arranged over decades into rough sculptures. The graveyard hums in 5/4 time. The source of the hum is unknown. The Verge does not investigate.

**The Canyon Sanctuary:** Hidden. Location passed by word of mouth. The Thren refuge — Solara Threns who refused conditional citizenship, Arkos Threns who tired of diplomatic status, Threns from everywhere who wanted to exist outside the question. "The entrance is a rumor you earn the right to know."
