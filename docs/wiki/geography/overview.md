# Geography of Aurelia — Overview

> *The land shaped the question. Five biomes, five countries, one federation built on terrain that asks different things of different bodies.*

## The Five Biomes

Aurelia's five countries occupy distinct ecological zones, separated by natural barriers that made the Collapse survivable. Each zone determined what infrastructure survived, which in turn determined who gathered there, which in turn determined each country's answer to the question.

### The Coastal Shelf — Solara

An 840-kilometer coastline running northwest-southeast. Shallow waters. Coral reefs extending kilometers offshore. Pre-Collapse solar farms float in formation along the shelf — automated arrays that survived the energy grid failure because they were self-contained. The coastal climate is temperate, humid, mild winters. The soil is thin — agriculture is limited to terrace farming and kelp cultivation. The reef provides protein. The sun provides energy. Solara's abundance is geographic before it is political.

### The Glass Wastes — Arkos

A deep desert extending inland from Solara's coastal range. The Glass Wastes earned their name from the pre-Collapse glass-sand deposits — industrial byproduct that fused into obsidian sheets during the resource wars. The desert is uninhabitable without the arcology. The arcology was built here deliberately — isolation as defense. The Nomad Camp survives at the southern wall where a natural aquifer breaks the surface. The water is mineral-heavy, barely potable. The camp drinks it anyway.

### Memory Marsh — Mirithane

A vast wetland at the southern edge of the federation, fed by a river system that originates in Valdris's highlands. The marsh is shallow — average depth 1.2 meters — threaded with channels, reed islands, and deep pools. The bioluminescent bacteria *P. memoriae* is endemic to the marsh, not introduced. Whether the bacteria predate the Collapse or emerged in its aftermath is unknown. The marsh's ecology was engineered before the Collapse as a water filtration system — the reeds and bacteria were designed. The design survived. The designers did not.

### The Mountain — Valdris

A mountain range running along the federation's eastern edge. The highest peak — Mount Gauntlet — reaches 4,100 meters. The summit is above the tree line; the base is temperate forest. The mountain's interior is a network of pre-Collapse mines, expanded over centuries until the shafts reach 800+ meters. The deepest sections are hot, pressurized, and partially unmapped. The mountain is geologically stable — the Collapse's seismics did not reach this far inland. The stability is why Valdris survived. The forge district occupies a canyon carved by a river that still runs at the valley floor. The river provides water, power, and transport downstream to Mirithane.

### The Crossroads — The Verge

Not a biome. A convergence point. The crossroads occupy a dry plateau at the geographic center of the five territories — equidistant from Solara's coast, Arkos's arcology, Mirithane's marsh, and Valdris's mountain. The terrain is flat, dusty, unremarkable. No resources. No water except a single well drilled into an aquifer that nobody has mapped. The crossroads became The Verge because they were nowhere — the place you reach when you're traveling between countries and run out of road.

## Borders & Barriers

- **Solara ⇄ Arkos:** The coastal range — a low mountain chain that captures moisture from the sea. The western slopes are green and inhabited. The eastern slopes descend into the Glass Wastes. The transition is abrupt — within ten kilometers, the climate shifts from temperate to arid. A solar rail line (partially completed) climbs the pass but does not extend into the desert.
- **Arkos ⇄ Valdris:** Open desert transitioning to foothills. The primary trade route follows a dry riverbed that floods during the Memory season — passable only during Forge and Glass.
- **Valdris ⇄ Mirithane:** The river that runs through the Valdris canyon continues downstream, widening into the marsh. The transition is gradual — the river slows, the banks widen, the first reeds appear. The border between Valdris and Mirithane is not marked. The marsh decides where the mountain ends.
- **Solara ⇄ Mirithane:** Coastline. Sea travel is possible but difficult — the coastal waters are reef-dense, and the reef damages hulls. Most travel between Solara and Mirithane goes overland through Valdris.
- **All ⇄ The Verge:** The crossroads are reachable from every country. The paths are not maintained. The Verge does not maintain anything.

## Weather & Climate

**Seasons:**
- **Dawn (Jan–Feb):** Mild across all territories. The desert is passable. The marsh is at its lowest water level.
- **Forge (Mar–May):** Warming. Valdris's summit snow melts, feeding the river. The marsh expands — water levels rise, reed growth accelerates. The desert becomes dangerous — daytime temperatures exceed 50°C.
- **Memory (Jun–Jul):** Peak heat. The marsh's bioluminescent bacteria peak in activity — the glow is brightest. The desert is impassable during daylight. Trade routes shift to night travel.
- **Glass (Aug–Sep):** Cooling. The desert is passable again. The marsh's bacteria begin their seasonal decline. Valdris's summit receives first snowfall in late Glass.
- **Dusk (Oct–Dec):** Cold. Valdris's summit is snowbound — the Summit Ascent pilgrimage occurs in late Memory, before the snow closes the path. The desert is cold at night, temperate during the day. The marsh's water level drops as the river flow decreases.

## Natural Hazards

- **Solara:** Storm surges during Forge season. The Floating Market has been rebuilt four times. The council considers this maintenance.
- **Arkos:** Sandstorms during Memory — visibility drops to zero. The arcology's walls are rated for 200 km/h winds. The Nomad Camp's tents are not.
- **Mirithane:** Flooding during Memory — the marsh expands, platforms are submerged. Mirithaneans consider flooding positive. "The water is rising. The reeds are growing. We rebuild."
- **Valdris:** Rockfalls, gas pockets in the deep shafts. The primary hazards are underground. The mountain moves. The mountain decides.
- **The Verge:** Everything. The Verge has no infrastructure and no defenses. The desert, the storms, the cold — the crossroads are exposed. The Memory Trader's stall disappears during sandstorms. It reappears after. The disappearance is not explained.
