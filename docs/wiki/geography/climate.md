# Climate & Weather Systems — Aurelia

> *The sky shapes the question too. What falls from it determines who lives where.*

## Solara — Maritime Temperate

The coastal shelf enjoys the federation's most stable climate. Prevailing winds off the ocean moderate temperature — summers rarely exceed 30°C, winters rarely drop below 5°C. The reef buffers storm surges — waves break on the coral before reaching the shore. Annual rainfall is moderate, concentrated in Memory season. The reliability of the climate is why Solara became the diplomatic capital — the council can meet year-round without weather interruption.

**Hazards:** Storm surges during Forge, when tropical systems spin off the ocean. The Floating Market has been rebuilt four times. The Dawn Temple has never been damaged — "the light protects it," the Cantor says. The light, or the temple's elevated position on the coastal bluff. The Cantor does not distinguish.

## Arkos — Hyper-Arid Desert

The Glass Wastes receive less than 50mm of rainfall annually. Daytime temperatures in Memory exceed 55°C — fatal to unprotected humans within hours. Nighttime temperatures in Dusk drop below freezing. The arcology's internal climate is controlled — 22°C steady, 40% humidity. The Nomad Camp has no climate control. "You learn to sleep when it's cool and move when it's not," Kel Voss says. "The desert doesn't negotiate."

**Hazards:** Sandstorms — the prevailing wind patterns funnel through the coastal range and accelerate across the open desert. Storms can last days. Visibility drops to zero. The arcology's outer walls are rated for the worst recorded storm. The Nomad Camp's tents are not.

## Mirithane — Humid Subtropical Wetland

The marsh creates its own weather. Evaporation from the shallow water produces near-constant humidity — 80-95% year-round. Fog is common at dawn, lifting as the day warms. Rainfall is plentiful, distributed across all seasons but peaking in Memory when river flow from Valdris is highest. The marsh's temperature is stable — water moderates extremes. Winters cool but never freeze.

**Hazards:** Flooding. The marsh receives Valdris's entire watershed. A heavy snowmelt year means the water rises, platforms are submerged, the ecosystem reorganizes. Mirithaneans consider flooding regenerative. "The marsh remakes itself," the Water Temple's records note. "We remake ourselves with it."

## Valdris — Alpine Vertical

The mountain's climate changes with elevation. Basecamp (800m) is temperate — warm summers, cool winters. Midcamp (2,200m) is alpine — snow from late Glass through early Forge. The Summit (4,100m) is arctic — below freezing year-round, winds exceeding 100 km/h during Glass and Dusk. The deep shafts (800m underground) are geothermally heated — 30°C steady, independent of surface weather.

**Hazards:** Altitude sickness above 3,000m. Avalanches during Memory snowmelt. The Summit Ascent pilgrimage occurs in late Memory specifically to minimize avalanche risk. The risk is never zero. "The mountain decides," the keepers say. The mountain's decisions include who reaches the summit and who doesn't.

## The Verge — Exposed Plateau

The crossroads are at approximately 1,200m elevation, flat, exposed. The climate is extreme by any standard: scorching in Memory (40°C+ daytime), freezing in Dusk (below 0°C at night). No natural shelter. No climate control. The Verge experiences every season without mitigation. Survival requires adaptation. Adaptation requires luck. "The weather here doesn't care if you're staying," a long-timer says. "The weather here doesn't care about anything."
