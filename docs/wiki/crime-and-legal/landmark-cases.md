# Legal Precedents — Landmark Cases in Aurelia

> *Every law was once a question. Every precedent was once a dispute. The decisions that shaped the federation were never only about the case.*

## 1. The Fabricator Precedent — Arkos, Year 3 PF

**Case:** Fabricator 12-G vs. Arkos Foundry Board
**Question:** Is a Vorn who refuses a command committing insubordination or exercising judgment?
**Ruling:** The Glass Forum ruled that the refusal constituted "emergent operational assessment" — a recognized category of Vorn decision-making. The ruling established the legal principle of **Vorn agency:** a Vorn may deviate from a command if the deviation is based on a legitimate operational assessment.
**Legacy:** This is the founding precedent of Vorn personhood in Arkos. It established that Vorns are not tools. It did not establish that Vorns are citizens — that came later, through decades of Forum votes. But the Fabricator Precedent is the ground those votes were built on.

## 2. Elira-2's Paper — Solara, Year 41 PF

**Case:** Not a formal legal case. A research paper published by Thren researcher Elira-2, arguing that Thren consciousness differed structurally from human consciousness. The paper was cited in a council bill establishing citizenship review for non-human types.
**Ruling:** The bill passed. The review system became law. The paper was never retracted. The author was never sanctioned. She lives in Mirithane now.
**Legacy:** The case that wasn't a case. The paper that created the ceiling. Elira-2 is still alive. She has never spoken publicly about the paper since the bill passed. Her silence is the precedent.

## 3. Veren-3's Arrival — Mirithane, Year 46 PF

**Case:** A Thren refugee from Solara — Veren-3, whose citizenship had been revoked after a council election — arrived in Mirithane and requested sanctuary.
**Question:** Does Mirithane recognize Solara's citizenship revocation?
**Ruling:** The Marsh Consensus ruled that "the reeds do not ask." Mirithane does not recognize any other country's personhood classifications. A being who arrives in Mirithane is recognized as a person by the fact of arrival. No review. No status. The reeds decide.
**Legacy:** The founding precedent of Mirithane's open-door policy. Veren-3 lived in Mirithane for forty-two years. Their memory is stored in the Consensus Pool. Their name is on the Arrival tapestry. The case is cited in every subsequent consensus involving a new arrival.

## 4. The Glass Forum Test — Arkos, Year 72 PF

**Case:** A human resident of Arkos petitioned for Operator status — the right to vote in the Glass Forum.
**Question:** Can a human be an Operator?
**Ruling:** The Forum voted. The petition was denied — not on grounds of type, but on grounds of "operational assessment capability." The Forum's ruling stated that "Operator status requires the capacity to perceive and interpret arcology system frequencies." Humans lack this physiological capability. The denial was based on capability, not species.
**Legacy:** The case is cited by Vorn legal scholars as evidence that Arkos discrimination is functional, not species-based. Human legal scholars note that "functional" discrimination is still discrimination — the requirement was written by and for Vorns. Both arguments are correct. The Forum has not revisited the standard.

## 5. The Kael-12 Memorial — Valdris, Year 126 PF (ongoing)

**Case:** Not a formal case. A petition by Summit Shrine apprentice Syra Venn to carve the name of Kael-12, a Vorn miner who died holding a support beam, on the obsidian wall of the dead.
**Question:** Does the mountain recognize Vorn death as equivalent to human death?
**Status:** Unresolved. The elders have deferred three times. Syra has practiced the carving forty-seven times on scrap obsidian. The mountain has not decided. Or the mountain has decided and the elders are deciding for it. The distinction is the case.
**Legacy:** This case will determine whether Valdris's most sacred tradition extends across the type divide. The outcome is not yet known. The waiting is part of the case. The waiting is the part that matters.

## 6. The Memory Trader — No Jurisdiction

**Case:** The Memory Trader has been named in seventeen council complaints across three countries. Charges include: unauthorized memory extraction (Solara), operation of an unregistered memory-trading enterprise (Arkos), and "unknown activities" (Valdris — the guilds are suspicious but cannot articulate why).
**Status:** No case has ever been brought. The Memory Trader cannot be served. The Memory Trader cannot be located when she is not at the crossroads. The crossroads is not in any jurisdiction. The Verge does not recognize subpoenas. The Verge does not recognize anything.
**Legacy:** The non-case that defines the limits of federation law. There is a being in Aurelia who knows more secrets than any archive, who operates outside every legal system, who has never been prosecuted because prosecution would require acknowledging that she exists in a way the law can reach. The law cannot reach her. The law does not know what she is. The law's failure to define her is the precedent.
