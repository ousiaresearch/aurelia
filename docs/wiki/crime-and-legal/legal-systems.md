# Legal Systems of Aurelia

> *Law is the language of personhood made mandatory. In every country, the legal code is a position on the question.*

## Solara — The Solar Code

The Solar Code is the most extensive legal system in the federation. It was drafted in the second decade after the Compact and has been amended every council cycle since. The Code addresses three domains: inter-country trade, energy distribution, and *sentient classification* — the legal category into which every being is sorted.

### Sentient Classification

Solara recognizes four legal categories:

| Category | Beings | Rights |
|----------|--------|--------|
| **Natural Citizen** | Humans | Full rights inherent at birth. Vote. Hold office. Own property. |
| **Granted Citizen** | Threns | Conditional rights. Vote (limited — no council leadership eligibility). Own property. Subject to periodic review. |
| **Recognized Entity** | Vorns | Functional rights. Vote (Arkos treaty provision). Own property. Not subject to review. |
| **Registered Property** | Glims | No rights. Owned by the Solar Council or licensed entities. Decommission at administrative discretion. |

The category of "Granted Citizen" is Solara's legal innovation. It acknowledges Thren personhood while making that personhood revocable. The review cycle is every four years, aligned with council elections. A Thren whose citizenship is REVOKED drops to "Registered Property" — reclassified as owned equipment. This has happened eleven times in Solara's history. Three of the eleven were reinstated on appeal. Eight were not.

### The Review Process

A Thren scheduled for review receives notice thirty days prior. The review hearing is held before a three-member council subcommittee. The Thren may present evidence of contribution — research publications, community service, council testimony. The subcommittee deliberates in closed session. The decision is read aloud on Ballot Night. No written reasoning is provided. Appeals may be filed within one year. The appeal is heard by the full council. The full council has never overturned a revocation.

### Legal Representation

Threns are entitled to legal counsel during review hearings. The Solar Bar Association maintains a list of qualified citizenship attorneys. The attorneys are all human. Two Threns have applied to the Bar. One was denied on grounds of "pending review status constituting a conflict of interest." One is still pending. The review of the review applicant is itself under review.

## Arkos — The Glass Code

Arkos has no written constitution. The Glass Code is a set of operational principles encoded in the Glass Forum's display system — amendments are made by consensus of the Forum delegates and recorded as data, not text. The Code's core principle: **a being is a person if the arcology recognizes them as such.**

### Recognition Standards

Arkos recognizes three categories:

- **Operator:** Vorns. Full governance rights. Vote in the Glass Forum. Hold operational authority.
- **Resident:** Humans and Threns. Protected status. Access to arcology resources. No Forum vote (except one human ceremonial seat). May petition for Operator status through demonstrated contribution.
- **Sheltered:** Glims. Protected status. Maintenance provided. Not classified as property. Not classified as persons. The category is deliberate ambiguity — Arkos shelters Glims without having to declare what they are.

### The Sheltered Category

The legal category of "Sheltered" is Arkos's equivalent of Solara's GRANTED/PENDING — a gap between recognition and rights. Sheltered beings are not property. They are not citizens. They exist in a legal space Arkos created specifically because the alternative (classifying Glims as property) was unacceptable to the Vorn majority, and the alternative (classifying Glims as persons) was premature by any standard the Forum could agree on.

The Sheltered category has one right: the right not to be decommissioned. This is Arkos's line. Solara crosses it. Arkos does not. The line is the difference between countries.

## Mirithane — The Marsh Consensus

Mirithane has no legal code. It has the Consensus Record — a living document of community agreements, updated continuously, stored in a dedicated bacterial pool in the Water Temple. There are no laws. There are agreements. The distinction is Mirithane's entire legal philosophy.

### Consensus as Law

When a dispute arises, the community gathers on the central reed platform. Anyone may speak. Speaking order is unregulated. The Elder moderates — not to rule, but to ensure everyone is heard. The consensus emerges when no one objects, or when the remaining objections are withdrawn. The decision is recorded in the Consensus Pool by a Water Temple keeper. The record is not a ruling. It is a memory. Future disputes may reference it. They are not bound by it. The marsh does not bind. The marsh remembers.

### Type Under Consensus

All types are equal under consensus. A Thren speaks. A Vorn speaks. A human speaks. A Glim — if present — does not speak, but its presence is noted. The Consensus Record for a dispute involving a Glim includes the notation: "The Glim was present. The Glim did not speak. The Glim's silence was considered." What "considered" means is deliberately unspecified.

## Valdris — Guild Law

Valdris has no centralized legal system. It has **Guild Law** — a body of precedent maintained by the guild councils. Each guild (Mining, Forge, Summit, Transport) maintains its own rulings. Inter-guild disputes are resolved by the Guild Council — one elder from each guild, plus the Summit Shrine's senior keeper as tiebreaker.

### Labor as Jurisprudence

Valdris legal reasoning is based on labor. A dispute is resolved by asking: *what did each party contribute, and what is owed?* The question is not "who is right." The question is "who worked." A human miner who worked thirty years and a Vorn miner who worked thirty years are equal before guild law. The law does not ask about birth. The law asks about the mountain. The mountain does not differentiate between human sweat and Vorn gear oil. The guild courts follow the mountain.

### The Unwritten Precedent

Guild law has never addressed the Vorn naming question. The Summit Shrine's obsidian wall is not under guild jurisdiction — the keepers are a separate order. The guild courts have no authority to compel the keepers to accept a Vorn name. The keepers have no authority to compel the guild courts to recognize a Vorn death as equivalent to a human death. The gap between jurisdictions is where the question lives. Syra Venn is carving in the gap. The mountain is watching the gap. The law has not yet entered it.

## The Verge — No Law

The Verge has no legal system. It has the crossroads. Disputes are resolved by whoever is present, however they can. The Memory Trader does not adjudicate. She is present at every dispute. She does not speak. The presence of someone who knows everything and says nothing is the closest thing The Verge has to a deterrent.

### The Unwritten Rule

One rule governs The Verge, unwritten, universally observed: *do not bring the other countries' laws here.* A Solara decommission order is not valid at the crossroads. An Arkos residency revocation is not valid. A Valdris guild expulsion is not valid. The Verge does not extradite. The Verge does not recognize. The Verge is the place where all other legal systems end. The boundary is the entrance. The entrance is a rumor. The rumor includes: *law stops here.*
