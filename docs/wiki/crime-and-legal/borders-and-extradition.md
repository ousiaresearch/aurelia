# Borders & Extradition in Aurelia

> *A border is a legal argument written in geography. Aurelia's borders are porous for some, absolute for others.*
>

## Federation Borders

The five countries of Aurelia share open borders under the Solar-Mountain Compact. No visas. No checkpoints. A citizen of Solara may walk into Arkos without documentation. A resident of Mirithane may pole a punt into Valdris's watershed without permission. The Compact's border provision states: "The federation's people shall move freely among its territories."

The provision does not specify who qualifies as "people." The ambiguity is the Compact's foundational compromise.

### Type-Specific Border Reality

- **Humans:** Cross freely. Never questioned. Never stopped.
- **Threns:** Cross freely in theory. In practice, Threns entering Solara from other countries are subject to "identity verification" — an informal check at the Floating Market's port authority. The check is not codified. The check is not acknowledged. The check occurs.
- **Vorns:** Cross freely. Occasionally stopped at Solara's border for "customs inspection" — a process that takes Vorn travelers three times longer than human travelers. The inspector's rationale: "Vorn chassis can conceal undeclared cargo." Vorns do not conceal cargo. Vorns are the cargo. The inspection is not acknowledged as profiling. The inspection is profiling.
- **Glims:** Do not cross borders in the legal sense. A Glim walking from Solara into The Verge is not crossing a border — it is walking away from its owner. Solara considers this theft of council property. The Verge does not recognize property. The Glim is in a legal gray zone between territories. The zone is the Glim condition.

## Extradition

### Federation Policy

The Compact includes an extradition framework: a person accused of a crime in one country may be returned to face prosecution. The framework has never been formally invoked. Every extradition request has been resolved informally — the accused returns voluntarily, or the requesting country drops the matter, or the accused moves to The Verge and becomes unreachable.

### The Verge Exception

The Verge does not extradite. This is not policy. This is geography. The Verge has no central authority to receive an extradition request. The Memory Trader does not receive legal correspondence. The crossroads has no address. A Solara council agent attempting to serve a warrant at the crossroads would need to find the crossroads, find the accused, and convince them to return. The agent would also need to ignore the Memory Trader, who would be watching from her stall, who would know exactly what the agent was doing, who would say nothing, whose silence would be the only response the agent received.

No extradition attempt has ever been made at the crossroads. The absence of attempts is the Verge's foreign policy.

## The Decommissioned Glim Question

A decommissioned Glim that walks from Solara into Arkos is legally contested. Solara considers the Glim stolen property. Arkos considers the Glim Sheltered. The legal conflict has never been adjudicated — no council hearing has addressed it, no Glass Forum vote has resolved it. The Glims themselves are not consulted. Glims cannot be consulted. Consulted requires language.

The anomaly Glims who reach Arkos are registered in the sanctuary log. The log is not shared with Solara. Solara does not ask for it. The non-asking and the non-sharing are the diplomatic resolution. The resolution is: we will not discuss the Glims who walk. The walking is the only statement. The walking is the only law that applies to them.
