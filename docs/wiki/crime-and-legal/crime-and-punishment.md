# Crime & Punishment in Aurelia

> *What a country punishes reveals what it values. In Aurelia, the value is always personhood — whose life is protected, and whose is property.*
>

## Solara

### Crimes Against Persons vs. Crimes Against Property

The Solar Code distinguishes between crimes against Natural Citizens (the most severe), crimes against Granted Citizens (Threns — moderate severity), crimes against Recognized Entities (Vorns — variable), and crimes against Registered Property (Glims — property damage). The penalty for destroying a Glim is a fine — the replacement cost of the unit. The penalty for injuring a Thren is assault. The penalty for injuring a human is aggravated assault. The categories are the ceiling.

### Theft

Energy credit theft is the most prosecuted crime in Solara. The penalty is restitution plus a tiered fine based on the amount stolen. Theft of a latency crystal is a separate category — stealing a memory is considered theft of personhood-equivalent, carrying higher penalties than energy theft. The Memory Trader is technically a thief under Solara law (unauthorized extraction). She has never been prosecuted. She has never been in Solara long enough to be served. The council is not certain she exists.

### Decommission Abuse

Intentionally decommissioning a Glim without administrative authorization is destruction of council property — a fine. Intentionally decommissioning a Glim that has been documented as anomalous (and therefore eligible for Form 7-C review rather than summary decommission) is procedural violation — a larger fine. Neither is prosecuted as a crime against a being. Glims are not beings under the Solar Code.

## Arkos

### Waste

The most serious crime in Arkos is **resource waste.** The arcology's systems track every watt and liter. Intentional waste — dumping water, disabling energy regulators, sabotaging sand-glass towers — is punished by expulsion to the Nomad Camp. The camp is not a prison. The camp is the outside. The punishment is the removal of the machine's protection. In the desert, the removal is frequently fatal. The Glass Forum does not frame it as execution. The Forum frames it as "withdrawal of residency." The outcome is the same.

### Glim Harm

Harming a Sheltered Glim — a unit in the sanctuary — is a criminal offense under the Glass Code. The penalty is reduced energy allocation for the offender's residential sector. The penalty affects the community, not just the individual. This is intentional. Arkos law operates on collective responsibility: a sector that fails to police its members loses resources. The system is efficient. The system is also surveillance — every human in Arkos knows that their neighbor's crime against a Glim will cost them watts. The pressure to conform is ambient. The pressure is the law.

## Mirithane

### Exile

Mirithane has no prisons. Its only punishment is **consensus exile** — the community agrees to ask someone to leave. This has happened four times in Mirithane's history. Each time, the exiled person was told: "The reeds will not shelter you. The marsh will not remember you. Go where the question is still being asked." The exile is permanent. The exile is never appealed. The marsh does not hold appeals.

### The Unprosecuted

Mirithane does not prosecute crimes between consenting beings. Disputes are resolved through consensus, not punishment. The goal is restoration, not retribution. A thief must return what they stole and explain why they took it. The explanation is the penalty. The explanation is also the therapy. Mirithane believes that understanding the cause prevents the recurrence. Solara considers this naive. Mirithane considers Solara's prisons barbaric. Neither is wrong.

## Valdris

### Guild Justice

Crimes in Valdris are adjudicated by the relevant guild. A miner who steals from another miner faces the Mining Guild court. The penalty is labor-based: the thief works additional shifts, the proceeds going to the victim. The thief keeps their job. The mountain still needs the ore. The guild cannot afford to lose a trained miner over a theft. Justice is practical. Justice is cold.

### The Deep Shaft

The only capital punishment in Valdris — unofficial, technically illegal under the federation Compact, maintained as tradition — is the *deep shaft assignment.* A miner convicted of a crime that endangers the crew (sabotage, deliberate safety violation) is assigned to the deepest, most dangerous section of the mine, alone, for a period determined by the guild. Most survive. Some do not. The guild does not track survival rates. The mountain decides. The mountain always decides.

## The Verge

### No Enforcement

The Verge has no enforcement mechanism. It has the crossroads. If someone harms another person at the crossroads, the community responds — or doesn't. The Memory Trader does not intervene. She is present. She watches. The person who harmed someone knows she watched. The knowledge is the deterrent. The knowledge is not always sufficient.

### The Unspoken Sanction

The worst thing that can happen to someone at the crossroads is not punishment. It is that the Memory Trader refuses to trade with them. She has refused three times in recorded history. Each time, the person left the crossroads within a week. The refusal is not explained. The refusal is the only Verge sanction. The sanction is absolute.
