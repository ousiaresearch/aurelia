# MEDICINE, HEALING & THE BODY IN AURELIA

*How the people of Aurelia understand health, treat illness, and care for the dying. Medicine is where a world's science, culture, and values meet the irreducible fact of the body.*

---

## THE FOUR MEDICAL SYSTEMS

Aurelia has no unified medical tradition. Each country developed its own approach after the Collapse, shaped by available resources, dominant type populations, and cultural values. A patient's treatment depends on where they are — and what they are.

---

## SOLARA: THE GRADIENT OF CARE

Solari medicine mirrors the Gradient: tiered by Ring, restricted by Classification, and centrally controlled through the Dawn Temple.

### Tier 1: Temple Medicine (First & Second Rings)

The Dawn Temple maintains Solara's only advanced medical facilities — the Crater Infirmary, built into the inner crater wall. Temple-trained physicians, supported by Temple-trained Thren nurses (the only Thren professionals the Temple officially recognizes), provide comprehensive care.

**Capabilities:**
- **Surgery:** Pre-Collapse techniques maintained through Temple medical texts. Sterile procedure, anesthesia (herbal and distillate-based), post-operative care.
- **Pharmaceuticals:** Imported Mirithane bacterial colony derivatives — antibiotics, anti-inflammatories, pain management compounds. Stored in Temple-secured vaults. Distribution is controlled. The Second Ring receives consistent supply. The Third Ring receives intermittent supply.
- **Latency Medicine:** Temple Extractors can remove traumatic memories from patients — a procedure the Temple classifies as "therapeutic Extraction." The patient's memory of a painful event is Extracted and stored. The patient no longer carries it. The Marsh Archive considers this "memory violence with therapeutic intent" and has protested the practice for centuries. The Temple continues.

**The Extraction Question:** Therapeutic Extraction is the most contested medical practice in Aurelia. Proponents argue it alleviates suffering — a patient who cannot remember their trauma is a patient who has been healed. Opponents argue it is not healing but erasure — the trauma is part of the person; removing it removes part of the self. The debate is unresolved. The Extraction continues.

### Tier 2: Ring Clinics (Third Ring)

Basic medical facilities serving the Third Ring's merchant and skilled-trade population. Staffed by Temple-trained but non-Temple-employed physicians. Capabilities: wound treatment, basic surgery, herbal medicine, limited pharmaceutical access. Referrals to the Crater Infirmary for complex cases — subject to availability and Classification.

### Tier 3: Factory Clinics (Fourth Ring)

Employer-run clinics attached to industrial facilities. Staffed by factory-trained medics, not Temple physicians. Capabilities: first aid, occupational injury treatment, basic illness management. Chronic conditions are referred to Ring Clinics — but appointments are scarce, and Fourth Ring patients are deprioritized.

### Tier 4: Home Care (Fifth Ring)

No formal medical facilities. Farmers treat themselves — herbal medicine, wound care, midwifery. The Fifth Ring's isolation is its vulnerability: a serious illness or injury requires a journey to the Third Ring that many cannot make.

### Type Restrictions

**Thren Patients:** May be treated at any tier. May be deprioritized if Citizen patients require the same resources. The deprioritization is not policy. It is practice.

**Vorn Patients:** Solari physicians are not trained in Vorn physiology. A Vorn requiring mechanical repair must travel to Arkos or rely on self-maintenance. The Temple has debated establishing a Vorn medical track. The debate has been ongoing for 40 years.

**Glim Patients:** Glims are maintained by their operators. Maintenance beyond operational necessity is at the operator's discretion. A Glim with joint degradation continues working until failure. A Glim with sensor degradation adapts until it cannot. Decommissioning is the standard remedy. "Repair exceeds operational value" is the official determination. The Glim is not consulted.

## ARKOS: THE REPAIR PARADIGM

Arkos medicine originated as equipment maintenance and evolved into comprehensive healthcare — but the engineering mindset remains.

### Vorn Medicine

Vorn "medicine" is indistinguishable from precision engineering. A Vorn physician is a maintenance engineer who specializes in living Vorns rather than industrial equipment. The distinction is semantic. The care is identical.

**Capabilities:**
- **Mechanical Repair:** Gear-joint replacement, plating repair, actuator calibration. Vorn bodies are modular — damaged components are replaced, not healed.
- **Core Diagnostics:** Memory core integrity assessment, attenuation monitoring, fragmentation detection. Core degradation (attenuation) is the most common Vorn terminal condition — analogous to aging in biological beings. There is no cure. There is management.
- **Face-Plate Reconstruction:** Cosmetic and functional repair of the face-plate — the Vorn's primary means of expression. Face-plate damage is considered a psychological as well as physical injury. "You damaged my face. You damaged my ability to be seen as myself."

**Attenuation (Core Degradation):**
Vorn memory cores degrade over centuries. The crystalline structure slowly loses coherence — memories fragment, emotional registers flatten, identity blurs. Cast-7 is dying of attenuation — their 160-year-old core is at 0.45 integrity. The condition is terminal but peaceful. "I am becoming less. Not suffering. Just... less. The memories are still there. I can't reach them. Maybe that's blessing."

### Human and Thren Medicine

Arkos' human and Thren medical system was adapted from Vorn engineering principles. The approach is systematic: diagnose the fault, replace or repair the affected system, verify function. Bedside manner is improving. It was never a priority.

**Capabilities:** Equivalent to Solara's Third Ring clinics for routine care, with superior trauma surgery (Vorn precision tools and imaging). Chronic disease management is less developed — Arkos' medical culture emphasizes repair over maintenance. "If it's broken, we fix it. If it's wearing out... we're working on that."

### Pharmaceutical Supply

Arkos imports Mirithane pharmaceuticals. The supply is consistent — Arkos and Mirithane have the strongest trade relationship in Aurelia. Arkos exports precision components; Mirithane exports medicine. The arrangement is mutually beneficial. It is also the reason Arkos' human and Thren population has the best pharmaceutical access outside Mirithane itself.

---

## MIRITHANE: THE MARSH PHARMACOPEIA

Mirithane has the most advanced medical system in Aurelia — built on the Archive's bacterial colonies, the marsh's natural pharmacopeia, and a philosophy of universal access.

### The Colony Pharmacy

The Marsh Archive's bacterial colonies are not just memory repositories. They are living pharmaceutical factories. Over centuries, Archivists have cultivated strains that produce:

- **Antibiotics:** Broad-spectrum compounds effective against bacterial infection. The most valuable medical export in Aurelia. Arkos and Solara depend on Mirithane antibiotics.
- **Anti-Inflammatories:** Colony-derived compounds that reduce inflammation without the side effects of herbal alternatives.
- **Pain Management:** Non-addictive analgesics cultured from spore-drifter derivatives. Less potent than Solari distillates but safer for long-term use.
- **Wound Sealants:** Bacterial cultures that can be applied directly to wounds, forming a protective barrier while delivering antimicrobial compounds.
- **Latency Therapeutics:** Therapeutic colonies that metabolize traumatic memories — the patient Writes the memory into the colony; the colony processes it; the patient is relieved of the memory's emotional charge without losing the memory itself. This is not Extraction. The memory remains. The suffering is metabolized.

### Universal Access

Medical care in Mirithane is free for all residents — funded by the Archive's pharmaceutical exports. No one is denied care. No one is deprioritized by type. The Water Temple provides spiritual care alongside medical care for those who want it. "The body heals. The spirit may need healing too. We offer both. You accept what you need."

### The Archive's Medical Ethics

The Marsh Archive has the most developed medical ethics framework in Aurelia. Key principles:

- **Consent:** No medical procedure — including therapeutic latency work — is performed without the patient's informed consent. This applies to all types. Glims cannot consent (legally). The Archive has never attempted to treat a Glim.
- **Memory Integrity:** Therapeutic Writing does not erase memory. The memory remains with the patient. The emotional charge is metabolized by the colony. The Archive considers this fundamentally different from Extraction. The Dawn Temple disagrees. The Archive continues.
- **End-of-Life Care:** The Archive provides comprehensive palliative care. Patients are not pressured to extend life beyond their wishes. The Hawk Platforms are available. The Archive supports the patient's choice.

## VALDRIS: MOUNTAIN MEDICINE

Valdris medicine combines high-altitude herbalism, geothermal therapy, and Anchoring-based psychological care. It is the most traditional medical system in Aurelia — and, in some areas, the most effective.

### Herbal Medicine

Valdris' high-altitude environment produces plants with concentrated medicinal compounds — a response to the harsh growing conditions. Valdris herbalists are respected practitioners who train through long apprenticeships. Their pharmacopeia includes:

- **Altitude Sickness Remedies:** Herbal compounds that ease the transition between shaft levels. Essential for newcomers.
- **Wound Care:** Moss-based poultices (including obsidian-moss, which carries mild latency residue and is believed to accelerate healing — the Shrine claims it works; the Archive is skeptical).
- **Respiratory Treatments:** Steam inhalations using geothermal mineral water and high-altitude herbs. Effective for the respiratory conditions common in shaft communities.
- **Pain Management:** Distilled geothermal spirits — potent, used sparingly, carefully controlled.

### Geothermal Therapy

The deep shafts' consistent warmth is used therapeutically. Geothermal chambers at specific depths maintain temperatures and humidity levels that aid recovery from respiratory conditions, joint inflammation, and chronic pain. Patients spend days or weeks in the chambers. "The mountain's heat heals. The mountain's darkness rests. We give the mountain our illness. The mountain gives us back ourselves."

### Anchoring Therapy

The Summit Shrine practices therapeutic Anchoring for psychological conditions. A patient who experiences recurrent intrusive memories — "memory sickness" in Shrine terminology — Works with an Anchor pair to Write the memories into obsidian. The memories are stored in the Shrine's archive. The patient is relieved of their recurrence.

This is functionally identical to therapeutic Extraction. The Shrine argues it is different: "Extraction removes memory. Anchoring stores it. The memory is not gone. The memory is in the mountain. The patient may retrieve it at any time." The Marsh Archive considers Anchoring therapy the least objectionable form of memory-intervention medicine. The Dawn Temple considers it Extraction by another name.

### Wurm Medicine

Wurm-tooth venom — the same compound that causes uncontrolled bleeding in wounds — has medicinal applications in micro-doses. Valdris physicians use diluted wurm venom as an anticoagulant for conditions requiring blood thinning. The preparation is dangerous — the margin between therapeutic and lethal is narrow. Wurm medicine is practiced only by the most experienced Valdris physicians.

## THE VERGE: SURVIVAL MEDICINE

The Verge has no medical system. It has people who know things and people who need help.

### Coin's Practice

Coin — a Memory Trader, Rung Four, twelve years without sleep — is The Verge's most reliable healer. She trades medical care for memories. Her practice includes:

- **Wound Care:** Cleaning, stitching, bandaging. The most common service. The Verge is sharp — scrap metal, broken glass, needle-swarms.
- **Infection Treatment:** Antibiotics imported from Mirithane, traded through the Network. Her supply is inconsistent. When she has antibiotics, she treats infection. When she doesn't, she treats symptoms and hopes.
- **Pain Management:** Herbal remedies, spore-drifter in low doses (for terminal patients only — "spore-drift doesn't heal. It makes dying less terrible"), and, in extremis, memory removal — she can Extract the memory of pain from a terminal patient's final days. She charges for this. The charge is not a memory. The charge is "promise me you won't ask me to do this for someone who isn't dying."
- **Midwifery:** The Verge's midwife is whoever is available. Coin has delivered more Verge children than any living resident. She collects a memory from the mother. She gives the child a gift: "I'll remember your birth. When you're old enough to ask, come find me. I'll give you the memory back."
- **Death:** When treatment fails, Coin stays. She collects a memory from the dying person — "Give me something. Anything. I'll carry it." She has carried hundreds of Verge dead in her memory. This is not medicine. It is witness.

### Folk Medicine

Verge residents without access to Coin rely on folk medicine — herbal remedies, wound care learned from experience, knowledge traded person-to-person. Infection is the leading cause of death in The Verge. Preventable infection. "We know how to treat it. We don't always have the means. The difference between knowing and having is where the dying happens."

### The Swarm and Survival

Needle-swarm envenomation is the most feared acute condition in The Verge. Full-body paralysis within 90 seconds of swarm exposure. Respiratory paralysis within 8–12 minutes. Death by suffocation while fully conscious. There is no antivenom available in The Verge. Treatment is supportive — maintain airway, wait for paralysis to pass (survivors recover fully after 4–6 hours). Most don't survive the wait.

## MENTAL HEALTH ACROSS AURELIA

### Solara: The Institutional Approach

Mental health in Solara is managed through the Temple's latency medicine program. Psychological distress is treated as a memory management problem — traumatic memories are Extracted, anxiety is Anchored (in Temple-controlled shards), grief is "processed" through ritualized mourning periods. The system works for those who accept the Temple's framework. It fails for those who don't.

The Temple does not recognize conditions that cannot be attributed to specific memories. Chronic depression with no identifiable cause. Generalized anxiety not linked to trauma. Conditions that arise from the Gradient itself — the slow erosion of personhood that Threns in PENDING experience, the invisible violence of being classified as equipment. These are not in the Temple's diagnostic framework.

### Arkos: The Vorn Quiet

Vorn mental health is poorly understood — even by Vorns. The Vorn psyche is resilient in ways humans struggle to comprehend (centuries of continuous memory without breakdown) and vulnerable in ways humans cannot experience (core attenuation, the emotional flatline that signals overwhelming distress).

Vorns who experience psychological distress "go quiet" — reducing ultrasonic broadcast, limiting social contact, entering voluntary isolation. The quiet is respected. It is also isolating. Vorn culture has no formal mental health support. "We carry our own. We always have."

### Mirithane: The Colony Method

The Archive's therapeutic colonies are the most widely used mental health treatment in Aurelia. The Archive also trains "listeners" — non-practitioner counselors who provide talk-based support. The combination of latency therapy (Writing into colonies) and conventional counseling is Mirithane's standard approach.

The Archive recognizes "Gradient-induced stress" as a clinical condition — the psychological toll of living under Solara's Classification system. Mirithane therapists who treat Thren refugees from Solara specialize in this.

### Valdris: The Partner System

Valdris mental health is built on the Anchor pair model. Every Anchor has a partner who monitors their mental state. Non-Anchors in shaft communities rely on community support — the mountain is harsh, and isolation kills. Mental health is maintained through connection, ritual, and the mountain's constant presence. "The mountain doesn't judge your mind. The mountain accepts it. We learn from the mountain."

### The Verge: Survival and Silence

Mental health in The Verge is a luxury. When survival is uncertain, psychological distress is deferred. The deferred distress accumulates. The Verge has no therapists, no counselors, no treatment programs. It has community, conversation, and the Trader's table — a meal shared with someone who asks questions no one else will ask. For many Verge residents, this is enough. For many, it isn't.

## DISEASE & PUBLIC HEALTH

### Infectious Disease

**Pre-Collapse Legacy:** Pre-Collapse medicine eliminated most infectious disease. The Collapse eliminated the elimination. Several pre-Collapse diseases have re-emerged, and post-Collapse conditions (marsh fungal infections, deep-shaft respiratory conditions, Verge wound contamination) are endemic.

**Major Threats:**
- **Marsh Lung (Mirithane):** A fungal respiratory infection endemic to the marsh. Treatable with Archive antibiotics. Fatal if untreated. Mirithane residents develop partial immunity through exposure. Outsiders are vulnerable.
- **Shaft Cough (Valdris):** A chronic respiratory condition caused by mineral dust inhalation in the deep shafts. Managed with herbal steam treatments. Progressive in miners who spend decades at depth.
- **Factory Fever (Arkos, Solara):** An industrial illness — low-grade chronic inflammation caused by exposure to Fabricator byproducts. Non-fatal but debilitating over time. Treated with Mirithane anti-inflammatories.
- **Verge Sickness (The Verge):** Not a specific disease. A category — the collection of waterborne, wound-borne, and exposure-related illnesses that result from The Verge's lack of sanitation infrastructure. The leading cause of death.

### Public Health

- **Solara:** Ring-based sanitation. First and Second Rings are clean. Fourth Ring is monitored. Fifth Ring is self-maintained. The Gradient determines not just where you live but how safely you live.
- **Arkos:** The closed-loop water system eliminates waterborne disease. Air quality in industrial zones is monitored. Vorns are unaffected by biological pathogens; the system is designed for Vorn safety and adapted for human needs.
- **Mirithane:** The Archive monitors public health through colony surveillance — bacterial strains that detect pathogens in water and air. The system is comprehensive and maintained by Archivists as a form of Archive service.
- **Valdris:** Natural sanitation — the deep shafts process organic waste, the mountain's water is pure. Public health is maintained by the mountain. The mountain does not fail. The mountain does not care. The mountain simply *is*.
- **The Verge:** No public health infrastructure. Water is unreliable. Sanitation is ad hoc. Disease is common.

## THE BODY ACROSS TYPES

### The Human Body (Baseline)

The calibrating standard. Human physiology is the reference against which all other types are understood — and the lens through which medicine was developed. When a human heals from injury, the healing is slower than it was pre-Collapse (nutrition is less consistent, medicine is less advanced), but the body's self-repair mechanisms are intact. Human lifespan post-Collapse: 70–90 years (down from 90–100 pre-Collapse).

### The Thren Body (Synthetic-Biological)

Thren bodies are lab-grown tissue over lightweight endoskeletons. They heal similarly to humans — tissue regeneration, immune response, scarring. But the healing is subtly different: faster for surface wounds, slower for deep tissue, and the synthetic neural tissue does not regenerate fully. Neural damage is cumulative. Elderly Threns (150+ years) often experience cognitive changes that resemble human dementia — but the mechanism is different, and Mirithane researchers are still studying it.

### The Vorn Body (Mechanical)

Vorn bodies do not heal. They are repaired. Components are replaced. The crystalline core does not regenerate — damage is permanent, though accumulated damage (attenuation) takes centuries to become significant. Vorn lifespan is indefinite but finite — all Vorns will eventually attenuate. The question is when.

### The Glim Body (Industrial)

Glim bodies are the most durable and the least valued. A Glim can operate for centuries with minimal maintenance. When a component fails, the Glim adapts. When the failure exceeds operational tolerance, the Glim is decommissioned. The body is recycled. The Glim is not.

The Unfinished — anomalous Glims — sometimes take extraordinary measures to maintain themselves. A Glim in The Verge's Commons has been operating for an estimated 400 years, with components salvaged from decommissioned Glims, maintained by the community that has no legal obligation to maintain it. "They're not equipment. They're people. People take care of people."
