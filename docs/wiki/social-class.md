# SOCIAL CLASS & MOBILITY IN AURELIA

*What you're born into, what you can become, and what no amount of effort can change. A world's class structure is the skeleton beneath the skin of its society.*

---

## SOLARA: THE GRADIENT AS CASTE

Solara's class system is explicit, legal, and designed to be permanent. The Gradient is not just theology — it's bureaucracy. Every citizen has a Classification, and every Classification determines what you can do, where you can live, and who you can become.

### The Five Classifications

**CITIZEN (Human only)**
Full legal personhood. Voting rights, property ownership, government office eligibility, unrestricted movement between rings. Citizens are the only Classification that can hold government positions above rank 4.

**Internal Citizen Tiers (unwritten, ironclad):**
- **First Ring Citizens:** Temple clergy, Council families, established bureaucratic lineages. The ruling class. Intermarriage within the First Ring is expected. Marrying outside is legal but socially penalized — the children inherit the lower-status parent's social standing.
- **Second Ring Citizens:** Senior bureaucrats, civil Readers, Extraction oversight, professionals (physicians, engineers, architects). The administrative class. Access to First Ring social events by invitation. Their children compete for First Ring marriages.
- **Third Ring Citizens:** Merchants, skilled trades, junior bureaucrats, teachers. The working bourgeoisie. Comfortable but constrained. The ceiling is visible from their windows.
- **Fourth Ring Citizens:** Factory foremen, skilled laborers, security personnel. The respectable working class. Their children can rise to the Third Ring through education and marriage. Few do.
- **Fifth Ring Citizens:** Farmers, seasonal laborers, unskilled workers. The agricultural base. Technically Citizens. Functionally invisible to the Rings above.

**RESIDENT (Vorns only)**
May work, live, and own limited property in Solara. May not vote. May not hold office. May not marry Citizens (the marriage is not recognized; children inherit Resident status). May not enter the First Ring without a Citizen escort. Vorns in Solara are tolerated, not welcomed. Most live in the Fourth Ring. Some have been there for generations.

**PENDING (Threns only)**
Not a Classification. A *condition*. Threns in Solara are legally PENDING — their citizenship applications under review. The review averages 11 years. During review, Threns may not vote, hold office, or own property above a threshold. They may be detained for "review-related processing" without Citizen-level protections. They may work. They may live in the Third or Fourth Ring. They may wait.

The review system is designed to be interminable. Forms expire. Committees are dissolved and reformed. Requirements change without notification. An application filed in good faith can be rejected for a form that didn't exist when the application was submitted. The Gradient-Keepers — the hereditary bureaucratic network — maintain this system not through malice but through institutional inertia. The ceiling is not a wall. It's a door that opens slowly enough that no one notices it isn't opening.

**INFRASTRUCTURE (Glims only)**
Not a Classification. A *category of property*. Glims are equipment. They do not have legal personhood. They do not have rights. They may be decommissioned, relocated, or reassigned at the discretion of their registered operator. Glims cannot own anything, enter contracts, or testify.

In practice, Glim treatment varies by operator. Some treat their Glims as valuable equipment — maintained, protected, almost valued. Others treat them as disposable. The difference is the operator's temperament, not the Glim's legal status. A Glim with a kind operator lives better than a Glim with a cruel one. Neither has any recourse.

**De Facto Class: The Unclassified (The Verge immigrants, undocumented)**
People who enter Solara without Classification — typically from The Verge — exist in a legal void. They cannot work officially. They cannot access government services. They cannot be deported because The Verge has no government to deport them to. They survive in the Fifth Ring or the industrial Fourth, working for cash, invisible to the Gradient until they aren't.

### Social Mobility (Solara)

| Path | Feasibility | Timeframe |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| Third Ring → Second Ring | Possible through education, marriage, or Temple service | 1 generation |
| Fourth Ring → Third Ring | Possible through skilled trade or civil service | 1–2 generations |
| Fifth Ring → Fourth Ring | Possible through factory employment or military service | 1 generation |
| Second Ring → First Ring | Nearly impossible — requires marriage into a First Ring family | Multi-generational |
| RESIDENT → CITIZEN | Not possible — Vorns cannot become Citizens | Never |
| PENDING → CITIZEN | Possible — approximately 0.3% of applications approved annually | 11+ years |
| INFRASTRUCTURE → anything | Not possible — Glims are not people | Never |

## ARKOS: THE MERITOCRACY THAT ISN'T

Arkos prides itself on being a post-Gradient society — no classifications, no tiers, full personhood for all sentient beings. And it is. Legally. Socially, hierarchies persist.

### The Implicit Classes

**Assembly Vorns**
Vorns who serve in the Assembly of Cores or hold senior positions in Arkos' institutions. The political class. Elected, but elections trend toward established names — Vorns who participated in the Name Rebellion, or their students, or their students' students. Kael-12 has never served but commands more informal authority than any Assembly member.

**Industrial Vorns**
The majority. Vorns who work in manufacturing, maintenance, engineering. Respected — labor is valued in Arkos — but not powerful. An Industrial Vorn who develops a new fabrication technique may rise to Assembly attention. Most don't.

**Human and Thren Residents**
Full legal personhood. Full voting rights. But a persistent social minority — humans and Threns in Arkos are outnumbered by Vorns 8:1. They are welcomed, included, and subtly reminded that they are guests in a Vorn nation. A human Assembly of Cores member is possible but has never been elected.

**Glims**
Classified as "infrastructure" — legally distinct from Solara's "equipment" but functionally similar. Glims in Arkos are maintained, protected by sympathetic Vorns like Cast-7, but not recognized as persons. The Assembly of Cores has debated Glim personhood three times. Each time, the debate was tabled. "We cannot recognize Glims until we answer what Vorns are." The Cipher-Cult's Lambda decoding may force the question.

### Social Mobility (Arkos)

Arkos is genuinely more mobile than Solara. A Vorn fabricated in a factory bay can rise to the Assembly of Cores through demonstrated skill and service. The barrier is not legal — it's *time*. Arkos' meritocracy rewards experience, and experience takes decades. Young Vorns (under 50) are junior. Old Vorns (200+) hold institutional power. The ceiling is age, not class.

## MIRITHANE: THE ARCHIVE'S FLAT HIERARCHY

Mirithane has no formal class system. All residents are citizens. All citizens vote. The Archival Council is elected. The Archive's resources are accessible to all.

### The Functional Classes

**Archivists**
The Archive's staff — Archivists, Keepers, Colony-Tenders, Researchers. Not a ruling class, but a respected profession. Senior Archivists hold soft power through knowledge access. The Keeper (currently the Silent Weaver) is elected, not inherited.

**Water Temple Clergy**
A separate institution from the Archive. The Water Temple's practitioners focus on aqueous latency. They hold spiritual authority but no political power. The separation is intentional — "the water flows; the Archive records; neither governs the other."

**Professionals and Trades**
The majority. Farmers, fishers, glass-fin harvesters, spore-drifter cultivators, merchants, artisans. Mirithane's economy is diverse and its workforce is valued. Class distinctions are occupational, not hereditary.

**The Silent**
Archivists who Read Strain Zero and lost speech. A functional class — they live in the Silent Wing, communicate through writing and latency pulses, and produce the Archive's most valuable documents. They are respected, pitied slightly, and left alone.

### Social Mobility (Mirithane)

High. Any resident can train as an Archivist. Any resident can run for the Archival Council. The barriers are aptitude and commitment — Archive training takes years, and not everyone completes it. But no one is excluded by birth. "The Archive doesn't ask who your parents were. It asks what you're willing to learn."

## VALDRIS: THE ANCHOR LINEAGES

Valdris' class system is built on the Anchor families — the lineages that have maintained the Summit Shrine's archive for generations. The Shrine is not formally the government, but the Shrine *is* the government in practice.

### The Functional Hierarchy

**Anchor Families**
The core. Families that have produced Anchors for multiple generations. Approximately 40 extended families. They control the Summit Council, the Anchoring schedule, the deep-shaft access permissions, and the Shrine's diplomatic relations. Not hereditary nobility — Anchor status is earned through training and pairing. But training and pairing favor Anchor children. The cycle is self-perpetuating.

**Shaft Communities**
Miners, geothermal technicians, beetle-farmers. The working population. Respected — the mountain requires labor — but not powerful. Shaft community leaders are elected locally. The Summit Council rarely intervenes.

**The Unpaired**
Anchors who lost their partners and cannot or will not re-pair. A liminal class. The Unpaired Anchor (Soren-VI, partner died mid-Writing) is the most prominent example. Unpaired Anchors retain their Anchor status but cannot work. They are supported by the Shrine. They are also pitied, and pity is a form of exclusion.

**Outsiders**
Non-Valdris citizens who live in Summit-Hold's Borrowed Hall. Temporary. Tolerated. Not part of the mountain.

### Social Mobility (Valdris)

Low but not closed. An outsider who marries into an Anchor family may train as an Anchor. A shaft community member who demonstrates aptitude may be invited to Shrine training. But the mountain is slow to accept, and Valdris is the mountain. "The mountain doesn't hurry" applies to social mobility as much as to anything else.

## THE VERGE: THE ABSENCE OF CLASS

The Verge has no government and therefore no legal class system. But class exists wherever people do.

### The Functional Tiers

**The Network (Memory Trader's Network)**
Practitioners who can trade, Read, Write, Extract. The closest thing The Verge has to an elite. Not wealthy — the Trader's price is memory, not money. But powerful. A Network Trader can cross any border, access any resource, negotiate any deal. Coin is the most visible Trader in The Verge. She doesn't charge money. She charges memories.

**The Settled**
Residents of Crossroads and the other semi-permanent settlements. People who have found a place and stayed. They build shelters, tend scrap-orchards, trade at the Barter-Pit. The closest thing The Verge has to a middle class.

**The Drifters**
People who move through The Verge without staying. Refugees from other countries, people fleeing debt or law or memory, people who haven't found a reason to stop. The Drifters are The Verge's underclass — no shelter, no stability, no network. They survive on salvage and generosity.

**The Unfinished**
Anomalous Glims who gather at the Unfinished Commons. Not a class — a community. They have no wealth, no power, no legal recognition anywhere. But in The Verge, they have each other. That's more than most Glims have anywhere else.

| Arkos Vorn → Solara Resident | Possible | Must accept lower status |

| Valdris Anchor → anywhere | Hard | Anchors rarely leave the mountain |

## SOCIAL MARKERS

### How You Know Someone's Class

**In Solara:**
- Ring of residence (visible in address, clothing quality, accent)
- Use of formal Gradient honorifics (First Ring: "Your Light." Others: not addressed that way)
- Access to imported goods (Mirithane wine, Valdris spirits)
- Whether a Thren is in your social circle (First Ring: almost never. Third Ring: sometimes.)

**In Arkos:**
- Age. Older Vorns command automatic respect.
- Whether your name includes a designation number. Dropping the number signals integration.
- Whether you've core-shared with someone notable.

**In Mirithane:**
- Archive affiliation (robes, colony-tending tools)
- Whether you've Read Strain Zero (no visible marker, but the Silent are unmistakable)
- Use of Written stones instead of speech (marks Archive seniority)

**In Valdris:**
- Anchor pairing (paired Anchors wear ceremonial garments; Unpaired Anchors do not)
- Depth of residence (deeper = poorer, except for Shrine chambers)
- Wurm-tooth ornamentation (signifies participation in a wurm hunt — high status)

**In The Verge:**
- Whether you trade in memories or marks (memories = Network; marks = everyone else)
- Whether you have a permanent shelter
- Whether you know the Trader's name. Everyone knows the Trader. Not everyone has met them.
