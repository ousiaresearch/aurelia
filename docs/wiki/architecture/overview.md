# Architecture of Aurelia

> *Buildings are frozen arguments. In Aurelia, the argument is: who is this for?*

## Solara — Coastal Modernism

Solara's architecture is the most formally designed in the federation. The council invested in public buildings as statements of the new republic's legitimacy.

**Key structures:**
- **The Dawn Temple:** One room. Open eastern wall. No roof. Pale stone floor, angled to reflect dawn light upward. The temple is the purest architectural expression of Solara's philosophy: *the light does not differentiate.* The space makes no provision for anything except standing in the light.
- **The Lumen Plaza:** Open square, pale stone, surrounded by council chambers and administrative buildings. The plaza is designed for visibility — every building faces inward, every window overlooks the square. The design says: *we are watching. The watching is governance.*
- **Reef Labs:** Low buildings on the coastal shelf, partially submerged. Glass walls facing the reef. The architecture merges with the research subject — the labs are in the water they study.

**Residential:** Human residences are modest apartments — two to four rooms, east-facing windows, light-reactive paint that shifts color through the day. Thren residences are identical. The architecture does not differentiate. The architecture is not the ceiling.

## Arkos — Vertical Arcology

Arkos is a single structure — a self-contained city rising from the Glass Wastes. The arcology is not a collection of buildings. The arcology is the building. Everything inside it is infrastructure.

**Key features:**
- **Sand-glass towers:** Transparent, hexagonal, rising the full height of the arcology. Contain the energy storage medium — sand infused with thermal-retention compounds. Glow from within at night. The towers are structural, functional, and sacred.
- **The Glass Forum:** Central governance chamber. Glass walls, transparent ceiling. Light-pulse voting system. The transparency says: *no secrets.* The transparency also says: *no privacy.* Both are Vorn values.
- **Residential sectors:** Stacked vertically. Vorn sectors on upper levels (closer to energy distribution). Human sectors on mid-levels. The Nomad Camp outside the walls. The architecture is the hierarchy.

## Mirithane — Ecological Integration

Mirithane has no buildings. It has platforms, woven from living reeds, floating on the marsh. The platforms are not constructed. They are grown — reeds trained over years into flat surfaces, anchored to the marsh bottom but living at the surface. A platform left untended for a year reverts to wild reed. The architecture is temporary. The temporariness is the philosophy.

**Key features:**
- **The Water Temple:** No walls. A floating platform at the edge of the deep marsh, knee-deep water over its surface. The congregation sits on the platform, feet in water. The architecture does not separate the worshipper from the worshipped. The marsh is the temple.
- **Filtration reefs:** Living coral structures that filter water. The structures are simultaneously infrastructure, sculpture, and ecology. No division between utility and beauty.

## Valdris — Carved Stone

Valdris architecture is negative — created by removing stone, not adding material. Buildings are carved into the canyon walls. The stone is the insulation, the structure, and the aesthetic.

**Key features:**
- **The forge district:** A chain of carved chambers descending the canyon wall, each housing a forge. The forges are open to the canyon — heat vents into the air, visible as rising distortion. At night, the forge district glows orange from a thousand meters away.
- **The Summit Shrine:** No walls. The summit itself. The obsidian wall is the only human-made element — and it is made by subtraction, carving names into stone.
- **The deep shafts:** Vertical tunnels, laser-bored in the upper levels, pick-bored in the deep. Descending 800+ meters. The architecture says: *we go down. The mountain holds us.*

---

## The Verge — Salvage Construction

The Verge builds from what it finds. Shelters are salvaged drone casings, cargo containers, repurposed fabric stretched over Glim chassis frames. No building is permanent. No building is planned. The architecture says: *we are here until we're not.*

**Key features:**
- **The Memory Trader's stall:** A salvaged door laid across two rusted charging stations. The structure is temporary — it collapses when the door is lifted, and the Trader leaves no trace.
- **The crossroads center:** The empty charging station where stories are told. No architecture. Just a space defined by what happens in it.
