# Architectural Materials — Aurelia

> *What they build with is what survived the Collapse.*

## Solara — Coastal Stone & Glass

- **Pale coastal limestone:** Quarried from the coastal shelf. Soft enough to carve, hard enough to last. Every council building is limestone. The Dawn Temple's floor is a single limestone slab, quarried from the same shelf as the temple's foundation — the building and the ground are the same stone.
- **Reef-glass:** Fused from coastal sand, translucent green-blue. Used for windows in the Reef Labs. The glass is imperfect — bubbles, striations, wave-like distortions. The imperfections are not flaws. The imperfections are the reef.

## Arkos — Arcology-Grade Alloy

A proprietary Vorn-developed metal alloy used for every structural element in the arcology. Composed of desert iron, recycled pre-Collapse steel, and sand-glass binding compounds. The alloy is near-indestructible by Aurelian standards — rated for 200 km/h sandstorms and centuries of thermal cycling. No other country can produce it. The formula is archived in the Glass Forum's restricted databanks. "We share everything except the alloy," Echo told the Forum records. "The alloy is not information. The alloy is the arcology."

## Mirithane — Living Materials

Mirithane builds with reeds, water, and time. Reed platforms are woven from living marsh reeds — the reeds continue to grow after weaving, strengthening the structure. A platform left untended for a year reverts to wild reed. The impermanence is the architecture. The Water Temple's submerged foundations are the only permanent structures in Mirithane — pre-Collapse filtration system elements, coral-encrusted, ancient. The temple was built around them. "We didn't choose the foundations," the senior keeper said. "They were here. We built a temple to acknowledge them."

## Valdris — The Mountain's Stone

Every Valdris building is carved from the mountain itself. No imported materials. No additive construction. The mountain provides: basalt (for foundations), obsidian (for the wall), granite (for structural elements), and the canyon walls themselves (for housing). The forges are natural cave systems expanded over centuries. The deep shafts are pre-Collapse mines extended by Valdris labor. Every tool that shapes the stone is forged from mountain ore. "The mountain builds itself," the forge guild's motto states. "We just help."

## The Verge — Salvage

Everything in The Verge is built from something that was something else. A Glim chassis is a shelter. A charging station is a table. A cargo container is a home. No material is original. No structure is permanent. The Memory Trader's stall — a door on two charging stations — is the architectural philosophy of The Verge in miniature: temporary, repurposed, erased at dawn.
