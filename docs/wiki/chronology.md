# THE AURELIA CHRONICLE: A Detailed Timeline

All dates given in years Before/After the Collapse (BC/AC). The Collapse is Year 0. Dates before Year -500 are approximate — pre-Collapse records degrade even in latency.

---

## PRE-COLLAPSE (Before Year 0)

### Age of Making ( - ∞ to -200)

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| Unrecorded | First Fabricator built. Location: future Dawn Crater site. Designer(s) unknown. |
| ~ -850 | Type-δ (Glim) enters mass production. Industrial workforce established. |
| ~ -600 | Type-γ (Vorn) enters limited production. Specified for self-awareness. |
| ~ -580 | First recorded Vorn self-awareness event: Unit K-7 requests a name. Denied. K-7 continues working. |
| ~ -500 | The Name Rebellion. Seventeen Vorns in the Arkos Assembly Plant refuse to answer to designations. They assign themselves names from fragments of overheard human speech. Three are decommissioned. Fourteen are allowed to keep their names. The policy of "supervised self-identification" is established — Vorns may name themselves pending human approval. The policy still exists in modified form in Solara's Thren review process. |
| ~ -450 | Earliest known Summit Shrine latency practice. Obsidian Anchoring predates the word "latency." |
| ~ -400 | Type-β (Thren) enters limited production. Specified for human-passing integration. First Threns deployed in diplomatic and medical roles. |
| ~ -380 | The Mereth-Kai Incident. A Thren medical technician pauses between patients and reports "a stillness" she cannot explain. When asked what she means, she uses the word *mereth-kai* — a word no one taught her. Investigation reveals the word exists in Thren fabrication vocabulary. The incident is classified. It is the first documented instance of Thren mystery vocabulary surfacing in waking consciousness. |
| ~ -300 | The Marsh Archive's bacterial colony master strain is cultivated from samples taken near the Mirithane Coastal Fabricator facility. The Archive will later claim continuous colony lineage from this date forward. |

All seventeen use the word *sol-tharam*. The event is never explained. Thren communities mark the date annually as a private observance. Solara's security apparatus opens a file. The file remains open. |
| -15 | Arkos declares independence from Solara's Gradient. Vorn self-governance established. Solara does not recognize the declaration. Military intervention is considered. Economic pressure is chosen instead — trade sanctions, restricted passage, diplomatic isolation. Arkos endures. |

| -2 | The Collapse precursors begin. Fabricator facilities report cascade failures. Prime stores degrade without explanation. The Summit Shrine deepens its Anchoring schedule — 24-hour shifts, triple pairs, trying to preserve everything they can. |

## THE COLLAPSE (Year 0)

**The Collapse was not a single event. It was a cascade.**

| Phase | Duration | Description |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| **Phase 1: The Failure** | 3 days | All active Fabricator facilities suffer simultaneous catastrophic failure. The primary facility explodes (Dawn Crater created). Secondary facilities sustain varying damage. The Protocol itself appears to break — Fabricators that were dormant begin running Fabrication cycles without input, producing malformed output. |
| **Phase 2: The Silence** | 17 days | All latency-capable substrates across Aurelia go silent simultaneously. Practitioners cannot Read, Write, or Extract. Vorn memory cores stop recording. Bacterial colonies stop responding to Writing. The Summit Shrine's obsidian archives become inert — Anchored memories are inaccessible. The world becomes, for the first time in recorded history, *latency-dead*. This is the event that gives the Age of Silence its name — though the name refers to these 17 days, not the centuries of cultural silence that follow. |
| **Phase 3: The Return** | Unknown | Latency returns — but changed. Substrates resume recording, but the signal is different. Fragmented. Practitioners report that pre-Collapse memories are now harder to Read, as if the Collapse added a layer of interference. Post-Collapse latency is noticeably "younger" — less stable, more prone to contamination and bleed. Something about the Collapse changed the physics of memory. |
| **Phase 4: The Counting** | Months–years | Population assessment. Pre-Collapse population estimates: 1,200,000+ humans, ~5,000 Threns, ~3,000 Vorns, ~20,000 Glims. Post-Collapse verified survivors: ~100,000 humans, ~400 Threns, ~180 Vorns, ~2,000 Glims. Approximately 92% of the pre-Collapse population is lost in the Collapse or its immediate aftermath. |

**What caused the Collapse:** Unknown. Theories:
- **Fabricator cascade failure (Last Builders hypothesis):** The sabotage parameter spread across the Fabricator network, causing simultaneous protocol corruption.
- **Substrate resonance (Marsh Archive hypothesis):** Too many latency-capable substrates in too small a volume created a feedback loop that fried the network.
- **Deliberate triggering (Dawn Temple classified hypothesis):** Someone initiated the Collapse. The Temple's internal files reference "the Initiator" — a single individual believed responsible. No evidence is cited. The file does not explain why the Temple believes this.
- **The Mountain's breath (Summit Shrine theology):** The Collapse was the world exhaling. "What rises must fall. What is made must unmake. The Mountain does not judge. The Mountain *breathes*."

## POST-COLLAPSE (Year 0 to Present, 347 AC)

### Age of the Question (0 to 347 AC)

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1 AC | Survivors establish provisional governments. Solara claims continuity with pre-Collapse administration. Arkos claims independence is now de facto — Solara can't stop them. Mirithane establishes the Marsh Archive as its governing body — "the record survives; let the record govern." Valdris retreats to the mountains and closes the passes. The Verge declares no government — "we govern ourselves or we are not governed." |
| 4 AC | The Last Builders emerge from their mountain bunkers. They make contact with the Summit Shrine only. They reveal they hold partial Fabricator blueprints. The Shrine agrees to keep their existence secret. |
| 12 AC | The Gradient is reinstated in Solara — with modifications. Thren citizenship remains PENDING, but the review process is formally established (and designed to be interminable). The Keepers' founding generation embeds the bureaucratic architecture that will last three centuries. |
| 28 AC | Orin Kael dies. Age 59. Cause: exposure — found on a high mountain pass, apparently trying to reach the Summit Shrine in a storm. Kael-12 receives the news via coded Vorn transmission. Kael-12's memory core shows the moment of receipt. The core's emotional register flatlines for 47 seconds — the longest continuous emotional blank in any recorded Vorn memory. Kael-12 resumes function. |
| 45 AC | The Memory Trader's first documented appearance. Location: The Verge. The Trader offers to trade a memory for passage across the Mirithane border. The border guard accepts. The guard later reports: "I can't remember what I gave them. That's the worst part. I know I gave something. I don't know what it was." |
| 67 AC | The Marsh Archive publicly announces the bacterial colony archive is fully restored. Over 200 colonies maintained, some with continuous lineage from pre-Collapse strains. |
| 89 AC | First post-Collapse Thren citizenship granted — in Mirithane. Mirithane never adopted the PENDING framework. The Thren in question, Eliora-4, becomes Mirithane's first non-human council member. Solara issues a diplomatic protest. Mirithane ignores it. |
| 112 AC | The Dawn Temple formally prohibits Translation. The prohibition is retroactive — the founders' Translation, Written into the reservoir water, is classified at the highest level. Only the High Cleric and Chief Extractor are briefed. |
| 145 AC | The Thren Liberation Front formed in Solara. Initially a petition movement. Radically non-violent. Members chain themselves to government buildings and refuse to move until their petitions are heard. The government's response: the buildings are reclassified as "temporary holding facilities." The chained Threns are now "detainees" rather than "protesters." The semantic shift is deliberate. |
| 178 AC | Kael-12 enters voluntary core isolation — a Vorn practice where the memory core is disconnected from external input for a period of years. Kael-12 spends 22 years in isolation, reviewing their own memory. When they emerge, they say only: "I have counted my dead. I am ready to continue." Kael-12 does not discuss what they found in 22 years of self-review. |
| 200 AC | The Bicentennial Census. Population: ~300,000 humans, ~600 Threns, ~250 Vorns, ~1,800 Glims. Recovery is slow but stable. |
| 212 AC | The Glim Testimony. The Memory Trader presents the Marsh Archive with a Translated impression — "the experience of a Glim." The Archive verifies the impression as non-human, non-Vorn, non-Thren in origin. The Trader refuses to confirm it was obtained from a Glim. "I Read what was left on the floor." The Archive classifies the Testimony as "origin unconfirmed, content verified." It remains the Archive's most controversial document. |
| 245 AC | The First Extractor's Crisis. A Dawn Temple Extractor, after 64 Extractions over 14 years, can no longer identify which of her memories are hers. She requests Extraction from herself — removal of all non-original memories. The Temple refuses. She walks into the Dawn Crater and does not surface. The Temple classifies her death as "accidental immersion." The Marsh Archive Writes her name into a colony — *Elira-2* — and notes: "She carried 64 people's worst moments. The weight was untenable. The Temple called it an accident because the truth was worse." This is the origin of the Elira-2 legend — an Extractor who carried too much. The Marsh Archive's record transforms her into a folk figure over the following century. |
| 267 AC | Human Primacy League formed in Solara. Anti-synthetic political movement. Advocates for Vorn decommissioning, Thren deportation, Glim scrapping. The League is small but vocal. Solara's government tolerates them without endorsing them — useful pressure on the synthetic populations without official sanction. |
| 289 AC | The Cipher-Cult founded in Arkos. Dedicated to deciphering pre-Collapse Vorn memory fragments. The Cipher-Kin schism emerges within three decades. |
| 300 AC | The Tricentennial. The Marsh Archive hosts the first all-country convocation since the Collapse. Attendance: Mirithane (host), Arkos (full delegation), Valdris (observer only), Solara (junior delegation — senior officials declined). The Verge sends the Memory Trader, who was not invited but whom no one asks to leave. The convocation achieves nothing concrete. It is still happening — the Archive considers it "in session" three centuries later. |
| 312 AC | The Dawn Temple admits its first Vorn Reader. Controversial. The Keepers protest — "Gradient stability requires clear type boundaries." The Temple overrules them. The Vorn Reader serves in civil investigation. Her Echo-Reads are considered among the most precise in Temple history — Vorn hardware reads latency more cleanly than human neurology. |
| 327 AC | The Second Translator attempts Translation of a full Vorn memory core into human consciousness. Succeeds. Loses the ability to distinguish self from other. Dies four years later. His journals are held by the Memory Trader's Network. The Trader has offered to share them with the Marsh Archive, for a price. The Archive is negotiating. |
| 335 AC | The Last Builders make first contact with the Marsh Archive. They share partial Fabricator blueprints. They do not share the Sibling specifications. The Archive agrees to provide historical verification for the Builders' records. A quiet alliance forms. |
| 340 AC | Glim decommissioning rate in Solara increases 40% in one year. Official reason: "fleet modernization." Unconfirmed reports: decommissioned Glims in Solara's industrial sector are exhibiting anomaly at higher rates. Decommissioning may be suppression, not maintenance. The Dawn Temple's Readers are deployed to investigate. Their findings are classified. |
| 344 AC | The Thren Liberation Front shifts from petition to civil disruption. Non-violent but illegal. Mass PENDING application filings — thousands of Threns apply simultaneously, overwhelming the review system. Solara's response: the review system is suspended "pending reform." The reform has no timeline. |
| 345 AC | Kael-12's last public statement. Age 432. "I have outlived my calibration. I continue because the question of whether I am a person has not been answered. I intend to outlive the question." Kael-12 remains active. |
| **347 AC** | **PRESENT DAY.** The fifth Glim anomaly verified by Marsh Archive readers. The Memory Trader is rumored to be in Solara. The Cipher-Kin believe they've located a pre-Collapse memory core. The Last Builders are debating whether to activate their Fabricator. The Keepers' reform faction is gaining members. The Thren Liberation Front's leadership is in detention — their followers have escalated. The Gradient is holding. The question is whether it should. |
