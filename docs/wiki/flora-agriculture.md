# FLORA & AGRICULTURE OF AURELIA

*What grows where, what people eat, and how the Collapse changed the living world. A world's stomach is its foundation.*

---

## POST-COLLAPSE AGRICULTURAL ZONES

The Collapse did not just break machines. It broke growing seasons. Latency — the residue consciousness leaves on substrates — soaked into soil, water, and root systems. Plants that grew near Fabricator sites or dense population centers absorbed ambient memory. Most died. Some adapted. A few *changed*.

Five agricultural zones emerged from the Collapse's aftermath, each shaped by what survived and what the soil would accept.

---

### Zone 1: The Solar Plains (Solara)

**Climate:** Temperate, reliable rainfall, long growing season (8 months). The least Collapse-scarred arable land in Aurelia.
**Soil:** Pre-Collapse engineered loam — a cultivar designed for yield and stability. The soil still carries the engineering. It resists drought. It resists blight. It also resists *change* — crops that evolved post-Collapse won't take in Solar soil. The soil only accepts pre-Collapse cultivars.
**Primary Crops:** Solar grain (high-yield cereal, exports to Arkos), fiber-flax (textiles, rope, paper), root vegetables (tubers, alliums), orchard fruits (apple, pear, stone fruit).

**Solar Grain** (*Triticum solarae*)
The backbone of Solara's food supply and primary export. A pre-Collapse cultivar engineered for caloric density and shelf-stability. The grain is pale gold, nearly white, and mills into a flour that keeps for years without spoilage. Solar bread is the staple food of Solara's lower rings — affordable, filling, flavorless. The upper rings eat imported Mirithane grain products and pretend they prefer the taste. They prefer the status.

**The Soil Refusal:**
Solar soil will not accept post-Collapse cultivars. Farmers who attempt to introduce Arkos-hardy grains or Mirithane marsh-rice watch the seedlings wither within days. The Marsh Archive's theory: the pre-Collapse engineering included a competitive exclusion protocol — the soil actively suppresses non-engineered cultivars. The engineering was meant to prevent weed intrusion. It works too well. Solar agriculture is trapped in pre-Collapse genetics. If a blight ever evolves to target Solar grain, Solara starves.

### Zone 2: The Assembly Fringe (Arkos)

**Climate:** Arid to semi-arid, low rainfall, hot days and cold nights. The Assembly Plant's residual heat output creates a microclimate — the land within 50 km of the plant is 3–5°C warmer than surrounding terrain.
**Soil:** Thin, mineral-heavy, contaminated with trace Fabricator byproducts. Conventional agriculture is impossible on most Arkos land. Vorn engineers developed alternatives.
**Primary Crops:** Metal-fixed legumes (nitrogen-fixing plants that extract minerals from contaminated soil), tuber-variants that grow in gravel beds, fungal protein cultures grown in the Assembly Plant's waste-heat chambers.

**Assembly Protein:**
Arkos' primary food source is not a crop. It is a fungal culture — a pre-Collapse strain designed for Fabricator waste recycling, repurposed after the Collapse as a food source. The culture grows in dark, warm chambers heated by the Assembly Plant's residual thermal output. It produces a dense, protein-rich biomass that can be textured, flavored, and pressed into various forms. Vorns don't eat — they don't need to. The fungal culture feeds Arkos' human and Thren minority. It is efficient. It is sustainable. It tastes of nothing. Arkos cuisine is built around making nothing taste like something — spices, marinades, fermentation techniques that transform Assembly Protein into food people look forward to.

**Vorn Orchards:**
A recent development (last 50 years) — Vorn agriculturalists have begun cultivating fruit trees in shielded gravel beds on the Assembly Fringe. The trees are protected from mineral contamination by layered substrate barriers. The orchards are small, experimental, and deeply meaningful to the Vorns who tend them. "We were built. These grow. We are learning from them."

### Zone 3: The Marsh Basin (Mirithane)

**Climate:** Humid subtropical, abundant rainfall, seasonal flooding. The most fertile agricultural zone in Aurelia — and the most unpredictable.
**Soil:** Alluvial marsh soil, rich in organic matter, continuously replenished by seasonal floods. The soil is alive — the bacterial colonies that the Marsh Archive cultivates for latency work are native to this soil. Every handful of Mirithane topsoil contains bacterial strains that remember centuries of flood cycles.
**Primary Crops:** Marsh-rice (four varieties, adapted to different flood depths), spore-drifter (cultivated, not just wild-harvested), medicinal herbs, fiber-reeds, glass-fin aquaculture.

**Marsh-Rice Varieties:**
- **Deep-Rice:** Planted in flood zones. Stalks grow 3m+ to stay above water. Harvested from boats. Nutty, chewy.
- **Shallow-Rice:** Planted in drained paddies. The staple grain. Mild, versatile.
- **Black-Rice:** A pre-Collapse cultivar that absorbed latency during the Silence. The grains are black, faintly iridescent, and carry a trace memory of the 17 days the soil went silent. Eating black-rice produces mild synesthetic effects in latency-sensitive individuals — colors become sounds, sounds become textures. The Archive cultivates black-rice for ritual use. It is not sold. It is given.
- **Float-Rice:** Grown on floating platforms in deep marsh. The rice roots dangle in open water, feeding on dissolved nutrients. Tastes of the water it grew in — each harvest carries the flavor of that season's flood.

**Spore-Drifter Cultivation:**
Spore-drifters — the floating fungal colonies that produce latency-amplifying spores — were once only wild-harvested. In the last century, Mirithane agriculturalists developed cultivation techniques: floating frames seeded with drifter mycelium, anchored in slow-moving marsh channels. Cultivated drifters produce more consistent spore concentrations than wild specimens. The Archive uses cultivated spores for research. The recreational market uses them for experience. The distinction is blurring.

### Zone 4: The Mountain Terraces (Valdris)

**Climate:** Alpine, short growing season (4–5 months), extreme temperature variation. Agriculture is vertical — terraces carved into mountainsides at different elevations for different crops.
**Soil:** Thin, rocky, mineral-rich. Volcanic in origin — Valdris sits on dormant geothermal systems. The soil holds heat longer than surrounding terrain, extending the growing season by 2–3 weeks in terrace pockets.
**Primary Crops:** Cold-hardy grains (mountain barley, black oats), terrace vegetables (brassicas, alliums, root crops), high-altitude herbs, geothermal-greenhouse fruits.

**Mountain Barley:**
Valdris' staple. A short-season grain that matures in 90 days, produces in cool temperatures, and stores for years. Mountain barley is dense, chewy, and slightly sweet — the cold concentrates sugars in the grain. Valdris bread is heavy, dark, and sustaining. Summit Anchors carry barley cakes into the deep shafts — a single cake sustains an Anchor through a full Anchoring session.

**Geothermal Greenhouses:**
Valdris' deep shafts produce geothermal heat — the same heat that attracts deep-shaft wurms. Anchor families have built greenhouse chambers at strategic depths where the ambient temperature stays at 18–22°C year-round. These chambers produce fresh vegetables and fruits that cannot grow on the surface — tomatoes, peppers, soft herbs. Geothermal produce is a luxury. It is also a form of devotion — the greenhouses are tended by Anchors who Write their tending logs into the greenhouse walls. The walls remember every harvest.

### Zone 5: The Scavenged Land (The Verge)

**Climate:** Variable — The Verge spans multiple microclimates. The unifying feature is *ruin*. The soil is mixed with pre-Collapse debris: concrete dust, metal shavings, trace Fabricator residue. Nothing grows predictably.
**Soil:** Toxic in places, fertile in others, unpredictable everywhere. Verge farmers are scavengers first, agriculturalists second. They plant where the soil allows and move when it doesn't.
**Primary Crops:** Salvage-greens (edible weeds that colonized ruins), scrap-orchard fruits (trees growing through collapsed buildings), fungus cultivated in pre-Collapse basements, anything that will grow without asking permission.

**Salvage-Greens:**
Not a crop. A category. The edible weeds that colonized The Verge's ruins in the centuries after the Collapse — dandelion variants with broader leaves, purslane that accumulates minerals from debris, lamb's-quarter that grows taller than a person in the right rubble. Verge cuisine is built on salvage-greens. Every meal is a negotiation with what the land will give up.

**Scrap-Orchards:**
Pre-Collapse fruit trees that survived the Collapse and have been growing untended for three centuries. The orchards are tangled, wild, and produce fruit unpredictably — a tree that fruited last year may rest for three years before fruiting again. Verge residents tend the trees without owning them. "The orchard belongs to whoever's hungry when it fruits."

## UNIQUE FLORA

### Latency-Bloom
**Range:** Mirithane wetlands, Valdris deep shaft entrances, The Verge ruins
**Description:** A flowering plant that blooms only in the presence of strong latency fields. The flowers are pale blue-white, faintly bioluminescent, and open for approximately one hour when a latency-active substrate is within 10 meters. Latency practitioners use latency-bloom as a natural detector — if the flowers are open, something nearby remembers.
**Cultural significance:** Marsh Archivists plant latency-bloom around colony halls. The blooms serve as a silent alarm — if all the flowers open at once, a colony is dreaming.

### Crystalline Lichen
**Range:** Arkos industrial zones, pre-Collapse Fabricator sites
**Description:** A lichen that incorporates crystalline minerals into its structure — the same rare-earth minerals found in Fabricator cores. The lichen grows on metal surfaces, slowly consuming the metal and replacing it with crystalline tissue. In Arkos, crystalline lichen is considered a pest — it damages industrial equipment. In The Verge, it is harvested and ground into a powder used as a crude latency amplifier. The Marsh Archive has requested samples. The Verge has provided them — for a price.
**Research note:** Crystalline lichen's mineral composition is nearly identical to low-grade Fabricator substrate. The Last Builders have theorized that the lichen is a post-Collapse evolution — a lifeform that learned to grow crystalline structures by absorbing ambient Fabricator residue. If true, the lichen is a roadmap to understanding Prime.

### Memory-Reed
**Range:** Mirithane marsh shallows, exclusively
**Description:** A reed species whose cellular structure retains latency — each reed segment carries the echo of whatever happened near it during its growing season. Marsh Archivists harvest memory-reed for use in bacterial colony frames — the reed's native latency provides a stable substrate for colony attachment. Old memory-reed beds carry centuries of marsh memories. Archivists who walk through old reed beds report "hearing the marsh think" — fragmentary impressions of every flood, every harvest, every death on the water.
**Harvest restriction:** Memory-reed may only be harvested by licensed Archivists. Unauthorized harvesting is punished by mandatory Archive service — the offender must Write their memory of the harvesting into a colony, and the colony keeps it.

### Obsidian-Moss
**Range:** Valdris deep shafts, below 2 km depth
**Description:** A moss species that grows on obsidian deposits in the deep shafts. The moss is black, light-absorbent, and warm to the touch — it metabolizes geothermal heat rather than sunlight. Summit Anchors consider obsidian-moss auspicious. "The mountain feeds the moss. The moss feeds nothing. It exists because the mountain wants it to." The moss is harvested for ceremonial use — Anchor pairs wear obsidian-moss woven into their ritual garments during deep Anchoring sessions.
**Research note:** Obsidian-moss carries trace latency signatures from the obsidian it grows on. The moss on the oldest obsidian deposits carries pre-Collapse memories. The Shrine has not authorized harvesting of old-growth moss. "Some memories belong to the mountain."

### Scrap-Vine
**Range:** The Verge, universal in ruins
**Description:** An aggressive climbing vine that colonizes pre-Collapse structures. Grows rapidly — can cover a building facade in a single season. Produces small, bitter fruits in autumn. The fruits are edible but unpleasant. Verge residents eat them anyway. "Tastes like the old world. Bitter. You get used to it."

## LIVESTOCK & ANIMAL HUSBANDRY

### Wool-Beasts (Solara, Valdris)
Large, docile herbivores descended from pre-Collapse livestock. Bred for wool, milk, and meat. Wool-beast wool is Solara's primary textile fiber. The beasts are unremarkable — which is remarkable. They survived the Collapse unchanged. The Marsh Archive has studied wool-beast genetics and found no post-Collapse adaptation. "They didn't need to change. The world changed around them. They just... continued."

### Glass-Fin (Mirithane aquaculture)
The only domesticated aquatic species in Aurelia. Glass-fin are farmed in floating pens in Mirithane's coastal waters and marsh channels. Harvested for meat, fin material (surgical blades), and scales (decorative, used in Water Temple ritual garments). Glass-fin farming is a skilled trade — the fish are aggressive, the fins are sharp, and a careless farmer loses fingers.

### Cave-Beetles (Valdris)
A domesticated insect species that thrives in the deep shafts. Beetles are cultivated for protein — ground into a flour that supplements mountain barley in the deepest shaft communities. Beetle flour is dense, slightly nutty, and deeply unappetizing to anyone who didn't grow up eating it. Valdris children are raised on beetle-flour porridge. They don't know it's unusual. No one tells them.

### Spore-Feeders (Mirithane)
Small, rabbit-like herbivores that feed exclusively on spore-drifter mycelium. Their meat carries trace psychoactive compounds — eating spore-feeder produces mild latency amplification for 2–4 hours. The Marsh Archive regulates spore-feeder consumption. "Food should nourish. Food should not alter perception. If food alters perception, it should be labeled and dosed." The Verge does not label or dose.
