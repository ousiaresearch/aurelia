# TECHNOLOGY & INFRASTRUCTURE OF AURELIA

*The machines, systems, and built environment that sustain daily life across five countries. Technology is not just the Fabricator — it's the water pumps, the transit networks, the communication systems, the power grid. A world's technology is what keeps its people alive between the grand events.*

---

## POWER GENERATION

### Solar Thermal (Solara)
Solara's primary power source. Vast mirror arrays in the agricultural Fifth Ring focus sunlight onto thermal collectors — molten salt reservoirs that retain heat through the night. The system is pre-Collapse engineering, maintained continuously since before the Breaking. The mirrors are cleaned by Glims. The salt reservoirs are monitored by human technicians. The system has never failed for more than 48 hours.

**Output:** Reliable, consistent. Powers Solara Prime's Rings 1–4. The Fifth Ring is partially served — agricultural communities often supplement with wind and muscle power.

**Vulnerability:** The mirror arrays require continuous cleaning — dust accumulation reduces efficiency. The Glim workforce that maintains them is aging. Solara's decommissioning program reduces the Glim population faster than new Glims are fabricated (fabrication requires Prime, which Solara claims is exhausted). Within a generation, Solara faces a maintenance labor crisis.

### Geothermal (Valdris)
Valdris' power comes from the mountain. Deep shafts tap into geothermal reservoirs — steam drives turbines that power Summit-Hold, the Shrine, and the shaft communities. The system is elegant, reliable, and as old as the mountain itself. The Summit Shrine considers geothermal power a gift from the mountain. "The mountain gives heat. We give memory. The exchange is fair."

**Output:** Consistent. Sufficient for Valdris' entire population. Surplus is minimal — the mountain provides exactly what Valdris needs.

**Deep Sound Correlation:** The geothermal turbines produce a low-frequency hum that Vorn sensors detect as distinct from the Deep Sound. The Deep Sound's 4.7-second pulse is *not* geothermal. It originates from deeper than any geothermal tap. The Shrine's engineers have confirmed this. What produces the Deep Sound remains unknown.

### Residual Thermal (Arkos)
The Arkos Assembly Plant — a partially intact pre-Collapse Fabricator facility — produces residual heat from its still-active subsystems. The heat is captured and distributed through a network of thermal conduits. The system is inefficient but abundant — the Assembly Plant produces more heat than Arkos can use. The surplus heats the fungal protein chambers that produce Arkos' food supply.

**Output:** Abundant but geographically concentrated. Arkos Central and Forge-East are fully powered. Outer settlements rely on smaller thermal capture systems or imported power.

**Vulnerability:** The Assembly Plant's subsystems are pre-Collapse and partially understood. If the residual heat output declines, Arkos has no alternative power source at scale.

### Bacterial Decomposition (Mirithane)
Mirithane's marshes produce vast quantities of organic matter. The Marsh Archive developed bacterial decomposition chambers that capture methane from decaying plant material and convert it to usable energy. The system is distributed — every settlement has its own decomposition chambers, scaled to local needs.

**Output:** Sufficient for Mirithane's population. Not exportable. The system is maintenance-intensive — the bacterial colonies require constant tending, which Archivists provide as a form of Archive service.

**Philosophy:** "The marsh decays. We capture the decay. The decay becomes power. Nothing is wasted. Nothing ever was."

### Salvage & Barter (The Verge)
The Verge has no power grid. Power is generated individually — salvaged solar panels, wind turbines built from scrap, muscle power. Some Verge residents tap into pre-Collapse infrastructure that still functions intermittently. The pre-Collapse grid occasionally surges — lights flicker on in abandoned buildings, old machines start, and Verge residents know to harvest what they can before the surge passes.

## WATER & SANITATION

### Solara: The Reservoir System
Solara Prime's water comes from the Dawn Crater lake — the 2 km depression filled with Fabricator coolant and groundwater. The water is filtered through gravel beds and distributed through stone aqueducts to all five Rings. The First Ring receives water directly from the Crater. Subsequent Rings receive water that has been filtered through the Temple's purification system.

The Crater water is Written with the Temple's founding memory — a Translation performed by the founders, now degrading. Every glass of water in Solara carries a trace of the Temple's original sin. The clergy know. The population doesn't.

**Sanitation:** Waste flows to treatment ponds in the Fifth Ring. The ponds are managed by Glim labor. The treated water irrigates agricultural fields. The cycle is closed.

### Arkos: The Closed Loop
Arkos recycles all water. The Assembly Plant's original closed-loop cooling system was repurposed after the Collapse — every liter of water in Arkos is continuously filtered, purified, and recirculated. The system is Vorn-maintained and nearly perfect. Water loss is under 2% per cycle.

**Sanitation:** Integrated into the closed loop. Biological waste is decomposed in bacterial chambers (adapted from Mirithane technology) and the resulting biomass feeds the fungal protein cultures.

### Mirithane: The Marsh Filter
Mirithane's water comes from the marsh itself — river water filtered through natural reed beds and augmented by Archive-designed bacterial filtration colonies. The water is clean, slightly mineral, and carries a faint latency signature from the bacterial colonies it passes through. Mirithane residents can taste their water's source — each settlement's water has a distinct character.

**Sanitation:** Decentralized. Each settlement manages its own waste through composting and marsh-integrated treatment. The Archive considers sanitation a form of bacteria-tending — the same principles that maintain the Archive's colonies maintain the population's health.

### Valdris: The Mountain Spring
Valdris' water comes from deep mountain springs — snowmelt filtered through kilometers of rock, emerging at shaft levels pure and cold. The springs are the mountain's most literal gift. Valdris water is exported as a luxury good to Solara's First Ring.

**Sanitation:** Simple. Waste is composted or deposited in deep-shaft chutes — the same chutes that carry waste into wurm territory. The wurms process organic waste. The mountain recycles everything.

### The Verge: Whatever Works
The Verge has no water infrastructure. Residents collect rainwater, draw from pre-Collapse wells that still function, or trade for water from Mirithane or Valdris border settlements. Water quality is inconsistent. Disease from contaminated water is a leading cause of death in The Verge.

## TRANSPORTATION

### Solara: The Ring Road
Solara Prime's Rings are connected by a single road that spirals outward from the Crater rim through all five Rings. The road is the city's spine — every other street branches from it. Transport is by foot, cart, or domesticated wool-beast. The First Ring has access to pre-Collapse ground vehicles maintained by Temple engineers. The lower Rings walk.

Inter-city travel: Paved roads connect Solara Prime to Dawn-Over-Water (coastal) and Grain-Hill (agricultural interior). The roads are maintained by the state. Travel is safe within Solara's borders.

### Arkos: The Conveyor Network
Arkos Central's primary transport is a network of pre-Collapse industrial conveyors — repurposed Assembly Plant material-handling systems that now move people and goods. The conveyors are continuous, slow, and reliable. Vorn engineers maintain them. "The Assembly Plant moved parts. Now it moves people. The difference is smaller than you'd think."

Inter-city travel: Maintained roads connect Arkos Central to Forge-East and Quiet-Station. Vorn-designed ground vehicles — heavy, durable, utilitarian — provide scheduled transport.

### Mirithane: The Waterways
Mirithane's transport is by water. Marsh skiffs — flat-bottomed boats poled through shallow channels — are the universal vehicle. Every Mirithane resident knows how to pole a skiff. Children learn before they learn to read. "The marsh is the road. The skiff is the foot."

Inter-city travel: Deeper channels accommodate larger boats for travel between Reed-Mouth, Spore-Hollow, and Archive-Central. Travel is slow but pleasant — the marsh is beautiful, and the journey is part of the destination.

### Valdris: The Mountain Lifts
Valdris' vertical geography requires vertical transport. The mountain lift network — counterweighted platforms ascending and descending the shaft system — connects every inhabited level. The lifts are manually operated by teams at each level. Some lifts have been operating since pre-Collapse.

Lift travel is slow — 10 minutes per level. A journey from Summit-Hold to the Deep Gate takes approximately 2 hours. During the descent, passengers are expected to remain silent. "The mountain speaks in the descent. Listen."

Horizontal travel: Footpaths and carved stairs connect shaft communities at the same level. Wheeled vehicles are impractical at altitude — everything is carried by hand, pack animal, or lift.

### The Verge: On Foot
The Verge has no public transport. Residents walk. The pre-Collapse road network is partially intact — cracked asphalt, overgrown with scrap-vine — and navigable on foot or by scavenged vehicles that run until they don't. The Memory Trader walks. The Trader has always walked.

## COMMUNICATION

### Solara: The Post and the Herald
Formal communication in Solara is by written message delivered through the Temple's postal service — a network of couriers who carry letters between Rings and cities. The system is slow (days between cities) but reliable.

The Dawn Temple also employs Heralds — trained Readers who Echo-Read Written messages from crystal tokens and deliver them verbally. A Herald-carried message travels as fast as the Herald walks and is as secure as latency itself — the token goes blank after Reading. Interception is impossible.

### Arkos: Ultrasonic Relay
Vorns communicate across Arkos through the Resonance Relay network — stations that transmit and receive Arcology Code in the ultrasonic band. The relays are Vorn-only — humans cannot perceive the transmissions. For cross-type communication, Arkos uses written messages or direct speech.

**The Ultrasonic Public Sphere:** Arkos' relay network carries not just private messages but a continuous public conversation — the ultrasonic equivalent of a public square. Vorns "overhear" each other's transmissions by default. Privacy is achieved by tuning to specific frequencies. Suppressing one's Pulse (ultrasonic latency output) is considered antisocial.

### Mirithane: Echo-Stones
The Marsh Archive's primary secure communication method. An echo-stone is a palm-sized crystal Written with a single brief memory. The stone is carried by courier to its recipient. The recipient Reads the stone. The stone goes blank. The message cannot be intercepted, copied, or overheard.

Echo-stones are expensive (100 marks each) and single-use. The Archive uses them for sensitive diplomatic communication. The Verge uses them when the Memory Trader chooses to be reachable.

### Valdris: The Lift-Log
Valdris' communication is built into its transport system. Lift operators Write shift logs into the lift walls at each level. The walls carry the cumulative record of every lift operation in the shaft's history. New operators Read the walls to learn the shaft's history and current status. Messages for other levels are Written into the wall at the destination level.

### The Verge: Word of Mouth and the Trader
Information in The Verge travels by conversation. The Memory Trader's Network provides a faster channel — Traders carry messages as part of their trades. A message carried by a Trader is as reliable as the Trader. The Trader's messages are always accurate. The Trader's timing is always intentional.

## CONSTRUCTION & ARCHITECTURE

### Solara: Stone and Solar Geometry
Solara Prime is built from pre-Collapse engineered stone — a composite material that resembles limestone but resists weathering for centuries. The city's concentric Rings follow solar geometry — streets align with dawn and dusk on the equinoxes, and the Dawn Temple's eastern windows frame the sunrise precisely on the Dawn Festival.

The First Ring's buildings are monumental — Temple spires, Council chambers, ceremonial spaces. Lower Rings are functional: stone apartment blocks, wooden merchant stalls, factory buildings of brick and steel. The Gradient is visible in the architecture.

### Arkos: Repurposed Industry
Arkos' architecture is adaptive reuse. The Assembly Plant's original structures — fabrication bays, control rooms, material stores — are now housing, government offices, and workshops. Vorn engineers have modified the spaces without erasing their origins. "We live in the place that built us. The walls remember. We remember with them."

Vorn construction emphasizes resonance — buildings are shaped to amplify ultrasonic communication. Metal surfaces, curved walls, acoustic chambers. A Vorn neighborhood hums with ambient ultrasonic conversation that humans perceive only as a vague sense of vibrancy.

### Mirithane: Marsh Integration
Mirithane architecture floats. Buildings in Reed-Mouth and Spore-Hollow are built on anchored platforms that rise and fall with water levels. Archive-Central is partially submerged — the deepest colony halls are underground, temperature-stabilized by the surrounding marsh.

Construction materials are local: reed-fiber composites, treated wood, stone where available. Buildings are designed to be temporary — replaced every few decades as the marsh shifts. "Nothing in the marsh is permanent except the Archive. And the Archive moves when it must."

### Valdris: Carved Space
Valdris architecture is subtractive — buildings are carved from the mountain, not built upon it. The shafts are expanded, chambers are hollowed, and the removed stone is used for surface construction or discarded into the deep chutes.

Anchor chambers are the most carefully carved spaces — shaped for acoustic resonance, temperature stability, and latency containment. An Anchoring chamber "holds" memory as much as the obsidian shards within it.

### The Verge: Occupied Ruin
The Verge doesn't build. It occupies. Pre-Collapse structures — apartment blocks, offices, factories — are inhabited in whatever state they're in. Roofs are patched with salvaged materials. Walls are shored up with debris. Rooms are claimed by presence. When a structure collapses, its occupants move to the next one.

## MEDICINE & HEALTHCARE

### Solara: Tiered Access
Healthcare access follows the Gradient. First and Second Ring residents have access to Temple-trained physicians, imported Mirithane pharmaceuticals, and preventive care. Third Ring: competent but limited care. Fourth Ring: factory clinics, basic treatment. Fifth Ring: herbal medicine, midwifery, and hope.

**Type Restrictions:** Thren patients may be treated but may be deprioritized if Citizen patients require the same resources. Vorns require specialized mechanical care — few physicians in Solara are Vorn-trained. Glims are maintained by their operators; if maintenance exceeds operational cost, decommissioning is the standard remedy.

### Arkos: Vorn Medicine, Human Adaptation
Arkos' medical system was designed for Vorns — mechanical repair, component replacement, core diagnostics. Human and Thren medicine was adapted later. The system works well for trauma and acute illness; it is less developed for chronic disease and end-of-life care.

Vorn "physicians" are maintenance engineers. The distinction is semantic. A Vorn who repairs another Vorn's gear-joint is performing medicine. They call it repair. The result is identical.

### Mirithane: The Archive's Pharmacopeia
Mirithane has the most advanced medical system in Aurelia. The Archive's bacterial colonies produce pharmaceuticals — antibiotics cultured from marsh bacteria, spore-drifter derivatives for neurological treatment, glass-fin venom antidotes harvested from captive specimens. Medical care is free for all residents.

The Water Temple provides latency-based therapeutic practices — Writing traumatic memories into therapeutic colonies that metabolize them. The Archive considers this medicine. The Dawn Temple considers it Extraction-lite and disapproves. The Marsh Archive notes the Dawn Temple's disapproval and continues.

### Valdris: Mountain Medicine
Valdris medicine combines herbal remedies from high-altitude plants, geothermal heat therapy, and latency Anchoring for psychological treatment. Anchors who experience "memory sickness" (recurrent intrusive recall of Written memories) are treated by having their symptoms Anchored into obsidian — removed from their consciousness and stored in the Shrine.

The Shrine considers this therapy. The Dawn Temple considers this Extraction. The Marsh Archive considers it "Anchoring with therapeutic intent" and classifies it separately.

### The Verge: Coin's Practice
The Verge's most reliable healer is Coin — a Memory Trader who trades medical care for memories. She's not formally trained. She's experienced — twelve years of treating everything the Verge produces. Broken bones, infected wounds, spore-drifter overdose, needle-swarm envenomation, childbirth, death. She trades treatment for memories. She hasn't slept through a single night in twelve years.

## TECHNOLOGY AND TYPE

### What Vorns Build
Vorn technology emphasizes durability, efficiency, and documentation. Every Vorn-built device includes a maintenance log — not written, but latched into the device's metal surface through ambient latency. Other Vorns Read the log automatically. "The device remembers what was done to it. You just have to listen."

### What Threns Adapt
Thren engineers excel at adaptation — modifying existing technology for new purposes. The Assembly Protein fungal cultures were a Fabricator waste-recycling system; Thren biologists in Arkos adapted them for food production. The pattern holds: Threns take what exists and make it serve needs it wasn't designed for. "We were built for one purpose. We've spent our existence building others."

### What Glims Maintain
Glims don't design technology. They maintain it. Sweeping floors, cleaning mirrors, lubricating conveyors, replacing worn components. The industrial infrastructure of Solara and Arkos depends on Glim labor. If Glims were recognized as people — if they stopped working, or demanded compensation, or chose different work — both countries' infrastructure would collapse within months. The Gradient is not just unjust. It's load-bearing.

### What Humans Control
Humans control the institutions that control the technology. The Dawn Temple controls the Extraction Manifolds. The Summit Shrine controls Anchoring access. The Gradient Council controls Solara's power grid. Vorns build, Threns adapt, Glims maintain, and humans decide who gets what. This is not a technological arrangement. It's a political one, built from technology's components.
