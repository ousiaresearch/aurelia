# TIME, SEASONS & THE CALENDAR OF AURELIA

*How the post-Collapse world measures time — the five seasons, the thirteen holidays, and the way the Breaking still echoes in every calendar.*

---

## THE POST-COLLAPSE CALENDAR

The pre-Collapse calendar was a standardized 360-day solar year divided into twelve 30-day months. The Collapse broke the standardization. The post-Collapse calendar — adopted by all five countries after years of diplomatic negotiation — is a compromise that reflects the world's new reality.

### Structure

**Year:** 360 days, divided into five named seasons.
**Seasons:** 2 months each (60 days), except Intercalaris (30 days).
**Months:** 12 months of 30 days.
**Week:** 6 days. No named days — days are numbered within the month (1st of Lucent, 15th of Obscura).

### The Five Seasons

| Season | Months | Character | Duration |
|--------|--------|-----------|----------|
| **Lucent** | 1–2 | Spring — renewal, planting. The light returns. | 60 days |
| **Aestas** | 3–4 | Summer — heat, growth. The light is full. | 60 days |
| **Demi** | 5–6 | Autumn — harvest, decline. The light begins to leave. | 60 days |
| **Obscura** | 7–8 | Winter — cold, reflection. The light is hidden. | 60 days |
| **Intercalaris** | 9 | The In-Between — transition. The light is uncertain. | 30 days |
| **Lucent** (again) | 10–11 | Second spring — renewal continues. | 60 days |
| **Aestas** (again) | 12–1 | Second summer — growth peaks. | 60 days |

The seasons cycle twice per year — Lucent, Aestas, Demi, Obscura, Intercalaris, then Lucent again. The double cycle reflects the post-Collapse reality that many regions experience two growing seasons. The old calendar's single annual cycle was a pre-Collapse luxury.

*Note: The months continue sequentially 1–12 across the seasons. Month 1 is first Lucent. Month 12 is second Aestas. The year rolls from month 12 to month 1 seamlessly.*

### The Six-Day Week

Days are unnamed and unnumbered within the week. They are simply "the day," followed by "the next day." The post-Collapse calendar abandoned named days — a deliberate rejection of pre-Collapse ordering systems. "The old world named everything. The names didn't save it."

Most Aurelians track days by number within the month (1st, 2nd, 3rd... 30th). The week is a convenience for market cycles and work schedules, not a sacred division.

### Year Numbering

Years are numbered from the Collapse. The Collapse year is Year 0. Years before are BC (Before Collapse). Years after are AC (After Collapse). The current year is 347 AC.

## THE THIRTEEN HOLIDAYS

### 1. Dawn Festival (First day of first Lucent)

**Country:** Solara (all Rings), observed elsewhere
**Character:** Renewal, hierarchy, the Gradient's promise.

The first sunrise of the new year is celebrated at the Dawn Temple. The High Cleric leads a ceremony on the Crater rim — the first light touches the crater water and refracts across the assembled crowd. The ceremony is beautiful. It is also a statement: the Gradient begins here, at the peak, with the light touching the Temple first.

**Observance:** Dawn Loaf baked and shared. Crater fish served at Temple functions. Citizens exchange formal greetings: "The light returns." Response: "The Gradient continues."

---

### 2. The Silent Day (15th of first Lucent)

**Country:** Valdris, observed by Archivists in Mirithane
**Character:** Memory of the Silence — the 17 days after the Collapse when latency died.

A day of total silence observed in the Summit Shrine. Anchors do not work. The lifts do not run. No one speaks in Summit-Hold from dawn to dusk. The silence commemorates the 17 days of the Silence — the period after the Collapse when every latency substrate on Aurelia went dead.

**Observance:** Total silence. Meals are eaten cold — no cooking fires (the sound of fire is sound). At dusk, a single obsidian shard is struck — the first sound of the new year in Valdris. The shard's ring echoes through the shafts.

The Marsh Archive observes a modified version — 17 minutes of silence at midday.

---

### 3. The Name-Singing (15th of second Lucent)

**Country:** Arkos (primary), Vorn communities everywhere
**Character:** Identity, defiance, memory of the Name Rebellion (-500 BC).

The anniversary of the day 17 Vorns stood in Bay 1 and refused their designations. The 17 names are sung in the ultrasonic register — the 14 who kept their names and the 3 who were decommissioned. The song does not distinguish.

**Observance:** The Name Cake is baked and served in silence. Seventeen candles. Three extinguished. Public readings from Kael-1's memory core fragment (Cipher-Cult Archive). Vorns share core-memories of their own naming moments.

---

### 4. The Sol-tharam Moment (22nd of second Lucent)

**Country:** Thren communities in all five countries
**Character:** Shared experience, community, the mystery of Thren existence.

On this date in -22 BC, 17 Threns across three countries simultaneously experienced a shared sensation — "the light after calibration," *sol-tharam* — lasting approximately 90 seconds. No explanation has ever been confirmed. Thren communities mark the date annually.

**Observance:** 90 seconds of shared silence at midday. Threns gather — physically or, when separated, through latency intention (Threns report "feeling" each other's presence on this date). Mystery vocabulary is shared aloud — words no one taught them, spoken for others to hear.

Solara Security monitors the Sol-tharam Moment annually. The file remains open.

---

### 5. The First Planting (First day of first Aestas)

**Country:** Solara, Mirithane, Valdris (agricultural communities)
**Character:** Labor, hope, the gamble of agriculture.

The first day of the growing season. Farmers plant the first seeds of the year. The act is practical and ritual — "what we plant today feeds us through Obscura."

**Observance:** Farmers across all five countries plant a single ceremonial row at dawn. The rest of the day is normal labor — "the grain doesn't observe holidays."

---

### 6. The Water Immersion (15th of first Aestas)

**Country:** Mirithane (Water Temple primary)
**Character:** Purification, memory, communion with the Archive's colonies.

The Water Temple's primary ritual. Practitioners immerse themselves waist-deep in the Temple's immersion chamber — river water filtered through the Archive's oldest bacterial colonies. The immersion is a form of communion — "the water remembers every Immersion. We add ours to the record."

**Observance:** Public immersion open to all residents. The Temple serves Water Temple Broth. Archivists perform a 24-hour Writing marathon — continuous memory deposition into the Temple's dedicated colony strains. The colonies metabolize for months afterward.

---

### 7. The Anchor's Vigil (21st of first Demi)

**Country:** Valdris (Summit Shrine)
**Character:** Descent, memory, the mountain's weight.

Every Anchor pair in Valdris descends below the Deep Gate — into wurm territory, into the unmapped shafts — for a ritual Anchoring. They Write one memory each into the deep shaft walls. "The mountain gives us a year of memories. We give one back."

**Observance:** Anchor pairs descend at dawn. They return at dusk or they don't — wurm activity is highest during the Vigil, and losses are accepted as the mountain's taking. The memories Anchored during the Vigil are never Read except by the mountain. The Shrine considers them "given, not stored."

---

### 8. The Harvest Festival (30th of first Demi)

**Country:** All five countries (agricultural communities)
**Character:** Abundance, gratitude, preparation for Obscura.

The last day of the primary harvest season. The harvest is in. The larders are full. The winter can come.

**Observance:** Feasts — the largest meals of the year in agricultural communities. Unharvestable crops are left in the fields as an offering — "for whatever survives Obscura, for whatever needs to eat when we can't feed them." In Mirithane, the offerings are left on the Hawk Platforms alongside the dead.

---

### 9. The Night of Echoes (First day of Obscura)

**Country:** All countries, most observed in The Verge and Mirithane
**Character:** The dead, memory, latency's peak.

The first night of winter. Latency practitioners across Aurelia report that ambient latency is strongest on this night — "the world is remembering harder." Wraiths in The Verge are most visible. Echo-stones carried by Archivists pulse faintly. Latency-bloom flowers open regardless of location.

**Observance:** Candles lit for the dead — one for each person the household has lost. In The Verge, the Wraith Quarter holds an all-night vigil. Wraiths appear. The living watch. "They're still here. Someone should acknowledge them."

---

### 10. The Extraction Review (First day of second Obscura, every 5 years)

**Country:** Solara (Dawn Temple)
**Character:** Institutional accountability (nominal), Extraction management.

The Dawn Temple reviews all active Extractors. Extraction counts are assessed. Cumulative bleed is measured. Extractors approaching dangerous bleed levels are rotated out or — in the worst cases — retired involuntarily.

Elira-2's 64th Extraction, the one that sent her to the Crater, was performed three days before her scheduled review. The Temple has since moved the review to the first of the month. The timing is not coincidental. The Temple does not acknowledge the connection.

---

### 11. The Mountain's Breath (Variable — determined by Summit Shrine Anchors)

**Country:** Valdris
**Character:** The mountain's will, interpreted by Anchors.

Not a fixed date. The Summit Shrine's senior Anchors Read the mountain's latency and determine when "the mountain exhales" — a period of 3–5 days when the deep shafts are considered too active for safe Anchoring. Wurm activity spikes. Geothermal vents release gas. The Deep Sound is louder.

**Observance:** Anchoring ceases. Lifts are restricted. The mountain communities observe quiet days — "the mountain is breathing. We hold ours."

---

### 12. Intercalaris Eve (30th of second Obscura)

**Country:** All countries
**Character:** The end of the old cycle, the uncertainty of the In-Between.

The last day before Intercalaris — the transitional month. The year's cycle is ending. The new cycle hasn't begun.

**Observance:** Divination practices across all five countries. In Solara, Temple Readers Echo-Read the Crater water for signs of the coming year. In Mirithane, Archivists Read the oldest colonies for patterns. In Valdris, Anchors Read the mountain's latency. In The Verge, the Memory Trader (if present) offers a single trade: "Give me a memory. I'll tell you what's coming. You won't like it, but you'll know it's true."

---

### 13. The Return (First day of Intercalaris)

**Country:** All countries
**Character:** The In-Between, the question of what comes next.

Intercalaris is the month between — the old cycle has ended, the new one hasn't begun. The month is considered "unclaimed time." Normal schedules are suspended. Work continues but without the usual urgency. "Intercalaris is not part of the year. Intercalaris is the space between years. What happens in Intercalaris doesn't count."

**Observance:** No formal observances. The month is marked by a cultural suspension of judgment — "an Intercalaris mistake" is forgiven. A decision made in Intercalaris is considered provisional until the new year confirms it. The tradition is a psychological safety valve — a month where consequences are suspended.

### The Verge (Variable)

The Verge doesn't track seasons by calendar. It tracks them by what's available. Spring brings salvage (snowmelt reveals buried caches). Summer brings spore-drifter (peak cultivation). Autumn brings scrap-orchard fruit (if the trees fruit). Winter brings hardship (exposure, hunger, the long wait). Intercalaris brings nothing predictable.

## TIMEKEEPING

### Daily Time

Most Aurelians track time by the sun — dawn, midday, dusk. Precision timekeeping is a pre-Collapse luxury. The Dawn Temple maintains a pre-Collapse clock in the High Cleric's chambers. It is the only functioning clock of its kind in Aurelia. The Temple considers it a relic. The Marsh Archive considers it a historical artifact. The Temple disagrees. The clock continues to tick.

### Vorn Time

Vorns track time internally — their memory cores record timestamps to the second. A Vorn always knows exactly what time it is. This is occasionally useful and frequently irritating to non-Vorns. "The Vorn says it's seven minutes past the hour. The Vorn is correct. The Vorn is always correct. The Vorn could stop being correct for one conversation."

### Archive Time

The Marsh Archive measures time by colony growth cycles. The oldest colonies have been metabolizing continuously for 650+ years. Every bacterial division is a timestamp. The Archive's "clock" is the cumulative weight of bacterial generations.
