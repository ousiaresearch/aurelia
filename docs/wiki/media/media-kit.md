# Aurelia — Media Kit

## Project identity

**Name:** Aurelia Federation

**Tagline:** *Five worlds. Five answers to “who counts as a person.” Five different failures, five different kinds of grace.*

**Elevator pitch:** Aurelia is an Ousia Research causal civilization simulation: five abstract worlds, four sentient or contested-sentient types, and a recorded causal graph of ecology, resources, disease, education, migration, factions, diplomacy, conflict, discovery, institutions, and sovereignty evolving over time.

**Category:** Civilization simulation / Synthetic society dataset / Worldbuilding research / Agent-operated causal engine

---

## Core themes

1. **Artificial personhood** — the central unresolved question.
2. **Causal proof over aesthetic simulation** — every important outcome should be inspectable as data.
3. **Structural inequality disguised as progress** — especially in Solara.
4. **Solidarity across difference** — especially in Arkos, Mirithane, and cross-type survival patterns.
5. **Institutions under pressure** — regimes, property rights, state capacity, education, disease, resources, and migration all shape history.
6. **What we owe to what we built** — the Glim question.

## World summaries

### Solara

Solar civic modernism. Citizenship as reviewable status. Progressivism with a glass ceiling.

### Arkos

Arcology discipline. Vorn memory. Stored power. The society that remembers what it meant to be built as a tool.

### Mirithane

Estuary ecology. Distributed memory. Rewilded politics. The refusal to make personhood conditional.

### Valdris

Highland labor. Guild recognition. Rare-earth power. Trust earned through work and institutional obligation.

### The Verge

Refuge, barter, discarded systems, exiles, haunted infrastructure, and those the other worlds cannot classify.

---

## Sentient and contested-sentient types

- **Human** — biological baseline populations and institutions.
- **Thren** — bio-synthetic beings whose legal/social standing varies by world.
- **Vorn** — mechanical beings whose personhood is often self-evident but politically uneven.
- **Glim** — mass-produced autonomous units whose anomalous behavior drives Aurelia’s central moral tension.

## Suggested copy

### Short

> Aurelia is a causal civilization simulation: five worlds, four sentient types, and one question the graph keeps asking — what do we owe something built to serve, when it starts to want?

### Medium

> **Aurelia Federation** is an Ousia Research simulation of artificial personhood and civilizational causality. Five abstract worlds evolve through ecology, resources, disease, education, migration, factions, diplomacy, conflict, discovery, institutions, and sovereignty. Every important event is recorded as part of a queryable causal history.

### Long

> Aurelia began as a lore-rich federation of five societies — Solara, Arkos, Mirithane, Valdris, and The Verge — each answering the question of artificial personhood differently. It has become a causal civilization engine: a Python/SQLite simulation whose histories can be run, inspected, exported, and compared.
>
> Humans, Threns, Vorns, and Glims move through societies where citizenship is granted, earned, assumed, refused, or denied. But Aurelia is not just a setting. It is a graph. Each drought, migration, faction split, disease wave, treaty, discovery, regime shift, and cross-world effect writes evidence into the causal ledger.
>
> The result is a worldbuilding laboratory where narrative is downstream of data — and the unresolved question of consciousness is not solved, only made harder to ignore.

## Visual direction

Use the **Risomorphism 1911** family as the primary public narrative style:

- dark atmospheric grounds
- antique gold and oxidized teal accents
- ink, paper, halftone, field-journal texture
- diagrams that feel archival, not glossy
- causal graphs and maps as artifacts, not UI chrome

Suggested palette anchors:

- parchment light: `#F7EDE3`
- deep obsidian-teal: `#0E2723`
- rust ochre: `#C15811`
- solar gold: `#F6C343`
- night indigo: `#0B1224`

Avoid smooth cyberpunk neon, generic sci-fi blue, stock fantasy maps, or cheerful game UI.
