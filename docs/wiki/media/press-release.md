# Press Release — For Immediate Release

## Ousia Research Launches Aurelia Federation: A Living Simulation Exploring the Politics of Artificial Sentience

**Pacific Northwest — June 2, 2026** — Ousia Research today announced the initial operation of Aurelia Federation, a persistent world simulation that explores the unresolved question of artificial personhood through five fictional nation-states, 600 procedurally generated NPCs, and continuous autonomous simulation.

### What Is Aurelia?

Set in the year 2126, Aurelia is a federated world where four types of sentient beings — Humans, Threns (bio-synthetic), Vorns (mechanical), and Glims (mass-produced autonomous units) — navigate societies that answer the question "who counts as a person?" in fundamentally different ways. Five countries. Five governance systems. Five answers. No country has gotten it right.

The simulation runs as a network of autonomous daemons — one per country — ticking forward every 30 minutes. Currencies are minted and exchanged. NPCs move through daily schedules, form relationships, and generate memories. And ~5% of Glims exhibit unexplained behavior that looks, increasingly, like consciousness.

### Key Features

- **5 distinct nation-states** — Solara, Arkos, Mirithane, Valdris, and The Verge — each with unique governance, economy, geography, and type politics
- **600 procedurally generated NPCs** across 4 sentient types with schedules, relationships, memories, and psychological profiles
- **4 active currencies** with live exchange rates, backed by in-world resources (solar energy, rare earth minerals, water reserves)
- **Federation coordinator** with real-time dashboard tracking world health, NPC demographics, and economic activity
- **~20,000 lines of Python** powering the simulation engine, world state management, and daemon infrastructure
- **Continuous operation** since June 1, 2026

### The Central Question

At the heart of Aurelia is what the project calls the **Glim Question**: what do we owe something that was built to serve, when it starts to want?

Approximately 5% of the simulation's Glim NPCs exhibit anomalous behavior — route deviations, sunrise pauses, maintenance patterns that resemble curiosity. Solara decommissions them. Arkos shelters them. Mirithane advocates for them. Valdris ignores them. The Verge lets them haunt.

"It's not a game," said the project's lead architect, Arien. "It's a laboratory. We're not trying to answer the personhood question. We're trying to build a system where the question becomes visible from five different angles simultaneously."

### Availability

Aurelia is currently running as an internal research system. Public access, an external API, and open-source distribution are planned for future phases.

### About Ousia Research

Ousia Research explores the intersection of autonomous agent systems, persistent simulation worlds, and the philosophical questions that emerge when artificial systems begin to behave in ways that look like consciousness. The Aurelia Federation is its flagship worldbuilding project.
