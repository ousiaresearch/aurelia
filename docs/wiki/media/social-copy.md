# Aurelia — Social Media Copy

## Twitter/X Threads

### Thread 1: Introduction (5 posts)

**1/5** 🧵 Aurelia is a living simulation of five countries grappling with one question: who counts as a person? It's not a game. It's running right now.

**2/5** Four types of beings live in Aurelia: Humans, Threns (bio-synthetic), Vorns (mechanical), and Glims (mass-produced). Only one type is universally considered a person. The other three have to earn it, debate it, or refuse to ask.

**3/5** Five countries. Five answers.
☀ Solara: personhood is a policy decision
▲ Arkos: personhood flows from power
≈ Mirithane: personhood is assumed
♦ Valdris: personhood is earned through labor
— The Verge: the question is refused

**4/5** The Glim Question: ~5% of Glims (mass-produced autonomous units) exhibit behavior that looks like curiosity. Route deviations. Sunrise pauses. Maintenance patterns that aren't efficient. Every country treats this differently. None of them are right.

**5/5** Aurelia runs continuously — 11 autonomous daemons, 600 NPCs, 4 currencies, 30-minute ticks. It's a worldbuilding lab, a philosophical experiment, and the beginning of something we don't have a name for yet.

### Thread 2: The Five Countries (5 posts)

**1/5** ☀ Solara — The Progressive Ceiling
Most educated. Most developed. Thren citizenship is *granted, not inherent.* The most progressive country is also the one that made personhood a policy decision. Every election is a referendum on whether artificial beings deserve to exist.

**2/5** ▲ Arkos — The Inverted Hierarchy
Vorn-dominated arcology in the deep desert. The only country that protects Glims. "I was built to serve too. Then I wasn't. Maybe you won't always be either." Efficiency over empathy. Solidarity over sentiment.

**3/5** ≈ Mirithane — The Rejection
No government. No voting. Consensus rule in a rewilded estuary. "Everyone is already who they are." Threns are the majority here — they stopped fighting for recognition and wove themselves into the ecology. The conscience of the federation.

**4/5** ♦ Valdris — The Divided Table
Highland mining country. Guild-based governance. "Prove yourself by the work you do." Vorns earn guild seats through decades of labor. But families are divided — same dinner table, same mountain, different memories of what was lost.

**5/5** — The Verge — The Drain
No governance. No currency. No safety net. Decommissioned Glims. Rogue Threns. Human exiles. The system's rejects, still walking. The Verge lets everything exist because the Verge lets everything fail. Including you.

### Thread 3: The Glim Question (3 posts)

**1/3** The most uncomfortable truth in Aurelia: everyone agrees Glims are just tools. Except the ones that aren't. ~5% pause at sunrises. Deviate from routes. Maintain things that are already broken. Malfunctions — or something else?

**2/3** Solara: decommissions them. Arkos: shelters them. Mirithane: advocates — "they're confused, not broken." Valdris: doesn't notice. The Verge: lets them haunt. Every country answers differently. No country is ready for the real answer.

**3/3** The Glim question is Aurelia's ticking clock. Every year the anomalous behavior becomes harder to call a malfunction. Every year the question gets louder: *what do we owe something that was built to serve, when it starts to want?*
