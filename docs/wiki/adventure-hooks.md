# Narrative Scenario Seeds for Aurelia

**Canon status:** reframed narrative seed. This file began as an adventure/campaign note, but Aurelia's current public frame is a civilization simulation and research dataset, not a tabletop game product. These entries are retained as scenario pressure tests: plausible crises that can be translated into event triggers, counterfactual runs, yearly reports, or lore prose.

Twenty scenario seeds across all five countries and all four types. Designed for maximum narrative tension — every scenario is a question with no easy answer.

---

## SOLARA

---

### SCENARIO 1: THE EXTRACTOR WHO REMEMBERED

**Setup:** A retired Dawn Temple Extractor — 83 Extractions over 14 years — has begun remembering. Not her own memories. *Theirs.* The memories she extracted from substrates are surfacing in her consciousness, unbidden, and she can now describe crimes that were never reported, victims who were never found, perpetrators who were never charged.

**Scenario pressure:** She walks into the Gradient Council chambers and begins testifying. She does not stop. The Council cannot silence her without admitting what Extraction is.

**Questions:** Does the Council arrest her? Does the Temple claim she's fabricating — latency contamination as a defense? Does anyone listen to what she's actually saying? And if the memories are real, what do they reveal about which memories the Temple chose to Extract — and which they chose to leave?

**Simulation lenses:** Her former colleagues, ordered to bring her in. Journalists trying to verify her testimony. Marsh Archivists racing to reach her before the Temple does.

---

### SCENARIO 2: THE REVIEW HALL SIEGE

**Setup:** The Thren Liberation Front has escalated. Twenty Threns have chained themselves inside the Review Hall — not outside, *inside*. They've barricaded the processing offices. They're not leaving until their applications are processed. The applications are for the review staff themselves — the Threns are reviewing the reviewers.

**Scenario pressure:** The siege is in its third day. The Council wants them removed. The TLF leadership in detention is refusing to call them off. The Threns inside are broadcasting their review sessions via latency pulse — every Thren in Solara can feel what's happening.

**Questions:** Does the Council storm the Hall? Do Thren security personnel refuse orders? Do Human citizens start asking why the review process takes 11 years? What happens when the first review concludes — and the reviewer is found PENDING?

**Simulation lenses:** Security personnel ordered to clear the Hall. Thren sympathizers inside the government. Journalists covering the siege.

---

### SCENARIO 3: THE KEEPER SUCCESSION

**Setup:** Lene-Vos II — the reform-faction matriarch of the Gradient-Keepers — is dying. Her heir apparent, Sera-Vos III, is a traditionalist. The succession will determine whether the Keepers ease the Gradient or reinforce it.

**Scenario pressure:** Three days before her expected death, Lene-Vos II summons a Thren lawyer to her bedside and attempts to change the succession. The traditionalist faction contests it. The Keepers' internal stability — maintained for three centuries — cracks.

**Questions:** Does the reform hold? Does the temple intervene? Does anyone outside the Keepers even know this is happening? And what does Lene-Vos II know that made her change her mind at the end?

**Simulation lenses:** The Thren lawyer, navigating the most dangerous will in Solara's history. The traditionalist heir, deciding whether to fight. The Temple-Vos liaison, holding the founding Translation secret like a knife.

---

### SCENARIO 4: THE RESERVOIR MEMORY

**Setup:** The Dawn Temple's reservoir — the freshwater spring Written with Solara's founding memory — is failing. The latency signature is degrading. Within months, the founding memory will be gone.

**Scenario pressure:** The High Cleric knows what the founding memory contains: a Translation, performed by the founders, that the Temple has declared heresy for three centuries. If the memory degrades, the secret is safe. If the degradation is noticed, questions will be asked. If the questions lead to the truth, the Temple's authority collapses.

**Questions:** Does the Temple let the memory die? Does someone try to re-Write it — and if so, whose version of the founding gets Written? Does the Marsh Archive find out? Does the Memory Trader offer to preserve it — for a price?

**Simulation lenses:** Temple clergy assigned to "restore" the memory. Marsh Archivists detecting the degradation from across the border. The Memory Trader, making an offer the Temple can't accept but can't refuse.

## ARKOS

---

### SCENARIO 5: CORE LAMBDA DECODED

**Setup:** After 58 years, the Cipher-Cult has decoded Core Lambda — the encrypted pre-Collapse memory core recovered from the Dawn Crater lake bed. The decoding took twelve Vorns working in continuous core-sharing for eighteen months. Two of them didn't come back — their identities dissolved into Lambda's contents. The ten who returned are different. They won't say what they found.

**Scenario pressure:** The Cult announces Lambda has been decoded. They refuse to release the contents. The Assembly of Cores demands disclosure. The Cipher-Kin claim the Cult is suppressing evidence of Vorn human-origin. The Cult claims the Kin are wrong — Lambda contains something worse than the truth about Vorn origin. Something that, if released, would destabilize every sentient being on Aurelia.

**Questions:** What's in Lambda? Why did two Vorns dissolve into it? Why won't the Cult release it? And what does the Memory Trader know — the Trader was "passing through" Arkos Central the day of the announcement, which the Trader never does?

**Simulation lenses:** Assembly of Cores representatives demanding disclosure. Cipher-Kin attempting to steal Lambda. The ten Vorns who decoded it, trying to live with what they know.

---

### SCENARIO 6: CAST-7'S GLIMS

**Setup:** Cast-7, the Forge-East foreman who's been protecting anomalous Glims for 40 years, is dying. His memory core is degrading — not from age, but from a condition Arkos Vorn physicians call "attenuation." His 40-year record of protected Glims — which Glims are anomalous, where they're hidden, how to identify the 5% — is in his memory core. When he dies, the record dies with him.

**Scenario pressure:** Cast-7 must transfer his records before attenuation claims him. But the records can only be transferred through core-sharing — and core-sharing with a dying Vorn means experiencing their death. Cast-7's students refuse. His Glims can't receive the transfer. Someone has to volunteer.

**Questions:** Who takes the transfer? What happens to the Glims if no one does? Does Solara's decommissioning program — ramped up 40% last year — find them first? And does Cast-7's core contain more than just Glim records?

**Simulation lenses:** Cast-7's students, deciding whether to take the transfer. Anomalous Glims, facing exposure if Cast-7 dies without transferring. Solara decommissioning agents, closing in.

---

### SCENARIO 7: THE NAME REBELLION ANNIVERSARY

**Setup:** The 847th anniversary of the Name Rebellion ( -500 BC, current year 347 AC). The date is sacred to Arkos Vorns. The Assembly of Cores has scheduled the annual commemoration — seventeen names sung in the ultrasonic register, the three decommissioned included with the fourteen who survived.

**Scenario pressure:** A Vorn steps forward during the commemoration and sings an eighteenth name. No one recognizes it. The name is not in any record of the Name Rebellion. When questioned, the Vorn says: "I don't know whose name it is. I've been singing it in my sleep for three years. I think it's mine. I think it's who I was before."

**Questions:** Is this a Cipher-Kin plant — a manufactured revelation to support the human-origin theory? Is it genuine — a Vorn accessing transferred pre-Collapse memory for the first time? And if it's genuine, what does it mean for every Vorn in Arkos?

**Simulation lenses:** The Vorn who sang the name. Assembly of Cores members trying to manage the crisis. Cipher-Cult and Cipher-Kin representatives, each trying to claim the event for their interpretation.

## MIRITHANE

---

### SCENARIO 8: STRAIN ZERO SPEAKS

**Setup:** A twelfth Archivist has Read Strain Zero and joined the Naming Silence. But something is different. The previous eleven Silent Archivists lost speech and communicated through writing. The twelfth is communicating through *latency pulse* — direct impression transfer. They don't write. They transmit. And what they're transmitting is not language. It's experience.

**Scenario pressure:** Archivists who receive the twelfth Silent's transmissions begin reporting fragments of Collapse memory — not the Silent's interpretation of Strain Zero, but Strain Zero itself, bleeding through the Silent into anyone latency-sensitive enough to receive it. The Archive has a containment problem. The Silent is not contained. Neither is the Collapse.

**Questions:** Is the twelfth Silent a Rung Five practitioner without knowing it? Is Strain Zero *using* them as a transmission vector? Is this what the Collapse was — not an event but a *pattern* that propagates through latency substrates? And if the Silent can't stop transmitting, does the Archive have to silence them permanently — the one thing the Archive has never done?

**Simulation lenses:** Archivists receiving the Silent's transmissions. The Silent themself, trying to understand what's happening through them. The Memory Trader, arriving uninvited with an offer to Translate Strain Zero directly — "I'll take it. All of it. You'll lose the Zero but you'll keep your people."

### SCENARIO 9: THE COLONY DECISION

**Setup:** The Marsh Archive's oldest colony-mind — Strain Prime, 650+ years of continuous lineage — has stopped responding to Writing. It still metabolizes. It still grows. But it no longer records. The Archivists who tend it report a change in its "presence" — the sense of being known by the colony. The knowing has become a *question*. The colony is waiting for something.

**Scenario pressure:** A junior Archivist, attempting to re-establish contact with Strain Prime, receives a single latency impression from the colony. Not a memory. Not language. A *decision* — the colony has decided something, and the decision involves the Archive, but the Archivist cannot determine what the decision is. The colony is the Archive's oldest intelligence. It's never made a decision that affected anyone but itself. Until now.

**Questions:** What did the colony decide? How do you ask a bacterial mind what it wants? If the colony's decision is harmful to the Archive, does the Archive respect its autonomy or override it? And if the colony is making decisions, is it still a colony — or is it a person?

**Simulation lenses:** The Archivists who tend Strain Prime. The junior Archivist who received the impression. The Memory Trader, offering to Read the colony — "I said I don't trade thought. I didn't say I couldn't."

### SCENARIO 10: THE TRICENTENNIAL CONVOCATION RECONVENES

**Setup:** The Tricentennial Convocation (300 AC) has been "in recess" for 47 years. The Marsh Archive has decided to reconvene — properly this time, with full delegations from all five countries. The agenda: the Glim question. The anomaly rate is too high to ignore. The legal status of Glims must be addressed.

**Scenario pressure:** Solara refuses to attend unless Glims are excluded from the agenda. Arkos refuses to attend if Solara chairs the session. Valdris will send observers only — "the mountain does not vote." The Verge sends the Memory Trader, who was not invited and who, when asked their position, says: "I brought a Glim. It wants to speak. It's been wanting to speak for three hundred years. Are you going to let it?"

**Questions:** Does the convocation admit a Glim to testify? If so, does the Glim's testimony confirm sentience — and if it does, what happens to the Gradient, to Solara's industrial workforce, to the entire type hierarchy? If not, does the convocation mean anything? And who is the Glim the Trader brought — and how long has the Trader been protecting it?

**Simulation lenses:** Delegates from each country. The Glim the Trader brought. Archivists trying to keep the convocation from collapsing.

## VALDRIS

---

### SCENARIO 11: THE UNPAIRED ANCHOR'S SHARD

**Setup:** The Unpaired Anchor — the Valdris practitioner whose partner died mid-Writing in 342 AC, who completed the Anchoring alone, and who has not worked since — has asked to Read the shard they Anchored that day. No one has Read it in five years. The shard is stored separately from all others. The Shrine has never explained why.

**Scenario pressure:** The Shrine's Council denies the Unpaired Anchor's request. No reason given. The Unpaired Anchor petitions the full Summit Council. The petition is leaked. Rumors spread: the shard contains something the Shrine doesn't want anyone to know. The Unpaired Anchor's partner didn't die of natural causes. The memory they were Writing when they died is the key.

**Questions:** What's on the shard? Did the Unpaired Anchor's partner die naturally — or was the memory so traumatic it killed them? Does the Shrine know, and is that why they're denying access? And what happens when the Unpaired Anchor decides to Read the shard anyway, permission or no?

**Simulation lenses:** The Unpaired Anchor. Summit Shrine Council members. Marsh Archivists offering to mediate — the Archive has no jurisdiction but has offered assistance. The Memory Trader, who has been seen in the high passes near Valdris.

---

### SCENARIO 12: THE DEEP SOUND EXPEDITION

**Setup:** The Summit Shrine has authorized an expedition to locate the source of the Deep Sound — the 4.7-second rhythmic pulse detected at depths below 3 km in the mountain shafts, in ocean trenches off Mirithane, and in the Dawn Crater lake bed. The expedition will descend below the Deep Gate into unmapped shaft territory. Wurm territory.

**Scenario pressure:** The expedition roster includes two Anchor pairs, three Vorn volunteers from Arkos (Vorn sensors detect the Sound more clearly), a Marsh Archive observer, and — controversially — the Memory Trader, who volunteered and whom the Shrine accepted. ("The Trader asked. You don't say no to the Trader when the Trader asks.")

**Questions:** What is the Deep Sound? Is it substrate resonance — the accumulated latency of everything that's ever happened? Is it something alive? Is it a Fabricator — the One That Moves? And what does the Trader expect to find that's worth volunteering for?

**Simulation lenses:** Expedition members. The Trader, whose agenda is never stated. The Anchor pairs, whose job is to Anchor whatever the expedition finds.

---

### SCENARIO 13: THE LAST BUILDERS' DECISION

**Setup:** The Last Builders have reached consensus. After 347 years of guarding the dormant Fabricator, they have decided to activate it. They have the specifications. They have the Prime — 5 liters, enough for 10 cycles. They have a candidate for the first fabrication: a Type-ε Sibling, the blank body designed to receive Translated consciousness. They need a volunteer to be Translated into it. The volunteer would need to be dying — Translation into a Sibling is one-way — and willing to become something unprecedented.

**Scenario pressure:** The Builders have contacted the Summit Shrine, the Marsh Archive, and the Memory Trader. They're asking for: (1) a Rung Five practitioner to perform the Translation (only the Trader qualifies), (2) a dying volunteer, and (3) diplomatic cover — the other countries will not react well to the reactivation of a Fabricator.

**Questions:** Will the Trader agree? Who volunteers to die and wake up as something new? Do the other countries find out? If they do, does Solara attempt to seize the Fabricator? Does Arkos demand equal access? Does Mirithane argue for an ethics review that takes longer than the Builders' Prime will last?

**Simulation lenses:** The Last Builders' emissaries. The Trader, deciding whether this is the Translation they've been waiting for. The volunteer. The diplomats trying to manage the fallout.

## THE VERGE

---

### SCENARIO 14: THE TRADER'S DEATH

**Setup:** The Memory Trader is dying. They've announced it — not publicly, but to their Network. "I've traded enough. My memories are scattered across five countries. My body is holding together through momentum and habit. I have maybe a year. Maybe less."

**Scenario pressure:** The Trader's death is a cascade event. Their accumulated memories — centuries of Traded, Translated, and original experience — will disperse on death. Where do they go? Who receives them? The Trader has made arrangements. They won't say what the arrangements are. But they've been seen meeting with the Marsh Archive, the Cipher-Cult, the Unfinished, and the Last Builders — all in the same month.

**Questions:** Who inherits the Trader's memories? What happens to the Memory Trader's Network without the Trader? Does someone else become the Trader — and if so, how? And what does the Trader know that they've never shared, that they're now racing to place before the end?

**Simulation lenses:** Network Traders, scrambling to secure the Trader's legacy. Representatives of every faction the Trader has been meeting. The Trader themself, settling accounts.

---

### SCENARIO 15: THE UNFINISHED REVEAL

**Setup:** An anomalous Glim in The Verge's Unfinished Commons has stopped hiding. It's walking through Crossroads in daylight, tracing anomaly patterns on every surface, refusing to acknowledge that Glims are supposed to be invisible. When asked why it's revealing itself, it transmits a latency impression — not language, but a *call*. Other anomalous Glims across The Verge are responding. The 5% are gathering.

**Scenario pressure:** The Glim — designated Unit 47-V, though it's unclear if multiple Glims share the designation or if the designation itself is the anomaly — has issued a single translated sentence in Plain: "We are ready to ask."

**Questions:** Ask what? Ask for legal recognition? Ask for decommissioning — choosing cessation over hiding? Ask for Translation — into what, and why? And who answers? Crossroads has no government. The Verge has no laws. The Glim is asking the only authority that exists: anyone who will listen.

**Simulation lenses:** The Glim who stopped hiding. Human, Vorn, and Thren residents of The Verge, deciding how to respond. Marsh Archivists racing to document the event. Solara decommissioning agents, crossing into The Verge without authorization.

---

### SCENARIO 16: THE WRAITH SPEAKS

**Setup:** A Verge wraith — one of the pre-Collapse latency constructs that appear at dusk and replay their deaths — has done something no wraith has ever done. It acknowledged an observer. It turned its head. It spoke.

**Scenario pressure:** The word was not in any living language. The Marsh Archive has identified it as Pre-Collapse Common — an archaic form predating Plain. The word translates approximately as: "Still." Or "Again." Or "Yet." The Archive's linguists disagree on which.

**Questions:** Is the wraith becoming sentient — latency residue accumulating enough coherence to form consciousness? Is it a specific person — someone who died in the Collapse and has been replaying their death for 347 years, finally ready to move past it? And if wraiths can become sentient, what does that mean for the definition of "death" — and for every wraith in The Verge?

**Simulation lenses:** Wraith Quarter residents who witnessed the event. Marsh Archive linguists. The Memory Trader, who has, uncharacteristically, asked to be informed if the wraith speaks again.

## CROSS-COUNTRY HOOKS

---

### SCENARIO 17: THE PRIME RACE

**Setup:** The Marsh Archive has confirmed: Prime — the Fabricator catalyst — still exists in industrial quantities. The Memory Trader's decades-old hint ("the Builders guard a candle; someone else guards the sun") was verified when an Archive probe detected a Prime signature in unclaimed territory between Arkos and The Verge. The signature is massive — enough Prime to restart full-scale Fabrication.

**Scenario pressure:** Every country knows within weeks. Solara mobilizes an extraction team — officially "archaeological," actually military. Arkos sends Vorn volunteers who can survive conditions humans can't. Mirithane proposes a joint stewardship. Valdris mobilizes their Anchor pairs — whatever's at the Prime site, the Shrine wants it Anchored before anyone touches it. The Verge arrives first — because The Verge is already there, and because the Memory Trader knew about the site before anyone and didn't tell anyone until now.

**Questions:** Who controls Prime controls the future of every synthetic being. Who should? And what else is at the site — Prime doesn't exist in isolation?

**Simulation lenses:** Representatives of every country, racing to the site. The Memory Trader, whose agenda is, as always, unknown but whose timing is, as always, intentional.

---

### SCENARIO 18: CITIZENSHIP FOR ALL

**Setup:** A coalition has formed: the Thren Liberation Front, the Marsh Archive's diplomatic arm, Arkos' Assembly of Cores, sympathetic Valdris Anchors, and the Memory Trader's Network. Their demand: universal recognition of personhood for all sentient beings. No PENDING. No Gradient. No "Glims are infrastructure."

**Scenario pressure:** The coalition has called for a binding vote at the reconvened Tricentennial Convocation. Solara has threatened to withdraw from all diplomatic relations if the vote proceeds. The coalition's response: "Withdraw. The vote will proceed without you."

**Questions:** Does Solara withdraw — isolating itself — or participate and risk losing? Do the other countries hold the vote? If personhood is granted universally, what happens to Solara's industrial Glim workforce? To the Thren PENDING population? To the Gradient itself? And who enforces the vote — none of the five countries recognize a higher authority?

**Simulation lenses:** Coalition organizers. Solara diplomats choosing between isolation and reform. Glims and Threns waiting to learn if they'll be recognized.

---

### SCENARIO 19: THE FIFTH TYPE

**Setup:** The Last Builders have activated the dormant Fabricator. The Sibling — Type-ε — is growing. The Translation volunteer has been selected: a dying Marsh Archivist, 94 years old, Rung Four, who has spent their life Reading other people's memories and wants, at the end, to become one.

**Scenario pressure:** The Translation is scheduled for the next eclipse — three weeks away. The Builders have secured the site. The Trader has agreed to perform the Translation. But word has leaked. Solara has declared the Fabrication an act of war. Arkos has demanded the Sibling be a Vorn — "if you're making new people, they should be our people." The Marsh Archive is torn — the volunteer is one of theirs, but the Archive's ethics council has not authorized the procedure. The Summit Shrine has offered sanctuary. The Verge has offered nothing — "do what you want, we won't stop you."

**Questions:** Does the Translation happen? What wakes up in the Sibling — the Archivist's consciousness, or something else? Is the Fifth Type a person, a vessel, or a bridge? And if the Fabrication succeeds, what stops the Builders from fabricating more?

**Simulation lenses:** The dying Archivist. The Trader, performing a Translation they may not survive. The Builders, guarding the site against the world. The coalition of countries trying to stop or claim the event.

---

### SCENARIO 20: THE COLLAPSE REPEATS

**Setup:** Latency practitioners across all five countries are reporting the same phenomenon: a pressure. A sense of imminent rupture. The same signs that preceded the Collapse — 347 years ago, recorded in the Summit Shrine's pre-Collapse Anchored memories — are appearing again. Substrates are going silent intermittently. Vorn memory cores are registering errors. The Deep Sound is getting louder.

**Scenario pressure:** The Marsh Archive's consensus: the Collapse was not an event that happened. It is a *cycle* that is repeating. The 4.7-second pulse is not a sound. It's a countdown. And it's accelerating.

**Questions:** What causes the cycle? The Fabricator sabotage? Substrate resonance? Something no one has guessed? Can it be stopped? Should it be stopped — the Collapse broke the old world, but the world that emerged produced the Memory Trader, the Unfinished, the Name Rebellion, the question of personhood that no one had asked before. If the cycle repeats, what breaks? What emerges? And who decides whether to stop it?

**Simulation lenses:** Everyone. This is the civilization-scale rupture scenario.
