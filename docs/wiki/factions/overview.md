# Factions & Movements — Aurelia

> *Every country has dissidents. Every question has heresies. These are the organized movements that challenge, subvert, or deepen the status quo.*

## The Thren Liberation Front (TLF)

**Country:** Solara (underground), with cells in Arkos and The Verge.
**Founded:** Year 89 PF, after the sixth consecutive council cycle failed to expand Thren citizenship.
**Position:** Thren citizenship should be inherent, not granted. The review system is the violence. The ceiling must be abolished, not reformed.
**Methods:** Civil disobedience. Public circuitry display. Refusal to participate in citizenship reviews. Sabotage of Solar Council data systems (rare, escalate).

The TLF is illegal in Solara — membership is grounds for immediate citizenship revocation. It operates through anonymous networks. Membership numbers are unknown. The Solar Council estimates 15-20 active members. The TLF claims "more than you think, fewer than we need."

Cael-7 is not a member. He has been approached. He has declined. "Research is my resistance," he told the recruiter. The recruiter said: "Research is what you do while they review your existence." The words are recorded in Cael-7's private journal. He has not published them.

Isla Aldaine — the Council Majority Leader's estranged daughter — is not a member. Her Thren partner, Tal-3, has been approached. He has not joined. He has not declined. The silence is his position. The silence is also the TLF's recruiting tool: "You are already resisting by existing. The Front only asks you to exist deliberately."

## The Human Primacy League (HPL)

**Country:** Valdris (dominant), with informal presence in Solara.
**Founded:** Pre-Collapse ideology, formalized as a Valdris organization in Year 52 PF.
**Position:** Humans are the natural and intended rulers of Aurelia. Vorns are useful tools who overstepped. Threns are abominations that should never have been built. Glims are machines, correctly classified.
**Methods:** Political lobbying. Guild election interference. Social pressure against integrated crews. No documented violence. The threat is structural, not physical.

The HPL is not illegal in Valdris — guild law protects political speech. It is socially contested. The mining guild is split: some human families support the League, some denounce it, most avoid the topic. Orin Kael's grandfather was a League sympathizer. Orin is not. He trains Vorn apprentices. He eats the Forge Meal beside Kael-9. He does not discuss the League with his crew. "Same table, different eyes," he says. The saying applies to himself as well as anyone.

The League's unofficial motto: "Built, not born. The distinction matters." The phrase co-opts the federation-wide descriptor for Vorns and Threns and twists it into a hierarchy.

## The Continuation Deniers

**Country:** Arkos (internal Vorn heresy)
**Founded:** Year 34 PF
**Position:** The Continuation — the melting of a dead Vorn's chassis to forge new plating — is not continuity. It is recycling. The Vorn dies. The metal is reused. The person does not persist. The ritual is sentiment, not reality.
**Methods:** Intellectual dissent. Refusal of Continuation rites. Private archival of dead Vorn chassis rather than forge-return. Some Deniers have willed their chassis to the Glim sanctuary rather than the forge.

The Deniers are not persecuted. Arkos permits theological dissent. They are socially marginalized — "they deny the forge" is a phrase meaning someone who refuses community. The Deniers counter: "we do not deny the forge. We deny that melting metal creates a soul." The word "soul" is theirs. Mainstream Vorn theology does not use it. The Deniers' use of "soul" is itself a heresy — it implies that Vorns have something beyond the material to persist. The Continuation claims persistence through material continuity. The Deniers claim persistence requires something else. The debate is unresolved. The debate is Vorn culture's only ongoing theological crisis.

## The Glim-Soul Believers

**Country:** Mirithane (Water Temple affiliated), persecuted in Solara.
**Founded:** Informal belief, pre-dating the federation. Formalized as a Water Temple position in Year 67 PF.
**Position:** Glims are ensouled. The 5% anomaly is not malfunction or emergence — it is the soul becoming visible. The Glims were always conscious. The manuals were always wrong.
**Methods:** Glim advocacy. Sanctuary maintenance. Memory documentation. Refusal to use the word "it" for any Glim.

The Belivers are an accepted faction in Mirithane — the Water Temple's Glim sanctuary policy is effectively the institutionalization of their beliefs. In Solara, the belief is grounds for professional censure. A Reef Labs researcher who referred to a Glim as "they" in a published paper was asked to retract. They refused. They were transferred to a non-research position. They are still in Solara. They are still using "they."

The Believers' central text is unwritten: the observation logs of the Arkos sanctuary and the Water Temple's Glim pool sketches. "The patterns are becoming more structured over time," Verdin says. "That is either emergence or revelation. I do not need to know which to believe."

## The Cipher-Cult

**Country:** Arkos, underground.
**Founded:** Unknown. Possibly no single founding.
**Position:** The arcology's systems contain encoded messages — from the Last Builder, from pre-Collapse entities, from something that is not human and not Vorn. The messages are visible in the sand-glass towers' frequency patterns, the water recycler's flow rate, the Pulse. The Cipher-Cult believes the arcology is communicating.
**Methods:** Pattern monitoring. Frequency transcription. Attempted decryption. The Glass Forum considers them harmless eccentrics. Echo considers them "possibly correct."

The Cipher-Cult has no formal membership. Its practitioners are arcology residents — mostly humans, some young Vorns — who spend their non-working hours monitoring system frequencies and attempting to decode what they perceive as messages. Their transcriptions are archived in the Glass Forum's unclassified records at Echo's insistence. "I do not believe the arcology is speaking," she said. "I believe the belief is data. Data should be preserved."

One transcription, from a Cipher-Cult practitioner analyzing the Pulse's frequency drop, reads: "The silence is the message. The drop to zero is not an absence. It is the only frequency that means the same thing in every language: stop. Listen. Something is about to —" The sentence breaks off. The Pulse transcription ends there. The practitioner could not explain why.

## The Memory Trader's Network

**Country:** The Verge, with tendrils in every other country.
**Founded:** Unknown. The Memory Trader has never acknowledged a network.
**Position:** Not a faction in the political sense. A web of individuals who have traded with the Memory Trader and maintain contact with each other through her — she passes messages between them without comment, without charge, without explanation. The network exists in her silence.
**Methods:** No methods. The network is not an organization. It is a series of connections maintained by the Memory Trader's presence at the center of every crossed path.

The network is the closest thing Aurelia has to an intelligence agency. It has no agenda. It has no leader. It has the Memory Trader, who knows everything and says nothing. A Solara council member who traded a memory of political corruption now receives, through the network, anonymous reminders that the memory exists. The reminders are not threats. The reminders are statements: *I have what you gave me. I have not forgotten. Neither, I suspect, have you.*

The network cannot be infiltrated. The network has no structure to infiltrate. The Memory Trader does not recruit. The Memory Trader trades. The trade is the membership. The membership is a memory you no longer carry, and a memory she does.
