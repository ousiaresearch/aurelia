# CLIMATE & WEATHER OF AURELIA

*What falls from the sky, what the seasons do, and how five countries survive their own weather. Climate is the first fact of any world — everything else responds to it.*

---

## THE FIVE CLIMATE ZONES

The Collapse didn't just break machines. It broke weather. The pre-Collapse atmosphere was engineered — climate-controlled, precipitation-managed. The Collapse ended the engineering. Weather returned to nature. Nature took decades to stabilize.

---

## SOLARA: TEMPERATE CONTINENTAL

**Classification:** Four-season temperate, modified by the Dawn Crater microclimate.
**Latitude:** Mid-latitudes, inland from the western coast.

### Seasonal Pattern

**Lucent (Spring — months 1–3):** Warming, wet. The Crater lake's water temperature lags behind air temperature, creating morning fog banks that blanket the First and Second Rings until mid-morning. The fog is considered auspicious — "the Gradient is veiled; contemplate what is hidden." Farmers in the Fifth Ring plant Solar grain as soon as the soil thaws.

**Aestas (Summer — months 4–6):** Hot, dry. Temperatures reach 35°C in the lower Rings. The First Ring's stone buildings retain coolness; the Fourth Ring's factories amplify heat. Heatstroke is a seasonal hazard in the industrial districts. The Dawn Crater's water level drops 1–2 meters annually — evaporation exceeds inflow in summer. The exposed crater walls are visible from the rim. The Temple does not comment.

**Demi (Autumn — months 7–9):** Cooling, variable. Harvest season. The Fifth Ring's grain harvest determines Solara's food security for the year. A poor harvest means grain rationing in the Fourth Ring. The First Ring is never rationed.

**Obscura (Winter — months 10–11):** Cold, dry. Temperatures drop below freezing at night. Snow is rare in Solara Prime — the Crater's thermal mass moderates temperatures — but common in Grain-Hill and the agricultural interior. The Fifth Ring's growing season ends. Farmers preserve, repair, and wait.

**Intercalaris (The In-Between — month 12):** The transitional month between winter and spring. Unpredictable. Warm days, cold nights, false springs that trigger premature planting. Farmers ignore the false springs. "Intercalaris lies. The grain believes it. We know better."

### Extreme Weather

**Crater Storms:** Thunderstorms that form over the Dawn Crater lake, feeding on the temperature differential between warm crater water and cool rim air. Lightning strikes the crater walls. The storms are violent and brief — 2–3 hours of intense electrical activity, then sudden calm. The Temple considers crater storms manifestations of the Gradient's power. "The light descends. The peak is struck. The root is watered."

**Drought Cycles:** Every 7–12 years, Solara experiences a drought cycle — 2–3 years of reduced rainfall. The Crater lake level drops dangerously. Grain harvests fail. The Temple declares a "Season of Contemplation" — fasting, reduced services, increased Extraction work (memories of the drought are removed from public spaces to prevent panic). The drought ends. The memories don't return.

## ARKOS: SEMI-ARID INDUSTRIAL

**Classification:** Semi-arid, modified by Assembly Plant residual heat.
**Latitude:** Mid-latitudes, inland, east of Solara.

### Seasonal Pattern

Arkos has two seasons: Hot and Less Hot. The Assembly Plant's residual thermal output creates a permanent microclimate — the land within 50 km of the plant is 3–5°C warmer than surrounding terrain, year-round. Seasonal variation is muted.

**Hot (months 3–9):** Daytime temperatures 35–42°C. Nighttime 20–25°C. The Assembly Plant's thermal output is most noticeable in summer — the plant itself is dangerous to approach without protective gear. Vorns are unaffected. Humans and Threns limit outdoor activity to morning and evening hours.

**Less Hot (months 10–2):** Daytime 20–28°C. Nighttime 5–12°C. The most comfortable season. Arkos' human and Thren population schedules outdoor work for these months.

### Extreme Weather

**Dust Storms:** Seasonal winds sweeping across the semi-arid terrain. Visibility drops to meters. The storms carry mineral dust from the Assembly Fringe — trace Fabricator byproducts. Prolonged exposure is a respiratory hazard for humans and Threns. Vorn joints require cleaning after dust storms. The storms are predictable — Arkos residents seal buildings and wait.

**Thermal Inversions:** Periodic events where the Assembly Plant's heat output is trapped at ground level by an atmospheric inversion layer. Temperatures spike. The inversions last 2–5 days. During inversions, Arkos Central's public spaces are cooled by geothermal heat-exchange systems. The poor — those in outer districts — endure. "The Plant gives heat. Sometimes too much. We were built to take it."

### Rainfall

Minimal — 200–300 mm annually, concentrated in the Less Hot months. Agriculture depends on irrigation from groundwater and the Assembly Plant's closed-loop water system. Arkos is perpetually water-conscious. Water waste is a social offense.

## MIRITHANE: HUMID SUBTROPICAL MARSH

**Classification:** Humid subtropical, modified by extensive wetlands.
**Latitude:** Coastal, southern latitudes.

### Seasonal Pattern

**Flood Season (months 3–5):** Spring rains combine with snowmelt from northern highlands. The marsh expands — water levels rise 1–3 meters. Reed-Mouth's floating platforms rise. Spore-Hollow becomes accessible only by boat. The Archive's lower colony halls are sealed. "The marsh takes what it needs. We adapt."

**Growing Season (months 6–9):** Warm, humid, abundant. Marsh-rice paddies are at full production. Glass-fin spawning season — the coastal waters teem with juvenile glass-fin. Harvest is restricted during spawning. The Archive monitors population levels.

**Harvest Season (months 10–11):** Cooler, drier. Marsh-rice harvest. The marsh water levels recede. Land previously submerged reappears — enriched by flood-deposited silt. Farmers plant winter cover crops.

**Still Season (months 12–2):** Mild, damp. Minimal temperature variation — Mirithane's coastal location moderates winter. The marsh is quiet. The Archive schedules intensive research during the Still Season. "The marsh rests. We work."

### Extreme Weather

**Marsh Surge:** Seasonal flooding amplified by coastal storms. Water levels rise 5+ meters in severe surges. The floating settlements are designed to survive them — anchored platforms that rise with water, never submerged. Severe surges have never breached Archive-Central's upper levels. The lower levels — where the oldest colonies are kept — are flood-proofed with multiple seal layers. The Archive has contingency plans for colony evacuation. The plans have never been used.

**Fog-Season:** Late autumn through early winter. Dense fog banks form over the marsh as warm water meets cool air. Visibility drops to arm's length. Travel by skiff is dangerous — navigational markers are unreliable in fog. Mirithane residents navigate by latency: the bacterial colonies emit a faint latency signature that trained Archivists can detect. "The colonies guide us. The marsh hides. The colonies reveal."

## VALDRIS: ALPINE VERTICAL

**Classification:** Mountain alpine, extreme vertical variation.
**Latitude:** Northern highlands.

### Seasonal Pattern

Temperature in Valdris is a function of altitude, not season. But seasons still matter.

**Pass-Open (months 5–9):** The high passes are navigable. Summit-Hold's surface entrances are accessible without cold-weather gear. Trade with Arkos and The Verge resumes. The short growing season begins — mountain barley, terrace vegetables, high-altitude herbs. "The mountain opens. We work."

**Pass-Close (months 10–4):** The high passes are closed — snow, ice, avalanche risk. Valdris is isolated except for the deep-shaft trade routes that connect internally and to Arkos through lower passes. The growing season ends. The mountain communities retreat downward — deeper shafts are warmer (geothermal). Surface structures are largely abandoned in winter.

### Extreme Weather

**Summit Storms:** Blizzards at altitude. Wind speeds exceed 120 km/h. Temperatures drop to -40°C at the summit. Summit-Hold is protected — the mountain is the shelter. Surface structures are designed to be buried in snow; entrances are at shaft level, not surface level. A Valdris winter is not survived by fighting the cold. It is survived by retreating from it.

**Avalanches:** Seasonal hazard on the high passes. The Shrine's Anchors predict avalanche conditions by Reading the mountain's latency — the snowpack carries memories of previous seasons' stability. "The mountain remembers every avalanche. We ask the mountain if another is coming."

**Deep Warmth:** Paradoxically, the deep shafts are warm year-round — geothermal heat from the mountain's core. The deepest inhabited levels (Deep Gate, 2.2 km) maintain 18–22°C regardless of surface conditions. Shaft communities at depth experience a mild, constant climate. Surface dwellers visit the deep shafts in winter for warmth.

## THE VERGE: DEGRADED VARIABLE

**Classification:** Variable, disrupted by pre-Collapse infrastructure decay.
**Latitude:** Spread across multiple zones (The Verge borders all four other countries).

The Verge has no single climate. It spans Solara's temperate edge, Arkos' semi-arid fringe, and Mirithane's humid coastal margin. What unifies The Verge's climate is *ruin*. Pre-Collapse structures — vast empty buildings, shattered road networks, decaying industrial sites — create microclimates. Concrete retains heat. Debris fields channel wind. Underground structures flood and breed insects.

### Seasonal Pattern

The Verge experiences whatever the adjacent country experiences, degraded. A storm in Solara reaches The Verge a day later, unfiltered by maintained infrastructure. A dust storm from Arkos blows through The Verge without warning. A marsh fog from Mirithane settles in The Verge's coastal ruins and stays for days.

The Verge has no agricultural calendar. People survive the seasons as they come. Winter is the hardest — exposure kills more Verge residents than violence or disease.

## THE DEEP SOUND & CLIMATE

The Deep Sound — the 4.7-second rhythmic pulse detected at depth in Valdris shafts, Mirithane ocean trenches, and the Dawn Crater lake — has no confirmed relationship to climate. But practitioners report:

- The Sound is louder during Obscura (winter) and Diminished during Aestas (summer).
- The Sound's pulse rate has been slowly increasing — 4.7 seconds between peaks in 347 AC, down from 4.9 seconds a century ago.
- Latency-bloom flowers (which bloom only near strong latency fields) open more frequently in winter — the Sound's increased intensity may be correlated.
- The Marsh Archive's working hypothesis: the Deep Sound is substrate resonance — accumulated latency from everything that has ever happened, compressed by depth. Seasonal variation may reflect the annual cycle of memory Writing and Extraction across Aurelia. "The world remembers more in winter. We stay inside. We reflect. The Sound reflects with us."

## CLIMATE CHANGE (POST-COLLAPSE)

The Collapse ended pre-Collapse climate engineering. The atmosphere has been self-regulating for 347 years. Trends:

- **Warming:** Average temperatures have risen ~1.5°C since the Collapse. The Assembly Plant's residual heat may be a contributing factor. Arkos is noticeably warmer than historical records suggest it should be.
- **Desertification:** The semi-arid zone around Arkos is expanding slowly — approximately 1–2 km per decade. Arkos' agricultural fringe is shrinking. The Assembly of Cores has commissioned a study. The study has not been completed.
- **Marsh Expansion:** Mirithane's marsh has expanded by approximately 15% since the Collapse. Rising water levels, possibly driven by post-Collapse hydrological changes. The expansion has been beneficial for Mirithane — more marsh, more rice paddies, more spore-drifter habitat.
- **Glacial Retreat:** Valdris' summit glaciers are retreating. The Shrine has recorded the retreat in Anchored obsidian. "The mountain is losing its crown. The mountain doesn't complain. We notice."

## WEATHER LORE

**"Red dawn, Gradient frown."** — Solara saying. A red sunrise means a crater storm is coming. The Temple's morning services are abbreviated. "The light is angry. We keep our heads down."

**"The Plant sweats, we drink."** — Arkos saying. Morning condensation on the Assembly Plant's cooling towers is collected for drinking water in outer districts during dry spells.

**"Fog carries memory."** — Mirithane saying. Marsh fog is believed to carry trace latency from the bacterial colonies it passes over. Archivists claim they can "Read the fog." Non-Archivists claim Archivists are dramatic.

**"The mountain's breath is cold."** — Valdris saying. A sudden temperature drop in the deep shafts means a summit storm is coming. The mountain "exhales" cold air before a storm. This is meteorologically real — pressure changes drive cold air down the shafts.

**"The sky doesn't remember."** — The Verge saying. Unlike latency-saturated surfaces, the sky is blank. Nothing that happens under open sky leaves a memory trace. This is comforting to some Verge residents. It is terrifying to others.
