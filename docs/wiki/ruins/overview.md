# Ruins & Exploration Sites — Aurelia

> *The Age of Making left its bones across the landscape. Some are inhabited. Some are sealed. Some have never been entered.*

## Solara — The Sunken City

**Location:** Coastal shelf, 2 km offshore from Reef Labs. **Depth:** 15–30 meters.
**Status:** Partially explored. Reef Labs has documented the upper structures. The lower levels are inaccessible without advanced diving equipment.

A pre-Collapse coastal settlement, submerged during the Collapse by sea-level rise or seismic subsidence. The upper structures are coral-encrusted — the reef has colonized the ruins. The lower levels are intact, sealed, dark. Reef Labs divers report windows that still hold glass, doors that are still closed, rooms that have not been entered since the water rose.

What might be inside: pre-Collapse records, intact technology, the remains of people who did not evacuate. The Reef Labs has limited exploration to non-invasive survey. "The reef has claimed the city," Cael-7 wrote in a research proposal. "Disturbing it would disturb the reef. The research question is whether the preservation is worth more than the discovery." The proposal was denied — not on scientific grounds, on funding. The denial's subtext: some things are better left sealed.

## Arkos — The Restricted Levels

**Location:** Lower arcology, below the Glim sanctuary. **Status:** Sealed by Glass Forum order.
**Access:** Echo holds the access codes. She has never used them.

The arcology extends deeper than the inhabited levels. Pre-Collapse schematics reference a "Foundation Sector" — purpose unspecified. The sector was sealed during the Collapse and has never been reopened. The Glass Forum's official position: structural instability. The Cipher-Cult's position: the sealed levels contain the arcology's true purpose.

The Last Builder's schematic notes for the Foundation Sector read: "Primary function: [REDACTED]. Secondary function: [REDACTED]. Access: Authorized personnel only. Authorization: [REDACTED]." The redactions are in the Builder's own notation. She sealed the sector herself and then redacted her own records of what she sealed. "The Last Builder was afraid of something," Echo told the Forum. "She did not tell us what. She told us to stay above. We have stayed above."

## Valdris — Section 4

**Location:** Deep mines, 1,200 meters. **Status:** Sealed since the Spore Winter (late Age of Making).

The deepest known section of the Valdris mines. Pre-Collapse bore, extended by unknown hands. Sealed after a fungal pathogen — the Spore Winter — contaminated the grain stores and spread into the shaft. The seal is a collapsed section of tunnel, deliberately triggered, never cleared.

Beneath the seal: unknown. The pre-Collapse mining records suggest Section 4 was a primary extraction zone — rare minerals, geothermal vents, something worth digging 1,200 meters for. The glyph carved at the entrance — circle, triangle, line — predates the seal. The glyph was carved by pre-Collapse miners. It was not carved to warn of the spores. It was carved to warn of something else.

Night-shift miners report sounds from behind the seal: rhythmic impacts, metallic scraping, a low hum at 5/4 time. The sounds have been reported for 120+ years. The guild attributes them to seismic settling. The mountain moving. The mountain deciding.

## Mirithane — The Deep Temple

**Location:** Deep marsh, beneath the Water Temple platform. **Status:** Submerged, partially accessible.

Pre-Collapse structure. Possibly a filtration control center. Possibly a temple. The Water Temple's platform was built above it deliberately. "We did not build the temple," the senior keeper says. "We found the foundations and built a platform on top. The original builders are unknown. The original purpose is unknown. The marsh has accepted the structure. We accept the marsh."

The Deep Temple's lower chambers are flooded. Keepers dive them intermittently. The walls are covered in notation — the same shorthand as the Builder's notation in Arkos. Not identical. Related. The builders of the Deep Temple and the builders of the arcology shared a language.

One chamber, the deepest accessible, contains a single object: a crystal, larger than any latency crystal known — approximately 30 cm in diameter, dark, inert. The crystal has never been touched. The keepers do not touch it. "It is waiting," the senior keeper says. "We do not know for what. We wait with it."

## The Verge — The Canyon Sanctuary

**Location:** Hidden, coordinates passed by word of mouth. **Status:** Active, inhabited.
**Access:** "The entrance is a rumor you earn the right to know."

The Thren sanctuary in the canyons beyond the crossroads. Founded by Thren refugees who refused every country's answer to the question. Population unknown. Governance unknown. The sanctuary trades with the crossroads occasionally — food, water, the rare Thren who decides to leave, the rarer Thren who decides to arrive.

The canyon's walls are covered in paintings — Thren temple circuitry, enlarged, repeated, transformed into art. "They paint what they were given," a sanctuary resident told the Memory Trader. She did not record the conversation. She has never traded a memory of the sanctuary. The sanctuary's privacy is the only privacy she protects.

## Unknown — The Second Arcology

**Status:** Rumored. Unconfirmed.

Pre-Collapse records reference a "sister site" to the Arkos arcology — a second self-contained city, located somewhere in the unmapped territory beyond Valdris's eastern range. The reference is fragmentary: a single line in the Glass Forum archives, in the Last Builder's notation: "Site B: foundations complete. Awaiting population. Status: [REDACTED]."

Whether Site B was completed, inhabited, or destroyed is unknown. Whether it exists is unknown. The Glass Forum has never mounted an expedition. "The arcology was built for us," Echo said. "If there is a second arcology, it was built for someone else. We will not disturb what was not meant for us."
