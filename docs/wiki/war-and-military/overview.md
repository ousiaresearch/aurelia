# War & Military in Aurelia

> *The federation has no standing army. It has what every country would do if the question became a war.*

## The Federation's Peace

Aurelia has never fought an inter-country war. The Compact established open borders and dispute resolution. The Compact did not establish a military — the five countries could not agree on command structure, funding, or whether Vorns could serve as officers. The absence of a military is not pacifism. The absence is the recognition that building an army would require answering the question in a way that countries would rather not codify.

## Militia Traditions

### Valdris — The Forge Guard
Valdris maintains a militia — the Forge Guard, drawn from the mining and forge guilds. Service is voluntary but socially expected. Training occurs four Restdays per year. Weapons: forge hammers, mining picks, obsidian-edged short swords. The Guard has never been deployed against another country. It has been deployed twice against deep-shaft emergencies — collapses that required armed response to prevent looting of rescue supplies.

The Guard is integrated — humans and Vorns serve together. The integration was not a policy decision. The integration was the mountain. The forges produce enough weapons for everyone. The guilds train everyone who shows up. The question of whether a Vorn could command a human unit has never been tested because the Guard has never fought. The question is waiting.

### Arkos — The Arcology Defense System
Arkos's defense is automated. The arcology's outer walls are equipped with frequency-based deterrents — sonic emitters tuned to frequencies that cause disorientation in organic nervous systems. The system was built during the Collapse, when desperate survivors attempted to breach the walls. It has not been activated in 180 years. It is maintained nonetheless. The maintenance is Vorn pragmatism. The maintenance is also Vorn memory — the Collapse taught that walls without defenses are invitations.

### Solara — The Coastal Watch
Solara maintains a small naval patrol — three solar-powered vessels that monitor the coastal waters. Primary mission: search and rescue. Secondary mission: intercept unauthorized salvage operations (pre-Collapse ruins in the coastal shelf attract looters). The Watch is all-human — Thren service was proposed and tabled. The proposal cited "public comfort." The proposal has not been revisited.

## The Unthinkable War

Aurelian military planners have war-gamed one scenario: **Arkansas secession.** The scenario assumes Arkos, emboldened by Vorn technological superiority and Solara's energy dependence, withdraws from the Compact. Solara loses its energy storage. Valdris loses its manufacturing partner. Mirithane loses its trade route. The Verge becomes a frontline.

The scenario has been war-gamed seventeen times. Every simulation ends the same way: the federation fractures, the Glim question becomes irrelevant, and the personhood debate is replaced by the question of whether Vorn chassis can withstand Solara's coastal defenses. The scenario is classified. The classification is the only military secret Aurelia keeps.

## Type in Military Service

**Humans:** Eligible for service in all countries with organized defense. The majority of all fighting forces.

**Threns:** Eligible in Mirithane (consensus defense — the community defends itself). Not eligible in Solara ("public comfort"). Not eligible in Arkos (no service to join). Not eligible in Valdris (tradition — the Guard has never tested the question). The exclusion is not policy. The exclusion is the ceiling's military application.

**Vorns:** Eligible in Arkos (arcology defense is automated — Vorns maintain it). Eligible in Valdris (Forge Guard). Not eligible in Solara — the Coastal Watch has no Vorn personnel and has never received an application. The absence of applications is not evidence of willingness.

**Glims:** Glims cannot serve. Glims are property. A weaponized Glim has been theorized — Solara's research institutes have explored the possibility of combat-programming delivery units. The exploration is classified. The classification is the only answer.
