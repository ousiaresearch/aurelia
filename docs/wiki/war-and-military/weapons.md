# Weapons of Aurelia

> *Every country has tools of violence. Most have never used them. The tools exist. The question of who may wield them is the military's version of the question.*

## Solara — Precision Instruments

Solara's Coastal Watch carries: solar-charged stun batons (non-lethal, designed for law enforcement, not war), reef-glass knives (obsidian-equivalent from fused coastal sand, sharp enough for surgical work), and crossbows (salvaged pre-Collapse design, manufactured by the council armory). The arsenal is small, maintained, and never deployed against another country.

## Arkos — Frequency Weapons

Arkos's defense is automated. The arcology's outer walls emit disorientation frequencies — sonic pulses that disrupt organic nervous systems. The system is non-lethal. The system is designed to incapacitate, not kill. The system was built during the Collapse when desperate survivors attempted to breach the walls. It has not been activated since.

## Valdris — The Forge Arsenal

Valdris forges produce: war hammers (forge hammers with extended hafts, designed to crush Vorn chassis), obsidian-edged short swords (the obsidian is sharper than steel; brittle, but devastating on impact), and mining charges (explosive compounds used for shaft-boring, weaponizable with modification). The Forge Guard trains with these weapons. The Guard has never fought.

## The Verge — Whatever Works

Verge residents carry what they can: salvaged tools, repurposed industrial equipment, a Glim gripper arm used as a club. The Memory Trader carries nothing visible. She has never demonstrated a weapon. She has never needed to.

## The Legal Void

The Compact does not regulate weapons. Each country maintains its own arsenal. There is no federation weapons treaty. The absence of regulation is not oversight — regulating weapons would mean regulating who may carry them, which would mean defining who counts as a legitimate combatant, which would mean answering the question in military terms. No country is ready to do that.
