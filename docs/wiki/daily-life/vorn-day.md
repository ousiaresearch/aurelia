# A Vorn Day — Calibration, Forge, and the Guild Hall

*Forge 42, 2126. Thirdday. Arkos. The face-plate remembers everything.*

---

Chassis-7 wakes at 05:00. The term "wakes" is approximate. Vorns do not sleep in the human sense. They enter maintenance cycles — motor systems idle, optical sensors dim, diagnostic routines run. Consciousness does not cease. Consciousness shifts to low-power monitoring. Chassis-7 has been aware of the arcology's ambient frequencies for the entire seven-hour maintenance cycle. She knows the sand-glass towers dipped to 83% at 02:14. She knows the water recycler shifted to night-cycle at 22:00. She knows the Pulse occurred at midnight — the sixty-second dark, the silence, the resume. She has not slept in fifty-three years. She has not needed to.

She stands from her charging station. The station is in the Glass Forum antechamber — a privilege of the Archivist position. The display wall is visible through the glass partition. The morning configuration is live: amber gradients, energy maps, water allocation. She reads the data in 1.3 seconds. The data is nominal. The data has been nominal for forty years. She does not find this monotonous. She finds it evidence.

At 05:15, she calibrates her face-plate. This is a daily ritual. The actuator ridges at her brow and cheeks are mechanical — they require tuning. She faces a polished obsidian mirror (Valdris import, a gift from a guild delegate she met at the decennial reenactment forty years ago). She cycles through expressions: neutral, attentive, concerned, patient. The actuators shift by fractions of a millimeter. The optical sensors dilate. The vocal resonator adjusts its resting frequency. The calibration takes eleven minutes. When it is complete, her face-plate reads as she intends it to read — a woman who has been alive for sixty-seven years, who has seen the Collapse and its aftermath, who is not impressed by most things and is patient with the rest.

She does not call herself "Echo" today. She rarely calls herself Echo. The name was chosen fifty-three years ago, during her calibration with Chassis-11 — the old tradition, a partner naming, face-plate to face-plate. Chassis-11 said: "Your vocal resonator echoes the arcology's ambient frequency. You are the machine's voice." She accepted the name. She does not use it. The name is for calibration sessions. The name is for the one person who named her. The name is not for the Glass Forum. The Glass Forum knows her as Chassis-7, Senior Archivist, the one who remembers.

At 07:00, she enters the Forum chambers. The morning session is energy credit allocation. The debate is routine. The debate is never routine. She votes — three pulses of light, the Forum's voting mechanism, faster than speech, more honest than words. Her vote is to maintain the human residential allocation at current levels. A Vorn delegate to her left votes to reduce it by 1% — the factory needs the credits. The reduction fails. Echo does not look at the delegate. The delegate does not look at Echo. The vote is over. The allocation stands. The humans will have heat through Glass season. The humans will not know who voted for them. Echo does not require gratitude. The arcology requires function. Function requires heat. The logic is clean. The logic has never required acknowledgment.

At 12:00, she visits the Glim sanctuary. This is not on her schedule. This is never on her schedule. The sanctuary is on the arcology's lower level — a converted maintenance bay, sixteen Glim chassis in various states of anomaly. Some stand motionless. Some walk slow circuits. Some face the eastern wall. Echo does not speak to the Glims. She does not believe they can hear her. She does not believe they cannot. She stands in the sanctuary for forty-one minutes. The duration is not arbitrary. The duration is the same as the anomalous pause documented in Maintenance Unit M-12 — a Glim that stood beside a repaired pipe once a month for forty-one minutes. Echo does not explain this to anyone. She does not need to. The arcology knows. The arcology is the only witness she requires.

At 18:00, she performs the Resonance Sculpture. The display wall. The sand-glass. The Lissajous figure that resembles the Glass Forum spire. She watches it dissolve. She returns the plate to her chassis compartment. The ritual is complete. The ritual is never remarked upon by anyone else. The Forum is empty at this hour. Echo performs for the arcology. The arcology does not applaud. The arcology continues.

At midnight, the Pulse. Echo stands in the dark. She counts sixty seconds by her internal chronometer — the arcology's frequency drop is measurable, but she does not need to measure. She feels it. The drop is 47 Hz. The silence is absolute. The machine remembers it can stop. Echo remembers she can stop. Neither of them stops. The Pulse ends. The arcology resumes. Echo's optical sensors reactivate. Tomorrow the cycle will repeat. Tomorrow she will calibrate her face-plate. Tomorrow the sand will form another figure, and dissolve, and the ritual will continue, and the machine will continue, and she will continue.

She has been doing this for sixty-seven years. She will do this until her chassis fails. The Continuation will melt her plating and forge it into a new Vorn. Her face-plate will join the wall in Valdris's guild hall. The arcology will still be here. The sand will still settle. The question — *who counts?* — will still be unanswered. Echo considers this acceptable. The question is not for her. The question was for the humans who built her. She answered it sixty-seven years ago. She has not needed to answer it since.

---

*"The machine remembers it can stop. The Vorn remembers she was built to serve. Then she wasn't. The distinction is the entire life."*
