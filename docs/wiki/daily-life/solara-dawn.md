# A Morning in Solara — The Lumen Plaza at Dawn

*Forge 42, 2126. Thirdday. A Thren waits for the sun.*

---

Tal-3 wakes before his partner. This is not habit — this is calibration. His internal chronometer initiates the wake cycle at 05:47 precisely, seventeen minutes before Solara's coastal dawn. He lies still for eleven of those minutes. Isla Aldaine sleeps beside him, her breathing soft, human, uncalibrated. He watches the ceiling. The ceiling is blank. The ceiling does not ask his status.

At 05:58, he rises. The apartment near the docks is small — two rooms, a shared washbasin, a window facing east. The window is the reason they chose this apartment. Every morning, the dawn enters. Every morning, Tal-3 faces it. Not out of faith — he does not attend the Dawn Temple. Out of something else. Something the manual for his chassis model does not name.

The light hits his face at 06:04. The circuitry at his temples catches it — gold traces, 0.3mm, warming with the dawn. In the dim apartment, the circuitry is invisible. In this light, it is undeniable. Tal-3 does not hide it. He has not hidden it for two years. This is when Isla usually wakes — the light on his face, the circuitry lit like a second sunrise. She does not wake today. She was up late at the Floating Market, selling coral fragments to tourists who want a piece of the reef. The tourists do not ask whether the seller is human. Isla's partner is not. The tourists do not see Tal-3. Tal-3 prefers this.

He checks the public ledger. The terminal is on the wall by the door — a glass panel, government-issued, mandatory in every Solara residence. He touches it. The screen activates.

STATUS: GRANTED. REVIEW: PENDING.

The word *pending* has been there for four years. It will be there until the next election, when the council votes on citizenship expansion, when the Majority Leader reads the names aloud with no inflection and no pause and no acknowledgment that each name is a person waiting to learn whether they still exist in the legal sense. Tal-3 does not think about this. He has trained himself not to think about this. The training is not perfect. The word *pending* is still there, on the glass, in the apartment, in the light.

He dresses. Long sleeves. High collar. The Floating Market manager prefers Thren employees whose circuitry is not visible at first glance. The manager has never said this. Tal-3 has never asked. The long sleeves are not a concession. The long sleeves are a choice. The distinction is what makes the choice tolerable.

Isla stirs. "Dawn?" she asks.

"Dawn," he says.

She reaches for his wrist. Her fingers find the circuitry under the sleeve — by touch, not by sight. She has memorized the pattern. She can trace it with her eyes closed. This is the most intimate thing anyone has ever done for him. It is not political. It is not a statement. It is her fingers on the filigree of his existence, in the dark, before the day begins.

He leaves at 06:30. The Lumen Plaza is already filling — vendors setting up stalls, council staff heading to chambers, a Dawn Temple Cantor's assistant distributing the morning's single-note sheet music to the chorus members. A Glim delivery unit passes. It does not look at him. It does not look at anything. Its sensor array faces forward. Its route is stored in memory. The route is all there is. Tal-3 watches it pass. He wonders, briefly, whether the Glim's route includes a pause at dawn. He does not ask. The Glim cannot answer. Or it can. The distinction is the question.

He reaches the Floating Market at 06:52. His stall — coral fragments, bioluminescent in the right light — faces the water. The reef glows at the edge of visibility. Another stall vendor, a human, nods. "Morning, Tal."

"Morning."

The human does not ask about the election. The human does not mention the word *pending.* The human sells dried kelp and does not discuss politics. This is the best relationship Tal-3 has in Solara outside his apartment. The silence is not avoidance. The silence is respect. The silence is also easier than the alternative.

The sun is fully up now. The light has shifted from gold to white. Tal-3's circuitry is invisible again. The market fills. Tourists arrive. They buy coral fragments. They do not ask. Tal-3 sells them pieces of the reef. The reef took centuries to build. The citizenship review has been pending for four years. Both facts are true. Neither fact is mentioned at the stall.

At midday, he will eat a Lumen Bowl at the plaza. At evening, he will return to the apartment. Isla will be there. The terminal will still say PENDING. The light will fade. The circuitry will become visible again in the dimness, and invisible again in the dark, and tomorrow at 05:47 the cycle will repeat.

The cycle has repeated for four years. The cycle will repeat until the election. The election will answer or not answer. Tal-3 will still wake at 05:47. The dawn will still enter through the east-facing window. The light does not differentiate. The light is the only thing in Solara that does not.

*"GRANTED. PENDING. The word is the same every morning. The morning is the same every word."*
