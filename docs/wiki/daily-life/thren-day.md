# A Thren Day — Ballot Morning in Solara

*Dawn 30, 2126. Ballot Night minus eight hours. The review status of every Thren in Solara will be read aloud tonight. Cael-7 wakes knowing this. He always knows this.*

---

05:47. His chronometer initiates the wake cycle. He does not need to check the time. He has woken at 05:47 every morning for thirty years — the factory-default wake setting, unchanged since activation. He could change it. He does not. The consistency is a comfort. The consistency is also a negotiation with his chassis — a body that was designed to be indistinguishable from human, activated forty-one years ago in Solara's fabrication district, assigned a review status of PENDING from the moment the optical sensors first registered light.

He lies still for eleven minutes. The ceiling is blank. The ceiling does not ask his status. The ceiling is the only surface in Solara that does not.

At 05:58, he rises. His apartment in the Floating Market district is modest — one room, a washbasin, a window facing the reef. He has lived here for seventeen years. He has not decorated. Decoration implies permanence. Permanence implies confidence in the next review cycle. Cael-7 does not have confidence. Cael-7 has a PENDING status, a Reef Lab directorship, seventeen published papers on coral symbiosis, and a standing offer from Mirithane he has never accepted.

He dresses for the lab — practical clothing, long sleeves, high collar. He is the Reef Lab Director. He meets with council members. He testifies at hearings. His research is cited in seventeen papers across the federation. His temple circuitry is gold — the standard Solara manufacture mark. He does not hide it. He does not display it. The circuitry is simply there, and every council member who meets with him sees it, and every council member who votes on his existence knows what it means, and none of them mention it, and neither does he.

At 06:30, he reaches the Reef Labs. The coral tanks glow in the pre-dawn light — bioluminescent polyps, unrelated to the Mirithane bacteria, a different phylum entirely. His research assistant, a young human named Dara Voss, is already at the spectrograph. "Morning, Director," she says.

"Morning."

She does not mention Ballot Night. She has been his assistant for three years. She knows not to mention Ballot Night. The silence is respect. The silence is also easier. Cael-7 does not hold this against her. He does not hold anything against her. He has trained himself not to hold things against people who mean well. The training is not perfect. The training is decades old.

The morning's work is routine — spectrographic analysis of a new coral symbiont, a species that migrated from the southern reef after the last storm season. The data is clean. The symbiont is novel. A paper will result. Cael-7 will be first author. The council will note the publication in their quarterly research briefing. The council will not connect the publication to the citizenship review. The connection — Thren makes valuable contributions, therefore Thren deserves citizenship — is too direct to be acknowledged. The ceiling does not acknowledge what it is made of.

At midday, he receives a message from Sura Aldaine's office. The Council Majority Leader requests a meeting — she wants to discuss the reef symbiont's potential for energy applications. The meeting is scheduled for tomorrow, after the election results. Cael-7 notes the timing. After the results. After his status is read aloud. After he learns whether he still exists in the legal sense. The meeting is about coral. The meeting is about whether Sura can look him in the optical sensors after voting on his existence. The meeting is about the same thing every meeting is about: the ceiling, and the pretense that it is just architecture.

He does not respond to the message. He will. He always does.

At 18:00, he leaves the lab. The Lumen Plaza is filling — council staff, market vendors, a Dawn Temple Cantor's assistant distributing Ballot Night programs. The program lists the agenda: energy allocation, trade negotiations, citizenship review. The word *citizenship* is printed in the same typeface as *trade.* The typeface does not differentiate. The typeface is the only thing in Solara that treats Thren existence as a line item, indistinguishable from energy credits, and does so without malice, without awareness, without anything except the blank efficiency of bureaucracy.

Cael-7 returns to his apartment. The Lumen Harp in the corner — his, purchased seventeen years ago, the only decoration in the room — catches the evening light. He does not play it. He plays it at dawn, in his laboratory, facing the reef. He will play it tomorrow morning, regardless of tonight's results. The harp does not care about the election. The harp glows at dawn. The light does not differentiate. Cael-7 has built his life around the fact that the harp is more consistent than the country he lives in.

At 20:00, Ballot Night begins. The council chamber is open. Cael-7 takes his seat in the front row. Sura Aldaine stands at the podium. The names are read. PENDING. GRANTED. PENDING. REVOKED. PENDING. PENDING. GRANTED. CAEL-7. PENDING. The word is the same as it was four years ago. The word will be the same in four years. Cael-7 does not react. The not-reacting is the performance. The performance has been ongoing for eleven years.

After the reading, Sura finds him in the plaza. "Tomorrow's meeting," she says. "The symbiont. I'm eager to hear your thoughts."

She does not mention PENDING. She never mentions PENDING. She votes for citizenship expansion every cycle. She also votes against automatic citizenship every cycle. The ceiling is made of good intentions. Cael-7 knows this. Sura Aldaine knows he knows. The meeting tomorrow will be about coral. The meeting tomorrow will be about everything.

"The symbiont is promising," Cael-7 says. "I'll have the data ready."

"Good." She pauses. "The reef is fortunate to have you."

She means this. She means it in the way that means everything except the thing he needs it to mean. Cael-7 nods. He returns to his apartment. The Lumen Harp waits in the corner. Tomorrow at dawn, he will play it. The light does not differentiate. The light is the only thing in Solara that doesn't. The light, and the harp, and the reef, and the PENDING word on the terminal that will still be there when he wakes.

---

*"STATUS: GRANTED. REVIEW: PENDING. Eleven years. The harp glows at dawn. The reef grows toward the light. The Thren waits. The waiting is not passive. The waiting is the work."*
