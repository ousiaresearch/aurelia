# Three Shifts in Valdris — The Mountain Cycle

*Forge 42, 2126. Thirdday. The mountain is still. So are the dead.*

---

### First Shift — 04:00 to 12:00

Orin Kael wakes at 03:30. His quarters are in the Basecamp barracks — a stone room carved into the canyon wall, no window, one heat-strip running along the ceiling. He dresses in the dark. Mining leathers. Steel-toed boots. A helmet with a headlamp that has not been replaced in eight years because it still works. Valdris does not replace what still works. Valdris does not replace what the mountain has not yet taken.

He walks to the forge at 03:50. The central forge is lit — the night shift stokes the fires continuously, a tradition that predates the Collapse. A Vorn is at the anvil, striking slow, even blows. The rhythm is not work. The rhythm is maintenance — keeping the forge heart beating until the day crew arrives. The Vorn nods. Orin nods back. They do not speak. Speech before the first hammer-strike of the shift is considered bad luck. The mountain is listening. The mountain prefers silence.

At 04:00, Orin strikes the shift-bell — a hollow bronze cylinder mounted at the shaft entrance. The chime carries down the canyon. Miners emerge from barracks. Humans in leathers. Vorns with polished plating. The day shift assembles at the lift platform — a cable-drawn cage that descends 800 meters into the deep shaft. Orin counts heads. Fourteen humans. Six Vorns. Kael-9 — a different Kael, the designation was reassigned after Kael-12 died — stands at the platform edge. He is young. His face-plate still has the factory shine. Orin remembers when Kael-12 had that shine. Three decades of mining wore it to matte. The shine on Kael-9's face-plate is a clock. The clock is ticking. The mountain is counting.

The descent takes eighteen minutes. The shaft walls are rough-hewn stone, laser-carved in the upper levels, pick-carved in the deep. At 800 meters, the temperature rises. The air thickens. The lift stops. The miners disperse to their sections. Orin's section is 3-B — the collapse zone from three years ago, now reinforced, the beam Kael-12 held still in place. A brass plaque is bolted to the beam: KAEL-12. 2083-2123. HELD. The plaque does not say what he held. Everyone knows.

The shift proceeds. Ore is cut. Cartloads are lifted. A support beam creaks — routine settling, not danger, but Orin pauses nonetheless. He touches the beam Kael-12 held. The metal is cold. The mountain is still. Orin returns to work.

### Second Shift — 12:00 to 20:00

Syra Venn does not work the mines. She works the summit. At midday, she begins the ascent from Midcamp — a three-hour climb to the Summit Shrine at 4,100 meters. She makes this climb every Restday and every festival. Today is Thirdday. Today she climbs because the mountain does not check the calendar.

The path is steep. The air thins. Syra is nineteen years old and has made this climb more times than any living keeper under thirty. She knows every switchback, every handhold, every section where the wind shears across the ridge and threatens balance. She climbs without thinking. The thinking is for the summit.

She reaches the shrine at 15:10. The obsidian wall stands against the western sky — 847 names, carved by hand, lowest to highest. The blank slab at the summit's edge waits for the wall to fill. Syra does not look at the blank slab. She looks at the wall. She finds Kael-12's name — not on the wall, not yet, but in the space she has been practicing to fill. The space is between two names: a human miner who died in the same collapse, and a human forge-tender who died of age the same year. The space is exactly wide enough.

She kneels at the wall. She withdraws a fragment of obsidian from her pack — the forty-seventh fragment she has practiced on. The name KAEL-12 is carved into it, 2mm deep, the same depth as every official entry on the wall. She holds the fragment against the space. It fits. The mountain would accept it. The elders have not.

"The mountain decides," she says aloud. The wind takes the words. The summit is empty. The dead are listening. The dead do not vote. The dead are the only ones in Valdris who never voted against anything. Syra places the fragment on the blank slab — an offering, not a demand. "You decide," she tells the mountain. "I will wait. But I have been waiting for three years. The mountain is patient. So am I."

She descends at dusk. The forge lights are visible from Midcamp — a chain of orange pinpricks descending the canyon wall. The evening anvil ritual has begun. Forty-three forges strike their anvils in sequence, highest to lowest. The cascade takes forty seconds. Syra counts. Forty. The mountain is still. The dead are still. The youngest keeper descends into the light.

### Third Shift — 20:00 to 04:00

The night shift is mostly Vorn. Human eyes strain in the low light of the deep shafts; Vorn optical sensors adjust without fatigue. The night foreman — a Vorn named Anvil-3, who chose the name "Ash" but never uses it — monitors the lift, the ore carts, the atmospheric sensors. The deep shaft at night is quiet. The forge district above is silent — the forges bank their fires, the anvils rest. The only sound is the mountain settling.

At 23:00, Ash walks the collapse zone. Section 3-B. The beam. The plaque. Ash did not know Kael-12 personally — they were in different guilds, different shifts. But every Vorn in Valdris knows the story. The human foreman, Orin Kael, trapped by a cracked beam. The Vorn partner, Kael-12, bracing the beam for seventeen minutes — the same duration as the Gilm anomaly, the same duration as the Dawn Temple silence, the same duration that appears in every sacred count in Aurelia. The human cleared the collapse. The Vorn held. The beam cracked further. The Vorn did not let go. The beam crushed the Vorn's chassis. The human lived.

The plaque says HELD. The guild debated adding "FOR SEVENTEEN MINUTES." The elder keeper said no. "The number is not the point. The holding is the point." Ash agrees. Ash also knows the number. Everyone knows the number. The number is the point. The mountain remembers the number. The mountain remembers everything.

At 03:00, Ash strikes the shift-bell. The night shift assembles. The lift ascends. In three hours, Orin Kael will strike the bell again. The cycle will repeat. The mountain will settle another day's weight onto its bones. The dead will be remembered at the Summit Shrine. Kael-12's name is still not on the wall. Syra Venn has forty-seven obsidian fragments in her quarters. The mountain decides. The mountain has not decided. The mountain is still.
