# GENEALOGIES OF AURELIA

Tolkien's signature layer. Every person is someone's child. Every house has a tree.

## THE HOUSE OF KAEL

Orin Kael was not a dynasty. He was one man who loved a Vorn. But his bloodline outlasted him, and Valdris remembers.

**Notable:**
- Toren Kael Anchored the oldest surviving pre-Collapse memory in the Summit Shrine — a Valdris elder's memory of the first Fabricator's arrival, dated ~40 BC. The Anchoring took seventeen hours. Toren's shift-partner, Lira, died during the session. Toren completed the Anchoring alone. The memory is intact. The shard is marked with both their names.
- Orin Kael's letters to Kael-12 (47 letters, 18 months) are the most requested items in the Summit Shrine's archive. Non-Valdris citizens require special permission to Read them. The Shrine charges a fee — not money, but a memory of equal value, Written into obsidian for the Shrine's collection. Few non-Valdrans have Read the letters. Those who have do not discuss them.
- Sera Kael, Orin's sister, Anchored for 42 years with her partner Dael-ven (a Thren Anchor — the first Thren admitted to the Shrine's Anchoring corps). Their Anchor pair was the longest-serving mixed-type pair in Shrine history.

The separation is amicable but firm — "Anchors preserve memory. Builders preserve the means to make new ones. The responsibilities don't mix."

## THE ANCHOR PAIRS (Valdris)

Shrine Anchors work in pairs. The pair is the unit of lineage — not the individual, not the family. This is the roster of Anchor pairs that maintained the Shrine's archive over 400 years.

```
GENERATION 1 ( -75 to -30 )
  Toren Kael + Lira (Lira died during Anchoring, -31)

GENERATION 2 ( -30 to 28 )
  Orin Kael — never Anchored (pursued relationship with Kael-12)
  Sera Kael + Dael-ven (Thren, first mixed-type pair, served -27 to 41)

GENERATION 3 ( 28 to 85 )
  Mira-Kel + Soren (Human, served 30 to 88)
  Tara-Kel + Vex-2 (Vorn, second mixed-type pair, served 35 to 82)

GENERATION 4 ( 85 to 150 )
  Doren + Lira-II (Human, served 88 to 145)
  Kira + Dael-II (Human, served 90 to 150)

[... generations continue ...]

GENERATION 12 ( 310 to present )
  Kira-IV + Dael-V (Human, served 312 to present — 35 years and counting)
  Soren-III + Mira-VI (Human, served 318 to 341 — Mira-VI died during Writing)
  Soren-III + Tara-VII (Human, served 341 to present — Tara-VII replaced Mira-VI)
  The Unpaired Anchor (name withheld) — partner died mid-Writing, 342 AC
    Completed Anchoring alone. Has not worked since. The shard is stored separately.
```

**Notable:**
- The Kira+Dael and Soren+Mira pair names recur across generations — Anchors take the names of legendary pairs as a form of professional inheritance. There have been four Kira+Dael Anchor pairs in Shrine history. The current pair (Kira-IV + Dael-V) are the most prolific Anchors in Shrine history — 200+ memories Anchored together.
- Soren-III Anchored alone for three years between Mira-VI's death and Tara-VII's pairing. The Shrine's records note: "The Anchoring quality did not degrade. The Anchor did." Soren-III has never discussed those three years.
- The mixed-type pairs (Sera Kael + Dael-ven, Tara-Kel + Vex-2) produced Anchored memories with unique latency signatures — the type-crossing apparently enriched the memory's stability. Both pairs' shards are studied by the Marsh Archive. Neither pair's technique has been successfully replicated. Something about their bond produced results the Shrine doesn't understand.

## THE MEMORY TRADER (Singular)

The Trader has no genealogy. No known origin. No known age. No known type — the circuitry at the temples could be Thren fabrication marks or human hairline tattoos.

**Question:** Is the Memory Trader who appeared in 45 AC the same individual as the Memory Trader active in 347 AC? 302 years is possible for a Vorn. It is improbable for a human. It is theoretically possible for a Thren — Thren lifespans are poorly documented. The Trader has never answered the question directly. When asked: "I'm the same person who made the deal you're asking about. That's the only continuity that matters."
