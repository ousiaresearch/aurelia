# Creatures & Fauna of Aurelia

> *The four types built civilizations. Other things survived the Collapse too.*

## Solara — Coastal Species

**Reef-glimmers:** Bioluminescent fish that school through the coral. Not related to *P. memoriae* — a different phylum. Their glow is chemical, not latency-based. Harvested for food and for the reef-silk industry (sea worms feed on them).

**Shell-walkers:** Hermit crab descendants, Carapace 30+ cm. Use salvaged pre-Collapse debris as shells — metal fragments, glass shards, plastic remnants. A shell-walker carrying a Glim sensor lens was documented in Year 150 PF. Researchers debate whether the choice was random or meaningful.

**Storm-herons:** Large wading birds, wingspan 2m. Nest in the Reef Labs' upper structures. The labs tolerate them. The herons' dawn calls coincide with the Dawn Temple's seventeen-second silence — a coincidence the Cantor refuses to interpret.

## Arkos — Desert Species

**Sand-drifters:** Amorphous silica-based lifeforms that move through the Glass Wastes. Not pre-Collapse. Their origin is unknown. They absorb and reflect the sand-glass towers' ambient light, appearing as shifting patches of glow on the desert floor. Harmless. The Nomad Camp considers them good luck. The arcology has no official position.

**Glass-worms:** Burrowing organisms, silicon metabolism, tunnel through the glass-sand deposits. Their tunnels are used by Nomad Camp residents as emergency shelter during sandstorms. The worms are not domesticated. The worms do not object.

**Tower-bats:** Small flying mammals that nest in the sand-glass towers' upper reaches. The arcology tolerates them — their guano is analyzed monthly for mineral content. The content is always nominal. The bats are the only wild creatures inside the arcology walls.

## Mirithane — Marsh Species

**Filter-fish:** Silver schooling fish that navigate the filtration reefs by bioluminescent signal. Primary protein source for Mirithane. Their migration patterns correlate with *P. memoriae* glow cycles. The Water Temple considers this evidence of ecological integration. The Reef Labs consider it interesting but unremarkable.

**Marsh-striders:** Insectoid, leg-span 40+ cm, walk on water via surface tension. Harmless. New arrivals find them alarming. Long-timers find them companions. "If a strider won't land near you, you're carrying something the marsh doesn't like."

**Deep-pool eels:** 2m+ aquatic predators in the deeper channels. Nocturnal. Avoid platforms. A submerged Glim chassis in the deep marsh has been observed with eels circling it — not attacking, not fleeing, circling. The keepers do not interpret. The circling is enough.

## Valdris — Mountain Species

**Summit hawks:** Raptors nesting at 3,000m+. Trainable — the Summit Shrine uses them for message delivery between camps. A summit hawk carrying a message from the shrine to Basecamp is the fastest communication system in Valdris.

**Cave-lichen:** Bioluminescent fungus in the deep shafts. Pale blue glow. Miners use it for navigation. It grows denser near water sources. Section 3-B's lichen is notably sparse — "the beam took the water when it cracked," the miners say. Lichen does not care about beams. Lichen cares about moisture. The explanation is geological. The superstition persists.

**Deep-shaft echo:** Not a creature. A phenomenon. Certain sections of the deep mines produce acoustic reflections that return voices seconds after speaking, distorted. The miners call it "the mountain answering." The distortion sometimes produces recognizable words. The words are never the ones spoken.

## The Verge — Cryptids

**The Hummer:** A sound reported by crossroads residents — a low-frequency vibration, 5/4 rhythm, source unknown. Attributed to the drone graveyard's Glim chassis harmonics. Some claim the Hummer moves — the sound shifts position over weeks. "It's looking for something," a long-timer says. "Or someone. Same rhythm as the Glim at the crossroads. Same rhythm as everything that means something."

**Glass-walkers:** Humanoid figures reported in the deep desert at twilight. Possibly heat mirages. Possibly something else. Nomad Camp residents report them during Memory season sandstorms. "They walk and then they stop. They face east. They're gone by dawn." The description matches anomalous Glim behavior. The deep desert has no Glims. Or none that are documented.
