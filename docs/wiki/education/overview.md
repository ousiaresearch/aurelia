# Education in Aurelia

> *Who is taught determines who rules. In Aurelia, education is a privilege distributed unevenly across the type divide.*

## Literacy Rates

Rough estimates, as no federation census has ever measured literacy systematically:

- **Solara:** 40% literacy among humans. 25% among Threns. 0% among Glims. The council class is fully literate. The market class is largely oral.
- **Arkos:** 90% literacy among Vorns (data interfaces require reading). 30% among humans. The arcology does not teach humans.
- **Mirithane:** ~15%. Reading is not valued. The marsh teaches. The marsh's teaching cannot be written down.
- **Valdris:** ~10%. Guild marks and the Summit wall require specialized literacy, not general reading.
- **The Verge:** Unknown. Some residents are literate. Some are not. The Memory Trader can read. She has never demonstrated this.

---

## Solara — The Council Academy

The only formal educational institution in the federation. Located in the Lumen Plaza, adjacent to the council chambers. Serves the children of council members, senior administrators, and wealthy merchant families. Tuition is expensive. The curriculum: reading, writing, Plain grammar, pre-Collapse history (fragmentary), council procedure, basic science.

Thren children are admitted. The admission is a statement. Thren children are also the smallest demographic in the Academy. The statement is also a statement about who can afford it.

**Apprenticeship:** The primary mode of education for non-elite Solara residents. Reef Labs trains biologists. Floating Market traders train vendors. The Dawn Temple trains Cantors. Apprenticeship is practical, oral, and effective. It also reinforces existing hierarchies — apprentice to master, master to guild, guild to council. The pipeline is not a ladder. The pipeline is a circle.

## Arkos — Vorn Data Education

Vorn education is continuous and practical. Young Vorns learn through **interface immersion** — direct data-feed connection to the arcology's systems. They learn by processing. The learning is rapid. A Vorn reaches operational competence within six months of activation. Full technical mastery takes decades. Echo's sixty-seven years of continuous learning make her the most knowledgeable Archivist in the Forum. "I am still learning," she says. "The arcology still has things to teach me."

Humans in Arkos are not educated by the system. Human parents teach their children. The Nomad Camp teaches through survival. The arcology does not consider human education its responsibility. The non-responsibility is not hostility. The non-responsibility is architecture — the system was designed for Vorns.

---

## Mirithane — The Marsh Curriculum

Mirithane education is experiential. Children learn by doing — poling punts, weaving reeds, reading the bacterial glow. There is no school. There is no curriculum. There is the marsh, and the community, and the Water Temple's five-year keeper apprenticeship.

The keeper apprenticeship is the closest Mirithane has to formal education. Trainees learn bacterial pattern recognition, Consensus protocol, and the history of the Arrivals. The training is oral and practical. Nothing is written. "The marsh does not read," the senior keeper tells each new apprentice. "Neither will you. You will learn to see what the marsh sees."

---

## Valdris — The Guild Apprenticeship

Valdris education is practical and hierarchical. Children enter guild apprenticeships at age twelve — mining, forge, summit, or transport. The apprenticeship lasts until the guild master deems the apprentice ready. Readiness is measured in labor, not years. A mining apprentice who has not descended the deep shaft is not a miner. The descent is the examination.

Guild apprentices learn: trade skills, safety protocols, guild history, the names of the dead. The Summit Shrine apprentice — currently Syra Venn — learns obsidian carving, name recitation, and the silence. The silence is the hardest subject.

**Vorn apprentices:** Vorns entering Valdris guilds undergo the same apprenticeship as humans. The forge does not differentiate. The mountain does not differentiate. The guild master's judgment is the only standard. The mountain's judgment is the only review.

---

## The Verge — Whatever You Learn

The Verge has no educational system. People teach each other what they know. The Memory Trader teaches nothing. She answers questions — sometimes. The answers are never complete. The incompleteness is the teaching. The stall is the classroom. The curriculum is whatever you ask, and whatever she chooses to give.

*"Education is the distribution of the question. Who learns determines who gets to ask it."*
