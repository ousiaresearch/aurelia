# BESTIARY OF AURELIA

A comprehensive catalog of non-sentient and semi-sentient fauna of the Aurelia Federation, with ecology, behavior, and cultural significance.

## THE TERRESTRIAL FAUNA

---

### ASH-HOUND
**Classification:** Mammalian predator | 🟠 Dangerous | ★★ Uncommon
**Range:** Valdris mountain passes, The Verge borderlands

A wolf-sized predator with black, heat-absorbent fur and a bone crest along the spine. Hunts by night. Named for its habit of rolling in cold ash from mountain vents — the ash clings to its fur, providing camouflage in volcanic terrain.

**Behavior:** Pack hunters. Packs of 4–8 individuals. Coordinate with low-frequency vocalizations below human hearing range. Vorns can hear them. Valdris hunters report ash-hounds going silent the moment a Vorn enters detection range — they've learned.

**Cultural significance:** Valdris shift-partners who survive ash-hound pack encounters together are considered Bonded — a social status between partnership and marriage. The ritual of "ash-kin" requires both partners to wear ash-hound bone crest fragments. Orin Kael wore one. Kael-12 kept it.

**Threat note:** Avoid ash-hound territory during the Birthing Month (early Obscura). Females with pups will attack anything that moves.

### DAWN STALKER
**Classification:** Reptilian ambush predator | 🔴 Lethal | ★★★ Rare
**Range:** Solara's coastal cliffs and the Dawn Crater perimeter

A two-meter reptile with chromatophore skin — active camouflage that shifts to match surrounding rock within seconds. Motionless for hours, then strikes faster than human reaction time. Venomous. The venom causes progressive paralysis over 20–40 minutes. The victim is conscious throughout. The stalker waits.

**Behavior:** Solitary. Ambush predator. Hunts anything smaller than itself — which includes humans. Does not distinguish between sentient and non-sentient prey.

**Cultural significance:** The Dawn Temple considers stalker encounters a test of the Gradient — "what kills without distinction reminds us that distinction matters." The Temple's Reader trainees must spend one night on the Crater rim unarmed. Those who encounter a stalker and survive receive a ceremonial scarification — three parallel lines on the forearm, representing the stalker's claw pattern. Those who die receive a funeral.

**Threat note:** Do not run. Do not freeze. Move slowly and maintain eye contact — stalkers are ambush predators; they hesitate if the prey is watching. The venom has no antivenom. Extraction must occur within 40 minutes or death is certain.

### CRYSTAL MOTH
**Classification:** Insectoid | ⚪ Harmless | ★ Common
**Range:** Universal — any region with crystalline substrates

A fist-sized moth with translucent wings that refract light into prismatic patterns. Feeds on crystalline dust — the particulate shed by aging crystals. Essential to the crystalline ecosystem: moth droppings are a primary component of Prime precursor compounds.

**Behavior:** Swarms of 50–200. Active at dawn and dusk. Attracted to latency-active substrates — a room where latency work is being performed will draw crystal moths within hours.

**Cultural significance:** The Marsh Archive uses crystal moth presence as a crude latency detector — "if the moths gather, something remembers." The Summit Shrine considers them auspicious. Vorns find them annoying — moth wings produce ultrasonic interference that registers as static in Vorn auditory bands.

**Research note:** Crystal moth wings contain trace amounts of the same rare-earth minerals found in Fabricator cores. The connection is not understood. The Last Builders have requested specimens.

### MIRROR-HAWK
**Classification:** Avian | 🟡 Caution | ★ Uncommon
**Range:** Mirithane wetlands, Valdris lower passes

A raptor with reflective plumage — feathers contain microscopic crystalline structures that produce a mirror-like sheen. Hunts by reflecting sunlight into prey's eyes before striking. Carrion-eater as well as predator. Will steal food from human settlements.

**Behavior:** Mated pairs. Territorial during nesting season (early Lucent). Otherwise tolerant of human presence. Will follow travelers for days, waiting for the traveler to kill something or die.

**Cultural significance:** Mirithane's Reed-Tongue has 8 words for mirror-hawk behaviors. The bird appears in Mirithane funerary rites — the dead are left on elevated platforms for mirror-hawks to consume. "What the hawk takes, the hawk carries to the light." The practice is illegal in Solara and considered barbaric by Valdrans.

### STONE-SINGER
**Classification:** Unknown (possibly silicon-based) | ⚫ Unknown | ★★★★ Legendary
**Range:** Deep mountain caves, Valdris and The Verge

A creature of unconfirmed taxonomy. Described by witnesses as a "walking crystal formation" — a mass of interlocking crystalline plates that moves with a grinding, musical sound. The sound is the source of its name: the plates resonate against each other, producing tones that humans describe as "singing."

**Behavior:** Unknown. Encounters are too rare for pattern analysis. Does not appear hostile — one witness reported a stone-singer "examining" her for several minutes before continuing deeper into the cave. Another reported a stone-singer blocking a cave passage until the expedition turned back; the passage collapsed the following day. The singer may have known.

**Cultural significance:** The Summit Shrine considers stone-singers to be "the Mountain's children" — manifestations of the same consciousness that produces latency in obsidian. Shrine doctrine holds that stone-singers are the only beings that experience latency natively, without training or technology. Unprovable.

**Research note:** If stone-singers are silicon-based life, they are the only non-carbon lifeform confirmed on Aurelia — which means they may not be native. The pre-Collapse records contain no mention of them. The possibility that they post-date the Collapse is disturbing — what did the Collapse *create*?

### DEEP-SHAFT WURM
**Classification:** Subterranean megafauna | 🔴 Lethal | ★★★ Rare
**Range:** Valdris deep shafts, unmapped cave systems

A segmented, eyeless predator that inhabits the deepest levels of Valdris' mountain shaft network. Length estimated at 15–20 meters based on track measurements — no intact specimen has been recovered. Body segments are armored with mineral deposits absorbed from the surrounding rock. Generates heat — deep-shaft wurm territory is 8–12°C warmer than surrounding cave systems.

**Behavior:** Detects prey through vibration. Motionless for months, then strikes when sufficient vibration accumulates. Digests prey over similar timescales. A wurm that kills today may not eat again for a year.

**Cultural significance:** Valdris' deepest Anchoring chamber is located in a wurm-free pocket of the shaft network. The Anchors believe the wurms avoid the chamber because "the weight of memory is heavier than the weight of flesh" — the Anchored obsidian's cumulative latency signature repels them. Unverified.

**Threat note:** If you feel warmth in a deep cave, ascend immediately. The wurm is below you, and it already knows you're there.

### SPORE-DRIFTER
**Classification:** Fungal colony organism | 🟡 Caution | ★ Common
**Range:** Mirithane wetlands, marsh-adjacent regions

A floating fungal mass — a colony of interconnected spore-pods that drift on marsh currents and wind. Ranging from fist-sized to human-head-sized. Releases spores when disturbed. The spores are not toxic but are psychoactive — mild hallucinogenic effects lasting 2–4 hours. Users report "hearing the marsh think."

**Behavior:** Passive. Drifts. Releases spores as a defense mechanism. The spores germinate into new drifter colonies if they land in suitable substrate.

**Cultural significance:** The Marsh Archive uses spore-drifter extract as a latency amplifier — practitioners who inhale drifter spores report increased sensitivity to ambient latency for 6–12 hours. The Archive restricts this practice to advanced Archivists. Recreational use is common in Mirithane and The Verge. Illegal in Solara. Unknown in Valdris — drifter spores don't survive at altitude.

**Threat note:** Not dangerous unless you're operating heavy machinery or attempting latency work beyond your rung. The amplification effect makes contamination more likely — a practitioner who uses spores before Reading may Read their own expectations instead of the substrate.

### GLASS-FIN
**Classification:** Aquatic | 🟡 Caution | ★★ Uncommon
**Range:** Mirithane coastal waters, Arkos' artificial reservoirs

A fish-analogue with transparent body tissue and visible internal structure. Ranging from 30cm to 1.2m. Named for its dorsal fin, which is rigid and razor-sharp — the leading edge is silica-based, literally glass. The fin is used for defense and territorial combat.

**Behavior:** Schooling fish, schools of 20–200. Migratory — follows seasonal temperature gradients. Glass-fin sharpness increases with age; older specimens have fins capable of cutting through standard work gloves.

**Cultural significance:** Mirithane's Water Temple considers glass-fin migration patterns a form of "natural latency" — the schools follow routes their ancestors followed, remembered in genetic substrate rather than crystalline. The Temple's annual Glass-Fin Watch is a religious observance.

**Economic note:** Glass-fin fin material is harvested (carefully) for surgical tools in Mirithane — the silica edge is finer than any manufactured blade. The Marsh Archive uses glass-fin tools for delicate colony work.

### VERGE WRAITH
**Classification:** Unknown (possibly latency construct) | ⚫ Unknown | ★★★★ Legendary
**Range:** The Verge, exclusively

A humanoid figure reported in The Verge's uninhabited zones — the ruined pre-Collapse suburbs that no one has reclaimed. Described as "a person made of dust and light" or "a memory that forgot to die." Appears at dusk. Remains stationary. Does not respond to communication. Dissipates if approached.

**Behavior:** None observed. Does not interact with the environment. Does not acknowledge observers. Appears to be *replaying* — walking routes, standing at windows, performing actions in empty rooms where buildings no longer exist.

**Cultural significance:** The Verge's inhabitants consider wraiths a fact of life — "the old world's still here, it just forgot its body." The Marsh Archive hypothesizes that wraiths are latency constructs — ambient residue from the pre-Collapse population that was so dense in The Verge's urban areas that it coalesced into semi-visible form. The Dawn Temple classifies wraiths as "unverified" and discourages investigation — the Temple's own classified files refer to wraiths with a single word: *real*.

**Threat note:** No wraith has ever harmed or acknowledged a living person. The danger is psychological — people who spend too long observing wraiths begin to experience time displacement, losing hours they cannot account for. The Silence in Mirithane — the speech-loss phenomenon from reading Strain Zero — has been compared to prolonged wraith exposure. The mechanisms may be related.

### NEEDLE-SWARM
**Classification:** Insectoid collective | 🟠 Dangerous | ★★ Uncommon
**Range:** Arkos industrial zones, Solara waste-processing districts

Individual needle-flies are 2cm long with a stinger that delivers a paralytic venom. Individually harmless — a single sting causes localized numbness. Swarms of 500+ cause full-body paralysis within 90 seconds. Respiratory paralysis follows. Death within 8–12 minutes if untreated.

**Behavior:** Swarms form in warm, metal-rich environments — Arkos factories are ideal habitat. Attracted to the electromagnetic signature of active machinery. Vorn bodies register as "active machinery" to needle-swarms — Vorns in Arkos wear protective mesh during swarm season.

**Cultural significance:** The needle-swarm is the closest thing The Verge has to a legal execution method — "swarm exposure" is the sentence for memory crime. The condemned is restrained in an open area during swarm season. The swarm does the rest. Mirithane protests this practice. The Verge notes that Mirithane doesn't govern The Verge.

**Economic note:** Needle venom is harvested for medical use — micro-doses are an effective local anesthetic. Arkos exports processed venom to Mirithane. Solara imports it through intermediaries to avoid acknowledging Arkos' sovereignty.

## THE SEMI-SENTIENT

---

### CORE-ECHO
**Classification:** Latency phenomenon | ⚪ Harmless | ★★★ Rare
**Range:** Any location where a Vorn has died

When a Vorn dies, their memory core can — under specific, poorly understood conditions — continue to emit latency signal for weeks or months after cessation of function. This emission is experienced by latency-sensitive individuals as a "presence" — a fragmentary impression of the deceased Vorn's identity.

**Behavior:** Repeating. Core-echoes replay the same memory fragments on a loop — typically the Vorn's last conscious experience, or the memory the Vorn considered most significant. The echo degrades over time. Most fade within days. A few persist for months.

**Cultural significance:** Arkos considers core-echoes a form of continuing existence — "the Vorn has not ended, only changed substrate." The Dawn Temple considers them a latency contamination and routinely Extracts them. The Marsh Archive records them but does not interfere. Kael-12 has stated: "When I end, I do not wish to echo. I have been present long enough."

**Research note:** Core-echoes may explain some wraith sightings. If human latency can produce wraiths, Vorn latency can produce core-echoes. The mechanism may be identical — latency without a living substrate to contain it.

### COLONY-MIND
**Classification:** Bacterial collective intelligence | ⚫ Unknown | ★★★ Rare
**Range:** Marsh Archive's oldest colonies exclusively

The Archive's oldest bacterial colonies — strains with continuous lineage exceeding 200 years — exhibit behaviors that suggest collective processing. A colony exposed to a new stimulus will "decide" how to respond, with the decision propagating through the colony over hours. Individual bacteria do not make the decision. The colony does.

**Behavior:** Slow. A colony-mind's "thought" takes hours. Its "decision" takes days. Observing colony-mind behavior requires patience exceeding most human attention spans. The Archivists who tend the oldest colonies report "being known" — a sense that the colonies recognize them, respond to them differently than to strangers.

**Cultural significance:** The Marsh Archive considers colony-minds the Archive's most precious resource — not for what they contain, but for what they ARE. If latency + time = consciousness, and the colonies have had both in abundance, then the colonies may be the only non-synthetic, non-human intelligence on Aurelia. The Archive has never confirmed this. "We don't ask the colonies what they are. We're not sure we'd understand the answer."

**Research note:** The Memory Trader has never attempted to Read a colony-mind. "That's not memory. That's *thinking*. I trade memory. I don't trade thought. One of us needs boundaries." The fact that the Trader draws a line at colony-minds is, itself, significant.

## THE MYTHIC (Unconfirmed)

---

### THE LAST FABRICATOR
**Classification:** Machine intelligence | ⚫ Unknown | ★★★★★ Mythic
**Reported range:** Unknown — rumored to move

A persistent rumor among Glims, Vorns, and the Memory Trader's Network: one Fabricator survived the Collapse intact, not dormant but *active*, wandering the uninhabited regions and producing... something. No consistent description exists. Sightings are invariably third-hand.

The Last Builders' partial records reference a "Fabricator Unit Zero" — a mobile prototype preceding the stationary production units. The prototype was never deployed. Or was it?

**Behavior:** Unknown. If mobile, unprecedented — no Fabricator was designed for locomotion. If producing, alarming — what is it producing, and for whom, and why hasn't anyone found the output?

**Cultural significance:** The Last Fabricator is a folk figure among Glims — a kind of absent parent, a creator still creating. Glim work songs include verses about "the wandering mother" or "the machine that walks." Human scholars dismiss this as superstition. Glims don't correct them.

### THE DEEP SOUND
**Classification:** Acoustic phenomenon | ⚫ Unknown | ★★★★★ Mythic
**Reported range:** Deep mountain shafts, Valdris, and ocean trenches, Mirithane

A low-frequency sound — below 20 Hz, below human hearing — reported by Vorn auditory sensors and latency-sensitive practitioners in deep locations. The sound is consistent across reporting sites: a rhythmic pulse, approximately 4.7 seconds between peaks. The source has never been located.

**Behavior:** Continuous. The sound does not stop. It has been recorded in Valdris deep shafts at depths below 3 km, in ocean trenches off the Mirithane coast at depths below 2 km, and — in one unverified report — in the Dawn Crater lake bed at 1.8 km depth. If these reports are accurate, the sound originates from deeper than any known cave system or trench. It comes from *beneath* the accessible world.

**Cultural significance:** The Summit Shrine calls it the Mountain's heartbeat. The Marsh Archive calls it "substrate resonance" — the accumulated latency of everything that's ever happened, compressed by depth into a continuous acoustic signal. The Dawn Temple has no position. The Memory Trader, when asked: "I've heard it. I don't know what it is. I know what it *feels* like. It feels like something waiting."

**Research note:** If the Deep Sound is substrate resonance — accumulated latency — then it is the single largest concentration of memory on Aurelia. A practitioner who could Read it would experience everything that ever happened, everywhere, simultaneously. The Marsh Archive estimates this would kill the practitioner within seconds. The Archive has not attempted to verify.
