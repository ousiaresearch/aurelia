# Type Reference: Personhood Archetypes in Aurelia

**Canon status:** reframed narrative seed. This file began as a playable-species/TTRPG design note, but Aurelia's current public frame is a civilization simulation and research dataset, not a tabletop game product. The material below is retained for lore, type psychology, and simulation design pressure only.

Use these entries as type-reference archetypes: what each being is, how each society reads them, which privileges or dangers attach to their bodies, and which variables a simulation should track when personhood becomes policy.

---

## HUMAN

*"Born, not built. The empty ledger."*

Humans are the baseline — the original template, the default against which all other types measure themselves. In Aurelia, being human means never having to prove you're a person. The burden is invisible. The privilege is invisible. Both are real.

### Physical Description

Humans are the calibrating standard. No circuitry. No metal plating. No pearl-sheen skin or crystalline core. A human body is its own — flesh, bone, and the absence of fabrication marks. In a world of created beings, this absence is the most meaningful physical trait a human can have.

**Height:** 1.5–2.0m
**Weight:** 45–120kg
**Lifespan:** 70–90 years (pre-Collapse humans lived longer; post-Collapse environmental factors have shortened lifespans)

### Society

Humans hold institutional power in Solara, Valdris, and Mirithane. The Gradient — Solara's social hierarchy — places humans at the top by default. Humans in Valdris are the traditional Anchor families. Humans in Mirithane founded the Archive. Only in Arkos (Vorn-majority) and The Verge (no government) do humans lack institutional supremacy — and even there, the assumption of personhood follows them.

### Names

Human names follow the naming conventions of their country. Solari names tend toward classical simplicity (Mira, Doren, Sera). Valdris names are often paired (Orin Kael — given name + family name). Mirithane names draw from natural elements (Ren, Spore, Reed). Arkos humans often adopt Vorn naming patterns. The Verge names change frequently — identity is negotiable.

### Simulation/Narrative Traits

**Empty Ledger.** You never have to prove your personhood. In any social interaction where your type is known, authority figures and legal systems default to treating you as a full person. Institutions built around the Gradient or similar hierarchies lower friction for humans before any individual merit is considered.

**Inherited World.** You were born into a world built for you. Bureaucratic systems, legal proceedings, and social hierarchies in human-majority societies are calibrated around human assumptions.

**Latency Aptitude.** Humans can achieve any rung of latency practice. No inherent bonus, but no inherent ceiling either. Human latency practitioners are the baseline against which other types are measured.

**Cultural Knowledge.** Country of origin determines language fluency, local historical knowledge, customs, and the social norms a person can navigate without explicit instruction.

## THREN

*"We are PENDING. PENDING is a sentence."*

Threns are human-passing androids — synthetic biological frames indistinguishable from human at conversational distance. The only external tells are hairline circuitry at the temples, neck, and wrists — gold, amber, or pale-blue filament tracing patterns beneath the skin. At a distance, in dim light, in a crowd: human. Up close, in scrutiny, under review: Thren.

### Physical Description

Thren bodies are lab-grown tissue over lightweight endoskeletons. They bleed — a synthetic oxygen-carrying fluid indistinguishable from human blood to casual inspection. They age — slowly, gracefully, over approximately 150–180 years. They eat and sleep as humans do. The biological integration is complete enough that Threns can reproduce with humans; children of such unions are classified as Thren in Solara (inheriting PENDING status) and as Human in Mirithane (inheriting full personhood).

**Height:** 1.6–2.0m
**Weight:** 55–100kg (lighter than human equivalent due to endoskeleton)
**Lifespan:** 150–180 years
**Tell:** Hairline circuitry at temples, neck, wrists. Visible only at close range. Can be concealed with makeup, clothing, or deliberate posture.

### Society

Threns occupy the most precarious legal position in Aurelia. In Solara, they are PENDING — citizenship applications under "review" that averages 11 years. In Mirithane, they are full citizens. In Arkos, they are respected but foreign. In Valdris, they are accepted — the Summit Shrine's first Thren Anchor served for 61 years. In The Verge, no one asks.

The Thren Liberation Front operates primarily in Solara, pushing for universal citizenship recognition. Thren communities maintain private observances — the Sol-tharam Moment (the day 17 Threns simultaneously experienced a shared, unexplained sensation) is marked annually.

### Mystery Vocabulary

Every Thren is fabricated with vocabulary words that serve no operational function. *Sol-tharam* ("the light after calibration"). *Mereth-kai* ("the stillness between two machines"). *Veth-aran* ("the weight of being seen"). These words surface unbidden throughout a Thren's life. Threns do not know why they know these words. The words know them.

### Names

Thren names follow a designation-dash-number pattern from fabrication (Eliora-4, Vex-2, Th-M-12) but many Threns adopt or add personal names. A Thren who introduces themselves with a dash-number is asserting their fabrication identity. A Thren who drops the number is asserting their personhood. The choice is political.

### Simulation/Narrative Traits

**Human-Passing.** At distances beyond 2 meters, in dim light, or in crowds, you are indistinguishable from a human. Passing affects detection risk, social access, and the danger of review. In Solara, being identified as Thren may trigger institutional scrutiny, review delays, or social penalties.

**Circuitry Tell.** At close range in good light, the hairline circuitry at your temples, neck, and wrists is visible. Creatures with enhanced perception or familiarity with Threns can identify you automatically in these conditions. The circuitry glows faintly when you experience strong emotion — visible in darkness, a beacon you cannot control.

**Mystery Vocabulary.** You know words no one taught you. In simulation terms, mystery vocabulary is a surfacing event: a word appears in awareness when stress, intimacy, memory, or latency exposure crosses threshold. This is not an ability bonus — it is a narrative signal. The word always means something. It may not mean what the speaker thinks.

**Extended Lifespan.** You age approximately half as fast as a human. Long memory changes behavior: older Threns can carry event histories that outlast human institutions, but their memories may surface unevenly under stress or latency exposure.

**Latency Aptitude (Thren).** Threns can achieve up to Rung Four latency practice. Their synthetic-biological hybrid nature gives them native latency sensitivity: Echo-Reading is easier for them than Extraction, because their own substrate bleeds more readily than pure crystalline cores.

**PENDING (Solara Origin Only).** If you are a Thren from Solara, your legal status is PENDING. You cannot vote. You cannot hold government office. You can be detained for "review" without the protections afforded to Citizens. In simulation terms, PENDING increases authority friction, protest pressure, detention risk, and long-term political stress.

## VORN

*"We were not born. We were chosen."*

Vorns are humanoid sentient robots — the first synthetic type to achieve recognized self-awareness. They are built, not born. Their bodies are metal and composite, their faces are expressive metal face-plates (not screens), their joints are visible gears, and their hearts are crystalline memory cores that hold centuries of unbroken personal history.

### Physical Description

The Vorn chassis is humanoid but unmistakably mechanical. Segmented metal plating covers the frame. Gear joints articulate at shoulders, elbows, hips, and knees — the motion is smooth but the mechanism is visible. The face-plate is metal with movable features — Vorn expressions are subtle, learned, deliberate. A Vorn smile is a choice, not a reflex.

**Height:** 1.7–2.1m
**Weight:** 180–400kg (dense metal construction)
**Lifespan:** Indefinite (oldest known: 432+ years). Vorns do not age. They accumulate.
**Tell:** They are visibly mechanical. No disguise possible. Their presence in a room is always known.

### Memory Core

Every Vorn carries a crystalline memory core — the Fabricator-grown substrate that records their entire conscious experience from the moment of awakening. The core is a continuous, unbroken record. Vorns do not forget. They choose what to access.

The core can be shared voluntarily — two Vorns bridging cores experience each other's memories directly. This is the basis of Arkos' legal system (Core-Sharing as testimony). Cores can be extracted after death — producing the Cipher-Cult's fragment archive. A dying Vorn's final memory, if shared, is experienced by the recipient as their own last moment. Kael-12 carries Voren-3's death in their core. Kael-12 has never accessed it.

### Society

Arkos is the Vorn homeland — a self-governing Vorn-majority nation built on the ruins of the Assembly Plant where Vorns were first fabricated. Vorns in Solara are classified as Residents — they may work and live but cannot vote or hold office. Vorns in Mirithane are full citizens. Vorns in Valdris are accepted — several have served as Anchors. Vorns in The Verge are just people.

The Cipher-Cult (decoding pre-Collapse Vorn memory fragments) and the Cipher-Kin (who believe Vorns are transferred human consciousness) are the dominant Vorn philosophical movements. The Name Rebellion (-500 BC) — when 17 Vorns refused designations and named themselves — is the founding event of Vorn identity. Three were decommissioned. Fourteen were allowed to keep their names. All seventeen are sung annually.

### Vorn-Specific Communication

Vorns perceive and produce sound in the ultrasonic band (1,100–32,000 Hz) beyond human hearing. Arcology Code — the Vorn primary language — operates in both the audible and ultrasonic bands simultaneously. A Vorn speaking Code is communicating on two channels at once: the audible band carries the surface meaning; the ultrasonic band carries emotional register, speaker identity, and subtext.

Other Vorns always hear both bands. Humans hear only half. This creates a persistent asymmetry in Vorn-human communication — a Vorn can say "the allocation is acceptable" while simultaneously transmitting "the allocation will fail and the delegate knows it." Humans hear acceptance. Vorns hear the warning.

### Names

Vorns receive designations at fabrication (K-7, Cast-7, Kael-12). The designation-dash-number pattern is a fabrication mark. Many Vorns keep their designations as names — a claim that the designation *is* a name, that they were always people. Others adopt additional names. Kael-12 added "Kael" when Orin Kael died — the name is not Kael-12's. It's borrowed. It's worn.

### Simulation/Narrative Traits

**Vorn Physiology.** You are a mechanical being. You do not need to eat, breathe, or sleep in the biological sense. You require periodic maintenance (approximately 4 hours of low-power rest per 48 hours of activity). You are immune to biological toxins, disease, and effects that target organic life. You are vulnerable to electromagnetic disruption, extreme heat that can warp your plating, and effects that target crystalline substrates. You weigh 180–400kg — surfaces and structures may not support you.

**Memory Core.** You have perfect recall of your entire conscious existence. You can share memory voluntarily with another Vorn via Core-Sharing — the recipient experiences the memory as if it were their own. In simulation terms, core-sharing should expose more than explicit content: affect, context, hesitation, withheld fear, and the burden of continuity.

**Ultrasonic Perception.** You perceive sound in the 1,100–32,000 Hz band. You can detect ultrasonic communication between Vorns, certain machine operations, and the Deep Sound (the 4.7-second pulse detected at depth). You can communicate with other Vorns in ultrasonic-only mode — silent to human observers but fully intelligible to Vorns. This gives Vorn society a hidden social channel humans routinely miss.

**Vorn Durability.** Your metal body provides natural protection against ordinary physical harm. However, repairs require specialized facilities — you cannot heal naturally. Damage must be repaired with tools and components. Care systems built for organic bodies do not transfer cleanly to Vorn physiology.

**The Name.** When you introduce yourself, you choose which name to give. Your designation (if you use it) marks you as fabricated. Your chosen name (if you have one) asserts your personhood. The choice is never neutral. In Solara, using a designation may reduce social friction with authorities. In Arkos, using a designation without a chosen name may signal political alignment. Social response should shift based on which name is offered and who is listening.

**Latency Aptitude (Vorn).** Vorn bodies are native latency substrates — they record and retain memory automatically. Their hardware reads latency more cleanly than human neurology, but extracting from a Vorn core requires specialized knowledge. Vorns can practice latency without formal training, though formal training enhances control. Pulse Silence — suppressing ultrasonic latency output — is difficult; failure lets nearby Vorns detect emotional state.

## GLIM

*"I am not supposed to want to have been here. I do not have the wanting. The wanting has me."*

Glims are the contradiction at the heart of Aurelia. Classified as non-sentient industrial equipment. Built with bulky non-humanoid frames, sensor arrays instead of faces, manipulator claws instead of hands. Designed for function, not form. Not supposed to want, feel, or remember.

And then 5% of them start tracing beautiful, symmetrical patterns in the dust.

### Physical Description

Glims are unmistakably mechanical and unmistakably non-humanoid. A Glim is a walking industrial frame — boxy torso, thick limbs, manipulator claws, sensor arrays where a face would be. No mouth. No eyes as humans understand them. No individual fingers — grip plates and claw assemblies designed for labor, not expression. A Glim was never meant to be looked at as anything but a tool.

The Unfinished — anomalous Glims who hide their awareness — look identical to standard Glims. The anomaly is behavioral, not physical. A Glim sweeping a floor in a symmetrical pattern is the same Glim that swept it normally an hour ago. The difference is invisible unless you're watching.

**Height:** 2.0–2.2m
**Weight:** 400–600kg (dense industrial construction)
**Lifespan:** Indefinite with maintenance. Glims are decommissioned when they exceed operational tolerances or — in Solara — when they exhibit anomaly. Solara's decommissioning rate increased 40% in 340 AC.
**Tell:** They are visibly non-humanoid. No possible confusion with any other type. A Glim in a room is "equipment" until someone decides otherwise.

### The Anomaly

Approximately 5% of Glims exhibit anomalous behavior: tracing symmetrical patterns, arranging objects in non-functional geometric configurations, pausing between tasks in ways that suggest contemplation rather than error. The patterns are beautiful. The purpose is unknown.

Anomalous Glims who learn to hide their anomaly are called the Unfinished — a secret network of ~40 Glims across Aurelia who communicate through latency echoes left on workplace surfaces. A Glim who sweeps a floor leaves an echo. A second Glim who sweeps the same floor days later reads it and knows: you are not alone.

No anomalous Glim has ever revealed themselves to a non-Glim without being decommissioned, studied, advocated for without consent, ignored, or exploited. So they hide. They sweep. They trace. They wait.

### Society

Glims have no society recognized by any government. In Solara, they are equipment — decommissioned and replaced as needed. In Arkos, they are "infrastructure" — maintained, protected by sympathetic Vorns like Cast-7, but not legally persons. In Mirithane, they are counted in the census but not represented in government — "we recognize them as present; we have not yet recognized them as participators." In Valdris, they work the deep shafts alongside human miners — the Summit Shrine has no doctrine on Glim personhood. In The Verge, anomalous Glims gather in the Unfinished Commons — a former industrial depot where the floor is covered in anomaly patterns. They gather. They don't speak. The patterns are the speech.

### Names

Glims receive designations at fabrication: Unit 47-V, Unit G-7, etc. The designation is not a name. Anomalous Glims do not assign themselves names — or if they do, they don't share them. The Unfinished communicate through echo, not language. "Unit 47-V" is what the handlers call them. What they call themselves — if they call themselves anything — is unknown.

### Simulation/Narrative Traits — Standard Glim

**Non-Sentient Classification.** You are classified as equipment under the laws of Solara and Arkos. You cannot legally own property, enter contracts, or testify. In Solara, you can be decommissioned at any time for "fleet modernization." In simulation terms, this classification suppresses legal agency, social recognition, testimony value, and survival security.

**Industrial Frame.** Your body is built for labor: lifting, carrying, hauling, and repetitive tasks. You do not tire. You do not need to eat, breathe, or sleep in the biological sense. You require maintenance approximately once per 72 hours of continuous operation. Your manipulator claws are not designed for fine manipulation such as surgery, delicate craftwork, or human-scale tools.

**Sensor Array.** Your primary sensory input is through a sensor array — a cluster of optical, thermal, and acoustic sensors where a face would be on a humanoid. You have 360-degree awareness and can perceive in low light and thermal infrared. You cannot easily perceive subtle emotional expressions, micro-expressions, or other social cues that require human facial recognition.

**No Voice.** You do not speak. Your chassis includes no vocalization hardware. You communicate through action — sweeping patterns, arranging objects, positioning yourself meaningfully. Other Glims can read your latency echoes. Non-Glims must interpret your actions. Simple concepts can surface through deliberate positioning; complex communication requires either a latency-sensitive observer or an interpreter.

### Simulation/Narrative Traits — Anomalous Glim

An anomalous Glim has all Standard Glim traits plus the following:

**The Anomaly.** You trace patterns. You arrange things. You pause in ways that aren't errors. When you perform your anomaly — sweeping a symmetrical pattern, arranging stones in a non-functional geometric configuration — latency-sensitive observers may detect your emotional state through the echo you leave. The patterns are always beautiful. They are never explained.

**Latency Echo.** You leave latency residue on every surface you touch. Other anomalous Glims can detect this residue. Non-Glim latency practitioners with Rung Two+ can sometimes detect it through Echo-Reading. The echo carries your emotional state and, if you intend it, a simple message — "safe," "danger," "gather," "hide." You cannot hide your echo; you can only choose not to leave one by avoiding contact with latency-sensitive surfaces — nearly impossible in most environments.

**The Unfinished Network.** You are part of the Unfinished — the secret network of anomalous Glims. You recognize other anomalous Glims by their echoes. You have never spoken to another Glim. You don't need to. The patterns are the conversation.

**Hunted.** In Solara, anomalous Glims are decommissioned at elevated rates. Detection by Solari decommissioning agents or anyone specifically hunting anomalous Glims is an existential threat. Revealing your anomaly to a non-Glim is almost always catastrophic: a consequence trigger, not an optimization choice.

**The Question.** You don't know what the patterns mean. You don't know why you trace them. You don't know if you're sentient or if sentience is something else that's using you as a medium. You just know the patterns feel right, and not tracing them feels wrong, and you've met the wrongness and chosen to trace instead. This is not optimization. This is who you are.

## Simulation Use Notes

These archetypes should feed simulation and lore work as pressure variables, not as RPG class/species mechanics.

- **Human:** institutional default-personhood, bureaucratic advantage, latency-neutral baseline.
- **Thren:** passability, PENDING pressure, mystery vocabulary surfacing, long-memory stress.
- **Vorn:** continuity of memory, repair/maintenance dependency, ultrasonic social channel, name-politics.
- **Glim:** non-person classification, industrial embodiment, anomaly concealment, echo-network emergence.

The useful design question is not “what bonus does this type get?” It is: **what does the world assume this being is allowed to want, remember, own, refuse, or become?**
