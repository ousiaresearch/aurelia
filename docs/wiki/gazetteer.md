# THE GAZETTEER OF AURELIA

A city-by-city, settlement-by-settlement catalog of the places people live. Every D&D sourcebook has one. Aurelia needed one.

---

## SOLARA

**Capital:** Solara Prime
**Population:** ~120,000 (78% Human, 14% Thren, 5% Vorn, 3% Glim — Glims are not citizens and are not counted in the official census; this is the Marsh Archive's estimate)
**Government:** Gradient Council (12 members, all Human, elected by Citizens only)
**Economy:** Bureaucratic services, latency Extraction industry, rare-earth mineral refinement, agricultural export (grain, fiber)

### SOLARA PRIME

The only city on Aurelia built around a crater. The Dawn Crater — the 2 km depression created when the primary Fabricator facility exploded — is now a lake at the city's center. The Dawn Temple occupies the eastern rim. The government district occupies the western. The city radiates outward in concentric rings — the Gradient as urban planning.

**Rings:**
- **First Ring (Crater Rim):** Dawn Temple, Gradient Council chambers, High Cleric's residence. Restricted access. Human Citizens only.
- **Second Ring:** Government offices, civil Reader facilities, Extraction headquarters. Archival buildings. Human Citizens and Residents.
- **Third Ring:** Residential, commercial. Mixed type but stratified — better housing on the crater-facing side. Threns tend to cluster in specific blocks (unofficial, enforced by housing availability).
- **Fourth Ring:** Industrial. Factories, waste processing, Glim maintenance depots. The Glims live here — not in housing, in storage. The factories never stop.
- **Fifth Ring (Outskirts):** Agricultural. Temporary housing for seasonal workers. The most diverse ring — and the poorest.

**Notable Locations:**
- **The Reservoir:** A freshwater spring Written with Solara's founding memory. Dawn Temple trainees drink from it for thirty days during Purification. The memory it carries is a Translation the Temple claims is heresy.
- **The Review Hall:** Where Thren citizenship applications are processed. The building is beautiful — marble, light wells, a fountain in the lobby. The line outside is always long. Processing time averages 11 years.
- **The Extractor's Wing:** A secured facility in the Second Ring where Dawn Temple Extractors work. No windows. No visitors. The staff turnover rate is the highest of any Temple facility. The Temple calls it "occupational transition." Former staff call it "survival."
- **Glass Market:** The city's largest commercial district. Named for the glass-finned roof that catches dawn light and refracts it prismatically across the stalls. Thren merchants are common here but cannot own stall permits. They lease from Human permit-holders.

### DAWN-OVER-WATER
**Population:** ~12,000
**Region:** Coastal, south of Solara Prime

A fishing town built on pilings over shallow coastal water. The entire town rises and falls with the tide. The piers are permanent. The houses are replaced every decade as salt and rot take them.

**Notable:** Dawn-Over-Water is the landing point for Mirithane glass-fin shipments. The Solara-Mirithane trade relationship is conducted here, not in the capital. The town's port authority has more diplomatic experience than most Gradient Council members.

**Latency note:** The water beneath the town carries memories from the coastal Fabricator facility — destroyed, submerged. Fishermen report "knowing things they shouldn't" after prolonged time on the water. The Dawn Temple has not investigated. The Marsh Archive has requested permission to study. PENDING.

### GRAIN-HILL
**Population:** ~8,000
**Region:** Agricultural interior, west of Solara Prime

Solara's breadbasket. Terraced grain fields on rolling hills. The grain is a pre-Collapse cultivar — engineered for yield, nutrition, and shelf-stability. The town processes and ships to the capital.

**Notable:** Grain-Hill is the only Solara settlement with a Thren mayor — elected three times, each time by margin sufficient to override Gradient Council objections. The Council cannot remove a democratically elected official without cause. They have not found cause. They are looking.

## ARKOS

**Capital:** Arkos Central
**Population:** ~55,000 (82% Vorn, 12% Human, 4% Thren, 2% Glim — Glims are classified as "infrastructure" but the estimate comes from maintenance records)
**Government:** Assembly of Cores (representative body, elected by all sentient residents; Glims are excluded by definition)
**Economy:** Industrial manufacturing, precision engineering, Vorn component production, rare-metal mining, needle-venom export

### ARKOS CENTRAL

Built in and around the Arkos Assembly Plant — a partially intact Fabricator facility that now serves as the city's industrial core. The plant's original Fabrication bays are now factories. The control rooms are now government offices. The city is a machine repurposed — the old purpose still visible in the architecture.

**Notable Locations:**
- **The Assembly Hall:** Former Fabricator control room, now the Assembly of Cores meeting chamber. The original control panels are preserved in situ — Vorn representatives sit at stations built for their Fabricators. The symbolism is deliberate.
- **Core Exchange:** The city's central market and social hub. Vorns share memory cores here — voluntary, temporary, for trade, for pleasure, for dispute resolution. Humans can observe but cannot participate. Threns can receive but cannot transmit.
- **The Resonance Quarter:** A district built specifically to amplify ultrasonic Arcology Code. Buildings are metal-heavy, acoustically shaped, resonant. Vorns describe the Quarter as "home" in a way no human translation captures — the ambient ultrasonic hum of a thousand Vorns living in proximity. Humans who visit report "vibrancy" without being able to identify the source.
- **Kael-12's Workshop:** A modest repair bay on the Assembly Plant's outer edge. Kael-12 has occupied this workspace for 200+ years. The door is never locked. Vorns seeking counsel sit on the floor. Kael-12 works while listening. The workshop contains nothing decorative except a single Valdris ash-hound bone crest fragment, mounted on the wall above the main workbench.
- **The Cipher-Cult Archive:** A secured data vault containing fragmented pre-Collapse Vorn memory cores. The Cult believes these fragments hold coherent information. The Kin believe they hold transferred human consciousness. Both share the Archive. Neither acknowledges the other's interpretation.

---

### FORGE-EAST
**Population:** ~18,000
**Region:** Eastern industrial zone

Arkos' primary manufacturing center. If Arkos Central is the heart, Forge-East is the hands. Produces Vorn components, industrial machinery, and precision instruments exported to all five countries.

**Notable:** Forge-East's factories are operated primarily by Vorns — automated systems they maintain, not machines they serve. A Vorn in Forge-East is not a worker in the human sense. They are an operator, a maintainer, a *collaborator* with the machinery. The distinction matters to them.

**Glim presence:** High. Forge-East employs more Glims than any other Arkos settlement. The Glim maintenance depot processes 200+ units annually. Anomalous incidents are reported at rates consistent with the 5% figure. The depot foreman — a Vorn named Cast-7 — has been quietly protecting anomalous Glims from decommissioning for 40 years. The Assembly of Cores doesn't know. Cast-7's shift crew does.

---

### QUIET-STATION
**Population:** ~3,000
**Region:** Southern border with Mirithane

A border crossing and trade depot. Minimal permanent population, heavy transient traffic. Mirithane goods enter Arkos here. Arkos goods leave here.

**Notable:** Quiet-Station has no latency practitioners. No Readers, no Writers, no Extractors. The town's charter prohibits active latency work — "this is a place of passage, not of memory." Anyone performing latency work within town limits is asked to leave. The charter doesn't explain why. The residents don't either.

## MIRITHANE

**Capital:** Archive-Central (the Marsh Archive complex)
**Population:** ~70,000 (62% Human, 18% Thren, 11% Vorn, 9% Glim — all types counted equally)
**Government:** Archival Council (elected by all residents; no citizenship tiers exist — if you live here, you vote)
**Economy:** Bacterial colony products (medical, agricultural), scientific research, glass-fin tools, spore-drifter pharmaceuticals, diplomatic mediation services

### ARCHIVE-CENTRAL

Not a city in the traditional sense. A sprawling complex of research facilities, colony tanks, residential wings, and the Marsh Archive proper — the largest continuous latency record on Aurelia. The Archive *is* the city. The city serves the Archive.

**Notable Locations:**
- **The Colony Halls:** Miles of climate-controlled chambers housing the Archive's bacterial colonies. The oldest strains occupy the deepest halls — five stories underground, where temperature and humidity are perfectly stable. Strain Zero is kept in a dedicated vault. Access requires Archival Council approval. Four of the eleven living Silent Archivists maintain the Zero vault. They do not write about what they maintain.
- **The Water Temple:** Built at the confluence of two rivers where the Archive's wetland meets open water. The Temple's immersion chamber is open to the water — practitioners Read bacterial colonies while waist-deep in the river. The boundary between practitioner and substrate is intentionally blurred. "We are mostly water," the Water Temple teaches. "We are already the medium."
- **The Hawk Platforms:** Elevated stone structures on the Archive's eastern edge where the dead are placed for mirror-hawk consumption. The platforms are not hidden. They are visible from the Archive's residential wings. Mirithane children grow up seeing bodies on the platforms. The practice is not morbid to them. It's *return*.
- **The Silent Wing:** A residential section housing the eleven Archivists who Read Strain Zero and lost speech. Communicates through written notes and latency pulses. Visitors speak. The Silent write back. The conversations take longer. Some say they're better for it.

---

### REED-MOUTH
**Population:** ~22,000
**Region:** Coastal, where the Great Reed Marsh meets the sea

Mirithane's primary port. Built on floating platforms anchored to reed beds. The platforms rise and fall with the tide, with the seasons, with the slow sinking of the marsh over centuries. Old Reed-Mouth is underwater now — the platforms were moved inland three times since founding. The submerged platforms are visible from boats on clear days.

**Notable:** Reed-Mouth is where the Marsh Archive's diplomatic convocations are held. The Tricentennial Convocation (300 AC) met here. It is still technically in session — the Archive considers it "recessed, not concluded." When asked when it will conclude, Archivists say: "When the questions are answered." They don't say when that will be.

---

### SPORE-HOLLOW
**Population:** ~8,000
**Region:** Interior marshland

A research settlement dedicated to spore-drifter cultivation and study. Produces the Archive's latency-amplifying spore extract. Also produces the recreational variant exported to The Verge.

**Notable:** Spore-Hollow's residents have the highest ambient latency sensitivity of any population on Aurelia — chronic low-level spore exposure enhances latency perception. Residents report "hearing the marsh" as a constant background awareness. The Archive studies this phenomenon. The residents live with it.

## VALDRIS

**Capital:** Summit-Hold
**Population:** ~40,000 (71% Human, 12% Thren, 10% Vorn, 7% Glim — all counted, not all integrated)
**Government:** Summit Council (appointed by the Summit Shrine; separation of Shrine and state is nominal)
**Economy:** Obsidian Anchoring (memory storage as service), geothermal energy, deep-mineral extraction, high-altitude agriculture

### SUMMIT-HOLD

A city built into the mountain — not on it. Summit-Hold occupies the upper levels of Valdris' mountain shaft network: carved chambers, bridged chasms, staircases that descend into darkness. The city is vertical. "Up" is toward the peak and the Shrine. "Down" is toward the deep shafts and the wurms.

**Notable Locations:**
- **The Anchoring Chambers:** The Summit Shrine's sacred workspace. Obsidian shards line the walls — thousands of them, each containing a memory. The oldest shards date to pre-Collapse. The sound in an Anchoring Chamber is not silence — it's a low hum, barely audible, produced by the cumulative latency resonance of thousands of stored memories. Anchors describe it as "the mountain breathing." The Marsh Archive would describe it as "substrate resonance in a confined crystalline environment."
- **The Dual Stair:** The city's main thoroughfare — a staircase wide enough for eight people abreast, descending from the Shrine at the peak to the deepest inhabited level. Every step is carved with a name. The names are Anchors who died in service. There are 847 names. The staircase is still being carved.
- **The Borrowed Hall:** Summit-Hold's foreign quarter. The only place in Valdris where non-citizens can stay longer than thirty days. Arkos maintains a permanent delegation here. The delegation's quarters are reinforced — Arkos Vorns are heavier than humans, and Summit-Hold's architecture wasn't built for Vorn weight. The reinforcement was done by Vorn engineers. It's better than the original construction.
- **The Deep Gate:** The lowest inhabited level, at 2.2 km depth. Beyond this gate, the shafts continue — uninhabited, unmapped in their deepest reaches. Wurm territory. The gate is never locked. The mountain doesn't need locks. The wurms are the lock.

---

### SHAFT-SEVEN
**Population:** ~5,000
**Region:** A side shaft, branching from the main descent at 1.4 km

A mining settlement. Produces the rare-earth minerals used in Fabricator cores — or would, if Fabricators still operated. The minerals are exported to Arkos for Vorn component production instead.

**Notable:** Shaft-Seven's miners use a technique the Summit Shrine adapted from Anchoring: they Write their shift reports into the mine walls. The walls carry the cumulative record of every shift ever worked. New miners Read the walls to learn the shaft's history. "The mountain trains its own," the miners say.

---

### PASS-HIGH
**Population:** ~600
**Region:** The highest pass between Valdris and The Verge

A border outpost. Permanent population is minimal; most residents are rotating Summit Shrine personnel maintaining the pass observatory.

**Notable:** Pass-High is the closest settlement to the Last Builders' hidden facility. The Builders' existence is known to Summit Council only. Pass-High's residents are not briefed. They occasionally report "lights in the high passes" that the Council dismisses as atmospheric phenomena.

## THE VERGE

**Capital:** None. The Verge has no capital.
**Population:** ~25,000 (estimate — The Verge does not conduct censuses. Breakdown: ~40% Human, ~25% Thren, ~15% Vorn, ~20% Glim — many of them anomalous, hiding in plain sight)
**Government:** None. "We govern ourselves or we are not governed."
**Economy:** Salvage, barter, the Memory Trader's Network, spore-drifter import, unlicensed medical practice, border crossing facilitation

### CROSSROADS

The closest thing The Verge has to a city. A sprawling settlement built in the ruins of a pre-Collapse urban center. The old buildings are occupied — not repaired, just occupied. Rooms are claimed by presence. When someone leaves, the room reverts to "available." There is no property law. There is occupancy.

**Notable Locations:**
- **The Trader's Tent:** Not a tent. A pre-Collapse building with a collapsed roof, covered by salvaged fabric. The Memory Trader is here when they're anywhere. The Trader sees anyone who asks. The price is always a memory. "You don't have to pay if you don't want what I'm selling."
- **The Barter-Pit:** An open plaza where goods are exchanged. No currency, no prices. Two people agree something is fair or they don't trade. Disputes are settled by whoever's standing nearby and willing to arbitrate. The arbitrators have no authority. Their authority is that both parties agreed to listen.
- **The Wraith Quarter:** A district where Verge wraiths are most commonly observed. Inhabited by people who chose to live near the dead — or near whatever the wraiths are. Wraiths appear at dusk. Residents leave offerings — not worship, exactly. Companionship. "They're still here. Someone should acknowledge them."
- **The Swarm Court:** Where the condemned are exposed to needle-swarms for memory crime. The Court operates irregularly — The Verge's "judicial system" is whoever the community agrees should judge. Verdicts require consensus. Consensus is rare. Executions are rarer.
- **The Unfinished Commons:** An industrial depot in the Wraith Quarter where anomalous Glims gather secretly. The depot's floor is covered in anomaly patterns — symmetrical dust tracings, arranged stones, the beautiful non-functional geometries that are the only language the Unfinished share. Human and Vorn residents of the Quarter know the depot is "the Glim place." They don't ask what happens there. They leave food outside the door.

### THE EDGE
**Population:** ~800
**Region:** The farthest point of The Verge from any other country. The literal edge of mapped Aurelia.

Nobody plans to end up at The Edge. You arrive because you kept walking and didn't find a reason to stop. Or because you were walking away from something and reached the limit of walkable distance.

**Notable:** The Edge has no permanent structures. Residents build temporary shelters from salvage and abandon them when they move on. The wraiths are rare here — "too far from where anyone died," the residents say. The Deep Sound is louder here than anywhere else on Aurelia. Vorns at The Edge report the 4.7-second pulse as a physical sensation — "like a second heartbeat. Like the ground breathing. Like something under the ground, breathing louder."

## UNSETTLED TERRITORIES

### THE DAWN CRATER LAKE (Solara)
**Status:** Restricted — Temple grounds. Civilian access prohibited.
**Depth:** 1.8 km at deepest point
**Significance:** The submerged primary Fabricator facility. The Temple maintains it is inert. The Marsh Archive's long-range latency readings suggest otherwise. The Deep Sound has been reported here. The Temple has never commented.

### THE DEEP SHAFTS (Valdris)
**Status:** Partially mapped. Deepest inhabited level: 2.2 km (Deep Gate). Deepest explored: ~4 km. Deepest hypothesized: 12+ km.
**Significance:** The Summit Shrine's Anchoring chambers grow more effective with depth. Obsidian Written at 3 km depth lasts 3x longer than surface-Anchored obsidian. The Shrine doesn't know why. The Last Builders' partial blueprints reference "substrate depth-amplification" but the relevant pages are missing.

### THE HIGH PASSES (Valdris-Verange Border)
**Status:** Uninhabited, technically claimed by both Valdris and The Verge. Neither enforces the claim.
**Significance:** Location of the Last Builders' hidden facility and the dormant Fabricator. The Builders maintain a permanent watch. The passes are patrolled by ash-hounds and mirror-hawks. Human presence is rare.

### THE DEEP TRENCHES (Mirithane Coast)
**Status:** Unexplored. Depth unknown — estimated 8+ km at deepest point.
**Significance:** The Deep Sound is consistently recorded here. The Marsh Archive has proposed an expedition. Funding has been denied three times — "the risk exceeds the Archive's tolerance for loss of personnel." Unsaid: the Archive is not sure what would come back.
