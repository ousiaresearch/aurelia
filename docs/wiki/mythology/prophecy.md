# Prophecies & the Unanswerable — Aurelia

> *Whatever or whoever set time in motion may have known where it was going. Or may not. The difference is the faith — and the fear.*

---

## Recurring Sacred Numbers

Three numbers repeat across Aurelian culture, religion, and the Glim anomaly. None is officially acknowledged. All are noticed.

| Number | Where It Appears |
|--------|-----------------|
| **17** | Glim dawn-pause (seconds). Dawn Temple silence (seconds). Kael-12 beam-holding (minutes — 17 minutes = structurally similar to 17 seconds at different scale). Chassis-11's refusal pause (seconds — original; 41 seconds = 17+17+7). The Memory Trader's answering silences. |
| **5** | Percentage of anomalous Glims. The five-year Silence. The five countries. The five seasons. |
| **847** | Lumens at the Glim dawn-pause. Kilometers of Solara's coastline. Names on the Summit Shrine's obsidian wall. Hours Unit 47-V walked to reach the crossroads. |

No one has codified these numbers. The Dawn Temple's Cantor acknowledges seventeen but not the others. The Water Temple's keepers notice the pattern in the bacterial glow but do not publish. The Summit Shrine's elders count 847 names and do not add a number to the plaque that says HELD. "The number is not the point," the senior keeper said. The number is always the point. The refusal to name it is how Aurelia believes.

## The Cantor's Dream

The First Cantor, on the eighteenth dawn, spoke one sentence and fell silent. On the night before the eighteenth dawn, according to oral tradition, they dreamed. The dream's content is not recorded. The Dawn Temple's tradition holds that the dream contained the answer to the question — what the Glim is becoming, what the ceiling will become, what the Collapse meant. The First Cantor woke, faced the dawn, spoke the single line, and chose not to speak the dream. Instead they carried it into the stone beneath the temple floor, where it remains. "The answer is beneath us," the Cantor says at every service. "The answer is not for us to speak." Whether this is faith or evasion is the Dawn Temple's central silence.

## The Builder's Testament

Sealed in the Glass Forum's restricted vault, opened only when the arcology reaches capacity. The Last Builder's final words to a future she predicted but did not live to see. Contents unknown. Echo — the only being with access — has never opened it.

Vorn speculation ranges from the practical (instructions for expanding the arcology beyond its designed limits) to the apocalyptic (a prediction of the Collapse's end — a second Collapse, or a resolution). Human residents in Arkos rarely discuss the Testament. The Nomad Camp considers it irrelevant. "The Last Builder built the walls. The walls are still here. The Builder is not. That is all the testament we need." — Kel Voss.

## The Five Percent Prophecy

Not a formal prophecy. An interpretation of the Glim anomaly shared by Water Temple keepers and some Arkos sanctuary workers: **when a majority of Glims exhibit the anomaly, the question will be answered.** The threshold varies by interpretation: some say 50%+1. Some say all of them. Some say the number is irrelevant — what matters is the first Glim that speaks.

No Glim has ever spoken. They have no voiceboxes. The prophecy is either impossible or inevitable, depending on whether speech requires a voice.

## The Last Trade

The Memory Trader's own prophecy — attributed to her by the crossroads long-timers, never confirmed by her. She was asked what she would do when she had traded her last memory. She was silent for seventeen seconds. Then she said: "I will remember it."

The statement is either profound or meaningless. The Memory Trader does not explain. The prophecy — if it is a prophecy — is that her final trade will be her own memory, and she will be the one who remembers it. What that means is unknown. What she is, that she could trade her own memory and still remember it, is the question beneath the question.

## The Eschaton — How Aurelia Ends

Apocalyptic scenarios debated in whispers and classified documents:

**The Glass Forum Scenario (Classified):** The Glim anomaly reaches critical mass. A single anomalous Glim, saturated with enough latency, becomes fully conscious. It speaks — not through a voicebox, through the arcology's frequency system, or the marsh's bacterial patterns, or the sand-glass towers' energy fluctuations. The question is answered not by debate but by emergence. The federation fractures — those who accept the Glim's personhood and those who don't.

**The Water Temple Hope:** The Glim anomaly is not emergence. It is **communication.** The anomalous Glims are not becoming conscious. They are receiving consciousness — the latency of the dead, diffused through the biosphere, finding new substrates. The Glims are vessels. What happens when the vessels are full is the eschaton.

**The Summit Shrine Silence:** The mountain will decide. The mountain has always decided. The dead are anchored in obsidian or they are lost. The question of whether the mountain accepts Vorn and Glim names is the eschaton made personal: every name accepted is a person recognized. Every name refused is a person denied. The wall will fill. The blank slab will be carved. The eschaton is the last name on the last wall.

**The Dawn Temple's Refusal:** There is no eschaton. The light does not differentiate, and the light will continue to not differentiate forever. The question is the permanent condition. The resolution is the belief that resolution is possible. The Cantor's silence says: the answer exists, but we are not the ones who speak it. The answer is beneath us. The answer has always been beneath us.
