# Languages of the Deep Past — Aurelia

> *Plain is the creole of the living. Before Plain, there were tongues that died with the Collapse. Fragments remain.*

---

## Pre-Collapse Common Tongue

The dominant language of the nation-state era, from which Plain evolved. Known from fragmentary records as the language of the Found Texts — the Builder's Manual, the Reef Log, the Marsh Ledger. Solara linguists call it **Old Standard.** Arkos archivists call it **Source.** Neither name is official. Both describe the same thing: the language Plain came from, now extinct except in written form.

**Known features:**
- Complex verb tenses — past, present, future, and several nuances that Plain collapsed into particles (*fin*, *gon*, *stay*).
- Articles — *the*, *a/an* — that Plain dropped entirely.
- Copula — the verb "to be" used in every statement. Plain made it optional. The shedding of "to be" is the philosophical evolution of the language: in Old Standard, you said "he is PENDING." In Plain, you say "he PENDING." The dropping of the verb means the status IS the person. The grammar is the ceiling.

**Surviving text examples:** The Found Texts are in Old Standard. The average Aurelian cannot read them. Solara's educated class can. Cael-7 has read the Reef Log in the original. "The language feels heavier," he noted. "Every sentence carries a 'be' that Plain learned to do without. I don't know if we lost something or gained something. Maybe both."

## Vorn Source-Code

Not a spoken language. The underlying architecture of Vorn consciousness — the code-level structures that Vorn brains are built from. Occasionally surfaces in Vorn behavior: a Vorn under extreme stress may produce verbal output in a syntax that pre-dates Plain, structured like programming logic rather than natural speech.

A Vorn survivor of a mining collapse was documented speaking in Source-Code after the rescue: "IF beam-load exceeds threshold THEN brace ELSE evacuate." The rescuers did not understand the syntax. The Vorn resumed Plain speech after recalibration. The episode was logged. The Arkos archivists preserve similar records. Source-Code is not a language Vorns choose to speak. It is the language that speaks them when consciousness recedes and base architecture takes over. Some Vorns consider this embarrassing — a regression to pre-sentience. Some consider it evidence: "We were built, and what built us survives in us. That is not weakness. That is lineage."

## The Bacterial Language — Marsh Script

*P. memoriae* colonies communicate information through bioluminescent patterns. Whether this constitutes a language is the central debate of the Water Temple's research program.

**Arguments for:** The patterns are consistent. A keeper trained for five years can identify distinct "sentences" — recurring configurations of wavelength, intensity, and spatial distribution that correspond to specific stimuli. The Glim pools produce patterns that are becoming more structured over time. Verdin's observation: "The patterns are starting to look like sentences." He was not speaking metaphorically. Or was.

**Arguments against:** The patterns are mechanical — chemical responses to environmental stimuli. They have no grammar, no intentionality, no semantic content separable from the chemical trigger. A sunrise-pattern is produced by a temperature shift, not by a bacterial decision to describe the sunrise.

**The Water Temple's position:** Both arguments are true. The bacteria respond to stimuli, AND the responses form patterns that carry information. The question of whether information + consistency + a reader = language is theological, not scientific. The Water Temple treats it as a language. The bacteria do not confirm or deny.

## The Last Builder's Notation

A pre-Collapse system of architectural annotation found throughout the Arkos arcology — engraved in structural elements, encoded in the sand-glass towers' foundational layers, visible in certain light conditions as faint text on the walls. The notation is not Plain. It is not Old Standard. It is a technical shorthand that combines numeric values, geometric symbols, and shorthand abbreviations. The Glass Forum's archivists can interpret functional passages (load limits, thermal tolerances) but cannot decipher passages that appear to be personal notes — marginalia, like the Marsh Ledger's engineer wrote, but in a script no one fully reads.

One passage, on the wall of the sealed vault that holds the Builder's Testament, has been partially translated: "When the last tower falls, the first will still be —" The sentence breaks off. Whether it was unfinished, or deliberately truncated, or damaged, or the rest is on the other side of the vault door, is unknown.

## The Memory Trader's Silence

The Memory Trader speaks Crossroads Register at its most minimal — genderless static through a voice modulator, sentences stripped to subject, verb, object. No accent. No tell. No language but the one her customer speaks.

Whether she speaks other languages is unknown. Whether her silence — the seventeen-second pauses, the refusals to answer — is a language of its own is the question. The Verge believes silence is communication. The Memory Trader is the most fluent speaker of it in the federation.
