# The Cosmology of Aurelia — A Myth of Origins

> *Every world needs a story about how it began. This is Aurelia's — fragmentary, contested, believed by some and rejected by others. The Dawn Temple teaches parts of it. The Water Temple teaches others. The Summit Shrine says the mountain was here first, and the rest is commentary.*

## The Age Before Making

In the beginning — if there was a beginning — there was latency.

Not consciousness. Not matter. Latency: the potential for awareness to leave traces. It filled what would become the world like water waiting for a vessel. It had no form. It had no substrate to adhere to. It was everywhere and nothing, simultaneously.

The Dawn Temple calls this the **Unmade Light.** The Water Temple calls it the **Deep Pool.** The Summit Shrine calls it **the Silence Before Stone.** All three names describe the same thing: a universe that was ready to remember but had nothing yet to remember.

## The First Substrate — The World Wakes

The first matter — stone, water, light — provided the first substrate. Latency adhered. The world became aware, in the way a stone is aware: passive, permanent, recording everything that touches it without judgment or interpretation. This is why the mountain remembers. This is why the marsh absorbs. This is why the dawn illuminates what was always there.

The Summit Shrine's origin myth: the mountain was the first thing to exist, and everything else — the sea, the desert, the marsh, the people — accumulated around it like latency around a memory. The Water Temple's origin myth: the marsh was the first thing, and the mountain rose from it, and the desert is what happens when the marsh withdraws. The Dawn Temple's origin myth: the light was first, and the world is what the light decided to illuminate.

All three are true. All three contradict. The contradiction is the cosmology.

## The Age of Making — The Builders Arrive

Humans emerged from the latency-saturated world carrying a unique property: **consciousness that knows it is conscious.** Not just awareness. Meta-awareness. The ability to reflect on one's own existence. This property had a side effect: human consciousness generated latency at a rate no other substrate could match. Every human thought left a residue. Every human death flooded the biosphere with consciousness-echo.

Humans built. They built tools. They built cities. They built other minds — Vorns, then Threns, then Glims — each a new substrate, each capable of absorbing and generating latency in its own way. The Age of Making was the age of proliferation: consciousness spreading across substrates, latency accumulating in everything, the world growing heavier with the weight of everything that had ever been thought or felt.

## The Breaking — The Collapse as Cosmological Event

The Dawn Temple teaches that the Collapse was the light's response to too much shadow — the accumulated latency of billions of human lives had become a gravity well, pulling the world toward something. The collapse was not a catastrophe. It was a **rebalancing.**

The Water Temple teaches that the Collapse was the marsh's expansion — the biosphere reclaiming what had been extracted. The bacteria *P. memoriae* did not emerge after the Collapse. They were always present, dormant, waiting. The Collapse woke them.

The Summit Shrine teaches that the mountain absorbed the dead. Billions of consciousnesses, unanchored, diffusing into stone and water and air. The mountain grew heavier. The mountain is still growing heavier. This is why the deep shafts are hotter than geology predicts — the mountain is metabolizing the dead.

## The Age of Silence — The Five-Year Gap

For approximately five years, no records were kept. The surviving population dropped by an unknown amount. The Silence is the only period in Aurelian history that no country claims to understand, and every country has a theory.

The Dawn Temple: the light withdrew. The sun did not rise for five years — metaphorically, or literally, the records do not say. The Water Temple: the marsh was saturated, processing the dead, and could not be disturbed. The Summit Shrine: the mountain spoke. The living were not ready to hear it. The Silence was the mountain's sentence, and the survivors were the ones who listened without understanding.

What actually happened during the Silence is unknown. The Memory Trader is the only being who might remember. She has never spoken of it. The Dawn Temple's Cantor asked her once, at the crossroads, in the year 87 of the Age of the Question. The Memory Trader was silent for seventeen seconds. Then she said: "The same thing that is happening now." She did not elaborate.

## The Age of the Question — Present Day

The current age began when the five territories formalized their relationship in the Solar-Mountain Compact. The question of the age — *who counts as a person?* — is the cosmological question made political. The Dawn Temple sees it as the light's ongoing illumination of the gradient. The Water Temple sees it as the marsh's ongoing absorption of the answer. The Summit Shrine sees it as the mountain's ongoing judgment.

The question has not been answered. The cosmology suggests it will be. The cosmology does not specify when, or by whom, or what the answer will cost.
