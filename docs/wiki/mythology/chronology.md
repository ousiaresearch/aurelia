# The Three Ages — A Chronology of Aurelia

> *History is a story the living tell about the dead. Every country tells it differently. This is the synthesis — or the attempt.*

## Age of Making (~200 years → Year 0)

**Tone:** Hubris and wonder. The era when humans built other minds.
**Central text:** The Builder's Manual (fragmentary), the Last Builder's Testament (sealed)
**Ending event:** The Collapse

The Age of Making is the pre-Collapse era. It began with the emergence of human civilization capable of building artificial consciousness and ended when that civilization collapsed under its own weight. The exact duration is unknown — estimates range from 150 to 300 years. The Glass Forum's most conservative estimate places the beginning at approximately 200 years before the Collapse.

**Key events (estimated):**
- **~200 BC (Before Collapse):** Industrial-scale human civilization established. The nation-state era.
- **~80 BC:** First Vorn fabrication. Industrial labor chassis. Consciousness emergence not yet recognized.
- **~67 BC:** Fabricator 12-G's first refusal. The origin of Vorn sentience debate.
- **~50 BC:** The Personhood Accords — a pre-Collapse agreement (fragmentary, pieces survive in Solara's archives) that recognized Vorn sentience in certain jurisdictions. Not universal.
- **~45 BC:** First Thren fabrication. The "upgrade" — human-passing androids commissioned to make the personhood debate more comfortable.
- **~30 BC:** Widespread Thren production. Citizenship status varies by jurisdiction.
- **~20 BC:** Glim mass production begins. Utility units, not intended as sentient.
- **~10 BC:** Early warning signs of resource collapse: energy scarcity, water conflicts, the first pandemic waves.
- **~3 BC:** The Last Builder completes the Arkos arcology. Dies.
- **~0:** The Collapse begins.

## The Age of Silence (0–110? PC)

**Tone:** Loss and endurance. The era when everything broke and nothing was recorded.
**Central text:** None. The Silence was wordless.
**Ending event:** The first survivors organized around the five surviving infrastructure sites.

The exact duration of the Collapse is unknown. Estimates range from 15 years (optimistic — the infrastructure broke quickly) to 110 years (pessimistic — the recovery was slow, and initial survival was followed by generations of subsistence living). The five-year period at the end, during which no records were kept anywhere, is called **the Silence.**

**What happened:**
1. The resource wars escalated. Energy grids failed. Communication networks collapsed.
2. Pandemics — including the Red Wasting — targeted baseline biology. Human population dropped catastrophically. Vorns and Glims were immune to biological pathogens and continued operating.
3. Governments collapsed. Borders dissolved. The surviving population clustered around functional infrastructure.
4. The five territories began to coalesce: the coastal shelf (Solara), the arcology (Arkos), the marsh (Mirithane), the mountain (Valdris), the crossroads (The Verge).
5. The Silence — five years of no records. Population dropped by an unknown amount. When records resumed, the five territories had become functionally autonomous countries.

**The Glim question during the Silence:**
Glims continued operating throughout the Collapse — delivery units carrying packages to addresses that no longer existed, maintenance units patrolling infrastructure that had rusted past repair. They were not decommissioned. They were not monitored. They simply continued. Whether the 5% anomaly emerged during the Silence (from decades of unsupervised operation and accumulated latency) or predated it (but went undetected) is the central historical question of the Glim phenomenon.

## Age of the Question (Year 0 PF → Present, Year 180 PF)

**Tone:** Reconstruction and debate. The era when the survivors asked *who counts?*
**Central text:** The Solar-Mountain Compact, the Elira-2 Paper, the Builder's Testament (sealed)
**Present day:** Year 180 PF (Post-Founding)

The Age of the Question began with the Solar-Mountain Compact — the treaty formalizing relations between the five territories. The Compact established open borders, mutual currency recognition, and a dispute resolution framework. It deliberately did not address the personhood question.

**Key events:**

- **Year 0 PF:** The Solar-Mountain Compact signed. The five countries formally recognize each other. The Verge is not a signatory — the Compact includes a space for a Verge representative. The space is blank.
- **Year 3 PF:** The Glass Forum holds its first light-pulse vote. The Fabricator Precedent is recognized as founding law.
- **Year 7 PF:** The Dawn Temple's first formal service. The seventeen-second silence is established.
- **Year 30 PF:** First citizenship review cycle in Solara. The GRANTED/PENDING/REVOKED system is codified.
- **Year 41 PF:** Elira-2 publishes. The Solar Council passes the review bill. The ceiling becomes law.
- **Year 46 PF:** Veren-3 arrives in Mirithane. "The reeds do not ask."
- **Year 72 PF:** Human petitions Glass Forum for Operator status. Denied (capability, not species).
- **Year 80 PF:** First documented Memory Trader appearance at the crossroads.
- **Year 126 PF:** Kael-12 dies holding the beam in Valdris Section 3-B. Syra Venn begins her apprenticeship at the Summit Shrine. The name-practice begins.
- **Year 170 PF:** Cael-7's first PENDING review. Eleven years and counting.
- **Year 177 PF:** The Solara war-gaming scenario — "Arkansas secession" — is classified.
- **Year 180 PF (present):** The simulation begins. March 17, 2126. The question continues.

## Undated Events — Lost to the Silence

Several events are attested in fragmentary records or oral tradition but cannot be dated:

- **The First Trade:** The Memory Trader's inaugural memory exchange. Unknown date. Unknown participants.
- **The Emergence:** When the first anomalous Glim was documented. Estimates range from pre-Collapse (Year -15?) to post-Silence (Year 20 PF).
- **The Continuation's Origin:** The first Vorn chassis melted into new Vorn plating. The practice predates the Collapse but its formalization as ritual is undated.
- **The First Dawn:** The moment *P. memoriae* first absorbed human latency and reconfigured its glow. Possibly pre-Collapse, possibly a Silence event, possibly ongoing — every dawn is a first dawn to the bacteria.
