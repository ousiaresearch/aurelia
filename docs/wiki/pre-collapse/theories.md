# The Collapse — Reconstructions & Theories

> *Nobody knows exactly what ended the nation-state era. Everyone has a theory. The theories say more about the theorist than the event.*

## Solara's Theory — Systemic Failure

The Solar Council's official history: the nation-states collapsed due to resource exhaustion, climate instability, and cascading infrastructure failures. No single cause. No single villain. A system that consumed more than it produced, until the debt came due. This theory is convenient for Solara — the Solar Council's governance is built on managing finite resources. The Collapse proves that mismanagement is fatal. The council uses this argument in every energy allocation debate.

## Arkos's Theory — Human Failure

The Glass Forum's position, never stated officially but embedded in every Archivist's presentation: the nation-states collapsed because human governance prioritizes short-term political gain over long-term systemic stability. Vorn governance — data-driven, efficiency-focused, unsentimental — would not have made the same errors. The theory is not stated as accusation. The theory is stated as data. "Humans governed the pre-Collapse world," the Archivist's standard presentation notes. "The pre-Collapse world collapsed." The two statements are adjacent. The adjacency is the argument.

## Mirithane's Theory — Ecological Release

The Water Temple's theology: the Collapse was the biosphere's immune response. The nation-states were a systemic infection — consuming, extracting, poisoning. The Collapse burned the infection out. What survived was what the biosphere could tolerate. Humans survived in reduced numbers. Threns, Vorns, and Glims survived because the biosphere did not recognize them as threats. The theory is not ecological analysis. The theory is faith. The faith does not require evidence.

## Valdris's Theory — The Mountain Remembers

Valdris has no official theory. The mountain was here before the Collapse. The mountain will be here after. The miners' working songs mention the Collapse only in passing — "Shaft-3 at 800 meters. Pre-Collapse bore. Watch the left wall." The destruction is just another layer of stone. The mountain does not rank catastrophes.

## The Verge's Theory — None

The Verge does not theorize about the past. The Verge is what happened to the people the past produced. The Memory Trader was asked once, by a customer at her stall: "What caused the Collapse?" She was silent for seventeen seconds. Then she said: "The same thing that causes everything." She did not specify.
