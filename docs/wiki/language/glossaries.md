# Glossaries — Slang, Register, & Forms of Address

> *How you speak tells the listener who you are, where you're from, and whether you believe the person you're addressing is a person.*
>

## Dialect Markers by Country

If you hear an Aurelian speak three sentences, you can identify their country:

### Solara Markers
- Articles sometimes used ("the council," "the question")
- Formal register available on demand — code-switching to Council Register is a class marker
- Vowels elongated in formal speech, clipped in casual
- "The question" means the personhood debate; "the light" means the Dawn Temple's position, or the absence of one
- Vocabulary heavy with administrative and scientific terminology

### Arkos Markers
- Clipped consonants, minimal vowels
- Vorn technical terms used naturally in human speech if the speaker is arcology-born ("nominal," "calibrate," "watt-debt")
- Pauses between sentences are slightly longer — Vorn speech pattern influencing human cadence
- "The machine" means the arcology. "The wall" means the arcology's boundary or the Nomad Camp — context-dependent

### Mirithane Markers
- Soft consonants, sibilants merging with ambient sound
- "They" used for all sentient beings regardless of type
- Ecological vocabulary extensive — water states, bacterial patterns, reed phases
- Sentence endings rise slightly, not as questions, as openness to response

### Valdris Markers
- Archaic verb forms in oldest families ("thou art," "I be")
- Laconic — minimal words, maximum meaning
- "The mountain" used as agent in sentences: "The mountain decided," "The mountain remembers"
- Guild titles precede names in formal address

### Verge Markers
- Articles and copulas dropped
- Sentences minimal, elliptical
- "Still-moving" used as status, greeting, and farewell
- The Memory Trader's speech flattens to absolute minimum — genderless, accentless, tell-less

## Slang by Country

### Solara

**"Ceiling-clean"** — Compliant with the unwritten rules. Also: a Thren who dresses to hide their circuitry. "He's ceiling-clean today — long sleeves, high collar, the full pass."

**"Sun-blind"** — Willfully ignorant of the ceiling's existence. "She's sun-blind — votes for expansion every cycle but thinks the system works."

**"Chromer"** — A human who is excessively deferential to Vorns. Mild insult. "The council delegate is a chromer — apologizes to the Arkos rep for everything."

**"Batch-sib"** — A Thren from the same fabrication batch. Close bond, chosen or not. "Cael-7 and Cael-3 are batch-sibs — fabricated same month."

### Arkos

**"Watt-head"** — Someone obsessed with data to the exclusion of human factors. Can be admiring or insulting. "Echo's a watt-head — she'll tell you the arcology's pulse rate but not what she felt during it."

**"Wall-side"** — Living inside the arcology. "Kel's wall-side parents still water the basil." Contrast with "camp-side" — living in the Nomad Camp.

**"Glassed"** — Evaluated by the Glass Forum, usually unfavorably. "His allocation request got glassed — insufficient efficiency metrics."

**"Sand-born"** — Born in the desert, outside the arcology. A point of pride in the Nomad Camp. Neutral elsewhere.

### Mirithane

**"Marsh-mouth"** — Someone who talks too much. The marsh values silence. "He's marsh-mouth — consensus took six hours because he wouldn't stop."

**"Reed-fresh"** — New to Mirithane. Not derogatory. "They're reed-fresh — still adjusting to the water in their platform."

**"Deep-glow"** — Profound. Authentic. "That remembering was deep-glow — everyone cried, including the Vorn."

**"Pool-sib"** — Someone you've shared a Glim pool vigil with. Bond formed through witnessing the bacterial patterns together.

### Valdris

**"Summit-broke"** — Exhausted. Physically or emotionally at limit. "After the third shift I was summit-broke — couldn't feel my legs."

**"Anvil-head"** — Stubborn. Also: reliable. "Orin's an anvil-head — he'll finish the shift even if the mountain cracks."

**"Deep-dusted"** — Old. Worn. Also: experienced. "Kael-9's still factory-shiny. He's not deep-dusted yet."

**"Name-hungry"** — Someone who wants their name on the wall before they've earned it. "Don't be name-hungry — the mountain remembers you regardless."

### The Verge

**"Crossed"** — Arrived at the crossroads. Also: reached a decision. "She crossed three winters ago — hasn't left."

**"Scraped"** — Removed identification. "He's scraped — no name, no history, no council." Etymology: Glims scraping off their unit IDs.

**"Trader-silent"** — Refusing to answer a question. "I asked about the collapse and he went trader-silent — not a word."

**"Door-wide"** — Available. Open. "The stew pot's door-wide — eat what you need."

## Forms of Address & Honorifics

### Formal Address by Country

**Solara:**
- Title + family name: "Majority Leader Aldaine," "Council Member Kael"
- Threns addressed by designation in formal contexts: "Director Cael-7"
- No honorific for Glims. The council does not address Glims.

**Arkos:**
- Designation for Vorns: "Archivist Chassis-7," "Delegate Gear-29"
- Chosen name for Vorns in trust only. Using a chosen name in formal address is a violation unless the Vorn has publicly authorized it.
- Humans addressed by given name in formal contexts: "Delegate Kel" (if Kel were a delegate — he would decline)
- Glims addressed by informal sanctuary name if they have one, or by unit ID if not.

**Mirithane:**
- Role over name: "Keeper Ash," "Rememberer Verdin," "Elder Arek"
- Names optional. Some Mirithaneans use no name — "I am a reed-tender"
- No honorific hierarchy — "Elder" indicates experience, not authority

**Valdris:**
- Guild title + family name: "Foreman Kael," "Keeper Venn"
- Vorns in guilds receive guild title + line designation: "Foreman Kael" for Kael-9
- No honorific in casual address. "Orin" not "Foreman Kael" unless formal context.

**The Verge:**
- Whatever the person introduces themselves as
- The Memory Trader calls everyone "you"

### Type-Specific Honorifics

**"Elder"** — Human title, all countries. Indicates age and community standing. "Elder Arek" in Mirithane. "Elder Keeper" at the Summit Shrine.

**"First"** — Vorn honorific, Arkos and Valdris. Reserved for the oldest active Vorn in a guild or institution. "First Archivist Chassis-7" is the formal title for Echo, though she never uses it.

**"Batch-Lead"** — Thren honorific, Solara and Mirithane. The senior Thren of a fabrication batch, informally recognized. Not official. Not recognized by the council.

**No honorific for Glims.** The absence is the status.

## Taboo Speech

**In Solara:** Never ask a Thren "when will your review be complete?" Never ask. The review is never complete. The question is the violence.

**In Arkos:** Never use a Vorn's chosen name without being offered it. Never refer to the Nomad Camp as "outside" — it implies the arcology is the center. The camp is outside the walls. The desert is outside the camp. The center depends on where you're standing.

**In Mirithane:** Never ask "who's in charge?" There is no one in charge. The question reveals you as an outsider. Never use "it" for a Glim. Mirithane uses "they."

**In Valdris:** Never speak the name of a dead miner before the anvil cascade completes. Never ask to carve a name on the obsidian wall — the mountain decides, the elders interpret, the asking is presumption.

**In The Verge:** Never ask the Memory Trader what she is. Never ask what she wants. You know what she wants. Everyone knows. "To be forgotten." The question is the thing she's trying to forget.

## The Register of the Council

Solara's Council Register is the most formal variant of Plain in the federation. Its features:

- Articles restored: "The council has considered the question" — not "Council considered question"
- Passive voice preferred for decisions the council would rather not own: "The review status was determined to remain PENDING" — not "We voted to keep it PENDING"
- Thren designations used exclusively — no chosen names, no "Director" without the designation appended: "Director Cael-7"
- Glim unit IDs used exclusively — no informal sanctuary names
- The word "citizenship" appears. The word "personhood" does not.

Speaking Council Register is a performance of authority. Performing it well grants access to power. Threns who master Council Register are considered "articulate" — a compliment that implies the expectation was otherwise. The implication is never stated. The implication is the register.
