# Verge Pidgin — The Crossroads Speech

> *The language of people who arrived with nothing. Nobody speaks it natively. Everyone at the crossroads understands.*

## Origin

Verge Pidgin is not a language in the traditional sense. It is a **contact language** — a fusion of all tongues spoken at the crossroads, simplified for mutual intelligibility. Its first documented form dates to early post-Silence, when survivors from different territories gathered at the crossroads and needed to communicate. The pidgin evolved. It continues to evolve. Every new arrival adds vocabulary. Every departure removes nothing.

## Substrate Languages

Verge Pidgin draws from:
- **Plain** (dominant substrate — vocabulary and basic grammar)
- **High Valdris** (structural influence — verb-final tendency, evidentiality particles)
- **Reed-Tongue** (ecological vocabulary — water states, silence words)
- **Arcology Code** (technical vocabulary — borrowed Vorn terms, stripped of ultrasonic subtext)
- **Thren Fabrica** (trace vocabulary — words for fabrication, calibration, memory)
- **Unknown sources** (words that arrived with Verge residents whose origin languages are extinct)

## Grammar

Verge Pidgin has stripped grammar to the minimum required for survival and trade:

- **One tense:** The present. Past is indicated by context or by the particle *fin* (borrowed from Plain). Future is indicated by *gon* (borrowed from Plain) or, more commonly, by not indicating it at all. "The Verge doesn't do future," a long-timer says. "Future is for people who know they're staying."
- **One copula:** *Stay.* Means "is/was/will be/might be/nobody knows." "She stay at the crossroads" means she is at the crossroads, and was, and will be until she isn't, and nobody knows when that is. The copula's ambiguity is the Pidgin's philosophy.
- **No articles.** Context determines definiteness. "Glim at crossroads" means THE Glim, the one everyone knows. The Pidgin does not need to specify.
- **Evidentiality marker:** Borrowed from High Valdris — a single particle (*seen*) that indicates direct observation. "Beam cracked, seen" means the speaker witnessed it. Without *seen*, the statement carries no truth claim. Verge Pidgin does not assume statements are true. The speaker must assert truth explicitly.
- **No negation particle.** Negation is indicated by a head shake while speaking, or by the word *none* placed after the negated element. "She trader none" means she is not the Memory Trader. The absence of a grammatical negation is a Verge philosophy: "Nothing here is a no. Nothing here is a yes. Everything is what it is until it isn't."

## The Memory Trader's Pidgin

The Memory Trader speaks the most minimal Verge Pidgin on record. Her sentences average three words. She never uses the evidentiality marker — she has never claimed to have witnessed anything, or she claims nothing, or she is beyond claiming.

Sample utterances recorded by crossroads observers:
- "Memory. Trade. You give. I give." — The standard trade offer.
- "Sunrise. Glim seen. Seventeen." — An observation. She has never explained why she counted.
- "Door close. Dawn. Go." — The stall closing message. No elaboration.
- "You forget. I remember. Trade done." — The completion of a transaction.

She also speaks whatever language her customer speaks — shifting to Solari High Speech, Reed-Tongue, or Plain seamlessly. The shift is unremarked. The shift is unnerving. "She speaks your language better than you do," a customer said. "She speaks it like someone who learned it before you were born. Or like someone who learned it from you, just now, by listening to you breathe."
