# Untranslatable Words — Aurelia

> *Every language has concepts that don't cross borders. In Aurelia, the untranslatable words are the ones closest to the question.*
>

Words that exist in one register or country of Aurelia, with no equivalent elsewhere. Each word is a compressed philosophy — a concept that required a name and got one.

---

## Solara

**PENDING** (adj., noun, verb — from council bureaucratic register)
The permanent condition of Thren citizenship review. GRANTED, but REVIEW: PENDING. The status that never resolves. The word means: *you are recognized but you are not safe.* The word means: *the review is the point.*

Used as a verb in Plain: "She's been pending for eleven years." Used as a noun: "The pending is worse than the denial." Used as a state of being: "He woke up pending and went to sleep pending and tomorrow he'll wake up pending again." No other Aurelian language has this concept because no other country has institutionalized conditional personhood.

**A dawn-person** (n., colloquial)
Someone who believes the light does not differentiate. An optimist. Also, sometimes, a naif — someone who thinks the ceiling will break because it's made of good intentions. "He's a dawn-person — thinks the next council vote will fix everything."

**Coral-time** (n., Reef Labs terminology)
The pace at which significant change becomes visible. Coral grows millimeters per year. A reef takes centuries. "Thren rights are on coral-time" — advancing, but too slowly to measure year by year. Cael-7 coined the term in an interview. The phrase spread. He did not intend it as a political statement. He does not clarify.

## Arkos

**Sand-true** (adj.)
True in the way the desert is true — indifferent, absolute, not kind. "Her assessment was sand-true — accurate and devastating." The word carries no moral judgment. Sand does not judge. Sand simply is what it is and does not care whether you survive it.

**Watt-debt** (n.)
An emotional obligation that cannot be quantified but feels real. "He saved my life in the collapse. I'll carry watt-debt until I die." The word acknowledges that Vorn precision-naming (watts) cannot capture the thing — but uses Vorn terminology anyway, because it's the closest available frame. Arkos humans coined this. Arkos Vorns find it imprecise but moving.

**Nomad-sight** (n.)
The view from outside. Perspective gained by leaving. "She got nomad-sight after three years in the camp — the arcology looks different from outside the walls." Kel Voss is the archetypal nomad-sight holder. He cannot explain what he sees to people who haven't left. The inability to explain is the definition.

## Mirithane

**Reed-rooted** (adj.)
Belonging. Not claimed. Not owned. Rooted — the way a reed is rooted in the marsh, drawing from the same water as every other reed, indistinguishable below the surface. "She came from Solara five years ago. She's reed-rooted now." The word implies permanence without ownership. Mirithane does not have "citizenship." Mirithane has reed-rootedness. The distinction is the whole philosophy.

**Glow-speech** (n.)
Communication through light. Specifically, the bacterial patterns that convey information without language. "The marsh gave glow-speech — the pattern was a sunrise." Verdin uses this term in his keeper training. The senior keepers accept it. The word acknowledges that the bacteria are communicating without claiming to understand what they're saying.

**Water-heavy** (adj.)
Grief-saturated. Carrying a loss that hasn't surfaced yet. "She's been water-heavy since the Remembering — it'll surface when it's ready." Etymology: the marsh water absorbs latency from the grieving. Water that has absorbed a death is measurably different — denser, slower, colder. The phrase transferred to people.

## Valdris

**Mountain-weighted** (adj.)
Carrying responsibility the way the mountain carries the dead. "The foreman is mountain-weighted — 847 names, plus the ones not yet on the wall." Etymology: the Summit Shrine's obsidian wall. The mountain holds the names. The living hold the mountain's weight.

**Deep-true** (adj.)
True at a level that surface objections cannot reach. "He doesn't believe in the anchoring — says it's superstition. But he still carries an obsidian fragment with his mother's name. That's deep-true." Etymology: the deep mines — 800+ meters, where pressure and darkness strip away everything except what you actually believe.

**Forge-bond** (n.)
A relationship forged by shared labor. Different from friendship. Different from love. "Orin and Kael-12 were forge-bonded — thirty years in the same shaft." The bond does not require agreement. Orin's grandfather lost his job to a Vorn. Orin trusts Kael-9 with his life. The forge-bond holds both truths without resolving them.

**Name-practice** (v., n.)
The act of carving a name you are not yet permitted to carve. "Syra is name-practicing Kael-12 — forty-seven times now." The verb describes a specific form of dissent: preparation for permission that may never come. The noun describes the person who practices: "She's a name-practicer — been carving Vorn names on scrap obsidian for three years." The elders know. The elders do not stop her. The not-stopping is not permission. The not-stopping is acknowledgment.

## The Verge

**Still-moving** (adj. — now federation-wide, Verge origin)
Continuing to exist without knowing why. A Glim walking after decommission. A Thren whose review has been PENDING for eleven years. A human who hasn't given up. "He arrived at the crossroads three years ago. Still-moving." The word does not mean "okay." The word means "still here." The distinction is what makes it a Verge word — the Verge does not claim to know why anyone is still here. The Verge just observes that they are.

**The cross** (n.)
The crossroads. Also: the decision point. Also: the place you end up when you've run out of road. "She hit the cross" — she reached a point where every direction was equally unknown. "He's at the cross" — he's here, at the crossroads, and he doesn't know why. Etymology: the literal crossroads at the center of The Verge. The word is used across the federation but originated here.

**Door-trade** (n.)
A trade where what you receive is not what you asked for. "I went to the Memory Trader to forget the collapse. She gave me the survivor's memory instead. Door-trade." Etymology: the Memory Trader's stall — a salvaged door laid across charging stations. A trade at the door is a door-trade: the door opens somewhere you didn't expect.

**Unstenciled** (adj.)
Unregistered. Uncounted. Free of all systems. "He's unstenciled — no ledger, no council, no review." Etymology: Glim unit IDs are stenciled on the chest plate. To be unstenciled is to have removed your tracking number. Glims in The Verge scrape off their IDs. Humans in The Verge never provide theirs. Threns in The Verge abandon their designations. The Verge itself is unstenciled — the federation census cannot count it. The uncountability is the point.

**Dawn-empty** (adj.)
The feeling of facing east with nothing to face. "The Glim at the crossroads is dawn-empty — it stands at sunrise and there's nothing in its sensor array but light." Etymology: the anomalous Glim dawn pause. A Glim facing east is facing the light. Whether it is facing anything else is the question. A dawn-empty person is someone who has stopped waiting for the question to be answered and stands there anyway.

## Cross-Type Untranslatables

**The holding** (Valdris → federation)
Bearing a weight you did not choose, for longer than should be required, because someone else needs you to. The word began with Kael-12's beam. It now applies to anything: a Thren waiting eleven years for citizenship. A human caring for an aging parent. A Glim walking after decommission. The word crossed borders because everyone in Aurelia is holding something.

**Glow-meal** (Mirithane → federation)
A communal experience that is more than the sum of its parts. Originally the Mirithane twilight meal — filter-fish, reed leaves, bioluminescent steam. Now: any gathering where the whole is greater. "The forum debate was a glow-meal — everyone brought something, the light came from the overlap."

**Face-plate** (Vorn → federation)
Not literally — the word in Plain refers to the public-facing self. "She showed her face-plate to the council" — she presented her professional persona. "We had a face-plate conversation" — polite, correct, nothing real exchanged. The metaphor is human borrowing. Vorns find the borrowing inaccurate but respectful. A Vorn's face-plate is their most engineered, most honest surface. The metaphor treats it as a mask. Vorns do not correct this. The correction would require explaining why a face-plate is not a mask, and the explanation would require describing what it feels like to express emotion through calibrated actuators, and the description would require words Plain does not have.

*"The words we don't have are the gaps in our thinking. In Aurelia, the gaps are where the question lives."*
