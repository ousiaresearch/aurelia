# Idiom Dictionary — Aurelia

> *Every country speaks in code. Every idiom is a compressed argument about the world.*
>

## Federation-Wide Idioms

**"The question"** — Never "the personhood debate." Never "the citizenship issue." Always "the question." The capitalization is audible. The refusal to name it more specifically is the political architecture. "He's spent his whole career on the question" — Solar Council member. "She stopped asking the question" — moved to Mirithane.

**"Built, not born"** — Vorns and Threns. Neutral in Mirithane. Slightly diminishing in Solara. A point of pride in Arkos. The tone tells you the country. The phrase is never used for Glims. Glims are "manufactured." The distinction between "built" and "manufactured" is the difference between personhood contested and personhood unimagined.

**"Five percent"** — The anomalous Glim population. Also: the exception that proves the rule is wrong. "He's five percent" — he doesn't fit the category but exists anyway. "That idea is five percent" — it's an outlier with implications for the entire system. Etymology: the documented 5% of Glims that exhibit anomalous behavior. The percentage became the metaphor.

**"Still-moving"** (Verge origin, now federation-wide) — Continuing to exist without knowing why. A Glim walking after decommission. A human who hasn't given up. A Thren whose review is still PENDING after eleven years. "She's still-moving" — she hasn't stopped. Not: she's okay. She's still here. The distinction is critical.

**"The seventeen"** — Any unexplained pause, any significant silence, any duration that matters without being named. "The meeting went seventeen at the end" — a long, weighty silence after the real business concluded. Etymology: the seventeen-second Glim dawn pause, the Dawn Temple silence, the beam-holding duration. The number is never officially acknowledged. The number appears everywhere.

## Solara Idioms

**"The glass doesn't break. It just stays."** — The citizenship ceiling. Not bitter, not hopeful. Accurate. "She ran for council. The glass doesn't break. It just stays." Etymology: the invisible glass ceiling of Thren citizenship, maintained by good intentions rather than explicit prohibition.

**"Keep the light on."** — Goodbye. Also: don't let the question die. "Wrote another citizenship bill?" "Yeah." "Keep the light on." Etymology: Dawn Temple — the cantor keeps the eastern wall open. The light is the only witness.

**"She's casting a vote."** — She's making a decision that affects other people more than herself. Etymology: Ballot Night — the council votes on Thren existence. A human council member casting a vote on Thren citizenship is "casting a vote." The phrase does not specify whose.

**"Dawn-born."** — Optimistic. Also: naive about the ceiling. "He's dawn-born — thinks the next election will fix everything." Etymology: Dawn Temple — the light does not differentiate. The light is also wrong about the council.

**"Lumen bright, lumen dim."** — What goes up comes down. Also: nothing lasts. "She was GRANTED last cycle. Lumen bright, lumen dim." Etymology: Lumen crystals deplete with use. A fully charged Lumen glows brightly.

**"Reef-deep."** — Complicated. Layered. "It's reef-deep — the symbiont, the paper, the council review, all of it." Etymology: Reef Labs — coral symbiosis research, famously complex.

**"The Cantor's pause."** — An uncomfortable silence that everyone knows the meaning of but nobody names. "They asked her about the ceiling. She gave them the Cantor's pause." Etymology: the Cantor's seventeen-second silence at dawn.

## Arkos Idioms

**"Count the watts."** — Be practical. Don't waste time on sentiment. "Should I apologize?" "Count the watts." Etymology: the arcology tracks every watt. Sentiment does not alter the energy balance.

**"Nominal."** — Working as designed. The highest Vorn compliment. "The factory floor is nominal" — everything is functioning. "She's nominal" — she's okay. The word carries no warmth. Vorns hear warmth in the precision. Humans in Arkos learn to.

**"He's running hot."** — Emotional. Slightly derogatory. "The council delegate was running hot — three minutes on a thirty-second allocation." Etymology: Vorn chassis temperature regulation. A Vorn under cognitive load runs slightly warmer. The metaphor transferred to humans.

**"Nomad camp."** — Done. Finished. Abandoned. "The deal went nomad camp." Derogatory inside the arcology. Neutral outside. Etymology: the Nomad Camp outside Arkos's walls — the place where people go when they can't or won't live in the arcology.

**"Sand-glass."** — Fragile but functional. Also: transparent, no secrets. "Glass Forum is sand-glass — you can see everything." Etymology: the sand-glass towers that store energy. They are transparent. They are breakable. They have never broken.

**"Resonance."** — Agreement without words. Also: a Vorn thing that humans don't fully get. "The vote was resonance — no debate." Etymology: Vorn frequency harmonics. Two Vorns on the same frequency need no discussion.

## Mirithane Idioms

**"The reeds remember."** — There are no secrets here. Also: the ecosystem is paying attention. "Don't lie to the marsh. The reeds remember." Etymology: *P. memoriae* absorbs latency from the water.

**"Still water."** — Peace. Respite. Home. "She found still water" — she found a home. Etymology: the deep pools of the marsh, where the water is unmoving and the dead's latency settles.

**"Different roots."** — Different types. Non-judgmental. "We're different roots but we drink the same marsh." Etymology: the marsh's reed platforms — Threns, humans, and Vorns all live on platforms rooted in the same water.

**"The marsh doesn't sort."** — Everyone is equal here. Also: the marsh doesn't care about your politics. "He keeps asking who's in charge. The marsh doesn't sort." Etymology: Mirithane consensus governance — no hierarchy, no voting.

**"Glow-meal."** — A shared experience. Also: something that's more than the sum of its parts. "The remembering was a glow-meal — everyone brought something." Etymology: the communal meal eaten at twilight, filter-fish wrapped in reed leaves, bioluminescent bacteria in the steam.

**"Third-season."** — Fading. Approaching the end. Also: memory that's about to be lost. "He's third-season — the bacteria are dying, the patterns are breaking up." Etymology: the three-season readable window of *P. memoriae* latency storage.

**"Reed-thin."** — Delicate. Also: stronger than it looks. "She's reed-thin but she climbed the summit. The reeds bend. They don't break." Etymology: marsh reeds — structurally fragile, functionally unbreakable in aggregate.

## Valdris Idioms

**"The mountain remembers."** — Nothing is forgotten. Also: your debts will be collected. "He cheated his crew on the ore count. The mountain remembers." Etymology: Summit Shrine — the obsidian wall holds every name.

**"Earn your forge."** — Prove yourself. Not hostile. The baseline expectation. "You want guild seat? Earn your forge." Etymology: forge district — forge ownership is earned through decades of labor.

**"Same table, different eyes."** — The human-Vorn divide. Two people who share everything and see different things. "Orin and Kael-9 — same table, different eyes." Etymology: Valdris guild halls — humans and Vorns eat at the same table but remember different histories.

**"The mountain decides."** — It's out of our hands. Fate. Also: the mine collapse that killed three last year. "Will the elders let Syra carve the name?" "The mountain decides." Etymology: Summit Shrine theology — the mountain, not the elders, determines whose name goes on the wall. In practice, the elders interpret the mountain.

**"Strike the bell."** — Begin. Start the shift. Also: do the thing you've been avoiding. "She filed the grievance finally." "Strike the bell." Etymology: the shift-bell at the shaft entrance.

**"Deep-shaft."** — Serious. No room for error. Also: the place where things go wrong. "This is deep-shaft — the beam, the plaque, all of it." Etymology: the deep mines, 800+ meters, where the collapse happened.

**"The holding."** — Bearing weight you didn't choose, longer than should be required, because someone needs you to. "She's been caring for her mother three years." "The holding." Etymology: the beam Kael-12 held. The plaque says HELD. The word means everything the plaque does not.

**"Summit-true."** — Absolutely honest. Also: said at altitude, where the air is thin and lies cost oxygen. "Give me summit-true — did you or didn't you?" Etymology: Summit Shrine — statements made at the summit are considered binding.

## The Verge Idioms

**"Still-moving."** — I'm alive. I haven't given up. I don't know why I haven't given up. "He's been at the crossroads three years." "Still-moving?" "Still-moving." Etymology: decommissioned Glims that walk after they should have stopped.

**"The entrance is a rumor."** — You have to earn the right to find it. Said of the Thren sanctuary in the canyon. Also: any exclusive knowledge. "How do I get into the Guild Council?" "The entrance is a rumor." Etymology: the hidden Thren sanctuary in The Verge's canyon, location passed by word of mouth.

**"She trades in memory."** — She knows things. Don't lie to her. Don't ask her anything you don't want to know the answer to. "Be careful with the Memory Trader." "I know. She trades in memory."

**"The stew pot's lit."** — You're welcome here. Also: we have nothing but we'll share it. "Arrived at the crossroads after three days walking. The stew pot was lit." Etymology: the communal pot at the crossroads center, maintained by whoever, never empty.

**"Crossroads-clear."** — Understandable without explanation. Obvious if you've been here long enough. "The Glim at the crossroads faces east at dawn. It's crossroads-clear." Etymology: Verge epistemology — some things are only clear from the crossroads.

**"Drone-chassis."** — Empty. Hollow. Also: something that was alive and isn't anymore but still holds shape. "After the collapse, I was drone-chassis for a year. Still moving, but empty." Etymology: the decommissioned Glim chassis in the drone graveyard.
