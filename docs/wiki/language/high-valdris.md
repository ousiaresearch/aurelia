# High Valdris — The Mountain Tongue

> *Spoken at altitude. The mountain preserves what the lowlands forget.*

## Survival

High Valdris is the most intact pre-Collapse language in Aurelia. Linguistic drift is near-zero — the mountain's isolation preserves speech the way it preserves the dead. A Valdrisan elder from Basecamp can read an inscription carved before the Collapse and recognize every word. This continuity is unique. No other Aurelian language can claim it.

The language is most fluent above 2,000 meters. The thin air affects human vocal cords — certain fricatives are easier to produce at altitude, and High Valdris evolved to favor them. Vorns speaking High Valdris at altitude must modulate their vocal resonators to match human capability in the thinner air. "The mountain equalizes," the guild saying goes. "Even Vorn speech must climb."

## Grammar

High Valdris is a **verb-final language** — the verb comes at the end of every clause. Objects precede verbs. Subjects are optional when context makes them clear. The structure means a sentence's most important word is the last one. Miners in the deep shafts use this property deliberately: they speak the hazard first, then the location, then the verb, and the verb tells you whether to act or not. "Beam Section-3-B cracks" means the beam is cracking. "Beam Section-3-B held" means it held.

**Archaic features retained:**
- **Dual number:** A separate pronoun and verb form for exactly two people. *Thou* (singular), *ye* (dual), *you* (plural). "Thou climbest. Ye climb. You climb." The dual is used for shift partners — Orin and Kael-9 are *ye*, a grammatical unit. When Kael-12 died, Orin continued to use *ye* for himself and Kael-12's memory. The grammar does not distinguish between the living and the dead.
- **Honorific register:** Verbs change form based on the speaker's relationship to the listener. Speaking to an elder: the verb takes a suffix indicating deference. Speaking to a guild master: different suffix. Speaking to the mountain: the highest register, rarely used, considered almost presumptuous. Syra Venn uses the mountain-honorific when speaking to the obsidian wall. The elders consider this appropriate. The mountain has not corrected her.
- **Evidentiality markers:** Every statement must indicate how the speaker knows what they're saying. Direct observation is one marker. Hearsay is another. Inference is a third. Tradition ("the mountain has always said") is a fourth. A Valdrisan cannot say "the beam cracked" without indicating whether they saw it crack, heard it from someone, deduced it, or know it because the mountain records such things. The evidential requirement makes Valdris legal testimony extremely precise — and makes Valdrisan speech feel heavy to outsiders. Every sentence carries its own footnotes.

## Vocabulary

The High Valdris lexicon is dominated by three domains:

**Mining:** Eighty-seven words for rock states (fractured, stable, shifting, water-veined, ore-rich). Twelve words for darkness (the dark of the upper shaft, the dark of the deep, the dark before the cave-lichen blooms). Five words for silence. The silence vocabulary is the most studied by Summit Shrine keepers — each silence word describes a different quality of absence. "The mountain was *tel-kadar*" means the mountain was silent in the way that precedes an avalanche. "The mountain was *tel-mir*" means the mountain was silent in the way that means it is at peace.

**Guild hierarchy:** A full lexicon of craft ranks, apprenticeship stages, and labor valuations. A miner's status can be expressed in a single compound word: *tharn-khel-var* = "one who has descended the deep shaft and returned with ore." The compound is earned by doing it. Speaking it without having done it is a lie detectable by the evidentiality system.

**The dead:** Forty-one words for death states. Death by rockfall. Death by gas. Death by age. Death by the mountain's choice (the rarest — only the Summit Shrine keepers are permitted to use these). Kael-12's death was *mir-kadan* — "held-while-another-fled." Orin does not use this word. He uses *kadan-del* — "held-for-another-to-live." The difference is one syllable. The difference is everything.

## Unique Features

**Altitude fluency:** High Valdris is measurably easier to speak above 2,000 meters. The language's sound inventory is adapted to thin air — fewer voiced stops, more fricatives and approximants. A Valdrisan descending to Basecamp notices the shift. "Words feel heavier at sea level," a miner said. "The mountain lifts them."

**Whistled Valdris:** A secondary register used for communication across canyons. The tonal contours of High Valdris words are reproduced as whistled pitches, audible for kilometers. Summit Shrine keepers use Whistled Valdris to signal between the shrine and Midcamp. The messages are simple — "the ascent begins," "severe weather," "the mountain is still." Syra Venn learned Whistled Valdris in her first year of apprenticeship. She has never used it to say what she carves on the obsidian. Some things cannot be whistled.

**The Forge Dialect:** An industrial variant spoken in the forge district. Incorporates onomatopoeic vocabulary from the forge's sounds — hammer strikes, bellows cycles, the hiss of quenched metal. The dialect is considered coarse by mountain families but respected by forge workers. "The forge speaks its own tongue," Anvil-3 says. "The mountain understands."

## Status

Stable. High Valdris is the primary home language for all Valdris mountain families and guild elders. Young Valdrisans learn High Valdris alongside Plain — the mountain tongue for home and the creole for trade. The Summit Shrine apprenticeship requires fluency. Syra Venn speaks High Valdris with the evidential markers of someone who has witnessed what she describes. The obsidian wall requires no less.
