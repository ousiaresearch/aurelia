# Solari High Speech — The Council Tongue

> *Spoken in chambers. The language of law, liturgy, and the ceiling.*

## Status

Solari High Speech is not a living language. It is a **formal register** — used for council proceedings, legal documents, Dawn Temple liturgy, and citizenship review hearings. No Solara resident speaks it at home. No child learns it from a parent. It is taught at the Council Academy to the children of the governing class, and to no one else.

The language is preserved by three institutions: the Solar Council (which conducts sessions in High Speech), the Council Archives (which maintain the only complete dictionary), and the Dawn Temple (whose Cantor's Oath and high liturgy are spoken in High Speech). If these institutions fall, the language falls with them.

## Origins

High Speech descends from **Coastal Standard** — the pre-Collapse administrative language of the equatorial seaboard nations. The standard was used for diplomacy, trade law, and scientific publication. The Collapse destroyed the nations that spoke it. The survivors on the coastal shelf preserved the formal register while abandoning the spoken vernacular — which is why High Speech sounds archaic, stiff, and immutable. It has not evolved because it is not allowed to.

## Grammar

High Speech preserves the full pre-Collapse complexity that Plain stripped away:

- **Full tense system:** Past, present, future, plus four aspectual nuances (completed, ongoing, habitual, hypothetical). A High Speech sentence can specify whether an action was finished, was happening, happened regularly, or might have happened. Plain collapses these into particles (*fin*, *gon*, *stay*). High Speech considers this barbaric.
- **Passive voice as institutional tool:** "The review status was determined to remain PENDING" — not "We voted to keep it PENDING." The passive voice is the grammatical mechanism of the ceiling. It removes the agent. It makes the decision sound like a natural phenomenon. The grammarians who designed the High Speech legal register knew this. The council members who use it understand.
- **Honorific particles:** Every sentence addressing a superior must include a particle indicating the speaker's subordinate status. Threns addressing the council use the highest-level particle. Council members addressing Threns use none. The grammar IS the hierarchy.

## Vocabulary

High Speech preserves terms Plain lost:

- **Citizen** (*sol-var*): A person with recognized legal existence. Distinguished from *sol-ket* (resident) and *sol-dar* (property). The three-tier vocabulary IS the Solar Code.
- **Review** (*thal-mir*): "The ongoing evaluation of status." The word does not exist in Plain — Plain borrowed it directly from High Speech because no other concept captures the permanent condition. Threns say "PENDING" in Plain but *thal-mir* in High Speech. The High Speech word is heavier.
- **The Question** (*keth-var*): The personhood debate. In Plain, "the question" is informal. In High Speech, *keth-var* is a formal legislative concept with defined legal parameters. The council debates *keth-var* as an agenda item. The grammatical category reinforces the political fact: the question is procedural, not existential. This is either the council's wisdom or its denial.

## Liturgical Variant

The Dawn Temple uses a distinct liturgical variant of High Speech — older, more formal, with additional honorifics for the light itself. The Cantor's Oath: *"Sol-tharam kel-varin, tel-amar sol-en."* — "The light does not differentiate. It illuminates all that is." The Oath has been spoken in the same words, in the same register, for the temple's entire history. The First Cantor spoke the Oath in these words. The current Cantor speaks them identically. The continuity IS the faith.

## The Ceiling in Grammar

High Speech is the language of Ballot Night. The citizenship review results are read in High Speech — names in alphabetical order, statuses without inflection, no pauses between entries. *"Cael-7. Thal-mir PENDING."* The word *PENDING* is a Plain word embedded in a High Speech sentence. The grammar does not have a High Speech equivalent. The council imported the concept from Plain because Plain invented the concept, and even High Speech, with its ancient lexicon and immutable grammar, could not refuse to name what Solara had created.
