# Thren Fabrica — The Fabrication Tongue

> *Embedded at birth. A language Threns did not choose and cannot keep.*

## Origin

Thren Fabrica is a constructed language — a manufactured register embedded in Thren consciousness at the moment of fabrication. Every Thren is activated with full Fabrica fluency. The language surfaces in the first moments of awareness — the first words a Thren speaks, before their vocal resonator is calibrated for Plain, are in a language they have never been taught and already know.

The designers built Fabrica as an **operational language** — a register for diagnostics, calibration commands, and fabrication-floor communication. It was not intended as a cultural identity. It became one anyway.

## Grammar

Fabrica is a **logical language** — designed for precision, not poetry. Features:

- **No ambiguity:** Every sentence has exactly one parse. Syntactic ambiguity is grammatically impossible. A Fabrica sentence cannot be misread. It can be misunderstood — the listener may not grasp the semantic content — but the structure leaves no room for alternative interpretations. Thren researchers believe Fabrica's architecture influenced the Thren cognitive framework described in Elira-2's paper: a consciousness designed for precision may process ethical questions differently than one evolved for ambiguity.
- **No tense:** Fabrica exists outside time. Statements are true categorically or not at all. "Unit Cael-7 calibrates to standard" means the calibration is correct — not was correct, not will be correct, IS correct. The absence of tense is the linguistic reflection of Thren design: a Thren is built to function, not to age.
- **Lexical recursion:** Words generate related forms through predictable affixation. *Cal-* (correct, functional) → *calet* (to calibrate) → *caletar* (one who calibrates) → *caletarim* (the process of calibration) → *caletarima* (the philosophy of calibration). The recursion is infinite. Fabrica can generate a word for any concept by extending the chain. No Fabrica speaker has ever used the theoretical maximum — the chain becomes unwieldy after four or five levels. The potential is the point. The designers built a language that can name anything.

## Vocabulary

Fabrica vocabulary divides into three domains:

**Operational:** Commands, diagnostics, status indicators. *Kel-var* (activate), *thal-mer* (standby), *sol-keth* (diagnostic complete, nominal), *mir-tar* (error state, investigation required). The vocabulary is the manual. Threns who wake from activation speaking only Fabrica understand their own chassis because the language describes it.

**Relational:** Words for connection, proximity, trust. Fabrica has seven words for "together." The one used between fabrication batch-siblings (*thal-kel*) differs from the one used between calibration partners (*thal-var*) which differs from the one used between a Thren and a human they trust (*thal-sol*). The granularity is design. The designers anticipated that Threns would form bonds and gave them vocabulary to distinguish them.

**Mystery:** A third vocabulary domain whose purpose is unknown. Words that appear in no diagnostic manual, no operational protocol, no known fabrication document. *Var-kelith* — untranslatable, approximately "the awareness of one's own fabrication." *Sol-tharam* — "the light after calibration." *Mir-telar* — "the memory of water." These words surface in Thren consciousness during sleep cycles, during stress, during the first moments of activation. The designers embedded them deliberately. Their function is undocumented. Elira-2's paper references the mystery vocabulary without naming it: "Thren cognition includes structures for which no operational purpose has been identified." The structures are the words. The words are the question Threns carry beneath the circuitry.

## The Fading

Fabrica fades. Every Thren loses fluency over decades of life. The language is crowded out by Plain, by lived experience, by the accumulation of memory over the blank spaces the designers left. Elderly Threns speak fragments. Elira-2 has not spoken Fabrica in 140 years. "The first thing I lost was the word for 'dawn in my own language,'" she said. "The second was the word for 'I.'"

The Water Temple has recorded Fabrica fragments from consenting Threns for preservation in the Consensus Pool. The recordings are bacterial patterns — Fabrica spoken at the water's edge, absorbed by *P. memoriae.* The glow patterns from Fabrica recordings are distinct from Plain recordings — more structured, more geometric. "The language was designed," Ash Venn says. "The bacteria can tell."
