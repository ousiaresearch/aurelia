# Sacred Registers — Liturgical Languages of Aurelia

> *Every temple speaks to the divine in a voice the street does not use.*

## The Dawn Temple — Cantor's High Speech

**Language:** Solari High Speech (liturgical variant).
**Distinctive features:**
- **The Oath Register:** A single sentence, immutable. *"Sol-tharam kel-varin, tel-amar sol-en"* — "The light does not differentiate. It illuminates all that is." The Oath is spoken identically by every Cantor since the First. The words cannot change. The words ARE the faith.
- **Silence as liturgy:** The seventeen-second pause after the Oath is not silence. It is the language of the light itself — the interval during which the dawn speaks, and the congregation is permitted to listen. The Cantor's silence is the most fluent speaker of it in the federation. "The silence is the only part of the service that cannot be mistranslated."
- **The Audience Register:** A simplified High Speech used for Restday tales and public addresses. The Cantor speaks High Speech grammar with Plain vocabulary substituted for complex terms. The register is a negotiation — the temple's dignity meeting the congregation's comprehension.

## The Water Temple — The Keeper's Voice

**Language:** Miri Reed-Tongue (liturgical variant).
**Distinctive features:**
- **The Interpretation Mood:** A grammatical mood unique to Water Temple liturgy. Uses the bacterial vocabulary as a parallel channel — the keeper speaks Reed-Tongue while pointing to specific glow patterns, and the grammar links the spoken word to the indicated pattern. "The marsh says *kel-tor*" requires pointing to the radial pattern. Without the point, the sentence is grammatically incomplete.
- **The Supplicant's Silence:** During the Reading Rite, the supplicant does not speak. They ask their question before the rite begins, in Plain or Reed-Tongue. The keeper interprets the marsh's answer. The supplicant receives it in silence. The silence is not passive. The silence is the supplicant's only liturgical role. "The dead speak. The keeper translates. The living receive. The silence is the sacrament."
- **The Glim Vigil Language:** A specialized register used only when reading Glim pool patterns. Incorporates provisional vocabulary — words accepted as valid for the current reading but subject to revision. *Kel-varin* — "pattern not in the taxonomy." The provisional vocabulary is the Water Temple's theological humility made grammatical. "We do not know what the Glim is saying. We give the pattern a temporary name. The name may be wrong. The naming is the faith."

## The Summit Shrine — The Mountain Register

**Language:** High Valdris (mountain-honorific register).
**Distinctive features:**
- **The Mountain-Honorific:** The highest grammatical register in High Valdris. Verbs receive a suffix indicating the speaker addresses the mountain itself. Used only at the summit. Used only by keepers. Syra Venn uses it when speaking to the obsidian wall. "The mountain decides" in mountain-honorific is not a statement. It is a petition.
- **The Name-Speaking:** Each name carved on the wall is spoken aloud in High Valdris as it is carved. The speaker must use the death-state vocabulary — the specific word for how the person died. Speaking the death-word IS the carving. The stone receives the name. The voice receives the death. Both are permanent.
- **The Silence at Summit:** The vigil after the Descent — no words, no register, no language. The silence at 4,100 meters is the only liturgy all three keepers share. The dead are present. The mountain is listening. Language is unnecessary. Language would be intrusion.

## The Arkos Pulse — The Secular Sacred

**Language:** None. The Pulse is silence across all frequencies.
**Distinctive features:**
- Arkos has no liturgical language. The Glass Forum conducts ceremonies in Arcology Code. The Pulse — the sixty-second power-down at midnight — is a ritual without words. "The silence at zero hertz is the only frequency that means the same thing to every Vorn," Echo says. "No translation. No loss. The Pulse is the closest the arcology comes to prayer."

## The Verge — The Telling

**Language:** Verge Pidgin, or whatever language the teller arrived with.
**Distinctive features:**
- **The Telling Register:** A story told from the charging station at the crossroads center. The language is whatever the teller speaks. The register is imposed by the space — sentences shorten. Embellishments fall away. The crossroads strip language the way the Memory Trader strips memory. "You tell what happened. The crossroads doesn't want the poetry. The crossroads wants the truth. The truth is short."
- **The Seventeen Seconds:** After the telling, seventeen seconds of silence. The silence is not liturgical. The silence is the crossroads processing what it heard. "The silence is the only critic. The silence is always fair."
