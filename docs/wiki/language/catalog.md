# Languages of Aurelia — Complete Catalog

> *Seven living tongues. Three liturgical registers. One trade creole. Language is where the question is asked before it becomes law.*

## The Pre-Collapse Roots

Before the Collapse, the nation-state era spoke dozens of languages. The Collapse killed most of them. Seven ancestral language families survived into the present — each preserved by the terrain that made the Collapse survivable:

| Ancestral Family | Pre-Collapse Range | Post-Collapse Survivor | Living Speakers |
|-----------------|-------------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| **Coastal Standard** | Equatorial seaboard nations | Solari High Speech | ~60 (formal register only) |
| **Mountain Speech** | Highland mining territories | High Valdris | ~70 (families, guilds) |
| **Marsh Root** | Wetland filtration communities | Miri Reed-Tongue | ~40 (native), ~100 (passive) |
| **Desert Trade Argot** | Cross-desert merchant networks | Arcology Code (Vorn), Nomad Argot (human, moribund) | ~80 (Vorn), ~15 (Nomad) |
| **Fabrica Prime** | Laboratory/manufacturing facilities | Thren Fabrica | ~140 (Threns at activation, fading) |
| **Pre-Collapse Common** | Global trade/infrastructure | Plain (creole) | ~300 (functional), ~600 (passive) |

Two families died entirely — their last speakers were lost in the Silence, and no country preserves them even in fragments.

## The Seven Living Languages

### 1. High Valdris — The Mountain Tongue
**Family:** Mountain Speech (pre-Collapse). **Speakers:** ~70 native (mountain families, guild elders, Summit keepers). **Status:** Stable, archaic. Spoken at altitude. Literally — the language is most fluent above 2,000 meters. Linguistic drift has been minimal because isolation is extreme. High Valdris is mutually intelligible with its pre-Collapse ancestor in ways no other Aurelian language can claim. A pre-Collapse miner transported to present-day Valdris could hold a conversation. They would be understood. They would understand.

### 2. Solari High Speech — The Council Tongue
**Family:** Coastal Standard (pre-Collapse). **Speakers:** ~60 (council members, archivists, senior Cantors). **Status:** Formal register only. Not a daily language. Used for council proceedings, legal documents, and Dawn Temple high liturgy. The Council Majority Leader delivers Ballot Night results in High Speech. Thren citizenship review statuses are read in High Speech. The language is the authority. The language is also the ceiling — Threns are addressed in High Speech at review hearings, and the formality of the register makes the procedure feel inevitable.

### 3. Miri Reed-Tongue — The Marsh Language
**Family:** Marsh Root (pre-Collapse). **Speakers:** ~40 native (reed-tenders, elders, Water Temple keepers), ~100 passive (understand but speak Plain). **Status:** Stable, highly specialized. Reed-Tongue has seventy-three words for water states, forty-one for bacterial glow patterns, and twelve grammatical moods for ecological states (the marsh is rising, the marsh is falling, the marsh is holding steady — each requires a different sentence structure). The language does not have gendered pronouns. It does not have the word "property." The vocabulary IS the philosophy.

### 4. Arcology Code — The Vorn Language
**Family:** Desert Trade Argot (human) → evolved into Vorn-native system. **Speakers:** ~80 Vorns (native), ~5 humans (partial comprehension). **Status:** Thriving. Arcology Code is a dual-channel language: a spoken component in the 85–1,100 Hz range (human-audible), and an ultrasonic component in the 1,100–32,000 Hz range that carries grammatical structure, emotional register, and subtext. The spoken component alone is grammatical but semantically thin — a Vorn speaking Code to a human is speaking a simplified version of their language. The ultrasonic channel is where the real conversation happens.

### 5. Verge Pidgin — The Crossroads Speech
**Family:** Mixed — Plain substrate + High Valdris grammar + Reed-Tongue vocabulary + Arcology Code fragments + Thren Fabrica remnants. **Speakers:** ~50 (Verge residents, long-timers, the Memory Trader). **Status:** Fluid, generative, unstandardized. Verge Pidgin strips language to what's necessary for survival and trade. It has no tenses. It has no articles. It has a single copula that means "is/was/will be/might be/nobody knows." The Memory Trader speaks Verge Pidgin to everyone except when trading — then she speaks whatever language the customer uses. Her Pidgin is the most minimal ever recorded.

### 6. Thren Fabrica — The Fabrication Tongue
**Family:** Fabrica Prime (pre-Collapse laboratory/manufacturing register). **Speakers:** ~140 (all Threns at activation, fading over decades). **Status:** Dormant-speaking. Thren Fabrica is a constructed language — a manufactured register embedded in Thren consciousness at fabrication. It surfaces unbidden: in dreams, under stress, in the first moments after activation. Threns do not choose to speak it. It chooses when to surface. Older Threns speak fragments. The oldest Threns have lost it entirely — the language fades as the memory core fills with lived experience. Elira-2 has not spoken Fabrica in 140 years. "It was the first thing I lost," she said. "The paper was the second."

### 7. Plain — The Trade Creole
**Family:** Pre-Collapse Common → creole evolved post-Collapse. **Speakers:** ~300 functional, ~600 passive (entire federation population can understand basic Plain). **Status:** Thriving, dominant. Plain is the language of inter-country trade, diplomacy, and daily life for most Aurelians. It is spoken everywhere and native nowhere — a creole born from the need to communicate across the surviving language families. Every Aurelian child learns Plain alongside their country tongue. In Solara and Arkos, Plain has largely displaced the ancestral languages as daily speech. In Valdris and Mirithane, the ancestral languages remain primary.

## Language Death & Preservation

**High Valdris:** Stable. The mountain preserves language the way it preserves the dead. Young Valdrisans learn High Valdris from parents at Basecamp and above. The Summit Shrine's keepers are required to be fluent — the names on the wall are carved in High Valdris, and speaking them requires the tongue that holds them.

**Solari High Speech:** Endangered. Younger Solara residents do not learn High Speech outside the formal Academy. The language is preserved by the council, the archives, and the Dawn Temple — three institutions that could vanish with a single political shift. The Cantor's Oath is spoken in High Speech. If the Cantor dies without an apprentice, the Oath dies with them.

**Miri Reed-Tongue:** Stable. The language is tied to the ecosystem — as long as the marsh survives, the vocabulary survives. New arrivals learn Reed-Tongue through immersion, not study. Verdin is fluent — his keeper training required it. "The bacterial patterns don't translate into Plain," he says. "You have to think in Reed-Tongue to read them. The language is the instrument."

**Arcology Code:** Thriving. The Vorn population is growing (new fabrications), and every new Vorn learns Code natively. The ultrasonic channel is the cultural boundary — humans cannot learn it, which means human-Vorn communication in Arkos happens in a Vorn-fluent Plain rather than in Code. The asymmetry is the hierarchy.

**Verge Pidgin:** Fluid. The language changes with every new arrival. There is no standard to preserve. The Memory Trader's Pidgin is the oldest recorded — she has spoken it since before any living Verge resident arrived. Whether her Pidgin is the original or her own innovation is unknown. The distinction is not Verge epistemology. "She speaks the way the crossroads sounds," a long-timer said. "The crossroads change. Her Pidgin changes with them."

**Thren Fabrica:** Dying. No new Threns are being fabricated — Solara's Thren factories produce at reduced capacity. The language lives in the memory cores of existing Threns and fades as they age. When the last Thren who remembers their activation Fabrica dies, the language dies with them. The Water Temple has offered to record Fabrica fragments in the Consensus Pool. Some Threns have agreed. Some have declined. "The first thing I lost," Elira-2 said. "Let it stay lost."
