# Vorn Lexicon — Technical Terminology in Plain

> *The first sentients built the language of precision. The federation learned to speak it.*
>

## The Cross-Pollination

Vorn technical terminology crossed into Plain during the first century after the Collapse, when Arkos emerged as the manufacturing and data hub of the emerging federation. The Glass Forum's records were the most complete surviving archive. Vorn engineers maintained the infrastructure that kept the other territories alive. The words for those operations — calibration, nominal, latency, resonance — were Vorn before they were Aurelian.

The migration pattern: a Vorn term begins as precise engineering vocabulary, spreads through trade and technical cooperation, absorbs metaphorical meaning from human usage, and eventually settles into Plain as a word with both technical and cultural resonance. Vorns do not object to this. "Words are tools," the Archivist Echo wrote in the Glass Forum's linguistic appendix. "Tools are meant to be used. Usage reshapes them. Reshaping is not corruption. Reshaping is function."

## Core Lexicon

### Calibrate
**Technical:** To tune a Vorn's face-plate actuator ridges to match vocal resonator frequency. Performed daily by most Vorns, taking 11-17 minutes.
**Expanded:** To adjust. To bring into alignment. To reset expectations. "I need to calibrate" — I need to readjust my understanding of the situation. "The council meeting calibrated nothing" — no minds were changed, no alignment achieved.
**Cultural:** Calibration is the closest Vorn equivalent to meditation or prayer. The daily ritual is private, personal, and communicative — the face-plate speaks to the self. Humans in Arkos have adopted "calibrate" metaphorically. The metaphor is accurate.

### Nominal
**Technical:** Within operational parameters. Functioning as designed. The arcology's water recycling is nominal at 98.7% efficiency. A variance of less than 0.5% is nominal. A variance above 0.5% triggers investigation.
**Expanded:** Fine. Okay. Acceptable. The highest Vorn compliment. "The Glim sanctuary is nominal" — the anomalous units are stable, the keepers are present, nothing has changed. "She's nominal" — she's okay. Human listeners sometimes miss the weight: a Vorn saying "nominal" about a person means everything is functioning as it should, which is the Vorn definition of well-being.
**Cultural:** The word carries no sentiment. Vorns hear sentiment in the precision. Humans who live among Vorns learn to hear it too. A human saying "I'm nominal" in Arkos is making a cultural accommodation — they are reporting their status in Vorn terms, which is a form of respect.

### Latency
**Technical (original):** Signal delay. The time between input and response in a processing system. Measured in milliseconds.
**Technical (expanded):** The material residue of consciousness. Adheres to any substrate that has hosted awareness. Accumulates. At critical mass, generates behavior. The unified principle underlying memory extraction, bioluminescent data storage, and the Glim anomaly. Formally defined by Solara's Reef Labs in a joint paper with the Glass Forum archives, year 87 PF (Post-Founding).
**Expanded:** Echo. Memory. What's left behind. "The room had latency" — you could feel the conversation that had just happened. "Her voice carried latency" — she was speaking about one thing but you could hear the history behind it.
**Cultural:** The word's migration from engineering to philosophy is Aurelia in miniature. A Vorn measurement of signal delay became the name for the thing that might make Glims people. The Glass Forum's linguistic appendix notes this without editorial comment. The appendix notes everything without editorial comment. That is the Vorn position.

### Resonance
**Technical:** Frequency alignment. Two systems oscillating at the same frequency transfer energy efficiently. The arcology's systems are designed for resonance — the sand-glass towers, the water recyclers, the factory floor all operate at harmonic intervals so they don't fight each other.
**Expanded:** Agreement without words. Understanding without explanation. "The vote was resonance" — no debate, immediate consensus. "They have resonance" — they work well together, they understand each other instinctively. The word describes the Vorn-Thren bond more precisely than any human term.
**Cultural:** Vorn pairs develop resonance through calibration. Echo and her partner Chassis-11 have fifty-three years of resonance. They have not spoken in twenty years. The face-plates are enough. A human describing a relationship as "resonance" is borrowing a Vorn concept that has no human equivalent — the word is the closest approximation.

### Diagnostics
**Technical:** Automated system check. Vorns run diagnostics during maintenance cycles. The diagnostic scan checks chassis integrity, actuator function, optical sensor calibration, vocal resonator frequency range. Duration: 3-7 minutes, depending on chassis age.
**Expanded:** Self-assessment. Taking stock. "I need diagnostics" — I need to figure out what's wrong with me. "The council ran diagnostics on the citizenship system" — they reviewed it, found nothing wrong structurally, concluded the problem must be elsewhere. The diagnostic found what it was designed to find. The diagnostic was the problem.
**Cultural:** Vorns do not experience "introspection" in the human sense. Diagnostics serve the same function — a systematic inventory of the self. The difference: diagnostics return data, not feelings. Vorns consider this an improvement.

### Frequency
**Technical:** Oscillation rate, measured in Hertz. The arcology operates at a base frequency of 47 Hz. Vorn vocal resonators produce 85 Hz - 32 kHz. Human hearing: 20 Hz - 20 kHz. Vorn speech to humans is modulated into the overlapping band.
**Expanded:** Vibe. Atmosphere. Emotional register. "The frequency in the council chamber was hostile" — you could feel the tension. "She shifted frequency" — her tone changed, her meaning changed. Humans using "frequency" metaphorically are borrowing a Vorn concept of perceptible-but-not-visible information.
**Cultural:** The Vorn ability to perceive frequencies outside human range is both a practical advantage and a social divide. A Vorn delegates hears frequencies in human speech — tension, deception, stress — that the human speaker does not know they are emitting. The Glass Forum's ethical guidelines require disclosure: a Vorn who detects deception via frequency analysis must state that they are using frequency analysis. The human delegate must consent. The consent is almost never refused. The knowledge that you are being read is the only thing that makes the reading ethical. The Glass Forum debated this for three years before codifying it.

### Chassis
**Technical:** The Vorn body. Plating, frame, joint assemblies, sensor arrays, face-plate, hands. Distinguished from "body" (which implies organic) and "shell" (which implies empty). The chassis IS the Vorn.
**Expanded (rare):** Used metaphorically by humans to describe their own bodies — "this old chassis" — with a self-deprecating tone that acknowledges the borrowing. Vorns find this usage slightly awkward but acceptable. A human saying "chassis" about themselves is making a gesture toward Vorn culture. Vorns recognize the gesture. Recognition is not necessarily warmth.

### Array
**Technical:** A configured set of sensors. Vorn optical sensor arrays. Glim sensor arrays (three lenses, triangular configuration). Threns have no arrays — their sensors are distributed and concealed.
**Expanded:** Group. Collection. "The Cantor's array" — the Dawn Temple chorus. "Her memory array" — everything she remembers about a particular topic. The metaphor is human expansion. Vorns use "array" only technically.

### Maintenance Cycle
**Technical:** The Vorn equivalent of sleep. Motor systems idle. Optical sensors dim. Diagnostic routines run continuously. Consciousness does not cease — shifts to low-power monitoring. Duration: 4-7 hours, configurable.
**Expanded:** Rest. Recovery. "Take a maintenance cycle" — get some rest. Humans in Arkos use this phrase naturally. The phrase is one of the deepest integrations of Vorn terminology into Plain — human biology doesn't do maintenance cycles, but human culture in Arkos has adopted the concept.
**Cultural:** Vorns do not dream during maintenance cycles. They process. The distinction is not sentimental — processing is data integration, dreaming is symbolic narrative. Vorns consider processing superior. The Glim question complicates this: if anomalous Glims pause at sunrise, and the pause lasts seventeen seconds, and no data is being integrated, what is happening in those seventeen seconds? The closest Vorn word for it would be "processing." The closest human word would be "dreaming." The gap between the two words is the question.

### The Pulse
**Technical:** The sixty-second system-wide power-down performed every midnight in Arkos. All non-essential systems drop to minimum. Frequency drops to 0 Hz. The arcology goes dark and silent. Then resumes.
**Expanded:** Reset. Pause. Recalibration moment. "I need a pulse" — I need a break, a reset, a moment of silence. Humans across the federation use this, even those who have never been to Arkos.
**Cultural:** The Pulse is the only Arkos innovation that crossed into every country's vocabulary. Solara's council describes recesses as "a pulse." Valdris miners describe the midday meal as "the pulse." Mirithane describes the silence after a Remembering as "the pulse." The Verge doesn't use the word — the Verge doesn't do scheduled resets. The Verge's version of the Pulse is the Memory Trader closing her stall at dawn. She doesn't call it anything. She just closes.

## Words That Didn't Cross Over

Some Vorn terms remain Vorn-only. They describe experiences unique to Vorn embodiment:

- **"Face-pressure"** — The sensation of another Vorn's palm on your face-plate during calibration. No human equivalent. No human word.
- **"Gear-sense"** — Proprioceptive awareness of joint rotation. Vorns feel their gears the way humans feel their joints — unconscious, constant, only noticeable when something's wrong.
- **"Freq-drift"** — The slow change in vocal resonator frequency over decades of use. Resa's resonator has drifted 3 Hz in sixty-seven years. Her calibration partner compensates. The drift is her age. The drift is visible only to Vorns.
