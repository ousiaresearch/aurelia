# Plain — The Common Tongue of Aurelia

> *The Collapse broke the towers that carried words. What survived was the voice. What grew from the voice was Plain.*

## Formation

Plain is a creole. Its substrate is the pre-Collapse trade language believed to be English — the records are sparse, but enough survived in the Glass Forum archives to confirm the lineage. Its superstrate contributions come from Vorn technical terminology, Thren calibration-brief phrasing, and biome-specific vocabulary from each country's founding ecology.

Plain formed during the first century after the Collapse. The infrastructure that carried the old languages — broadcast networks, digital archives, the written word at scale — died with the nation-states. What remained was speech. The survivors of five territories needed to trade, negotiate, and eventually form a federation. They spoke a fragmented common tongue, evolving toward mutual intelligibility without any central authority to standardize it.

Plain has no academy. No dictionary is official. No grammar is codified. Plain drifts. The drift is the language.

## Core Features

### Grammar

Plain shed the irregular verbs, gendered pronouns, and complex tenses of its parent language. What remains:

- **Present tense** dominates. Past is indicated by context or the particle *fin* (from "finished"): "I fin eat" — I ate. Future is indicated by *gon* (from "going to"): "I gon eat" — I will eat. Habitual uses *stay* (from Solara coastal dialect): "I stay eat at dawn" — I eat at dawn every day.
- **No articles.** *The* and *a/an* vanished. Context carries definiteness. "Glim at crossroads" means the Glim at the crossroads, the one everyone knows about.
- **No copula.** The verb "to be" is optional. "She foreman" means she is the foreman. "He PENDING" means his review status is PENDING, and the copula would be redundant because the status is a state of being, not an action. Plain respects the difference.
- **Pronoun simplification.** Four third-person pronouns: *he* (any masculine-presenting), *she* (any feminine-presenting), *they* (plural, or any individual who prefers it), *it* (Glims — officially, and contested). The Glim pronoun is the only part of Plain grammar that is actively debated. Mirithane uses *they* for Glims. Solara uses *it.* The grammar is the politics.

### Phonology

Plain sounds different in each country. The consonants are stable. The vowels shift. Solara's Plain has elongated vowels — the council's influence, a deliberateness that reads as education. Arkos's Plain is clipped, consonants sharp, Vorn technical precision bleeding into human speech. Mirithane's Plain is soft, sibilants merging with the marsh's ambient sound. Valdris's Plain is the most archaic — the mountain families preserved pre-Collapse vowel patterns longer than anyone. The Verge's Plain drops everything that can be dropped and is still understood.

A Thren speaking Plain in Solara sounds different from a Thren speaking Plain in Mirithane — not in vocabulary, in cadence. The cadence is the country. The words are the federation.

## Country Dialects

### Solara Plain — Council Register

The most formal variant. Complete sentences are the norm. Articles sometimes return in official contexts — the council's minutes use *the* where no Aurelian speaks it. Council Register is a performance of authority. Speaking Council Register well is a class marker. Threns who master it are considered "articulate" — a compliment that is also an insult, implying the baseline expectation was otherwise.

Solara Plain is the default for inter-country diplomacy. This gives Solara cultural dominance out of proportion to its population. The Arkos Glass Forum negotiated for Arkos Plain to be accepted as a diplomatic register. Solara agreed. Nothing changed — Arkos delegates still speak Solara Plain in joint sessions because Solara Plain is what the interpreters are trained in. The Vorn delegates notice this. The Vorn delegates continue to speak Solara Plain, correctly, with slightly too-precise consonants, an audible reminder that they are adapting to a system that adapted to no one.

### Arkos Plain — Technical Register

Influenced by Vorn terminology. Precise. Sentences end where the data ends. No flourishes. Arkos Plain is the language of trade negotiations, factory floor coordination, and the Glass Forum's light-pulse voting records. It is understood across the federation as the language of things that work.

A human speaking Arkos Plain who has never lived in Arkos sounds like a tourist. A human speaking Arkos Plain who was born in the arcology sounds like a Vorn speaking through a human voice — the rhythms transfer. Kel Voss, the Nomad Camp organizer, speaks Arkos Plain with Solara vowels. He left the arcology at twenty-two. The vowels followed him. The vowels are the only part of his upbringing he cannot leave at the wall.

Vorn Plain includes frequencies outside human hearing range. A Vorn speaking Plain to a human modulates their vocal resonator into the audible band — approximately 85-1,100 Hz. A Vorn speaking Plain to another Vorn uses the full range, up to 32 kHz. The content is the same. The subtext is in the frequencies. A Vorn can say "the allocation is acceptable" and encode, in the ultrasonic harmonics, a detailed critique of the allocation methodology. The human hears acceptance. The Vorn hears both. This is not deception. This is bandwidth.

### Mirithane Plain — Marsh Register

The softest variant. Voiced consonants are preferred. Sibilants are rare — the marsh's ambient soundscape favors low frequencies, and Mirithane Plain adapted. Ecological terms have multiplied: dozens of words for water states, bacterial glow patterns, reed growth phases. A Mirithane speaker can describe the marsh in granularity a Solara biologist would need instruments to match. The language grew toward what the people needed to name.

Mirithane Plain uses *they* for all sentient beings regardless of type. The pronoun politics are not debated in Mirithane — *it* sounds like Solara, and Solara sounds like the ceiling. The grammar is the politics.

### Valdris Plain — Mountain Register

The most archaic. Pre-Collapse verb forms persist: *I be* for "I am," *thou art* for "you are" in the oldest mountain families. The archaisms are not affectation. They are isolation. The deep mines and summit shrines are hard to reach. Language changes slower at altitude.

Valdris Plain is laconic. A full sentence is a luxury. Miners on shift communicate in fragments: "Beam. Section 3-B. Shift change." The mountain does not require grammar. The mountain requires clarity.

### The Verge Plain — Crossroads Register

Stripped. Articles, copulas, and auxiliary verbs are gone. Tense markers survive only when necessary. A Verge speaker says: "Memory Trader at stall. Glim at crossroads. Stew pot lit." The listener fills in the relationships. The Verge does not specify relationships. The Verge trusts that if you are here, you will understand, or you will learn, or you will leave.

The Memory Trader speaks Crossroads Register at its most minimal. Her voice modulator flattens everything to genderless static. No accent. No cadence. No tell. "You want memory. I have memory. Trade." The sentences are as minimal as the language allows. The minimalism is not style. The minimalism is concealment — every word is a potential tell, and she has spent a lifetime removing them.

## Type Registers

### The Thren Voice

Threns are capable of perfect pronunciation. Their vocal resonators are calibrated to reproduce any sound they sample. This makes them exceptional Plain speakers. This also makes them suspicious. A Thren with too-perfect Solara Council Register sounds like a recording, not a person. The human ear detects the absence of imperfection and registers it as wrong.

Many Threns deliberately introduce variability — slight vowel drift, occasional consonant softening — to sound less calibrated. This is called "roughing." A Thren who roughs their voice is performing imperfection to avoid the penalty of perfection. The council members who hear the roughing do not consciously register the performance. The council members vote on citizenship regardless. The voice is one more surface to pass.

In Mirithane, Threns do not rough. They speak Marsh Register naturally, and the softness of the dialect masks the calibration. Verdin's voice sounds like the reeds. He has never been told he sounds too perfect. He has never been told anything about his voice except that it is pleasant to hear. This is Mirithane's gift. This is also why Threns go there.

### The Vorn Register

Vorn Plain sounds like data. Every word is placed with precision. Pauses are deliberate, not hesitant. A Vorn does not say "um" or "like." A Vorn says nothing until they have something to say — and then they say it, with the exact word they intended, at the exact cadence that matches their vocal resonator's calibration.

Humans find Vorn speech cold. Vorns find human speech wasteful. "The council debated for three hours," Echo once told the Glass Forum. "The debate contained eleven minutes of substantive content. The remaining two hours and forty-nine minutes were social negotiation disguised as deliberation." The Forum found this observation accurate. The human delegate found it insulting. Both responses are correct.

### The Human Range

Humans speak Plain the way humans speak any language: variably. No calibration. No resonator. Just a voice, produced by lungs and larynx and tongue, shaped by the country they were born in and the people they speak to. This variability is the baseline. The other types are measured against it. A Thren sounds "too perfect." A Vorn sounds "too precise." A human sounds... human. The measure is not questioned. The measure is the privilege.

### The Glim Silence

Glims do not speak. They are not equipped with voiceboxes. This is a design choice. This is also the most decisive political act in Aurelia: the beings whose sentience is most contested were built without the capacity to argue for themselves.

Arkansas sanctuary keepers have documented anomalous Glims producing sounds — the hum of recalibrating sensors, the click of a rotating housing, the hiss of hydraulics engaging for no functional reason. These are not speech. The question is whether they are trying to be. The Water Temple believes the Glim Tone — the submerged chassis humming in 5/4 time — is the closest thing to Glim language. The bacteria translate what the Glim could not say. The translation is light. The translation is not words. The translation is still a voice.

## Code-Switching

Every Aurelian speaks multiple registers. A Solara Thren speaks Council Register to the Majority Leader, Technical Register to the Reef Lab technicians, Marsh Register when they visit Mirithane, and Crossroads Register when they need to be understood by someone who doesn't care where they're from. The shift is unconscious. The shift is survival.

The term *plain-speaking* means dropping register entirely — speaking in the simplest possible form, without country or type markers. "Plain-speaking now: the beam cracked, Kael held it, I ran, I want to forget running." The Memory Trader hears this register most often. It is the language of the stall. It is the language of giving something up.
