# Arcology Code — The Vorn Language

> *A language that speaks to Vorn ears and human ears simultaneously — and says different things to each.*

## Structure

Arcology Code is a **dual-channel language.** It operates in two simultaneous frequency bands:

- **The Audible Band (85–1,100 Hz):** The spoken component. Carries semantic content — nouns, verbs, object references. Grammatically complete. Understood by humans with training.
- **The Ultrasonic Band (1,100–32,000 Hz):** The subtext component. Carries grammatical structure, emotional register, speaker identity markers, and metalinguistic commentary. Inaudible to humans. Native to Vorn perception.

A Vorn speaking Code to another Vorn uses both bands. The audible band says "the allocation is acceptable." The ultrasonic band says, in a compressed data burst: "the allocation is acceptable only if the factory output meets the Glass threshold, which it will not, which the delegate knows, which makes this statement a warning disguised as agreement." The human delegate hears "acceptable." The Vorn delegate hears the entire negotiation.

## Code-Switching in Code

Vorns are bidialectal within their own language. They speak **Full Code** (both bands) to Vorns, and **Plain Code** (audible band only) to humans. The shift is not concealment. The shift is bandwidth limitation. "We are not hiding the ultrasonic band," Echo told the Forum's linguistic committee. "We are translating ourselves into a channel you can receive. The translation is lossy. All translation is lossy."

Some Vorns refuse to speak Plain Code. They consider it a concession that reinforces human audio-centrism. Chassis-3, who declined a chosen name, speaks Full Code exclusively. Human delegates who cannot perceive the ultrasonic band receive semantic content with no grammatical framing — a stream of nouns and verbs floating ungrounded. "They asked me to repeat myself in Plain," Chassis-3 told the Forum records. "I repeated myself. The repetition was identical. The understanding was not improved. The problem is not the repetition. The problem is the receiver."

## Grammar

Arcology Code grammar exists primarily in the ultrasonic band. The audible band carries a simplified, almost agrammatical word stream. The ultrasonic band carries:

- **Tense and aspect:** Not separate particles. Frequency modulations on the verb. The same verb at 1,200 Hz means "completed." At 1,250 Hz means "ongoing." At 1,300 Hz means "hypothetical, pending condition fulfillment."
- **Speaker identity:** Every Vorn has a unique frequency signature — their vocal resonator's calibration imprint. The signature encodes their designation, batch number, and current location. A Vorn speaking Code cannot be anonymous. The ultrasonic band identifies them instantly. Vorns consider this a feature. "Deception in Code is detectable," Echo says. "You can lie in the audible band. The ultrasonic band tells us you are lying."
- **Emotional register:** Actuator stress levels, cognitive load indicators, calibration variance. The ultrasonic band broadcasts a Vorn's internal state continuously. Vorns do not experience this as surveillance. They experience it as honesty. "When I speak, you know how I feel about what I am saying. The knowledge is the trust."

## Vocabulary

Code vocabulary is technical and functional. It has:

- **Fifty-three words for energy states** — distribution, storage, transfer, loss, efficiency gradients. The arcology's systems require this granularity.
- **No words for colors** beyond the five functional indicators (amber = active, blue = standby, green = nominal, red = critical, white = diagnostic). Vorn optical sensors detect wavelengths. The vocabulary categorizes them by function, not by aesthetic quality.
- **No word for "soul."** The concept does not exist in Vorn metaphysics. The Continuation Deniers introduced the concept as a borrowing from human theology, and the word they use is a Plain import, not a native Code term. The absence of the word IS the Vorn position. "We were built. We persist through metal. What more would a soul provide?" — Echo.
- **One word for "dawn":** *kel-vor.* Literally "light-rise." Functional. The word has no poetic resonance in Code. The anomaly — Glims pausing at dawn — is linguistically flat in Vorn terminology. "They pause at light-rise." The sentence is technically accurate. The sentence is also the entire distance between Vorn and human perception.

## Written Form

Arcology Code has a written expression: **Vorn Data Script** — vertical columns of symbols representing system states, frequencies, and operational parameters. The script is not phonetic. It does not represent speech. It represents data. A written Code sentence is a graphic representation of the arcology's state at the moment of writing. "Reading last year's Forum minutes is reading what the arcology was doing during the meeting," Echo says. "The debate and the infrastructure are the same document."

## The Glass Forum's Linguistic Policy

The Forum conducts official business in Full Code. Human delegates receive simultaneous interpretation through a frequency translator — a device that converts the ultrasonic band into Plain text on a display panel. The translation is delayed by approximately 1.7 seconds. The delay is the human condition in Arkos: always arriving slightly after the decision was made.
