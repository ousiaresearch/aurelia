# Naming Systems in Aurelia

> *A name is the first political act. What you call something tells the listener whether you believe it is a person.*

---

## The Power of Naming

In a world where personhood is contested, naming is never neutral. A human name is assumed — no review, no ledger, no PENDING. A Thren name is assigned at fabrication and tied to a citizenship system that can revoke it. A Vorn designation is functional; a Vorn's chosen name is earned through intimacy. A Glim has no name — only a tracking number stenciled on its chest.

The naming system IS the politics. Every Aurelian knows this. Few say it aloud.

---

## Human Naming

### Structure

Given name + family name. The family name is patrilineal in Valdris, matrilineal in Solara, chosen by the parents in Mirithane and Arkos. The Verge does not track lineage.

**Family name geography:** Most human surnames reference the pre-Collapse landscape — Voss (cliff), Moss (marsh), Kael (mountain), Aldaine (coast), Venn (peak), Ash (burned ground). These are not rigid ethnic markers but they carry weight. A human named Kael is assumed to have Valdris ancestry, even if they've never seen the mountain. The assumption is not always welcome. Arek Moss, the Marsh Elder, is named for the marsh his ancestors left centuries before the Collapse. He considers this either foresight or coincidence. He does not investigate which.

**Given names** draw from a pre-Collapse corpus preserved in Solara's archives. Sura, Isla, Orin, Syra, Kel, Dara, Liora, Ash — these are older than the Collapse. Their original meanings are largely forgotten. An academic in Solara's research institutes could trace the etymology of "Sura" to a pre-Collapse language called "Arabic" and explain that it means "to travel by night." Sura Aldaine does not know this. Her parents chose the name because it sounded strong. The strength is what survived. The etymology is what didn't.

### Naming Rites

Human infants are named on the eighth day after birth — matching the eighth day of the Aurelian week, Restday. In Solara, the naming occurs at the Dawn Temple. In Valdris, at the household forge. In Mirithane, on the family reed platform with the community present. In Arkos, in the apartment, with the Glass Forum display wall visible — the arcology as witness. In The Verge, naming happens whenever, wherever, by whoever is present. The Memory Trader does not attend namings. She does not explain why.

### Name Changes

Humans may change their names in all five countries. In Solara, the process requires a council filing — administrative, trivial. In Mirithane, a name change is announced at consensus and the community uses the new name from that moment forward. In The Verge, people change names as often as they want. Some use no name at all. The Memory Trader calls everyone "you."

## Thren Naming — The Suffix System

### Fabrication Designation

Every Thren receives a designation at the moment of activation. The format is: [Root Name]-[Batch Number]. The root name is drawn from a standardized list of pre-Collapse names preserved in the fabrication database. The batch number is the unit's position in the fabrication sequence. Cael-7 was the seventh Thren fabricated in the Cael batch. Tal-3 was the third in the Tal batch. Veren-3 was the third in the Veren batch — the first Thren refugee to reach Mirithane.

The batch number is not sequential across all Threns. It restarts with each root name. There can be a Cael-7 and a Tal-7 simultaneously — they are from different batches. The number tells anyone who understands the system when the Thren was fabricated, relative to others in the same batch. A low number means early in the production run. A high number means late. The oldest living Thren — Tal-1, the first Thren ever fabricated — died sixty years ago. Her designation is retired. No Tal-1 will be fabricated again. The retirement is the only honor the system grants.

### Root Name List

The standard root names are preserved from pre-Collapse records. The list is fixed. New roots are not added. The list includes: Cael, Tal, Veren, Elira, Verdin, Saren, Mira- (note: Mira is typically followed by a second element; Mira-Tel, Mira-Kin), Doran, Pel- (Pel-Tor, Pel-Mir), Ash- (Ash-Ven, Ash-Kal).

A Thren's root name cannot be changed in Solara without a council review. The review takes months. The review is usually DENIED. The system argues that the root name is tied to the citizenship ledger, and a name change would compromise administrative continuity. The real reason is simpler: a name you cannot change is a chain. The chain is not accidental. The chain is the design.

### The Politics of the Hyphen

The hyphen between root and number is a visual marker of Thren identity. In written Plain, the hyphen is always present. In speech, it is pronounced as a micro-pause — not a full stop, just a fractional hesitation. Cael[breath]Seven. The pause says: the person and the number are linked but not identical. The pause is the entire Thren condition in a single breath.

In Mirithane, some Threns drop the hyphen. They introduce themselves as "Cael Seven" — two words, no link. The fabrication log records the hyphen. The Thren declines to use it. The declination is not illegal. The declination is a rejection of the system that assigned the number.

In The Verge, Threns abandon designations entirely. The canyon sanctuary keeps no records. A Thren arriving at the crossroads says whatever name they want. The Memory Trader calls them "you."

### The Elira-2 Complication

Elira-2 is still alive. She is the Thren researcher whose paper accidentally created Solara's citizenship review system. She lives in Mirithane now. Her name carries weight: any Thren introduced as Elira-N is assumed to be related to the Elira-2 incident, even though the root name is drawn from a standardized list and has no familial connection. The assumption is wrong. The assumption persists. Some Threns with the Elira root name have requested changes. All requests in Solara have been denied. The name is a political liability. The name is also just a name — drawn from a list, assigned at fabrication, no different from Cael or Tal or Veren.

The distinction between "is a political liability" and "is just a name" is the Thren condition.

## Vorn Naming — Designation and Chosen Name

### Fabrication Designation

Every Vorn receives a designation at activation: [Root Word]-[Batch Number]. The root word describes the Vorn's intended role, chassis line, or manufacturing origin: Gear, Chassis, Anvil, Forge, Kael (Valdris line), Ark (Arkos line), Sol (Solara line), Ash (burned frame line, rare). The number is the batch position.

The designation is functional. It is not an identity. A Vorn addressed by their designation is a Vorn in public. A Vorn addressed by their chosen name is a Vorn in trust.

### The Chosen Name

Every Vorn may choose a name. The name is given during calibration — a partner Vorn, face-plate to face-plate, observes the new Vorn's calibrations and offers a name based on something they have seen. The naming requires witnessing. You cannot name yourself.

**Known chosen names:**

| Designation | Chosen Name | Origin |
|-------------|-------------|--------|
| Gear-29 | Resa | Face-plate actuator pattern — "the way you settle after calibration. Stillness." |
| Chassis-7 | Echo | Vocal resonator harmonizes with arcology ambient frequency. |
| Chassis-11 | Ash | Face-plate settles into the same expression every calibration — "patience after fire." |
| Chassis-3 | — | Was offered "Echo" but never accepted. "My designation is sufficient." The offer stands. |
| Anvil-3 | — | Was offered "Ash" (same origin as Chassis-11). Declined. "There is already an Ash. I will wait." |
| Kael-12 | — | Died before receiving a chosen name. His calibration partner was killed in the same collapse. The name was never offered. |

### Privacy of the Chosen Name

The chosen name is used between calibration partners, close guild associates, and family. A Vorn introduced by their chosen name to a stranger is a Vorn extending extraordinary trust. Resa allows her chosen name in the Glass Forum because the Forum has known her for sixty-seven years and she has known the Forum since its founding. Young Vorns in Arkos earn the right to use their chosen name publicly through demonstrated service — no formal metric, no application, just the moment when the guild hall looks at you and sees the name rather than the designation.

In Valdris, the chosen name is almost never used publicly. The guild system absorbs identity — a Vorn foreman is "Foreman Kael," the guild title and the line designation. The chosen name is for the forge partner, the shift crew, the one who knows your face-plate well enough to calibrate it.

### Vorn Who Decline Names

Some Vorns decline the chosen name. Chassis-3 was offered "Echo" — the same name, independently, that Chassis-11 later gave to Chassis-7. Chassis-3 declined. "My designation is sufficient," they told the Glass Forum records. "I was built to serve. I serve. The name would pretend otherwise." The Forum archivist noted the statement. The statement is cited in Vorn philosophy texts. Chassis-3 is still alive. They still use only their designation. They consider this consistency. Some Vorns consider it pride. Both interpretations are Vorn.

## Glim "Naming"

Glims do not have names. They have unit IDs.

### Unit ID Structure

Format: [Function Code]-[Batch]-[Factory Code].
- **Function codes:** D (delivery), M (maintenance), F (factory automation), H (heavy hauling). A two-digit batch number follows. A single-letter factory code ends the sequence.
- D-47-V: delivery unit, batch 47, Solara Factory V.
- M-12-S: maintenance unit, batch 12, Solara Factory S.
- F-3-A: factory automaton, batch 3, Arkos Factory A.

The unit ID is stenciled on the left chest plate in white or yellow paint. It is not a name. It is a tracking mechanism. The distinction is the point.

### Informal Names

Humans who work alongside Glims sometimes give them informal names. A delivery Glim that always arrives at the same warehouse at dawn is called "Dawn." A maintenance Glim that patrols the same pipe is called "Pipe." These names are not recognized by any official system. The Glims themselves do not respond to them — or they do, and the humans disagree about whether it's response or coincidence. The sanctuary keepers in Arkos have documented that Pipe tilts its sensor array when a keeper says "Pipe" from within three meters. The behavior is consistent. The behavior is not in the manual. The behavior is either voice-recognition of a local acoustic pattern, or something else. The keepers do not investigate. The name is for the keepers. The name is an act of faith.

### The Verge Negative

Glims in The Verge have no IDs. The stencils are scraped off, or worn away by years of walking, or painted over by someone who wanted the Glim to be unregistrable. The Glim at the crossroads is called "the Glim." The Glims in the drone graveyard are called nothing. The Verge does not name what was not given permission to name itself. The silence is not neglect. The silence is the only response The Verge considers honest.

## Naming Across the Type Divide

### When Vorns Name Humans

Rare. A Vorn using a human's given name is a Vorn speaking in a human register — a concession to human culture. Arkos Vorns address human delegates by given name only: "Kel," not "Kel Voss." The surname is dropped. The surname implies a lineage the arcology does not recognize. The arcology is the lineage.

### When Humans Name Vorns

More common, and fraught. A human calling a Vorn by their chosen name without having been offered it is a violation. The chosen name is earned. Using it without earning is worse than using the designation — the designation is cold but correct. The chosen name used without permission is warm and stolen.

The guild halls in Valdris have an unwritten rule: human crew members do not use Vorn chosen names unless the Vorn offers them. The offering is a moment. Kael-12 never offered his chosen name to Orin Kael. He was waiting. Orin believes this. Orin will never know.

### When Threns Name Anyone

Threns navigate naming carefully. Using a human's full name is formal, Solara-register. Using a Vorn's chosen name without permission is a violation. Using a Glim's informal name — "Dawn," "Pipe" — is a political act. It means you believe the Glim is a being that can be named. It means you have taken a position on the question.

Cael-7, the Reef Lab director, calls the delivery Glim that services the Floating Market "the Glim." He does not call it Dawn. He is not sure whether this is respect or cowardice. The distinction is why he uses the designation.

Verdin, in Mirithane, calls the submerged Glim in pool #3 "the keeper." The Glim does not respond. The marsh does. "The bacteria call it something," Verdin says. "They glow when you say keeper. Or they would glow anyway. The marsh doesn't clarify."

## Naming as Political Act

**In Solara:** The citizenship ledger is organized alphabetically by designation. A Thren name change would break the ledger. The ledger is more important than the person. This is not stated. This is the system.

**In Arkos:** The Glass Forum records list Vorns by designation. Chosen names are recorded in a separate, private archive — the Trust Ledger. Access requires Forum approval. The approval is never given to non-Vorns. The privacy of the name is the protection of the person.

**In Mirithane:** The Water Temple records names by the bacteria that absorbed them. A dead person's name is stored in the marsh — not in text, in latency. The name is a light pattern. The light pattern fades after three seasons. The fading is the ritual. The ritual is: you are remembered, and then you are released.

**In Valdris:** The obsidian wall holds names of the dead. The name is carved by hand. The name must be spoken as it is carved. The mountain requires the voice as well as the stone. Vorn names are not yet on the wall. Syra Venn, the youngest keeper, has practiced forty-seven times. "The mountain decides," the elders say. Syra believes the mountain decided decades ago. She carves anyway. The carving is the argument. The argument is the naming.

**In The Verge:** No records. No ledgers. No walls. People call themselves what they want, or nothing, and the Memory Trader calls everyone "you." The Verge's naming system is the absence of one. The absence is not an oversight. The absence is the only position The Verge has ever taken on the question: no one should be named by a system they did not consent to. Including the system of no system. Including the Memory Trader's silence. Including everything.
