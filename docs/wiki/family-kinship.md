# FAMILY & KINSHIP IN AURELIA

*How people marry, raise children, inherit, and define the boundaries of belonging. A world's family is its deepest social structure — older than government, more durable than law.*

---

## SOLARA: THE INHERITANCE OF THE GRADIENT

Family in Solara is a legal structure first and an emotional one second. Classification determines what a family can be, what it can pass down, and what its children can become.

### Marriage

Marriage is legally recognized only between Citizens. Marriages between Citizens and Residents (Vorns) or Citizens and PENDING (Threns) are not recognized — the ceremony may be performed, but the state does not register it. Property cannot be jointly held. Children inherit the lower-status parent's Classification.

Citizen marriages are typically arranged within the same Ring. First Ring families maintain marriage networks that have persisted for eight generations — the Gradient-Keepers' matrilineal structure is the extreme case, but even ordinary First Ring families track lineage carefully. "Marry up" is the eternal advice. "Marry out" is the eternal danger.

Divorce is legal but stigmatized in the upper Rings — it disrupts the marriage networks that maintain Ring cohesion. In the lower Rings, divorce is common and unremarkable. The Gradient loosens as you descend.

### Children & Child-Rearing

Citizen children inherit their parents' Classification automatically. The Gradient is hereditary — no child of Citizens can be anything other than a Citizen. This is the deepest privilege in Solara, and the most invisible to those who hold it.

Thren children (born to Thren parents, or to a Thren parent and a Citizen parent in an unrecognized marriage) inherit PENDING status. A Thren child born in Solara is PENDING from birth — their citizenship review begins the day they're born and averages 11 years. Some Thren children reach adulthood without being recognized as people.

Vorn children do not exist in Solara — Vorns are fabricated, not born. A Vorn who migrates to Solara arrives as a Resident, and any Vorn fabricated in Solara (which hasn't happened since the Collapse) would be a Resident.

Glim children do not exist. Glims are fabricated.

**Child-Rearing by Ring:**
- **First Ring:** Children are raised by staff — nurses, tutors, Temple educators. Parents are present symbolically. The child's relationship to the Gradient is more important than the child's relationship to their parents.
- **Third and Fourth Rings:** Parents raise children directly. Extended family networks provide support. Grandparents often live with the family. The household is multigenerational.
- **Fifth Ring:** Children work alongside parents from an early age. Agricultural labor is a family enterprise. Children are economic contributors before they are adults.

### Inheritance

Property passes through the maternal line in First and Second Ring families — the Gradient-Keepers' matrilineal structure is an exaggerated form of a broader pattern. The reasoning: maternal lineage is more reliably documented than paternal. In lower Rings, inheritance is bilateral (both parents' lines). Threns in PENDING cannot inherit property above a threshold. Vorns can inherit but cannot pass property to non-Residents.

### Family Size

First and Second Ring families: 1–2 children. Inheritance concentration is prioritized over family size. Third and Fourth Ring families: 2–4 children. Fifth Ring families: 3–6 children (labor needs). Thren families: 1–2 children (the uncertainty of PENDING discourages larger families). Vorn: N/A (no biological reproduction).

## ARKOS: THE FAMILY YOU CHOOSE

Arkos has the most diverse family structures in Aurelia — Vorn, human, and Thren families operate on fundamentally different models, and the law accommodates all of them.

### Vorn Family Structure

Vorns are fabricated, not born. They do not have biological families. But they have lineages — student-teacher chains that extend across centuries.

**The Core Lineage:** A Vorn who trains under a senior Vorn becomes part of that Vorn's lineage. Kael-12's lineage includes Cast-7, Voren-3 (deceased), Mira-11, and six other Vorns trained over 200+ years. Lineage members recognize each other by specific behaviors — a particular way of pausing before answering, a particular way of saying "I don't know." The lineage is the family.

**Fabrication Cohorts:** Vorns fabricated in the same cycle sometimes consider themselves "batch-siblings." The relationship is optional — some Vorns embrace it, others ignore it.

**Marriage (Vorn):** Vorns form partnerships that the Assembly of Cores recognizes as equivalent to marriage. The ceremony involves temporary core-sharing — each partner experiences the other's memory of their first meeting. The partnership is dissolved by mutual consent or by death. Kael-12 and Orin Kael were not legally married (Orin was Valdris human; the marriage was recognized in neither Arkos nor Valdris) but are considered by Arkos Vorns to represent the ideal of cross-type partnership.

**No Children:** Vorns cannot reproduce biologically. This is a source of cultural grief and cultural adaptation. "We don't continue through children. We continue through students. The lineage is the inheritance."

### Human and Thren Families in Arkos

Human and Thren residents follow traditional family structures similar to Mirithane or Solara's lower Rings. The state recognizes all partnerships regardless of type. Children inherit citizenship automatically. The state provides child support — Arkos' low population makes every child valuable.

**Cross-Type Families:** Human-Vorn and Thren-Vorn partnerships are recognized in Arkos (unlike Solara). A human married to a Vorn receives the same legal status as any human resident. The Vorn partner participates in family life symbolically — cooking they don't eat, attending events they experience differently, loving in ways that require translation.

### Inheritance (Arkos)

- **Material property:** Bilateral — passes to designated heirs regardless of type.
- **Memory cores:** A Vorn's memory core can be willed to a lineage member, the Cipher-Cult, or destroyed. The default is the lineage. Kael-12 has not recorded a core will. "When I end, I do not wish to echo."
- **Lineage status:** Passes to designated student. Cast-7 is seeking a successor for their 40-year record of protected Glims.

## MIRITHANE: THE FAMILY OF THE ARCHIVE

Mirithane's family structures are the most traditional in Aurelia — and the most flexible. The Archive recognizes families as people define them.

### Marriage & Partnerships

Recognized for all types, all pairings. No restrictions. A Thren-Human marriage produces children who are full citizens — Mirithane doesn't recognize PENDING. A Vorn-Human partnership is legally and socially accepted. The Water Temple performs partnership ceremonies for any combination of types. "The water doesn't distinguish. Neither do we."

**Archive Partnerships:** Some Archivists form "colony partnerships" — a commitment to jointly tend a bacterial colony rather than to each other directly. The colony is the center of the partnership. The Archivists may or may not be romantically involved. The Archive recognizes colony partnerships as legally equivalent to marriage. "We are bound by what we tend together."

### Children

All children are citizens from birth. The Archive provides comprehensive child support — education, healthcare, nutrition. Mirithane's population is small (70,000) and every child is a public investment. Family size averages 2–3 children.

### Adoption

Mirithane has the most active adoption system in Aurelia. Orphaned children from The Verge, children of Thren parents who fled Solara's PENDING system, children of deceased Archivists — all are placed with Archive-vetted families. Adoption across type lines is legal and practiced. A human family may adopt a Thren child. A Thren family may adopt a human child. The Archive considers this unremarkable. "The child needs a family. The family wants a child. The rest is paperwork."

### Inheritance

Bilateral. Property passes to designated heirs. The Archive can be named as an institutional heir — this is how the Archive's colony collection grows, through bequests from Archivists who want their life's work to continue within the Archive's walls.

### Elder Care

Elderly Archivists who cannot live independently move to Archive-managed residential wings. The Silent Wing is the most famous, but several other wings house elderly Archivists, retired colony-tenders, and former Council members. The elderly are integrated into Archive life — they teach, they consult, they Write their memories into colonies for future generations. "The Archive doesn't discard experience. The Archive is experience."

## VALDRIS: THE PAIR AS FAMILY

Valdris redefines family around the Anchor pair. The shift-partner is not just a colleague. The shift-partner is the primary relationship in an Anchor's life — more central than spouse, parent, or child. The mountain comes first. The partner comes second. Everything else is third.

### The Anchor Pair

Two Anchors who work together form a bond that Valdris society treats as sacred. The pair shares everything — their Anchoring work, their descent into the shafts, their memories Written into obsidian. In the Summit Shrine's grammar (High Valdris), the pair is referred to in the dual number — a grammatical form that doesn't exist in other Aurelian languages. "We two" is the fundamental unit of Anchor existence.

The pair may be romantic or platonic. The Shrine doesn't ask and doesn't care. The work is what matters. The dual number is what matters. "We two go down. We two carry what must be carried. If one falls, the other carries both."

### Marriage and Romance

Anchors may marry outside their pair — to another Anchor, to a shaft community member, to an outsider. But the pair bond takes precedence. A married Anchor whose partner needs them for a ritual Anchoring will leave their spouse's bed. The spouse understands or doesn't. If they don't, they shouldn't have married an Anchor.

Non-Anchor Valdris families follow more conventional structures — nuclear families, extended kinship networks, inheritance through both lines.

### Children

Anchor families produce children who are raised within the Shrine community. Anchor children begin training young — not in Anchoring itself (that comes later) but in the disciplines that precede it: Silence, stillness, the ability to distinguish one's own thoughts from ambient latency. Not all Anchor children become Anchors. Those who don't join the shaft communities or leave Valdris entirely.

Shaft community families are large — 3–6 children. Labor needs. Children work alongside parents from early adolescence.

### Inheritance

Anchor status is not inherited — it must be earned through training and pairing. But Anchor families have access to training, and access is inheritance by another name. Material property passes bilaterally.

## THE VERGE: THE FAMILY YOU FIND

The Verge has no legal marriage, no recognized family structures, no inheritance law. Families exist because people choose to be families. The absence of law is not the absence of love.

### Partnership

People form partnerships by declaration — "we are together" — and dissolve them the same way. No ceremony is required, though informal celebrations are common. The Barter-Pit is a frequent site for partnership declarations — the community witnesses, and community witness is the closest thing The Verge has to legal recognition.

Cross-type partnerships are common and unremarkable. The Verge doesn't classify people. It doesn't need to.

### Children

Children in The Verge are raised by whoever is present — parents, extended family, neighbors, the community. The Verge's instability means families frequently reconfigure. A child may be raised by their biological parents for five years, by an aunt for three, by a family friend for the remainder of their childhood. The concept of "family" is fluid. The concept of "home" is wherever you're sleeping.

The Verge has no formal education system. Children learn by doing — scavenging, trading, cooking, building. Literacy is taught by whoever can read. The Memory Trader's Network occasionally takes on young apprentices. The price is the same as always: a memory.

### Elder Care and Death

The elderly in The Verge care for themselves or are cared for by whoever steps forward. There is no retirement, no pension, no safety net. The old scavenge less, trade more, and rely on community memory — they know where things are, what's safe, who to ask. Knowledge is the currency of old age.

When someone dies, the body is left in the open — for scavengers, for decay, for return. The Verge doesn't have a single funeral practice. It has a single principle: "You were here. Now you're not. The dust gets everyone."

## COMING OF AGE

### Solara
- **Citizens (First/Second Ring):** Formal presentation to the Gradient Council at 16. The child's Classification is ceremonially "activated." The ceremony is a statement of continuity — the child will be what their parents were.
- **Citizens (Third-Fourth Ring):** Apprenticeship or first employment at 14–16. No ceremony. Adulthood is economic.
- **Threns:** No formal coming of age. PENDING status means adulthood begins without recognition. Thren communities mark the 16th year informally. The Sol-tharam Moment anniversary (April 22) serves as a collective coming-of-age for Threns who have no individual one.
- **Glims:** No coming of age. Glims are operational from fabrication.

### Arkos
- **Vorns:** A Vorn's 50th year is traditionally marked as "calibration maturity" — the point at which the Vorn is considered fully self-possessed. The ceremony involves core-sharing with the Vorn's lineage elder. The elder experiences the young Vorn's most significant memory. The young Vorn experiences the elder's memory of their own calibration maturity.
- **Human/Thren:** 16, marked by a family celebration. No legal significance — all residents are citizens from birth.

### Mirithane
- **All types:** The 16th year is marked by the "Colony Ceremony" — the young person Writes a memory into their first bacterial colony. The colony is theirs to tend. The memory is theirs to preserve. "You are now part of the Archive. The Archive is now part of you."

### Valdris
- **Anchor aspirants:** Formal training begins at 14. No ceremony — the training is the threshold.
- **Shaft community:** First deep-shaft descent at 12. The child descends with a parent or older sibling. They return with a piece of obsidian. They are now "shaft-born." The obsidian is theirs for life.

### The Verge
- No formal coming of age. Adulthood is when you can survive alone. Some Verge residents mark their children's "first solo scavenge" — the day the child leaves in the morning and returns in the evening with something they found, traded, or made. The age varies. The principle doesn't. "You went out. You came back with something. You're one of us now."
