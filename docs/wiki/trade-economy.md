# TRADE, ECONOMY & COMMERCE IN AURELIA

*How goods, money, and value move between five countries that share no common currency and no common government. Trade is what diplomacy pretends to be.*

---

## CURRENCY SYSTEMS

Aurelia has five currencies, no central bank, and no fixed exchange mechanism. Trade functions through mutual agreement, historical precedent, and the quiet understanding that refusing to trade is worse than accepting a bad rate.

### The Solar Credit (☀)

**Issuer:** Solara Gradient Treasury
**Backing:** Solar energy reserves — each credit is theoretically exchangeable for a fixed quantity of stored thermal energy. In practice, the exchange is never made. The backing is symbolic.
**Denominations:** 1☀, 5☀, 20☀, 100☀ (physical tokens: ceramic discs with embedded crystalline strips)
**Exchange Rate:** 0.8☀ = 1 mark (baseline)

The Solar Credit is the most "stable" currency in Aurelia — not because Solara's economy is strongest, but because the Gradient Treasury maintains the fiction of stability through aggressive currency controls. Credit exchange outside Solara is technically illegal. It happens anyway.

**Who Uses Credits:** Solara Citizens, Residents (Vorns with limited access), Thren PENDING (restricted accounts). Glims cannot hold currency. Infrastructure doesn't get paid.

---

### The Kael (♦)

**Issuer:** Arkos Assembly of Cores
**Backing:** Rare-earth mineral reserves — the same minerals used in Fabricator substrates. Every Kael in circulation is nominally backed by a specific quantity of refined mineral held in the Assembly Plant's vaults.
**Denominations:** 1♦, 10♦, 50♦, 200♦ (physical tokens: hexagonal metal plates, machine-stamped)
**Exchange Rate:** 1.2♦ = 1 mark (baseline)

The Kael is named for the original Kael-1 — the Vorn who first requested a name and was denied. The currency was established after the Name Rebellion as a deliberate statement: "We name our own value."

**Who Uses Kaels:** Everyone in Arkos. Humans and Threns hold Kaels alongside Vorns. The currency is not type-restricted.

---

### The Miri (≈)

**Issuer:** Marsh Archive (not a treasury — the Archive manages currency as it manages everything: by recording)
**Backing:** Water reserves — Mirithane's most abundant and renewable resource. The backing is genuine: a Miri can be exchanged for a fixed volume of purified water at any Archive facility.
**Denominations:** 1≈, 3≈, 10≈, 30≈ (physical tokens: polished reed-wood discs, colony-stamped with Archive strain markers)
**Exchange Rate:** 1.0≈ = 1 mark (baseline)

The Miri is the only currency in Aurelia with genuine, exchangeable backing. Anyone can walk into an Archive facility, present a Miri token, and receive water. This makes the Miri the most trusted currency across borders — even countries that dislike Mirithane accept Miri tokens at near-face value.

---

### The Ark (▲)

**Issuer:** Summit Shrine (de facto; Valdris has no treasury)
**Backing:** Stored energy — each Ark represents a fixed quantity of geothermal power stored in the Shrine's battery chambers. Like the Solar Credit, the backing is rarely exchanged. Unlike the Solar Credit, the Shrine doesn't pretend otherwise.
**Denominations:** 1▲, 5▲, 25▲ (physical tokens: obsidian shards, laser-etched with denomination and Anchor pair marks)
**Exchange Rate:** 1.5▲ = 1 mark (baseline)

The Ark is the least-traded currency in Aurelia. Valdris' economy is largely internal. Arks are used for external purchases of goods Valdris cannot produce (primarily from Arkos). The obsidian tokens are beautiful — small Anchored shards, each carrying the Anchor pair's certification memory. Collectors in Solara's First Ring pay premiums for Ark tokens. The Shrine disapproves. The Shrine doesn't stop it.

---

### The Verge (Barter)

The Verge has no currency. Value is determined by need, scarcity, and the mutual agreement of the parties. Common barter units include:

- **Salvaged components:** Pre-Collapse electronics, intact wiring, usable structural materials
- **Spore-drifter:** Recreational and ritual use; consistent demand across borders
- **Memories:** The Memory Trader's Network accepts memories as payment. The Trader determines the value. The Trader's valuation is never questioned.
- **Labor:** Work traded for goods. A day's scavenging for a week's food.
- **Information:** Knowledge of salvage locations, safe routes, or border crossing conditions is tradeable.

The Memory Trader's Coin — a blank crystalline token Written with a single traded memory — functions as an informal high-value currency within the Network. A Coin token can be exchanged between Traders. Its value is the memory it contains. That value is known only to the Trader who Wrote it.

## TRADE ROUTES

### The Grain Road (Solara → Arkos)
**Route:** Solara Prime → Quiet-Station (Arkos border) → Arkos Central
**Primary Goods:** Solar grain (800 tons/year), fiber-flax, root vegetables
**Return Goods:** Assembly Protein, precision components, needle-venom
**Mode:** Caravan — wool-beast-drawn carts on the maintained road network
**Travel Time:** 8–10 days each direction
**Hazards:** Minimal. The route is patrolled by Solara and Arkos security. The border crossing at Quiet-Station is the fastest customs process in Aurelia — Arkos values speed over thoroughness.

### The Marsh Channel (Mirithane → Solara)
**Route:** Reed-Mouth → coastal waters → Dawn-Over-Water
**Primary Goods:** Marsh-rice (400 tons/year), glass-fin (60 tons/year), spore-drifter (50 tons/year)
**Return Goods:** Solar grain products, fiber-flax textiles, Temple-authorized latency tools
**Mode:** Boat — marsh skiffs for shallow water, larger trading vessels for coastal routes
**Travel Time:** 5–7 days (coastal route), 12–15 days (inland marsh route)
**Hazards:** Seasonal storms. Glass-fin schools — a vessel striking a glass-fin school suffers hull damage. The route is safest in summer.

### The Mountain Pass (Valdris → Arkos)
**Route:** Summit-Hold → Pass-High → The Verge (skirted) → Arkos Central
**Primary Goods:** Rare-earth minerals (30 tons/year), geothermal produce (luxury), mountain barley
**Return Goods:** Vorn components, imported Mirithane pharmaceuticals, tools
**Mode:** Pack animal — the mountain passes are too narrow for carts
**Travel Time:** 14–18 days
**Hazards:** Weather (the pass closes in winter), ash-hound packs, altitude. Valdris traders are experienced mountaineers. Accidents still happen.

### The Silent Road (Mirithane → Valdris)
**Route:** Archive-Central → Reed-Mouth → coastal waters → The Verge (bypassed) → Pass-High → Summit-Hold
**Primary Goods:** Pharmaceuticals, bacterial colony products, glass-fin tools
**Return Goods:** Obsidian (commercial grade), geothermal spirits, Anchored memory shards (for Archive study)
**Mode:** Boat + pack animal — goods transfer at The Verge's coastal edge
**Travel Time:** 20–25 days
**Hazards:** Complex logistics, The Verge border crossing (informal but unpredictable). This is the longest-established trade route between Mirithane and Valdris.

### The Verge Circuit
**Route:** Circular — The Verge borders all four other countries. Traders circle The Verge's perimeter, trading at border settlements.
**Primary Goods:** Salvage, spore-drifter, information, labor
**Mode:** On foot. The Verge has no transport infrastructure.
**Travel Time:** Variable. A full circuit takes 30–60 days.
**Hazards:** Everything. The Verge is ungoverned. Trade happens at personal risk.

### The Trader's Walk
**Route:** Not a route. The Memory Trader walks where they walk.
**Primary Goods:** Memories, knowledge, questions no one else will ask.
**Mode:** The Trader walks. The Trader has always walked.
**Travel Time:** Unknown. The Trader appears where they're needed. The Trader leaves when the trade is done.

- Solara's grain exports are politically potent — Arkos depends on Solar grain for its human and Thren population. Solara has never used this leverage openly. Everyone knows they could.

## TRADE REGULATION

### Solara: State-Controlled
All imports and exports pass through the Gradient Treasury. Import licenses are required. Export quotas are set annually. The system is designed to maintain Solara's trade advantages while preventing dependency. It is also corrupt — import licenses are allocated through connections, not merit. Third Ring merchants who need licenses buy them from Second Ring officials who don't.

### Arkos: Open Ports
Arkos maintains open trade — minimal tariffs, no import licenses, no export restrictions except on military-applicable components. The policy is ideological: "Vorns were built to serve a closed system. We will not build another." It is also pragmatic — Arkos needs imports and has no leverage to restrict them.

### Mirithane: Archive-Managed
The Marsh Archive manages trade through a licensing system that prioritizes sustainability over profit. Export quotas are set based on ecological impact, not market demand. Glass-fin harvests are capped. Spore-drifter cultivation is regulated. The Archive would rather lose a trade deal than deplete a resource.

### Valdris: Mountain-Limited
Valdris exports only what the mountain can spare. Mineral exports are capped by extraction sustainability. Geothermal produce exports are capped by greenhouse capacity. The Shrine sets quotas based on "what the mountain provides" — a calculation that outsiders find opaque and Valdrans find obvious.

### The Verge: Unregulated
The Verge has no trade policy. Trade happens or it doesn't. Disputes are resolved by whoever is standing nearby. The Memory Trader's Network provides the closest thing to trade enforcement — a Trader who accepts a deal and doesn't deliver is no longer a Trader. The Network's internal enforcement is reputation. Reputation is everything.

## MERCHANT CLASSES

### Solara: The Ring-Wise
Merchants in Solara are Third Ring Citizens — wealthy by lower-Ring standards, invisible to upper-Ring society. The most successful Solar merchants specialize in one trade route and one commodity. Generational knowledge accumulates — a merchant family that has traded glass-fin for three generations knows Mirithane's coastal waters better than most Mirithane fishermen.

### Arkos: The Component Brokers
Arkos merchants are predominantly Vorn — industrial components are Arkos' primary export, and Vorns understand components better than anyone. Human and Thren merchants in Arkos specialize in imports — food, pharmaceuticals, goods Arkos cannot produce. The arrangement is functional, not segregated. It just worked out that way.

### Mirithane: The Colony-Licensed
The Archive licenses all Mirithane merchants. A merchant's license specifies what they may trade and at what volume. The license is a contract with the Archive, not a permission slip. Merchants who violate license terms lose their license. Without a license, you cannot trade. The system is restrictive. It is also fair — licenses are allocated by experience and record, not connections.

### Valdris: The Anchor-Authorized
Significant Valdris trade requires Anchor authorization. An Anchor pair certifies the goods as "mountain-acceptable" — a designation that considers not just quality but "mountain alignment": was the extraction sustainable, was the production respectful, was the trade necessary. Outsiders find this frustrating. Valdrans find it essential.

### The Verge: The Scavenger-Traders
Verge merchants are scavengers first, traders second. They trade what they find. Their expertise is not in a commodity but in finding commodities. A Verge trader who discovers a pre-Collapse cache of intact electronics is wealthy for a season. The next season, they're scavenging again.

## THE BLACK MARKET

### Memory Trade
The Memory Trader's Network is the black market's backbone — not because the Network is criminal (it operates openly in The Verge, Mirithane, and Arkos) but because memory trade is illegal in Solara and Valdris. A Solara Citizen who wants a memory removed cannot go to the Temple (who would record the removal). They go to the Network.

**Common black-market memory trades:**
- Removal of incriminating memories (crime, infidelity, heresy)
- Sale of pleasurable memories (harvested from willing sellers in The Verge)
- Memory forgery — Writing false memories into substrates for fraudulent purposes. Rare but devastating when it works.

### Extraction Equipment
Extraction Manifolds are restricted to Dawn Temple and authorized Network practitioners. Black-market manifolds exist — built from stolen Temple equipment or assembled from salvaged components. A black-market manifold is as dangerous to the operator as to the subject. The Marsh Archive estimates 12–20 illegal manifolds in circulation across Aurelia.

### Spore-Drifter (Unregulated)
Spore-drifter is legal in Mirithane (with Archive license) and The Verge (no regulation). It is illegal in Solara, restricted in Arkos, and unknown in Valdris. The black market moves spore-drifter from Mirithane to Solara's lower Rings, where recreational use is common despite prohibition.
