# Disease in Aurelia

> *The Collapse left pathogens behind. What survived shapes who gets sick and who doesn't.*

## The Collapse Pandemics

The resource wars, climate tipping points, and communication collapse that ended the nation-state era were accompanied by cascading pandemics. The records are sparse. What survived:

- **The Red Wasting:** A hemorrhagic fever that targeted baseline biology. Killed approximately 40% of the surviving human population in the first two decades after the Collapse. Threns and Vorns were immune — the pathogen required organic cellular machinery to replicate. Glims were unaffected.
- **The Spore Winter:** A fungal pathogen that contaminated grain stores across the pre-Collapse territories. Survived in Valdris's deep mines — the spores are still present in Section 4, which has been sealed for 180 years.
- **The Silence:** Not a disease. A period of approximately five years during which no records were kept and the surviving population dropped by an unknown amount. The cause is unknown. The Silence is the reason Aurelia's pre-Collapse history is fragmentary.

## Current Endemic Diseases

### Human

- **Marsh lung:** Respiratory condition from prolonged exposure to Mirithane's humid ecosystem. Treatable with reed-derived inhalants. Common among reed-tenders. Not fatal.
- **Deep cough:** Silicosis from Valdris mining. Chronic. Progressive. The mountain gives and the mountain takes.
- **Sand-fever:** Dehydration-related illness among Arkos Nomad Camp residents. Preventable with adequate water. The camp's water supply is inconsistent.

### Thren

Threns do not contract organic diseases. Their synthetic biology is immune to viral and bacterial infection — a design feature. Threns experience:

- **Circuit oxidation:** The temple and wrist circuitry can corrode in high-humidity environments. Mirithane's marsh climate accelerates oxidation. Threns in Mirithane require quarterly circuitry sealing. The procedure is performed by Water Temple keepers using bacterial enzyme applications. The treatment is effective. The irony — moving to the Thren haven accelerates physical degradation — is not lost on Mirithane's Thren population.

- **Power cell degradation:** Over decades, Thren energy cells lose capacity. A Thren in their sixties requires more frequent rest cycles than a newly fabricated unit. The degradation is called *cell-fade.* It is not treatable. It is the closest Thren equivalent to aging.

### Vorn

- **Joint variance** (covered in overview) — the most common Vorn condition.
- **Sensor haze:** Optical sensor degradation from prolonged exposure to Valdris forge light (high UV). Preventable with lens coatings. The coatings are expensive. Many Valdris Vorns forego them. The foregone protection is called "forge-loyalty" — a point of pride, and a slow blinding.

### Glim

Glims experience mechanical failure modes, not diseases. The most common: **hydraulic seal failure** (leaking fluid, reduced mobility), **sensor drift** (calibration loss, inaccurate data), **battery memory degradation** (reduced charge capacity). All treatable with standard maintenance. Whether a Glim that malfunctions in a way that is not mechanical — the 5% anomaly — is experiencing an illness or an awakening is the question.

---

## Cross-Type Infection

Cross-type disease transmission is impossible. Pathogens that require organic cellular machinery cannot replicate in synthetic Thren tissue or Vorn mechanical systems. The separation is a biological firewall. It is also a social divide — humans can infect each other; Threns and Vorns exist outside the disease ecology, separate, untouched. The untouchedness is either protection or exclusion.
