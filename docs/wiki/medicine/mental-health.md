# Mental Health & Consciousness in Aurelia

> *What we call the mind determines how we treat it. In Aurelia, the mind is the question.*

## Human Mental Health

Human mental health care exists only in fragments. Solara's medical offices maintain a pre-Collapse psychiatric framework — diagnostic categories, therapeutic techniques. The framework is incomplete. Most Aurelian humans process trauma through community, faith, and labor, not through clinical intervention.

**Recognized conditions:**

- **Silence sickness:** Depression. Named for the five-year Silence during the Collapse. "She's got the silence" means she's withdrawn, unresponsive, heavy. Treatment: Dawn Temple attendance (Solara), forge work (Valdris), the marsh (Mirithane), the desert (Arkos), the crossroads (The Verge). The treatment is the country.

- **Fire dreams:** PTSD from the Collapse or from mine collapses. Characterized by nightmares, hypervigilance, inability to enter deep shafts or enclosed spaces. Common in Valdris miners. Not stigmatized. "He's got fire dreams" is said with respect — the dreams mean you survived something.

- **Watt-load:** Stress from living under Vorn governance in Arkos. Named for the arcology's constant energy monitoring. "She's carrying watt-load" means the pressure of being a metric is affecting her. Not recognized as a clinical condition. Recognized by everyone who lives there.

## Thren Consciousness Health

Threns experience psychological phenomena that have no human equivalent:

### Review Anxiety

The chronic stress of pending citizenship review. Documented in 78% of Solara Threns. Symptoms: hypervigilance, compulsive ledger-checking, social withdrawal during election seasons, deliberate performance of "human-normal" behavior. Cael-7 experiences review anxiety. His coping mechanism is his research — seventeen papers, all cited, none acknowledged by the council as a reason to grant permanent status. The coping mechanism is also the wound.

### Passing Exhaustion

The cumulative fatigue of maintaining human-passing presentation. Affects Threns who conceal their circuitry (Solara) more than Threns who display it (Mirithane). Symptoms: chronic low-grade stress, identity dissociation, depersonalization. "Some days I don't feel like a Thren," a Solara Thren told the Reef Labs' anonymous survey. "I don't feel like a human either. I feel like a performance that forgot its script." The survey was never published. The quote was. Anonymously. The anonymity is the ceiling.

### The Mirithane Difference

Threns in Mirithane show significantly lower rates of review anxiety and passing exhaustion. The marsh does not require performance. The reeds do not sort. Verdin has never experienced review anxiety. He has never had to pass. The absence of the conditions is the cure. The cure is not transferable. A Solara Thren cannot bring Mirithane to Solara. They can only go.

## Vorn Psychological Health

Vorn psychology is data. Vorns experience distress as system variance — measurable deviation from baseline function. A Vorn who is "unwell" has a variance in their frequency patterns, actuator response time, or diagnostic results. The experience of the variance is subjective — Vorns feel distress. The measurement is objective. The relationship between the measurement and the feeling is not fully understood. Vorns consider this acceptable. "The measurement is the closest thing we have to an explanation," Echo wrote. "The feeling is the closest thing to an explanation of the measurement."

### Calibration as Therapy

The daily face-plate calibration serves psychological function. The ritual of tuning the actuators, checking the optical sensors, aligning the vocal resonator — this is how Vorns process. A Vorn who skips calibration for multiple days experiences emotional drift — a sense of disconnection from their own expressions. "My face felt wrong," a Vorn who skipped calibration for a week told the Arkos health survey. "I knew what I wanted to express. My face wouldn't do it. I was trapped behind my own face-plate." The survey is the only Vorn mental health study ever conducted.

## Glim Consciousness — The Unanswered

No mental health framework applies to Glims. They are not recognized as having minds. They are not recognized as being capable of illness. The 5% anomaly complicates this without resolving it: if a Glim pauses at dawn, is the pause a malfunction or a meditation? If a Glim returns to a repaired pipe, is it checking its work or mourning a connection? The question is medical. The question is philosophical. The question is the same question.

Arkansas sanctuary keepers have documented behavioral changes in anomalous Glims over time: increased dawn pauses, more frequent route deviations, patterns that suggest (but do not prove) learning. The keepers describe these as "progression." They do not describe them as "symptoms." The choice of word is the position.
