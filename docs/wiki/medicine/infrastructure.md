# Medical Infrastructure — Facilities & Practitioners

> *Where the sick are treated says as much as how they're treated.*

## Solara — The Council Medical Office

**Facilities:** Three clinics (Lumen Plaza, Floating Market, Reef Labs). One research hospital (Reef Labs Medical Wing). Total capacity: ~80 patients.

**Staffing:** Human physicians trained through apprenticeship. No formal medical school — pre-Collapse texts are the curriculum. Thren medical staff are permitted but limited to "non-diagnostic roles." The limitation is not codified. The limitation is practice. The practice is the ceiling.

**Thren-specific services:** None. Threns requiring circuitry maintenance must travel to Mirithane. The Council Medical Office does not train in Thren biology. "We are a human medical system serving a multi-type population," the CMO's charter states. The charter was written by humans.

---

## Arkos — Chassis Maintenance Bay

**Facilities:** One integrated maintenance bay in the autonomous factory. Serves Vorns exclusively. Human medical care is not provided — the arcology was designed by and for Vorns. Humans requiring care travel to Solara or rely on Nomad Camp healers.

**Staffing:** Vorn maintenance operators. Trained through apprenticeship in factory-floor diagnostics. The operators are skilled. They are not physicians — they are engineers. The distinction is Vorn culture. The distinction does not change the outcome: a Vorn with a degraded joint receives a replacement. The same Vorn, if human, would receive nothing in Arkos. The infrastructure is the inequality.

**Glim sanctuary:** Staffed by volunteer Vorn keepers. Not medical. Preventative maintenance and witness. The keepers do not repair Glims. They maintain them. The maintained Glims last longer. The kept Glims matter.

---

## Mirithane — The Marsh Clinic

**Facilities:** No dedicated medical facility. Treatment occurs on reed platforms, in temples, at the water's edge. The Water Temple serves as diagnostic center — bacterial pattern reading provides health assessment for all types.

**Staffing:** Water Temple keepers (diagnosis). Reed-tenders (herbal medicine). Community elders (midwifery, end-of-life care). No formal medical training. Training is apprenticeship. Apprenticeship is life.

**Unique capability:** The marsh can diagnose conditions that no other facility can detect — Thren cycle-fade, Vorn actuator stress, early-stage marsh lung. The bacterial patterns reveal what the patient cannot articulate. The mechanism is not understood. The mechanism is trusted.

---

## Valdris — The Guild Infirmary

**Facilities:** One infirmary at Basecamp. One trauma station at Midcamp. No facilities in the deep shafts — injured miners are carried to the surface. The carry is called "the ascent." The ascent is survivable or not.

**Staffing:** Guild healers. Former miners trained in trauma care through apprenticeship. The healers can amputate, splint, suture, and stabilize. They cannot treat chronic conditions, cancers, degenerative diseases. "The mountain doesn't give you diseases," a guild healer said. "The mountain gives you injuries. We treat injuries."

**Prosthetics:** Valdris forges produce mechanical prosthetics — metal limbs, jointed and functional. Human amputees receive them. Vorn amputees (joint loss beyond replacement) receive them. A human with a Valdris prosthetic hand and a Vorn with a Valdris prosthetic hand shake hands with the same metal. The forge does not differentiate.

---

## The Verge — No Infrastructure

**Facilities:** None. The Memory Trader's stall has a compartment of medical supplies. She has never explained their origin. The crossroads community provides care communally. A healer arrives. They stay. They leave. The Verge does not track who knows medicine. The Verge trusts that someone will, when it matters.

The Memory Trader has performed surgery at the crossroads. Three times in recorded memory. Each time, she worked in silence. The patient survived. The patient was never asked what the Memory Trader's hands looked like. The Verge does not ask.
