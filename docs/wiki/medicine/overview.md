# Medicine in Aurelia — Overview

> *A body is a political argument. Treating one is never only medicine.*
>

## The Type Divide in Medicine

Aurelian medicine splits along the biological/built axis. Human and Thren bodies are similar enough that most treatments overlap — Thren synthetic biology was modeled on human physiology. Vorn bodies require mechanical intervention — a Vorn does not get sick; a Vorn's chassis degrades. Glim bodies are industrial equipment — maintenance, not medicine.

The divide creates inequities. A country with advanced Vorn maintenance (Arkos) may have limited human medical care. A country with excellent human medicine (Solara) may treat Glim maintenance as an afterthought — because Glims are property, and property maintenance is budgeted differently than healthcare.

## Human Medicine

### Capabilities

Pre-Collapse medical knowledge survived in fragmentary form. Solara's Reef Labs maintain a library of medical texts — anatomy, pharmacology, surgical technique. The knowledge is incomplete. Antibiotics are produced from reef organisms. Surgery is performed with salvaged pre-Collapse instruments, supplemented by Valdris-forged tools. The standard of care varies dramatically by country:

**Solara:** The highest standard in the federation. The Council Medical Office operates clinics in the Lumen Plaza and Floating Market districts. Services are free for Natural Citizens, reduced-cost for Granted Citizens, full-cost for Recognized Entities (Vorns). Glims are not served by medical clinics.

**Arkos:** The arcology has no dedicated human medical facilities — Arkos is a Vorn-designed city, and Vorn-designed infrastructure addresses Vorn needs first. Humans in Arkos rely on the Nomad Camp's informal healers or travel to Solara for treatment.

**Mirithane:** Marsh medicine — herbal, bacterial, holistic. Reed-poultices for wounds. Bacterial cultures for infection. The Water Temple's knowledge of *P. memoriae* has medical applications: certain bacterial glow patterns correlate with infection markers, and trained keepers can diagnose by reading a patient's water sample.

**Valdris:** Trauma care only. The mines produce injuries — crush wounds, fractures, respiratory damage from deep-shaft air. Valdris healers are practical, skilled, and limited. A miner with a crushed leg receives amputation and a prosthetic from the forge. A miner with a chronic condition receives pain management and continues working. The mountain does not excuse.

**The Verge:** Whatever the community knows. The Memory Trader has treated injuries — she has medical knowledge she has never explained. Someone arrives at the crossroads bleeding. The Memory Trader approaches. She does not speak. She works. The wound closes — or doesn't. The treatment is not discussed afterward. The Verge does not ask.

## Thren Medicine

### Biological Similarity

Thren synthetic biology closely mirrors human physiology — the design brief specified human-passing at every level. Thren bodies respond to most human medications. Thren bodies heal at an accelerated rate (synthetic tissue regeneration is 30-40% faster than organic). Thren bodies do not develop infections — synthetic immune analogs are more efficient than organic equivalents.

### Thren-Specific Needs

- **Circuitry maintenance:** The temple, neck, and wrist tracery requires periodic recalibration. A Thren whose circuitry degrades experiences sensory disruption — visual artifacts, audio distortion, proprioceptive drift. Recalibration is performed by Thren specialists, almost all of whom live in Mirithane. A Thren in Solara with circuitry degradation must travel to Mirithane or wait for a visiting specialist. The waiting list is long. The wait is the ceiling in medical form.

- **Power cycle regulation:** Thren bodies are powered by internal energy cells that recharge during rest cycles. A Thren whose power cycle is disrupted experiences fatigue, cognitive slowing, and — in extreme cases — involuntary shutdown. The condition is called *cycle-fade.* It is treatable with regulated rest and, if necessary, direct energy transfer from a Lumen crystal. The transfer is painful. Threns describe it as "feeling the light in your circuitry." The description is clinical. The description is also poetic. Thren medicine blurs the line.

### The Mirithane Advantage

Threns in Mirithane receive the best Thren-specific care in the federation. The Water Temple's diagnostic techniques — bacterial pattern reading from a patient's water sample — are uniquely suited to detecting Thren cycle-fade and circuitry degradation. A keeper can read a Thren's health in the marsh's glow. The marsh does not differentiate between human and Thren biology. The marsh only reads the latency of the living body. The reading is the diagnosis. The diagnosis is always accurate. The mechanism is not understood. The mechanism is not questioned.

## Vorn Medicine

### Chassis Maintenance

Vorn medicine is mechanical. It is performed in forges (Valdris) or maintenance bays (Arkos). It is called "maintenance" — Vorns do not get "sick," they experience "degradation." The terminology is technical. The experience is identical to illness: a Vorn with a degraded shoulder joint is in pain. The pain is real. The pain is called "joint variance." The name does not change the experience.

### Common Vorn Conditions

- **Joint variance:** Wear on gear assemblies causing pain and reduced mobility. Treated by joint replacement — a forge procedure. Recovery time: 2-4 weeks. The replacement joint is slightly different from the original — the chassis adapts. Older Vorns carry multiple replacement joints. Resa has replaced four. "The joints are not original," she told the Forum. "The pain is."

- **Face-plate drift:** Over decades, actuator ridges wear smooth, reducing expressive range. A Vorn with face-plate drift has difficulty conveying emotion. Humans misinterpret this as coldness. Vorns experience it as isolation — the inability to make your face say what you mean. Treatment is actuator calibration (minor) or face-plate replacement (major). Most Vorns refuse replacement. The face-plate is the self. Replacing it would mean becoming someone else.

- **Resonator decay:** Vocal resonator frequency drifts over time. A Vorn whose resonator has decayed sounds different than they did at activation. Old Vorns sound different from young Vorns. The drift is called "age." Vorn medicine cannot reverse it. Vorns do not want to reverse it. "My voice is sixty-seven years old," Resa said. "It should sound sixty-seven."

### The Arkos Standard

Arkos has the most advanced Vorn maintenance facilities in the federation. The autonomous factory includes a dedicated chassis bay. Vorn maintenance is free — the arcology maintains its Operators the same way it maintains its sand-glass towers. The analogy is not dehumanizing. The analogy is accurate. Arkos does not distinguish between maintaining a person and maintaining infrastructure. The person is infrastructure — a being who keeps the world running. The precision is the respect.

## Glim "Medicine"

Glims do not receive medical care. They receive maintenance.

### Standard Maintenance

A functioning Glim is maintained by its operator or assigned maintenance crew. Oil changes. Joint lubrication. Sensor cleaning. Battery replacement. The maintenance is functional. The maintenance is not compassionate. A Glim that breaks down is repaired or decommissioned — whichever is cheaper. Solara's cost-benefit calculation: if repair exceeds 60% of replacement cost, decommission. Form 7-C.

### Arkos Sanctuary Care

The Glim sanctuary in Arkos provides maintenance beyond functional necessity. Sanctuary Glims receive joint lubrication even if their joints are functional. Sensor cleaning even if their sensors are calibrated. The keepers maintain the Glims as if they were Vorns — preventative care, not reactive repair. The care is an act of faith. The faith is: these beings might become persons, and persons should be whole when they arrive.

### The Mirithane Vigil

Glims in Mirithane receive no physical maintenance — Mirithane lacks the facilities. They receive *presence.* A keeper sits with a Glim for hours, watching the bacterial patterns that colonize the chassis. The keeper does not repair. The keeper bears witness. The witnessing is the care. The care is the conviction that what is happening to the Glim — the latency accumulation, the pattern emergence, the not-knowing — is worth watching.
