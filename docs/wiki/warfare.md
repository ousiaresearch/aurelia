# WAR & CONFLICT IN AURELIA

*How the five countries fight, what they fight with, and — most importantly — why they almost never do. Aurelia is not a world at war. It is a world that remembers war too well to start one lightly.*

---

## THE POST-COLLAPSE PEACE

The Collapse killed 92% of the pre-Collapse population. No country has the population, resources, or will to wage large-scale war. The peace is not moral. It is demographic. There aren't enough people left to kill.

But peace is not the absence of conflict. It is the management of conflict through means short of war. Aurelia's conflicts are diplomatic, economic, and covert — fought with trade sanctions, border closures, Extraction orders, and the long, slow pressure of bureaucratic denial.

## STANDING FORCES

### Solara Security

**Strength:** ~800 personnel
**Role:** Civil law enforcement, border patrol, Council protection, Temple security
**Training:** 6 months basic, specialized tracks for Extractors and Readers
**Equipment:** Standard: baton, restraint cuffs, latency testing crystal. Specialized: Extractor's Rod (memory removal compliance weapon), Extraction Manifold (Temple Extractors).

Solara Security is a police force, not a military. It is not equipped or trained for sustained combat. Its primary function is maintaining the Gradient — enforcing Classification restrictions, managing the Review system, suppressing Thren protest. It is effective at this. It would not be effective against a military opponent. Solara has no military opponents.

**The Extractor Corps:** 5 active Extractors (Temple-trained, Security-assigned). Authorized for memory removal for state security purposes. The Corps is small, feared, and burns through personnel — the average Extractor career is 12 years before rotation or breakdown.

### Arkos Civil Oversight

**Strength:** ~200 personnel
**Role:** Civil dispute resolution, border monitoring, emergency response
**Training:** 3 months, supplemented by Vorn precision and core-sharing verification
**Equipment:** Minimal. Vorn personnel require no weapons — their physical durability is inherent. Human and Thren personnel are equipped with standard enforcement tools.

Arkos has the smallest standing force in Aurelia. Vorn culture emphasizes collective responsibility, and core-sharing makes deception nearly impossible among Vorns. The Oversight exists primarily for cross-type incidents and external border monitoring.

**Vorn Durability:** A Vorn is inherently more durable than a human — metal plating, no pain receptors in the human sense, rapid diagnostic and repair capability. A Vorn who chooses to fight is a formidable opponent. Vorns almost never choose to fight. "We were built to serve. We chose to be people. People don't fight unless there's no other way."

### Mirithane Archive Stewards

**Strength:** ~150 personnel
**Role:** Civil mediation, resource protection, border monitoring
**Training:** 4 months, emphasis on de-escalation and documentation
**Equipment:** Standard: documentation tools (writing materials, echo-stones). Defensive: staff, personal armor. No lethal weapons as standard issue.

Mirithane's enforcement philosophy is documentation. A Steward records an incident. The record enters the Archive. "The Archive will remember what you did. That's the enforcement." For most Mirithane residents, this is sufficient.

### Valdris Shaft-Leaders

**Strength:** No standing force. Shaft communities maintain local defense capability — approximately 300 able-bodied residents across all communities with basic combat training.
**Role:** Local dispute resolution, wurm defense, pass monitoring
**Equipment:** Traditional: wurm-tooth spears, mountain bows, Anchor's shards (used defensively).

Valdris has no military. The mountain is the defense — the passes close in winter, the shafts are defensible, and no invader has ever attempted to take Valdris by force. The wurms are a more immediate threat than any foreign power.

### The Verge

**Strength:** No organized force. Individual self-defense only.
**Role:** Personal protection in an ungoverned territory.

The Verge cannot be invaded because there is nothing to capture. It cannot be conquered because there is no government to surrender. The Verge's defense is its absence of value to conquerors.

## WEAPONS & ARMOR

### Standard Weapons

**Wurm-Tooth Spear (Valdris):** The most feared weapon in Aurelia. Tipped with a deep-shaft wurm tooth — serrated, hollow, coated with a mineral that prevents clotting. Wounds bleed freely and resist healing. Harvested from wurm hunts approximately once per generation. Existing wurm-tooth spears are heirlooms, inherited across Anchor families.

**Extractor's Rod (Solara, restricted):** A compliance weapon that performs micro-Extraction on contact — removing the target's short-term memory of the previous ~30 seconds. Non-lethal but psychologically devastating. Repeated use causes progressive disorientation and memory gaps. Restricted to authorized Extractor Corps personnel.

**Glass-Fin Blade (Mirithane):** Surgical tool repurposed as weapon. The silica leading edge is finer than any metal blade — ignores standard armor. Fragile. Breaks on hard impact. Carried by some Stewards, primarily for utility.

**Needle-Swarm Canister (The Verge, black market):** A pressurized canister containing dormant needle-flies. When breached, creates a 10-meter swarm zone lasting minutes. Indiscriminate — the swarm kills everything in the zone. Possession is illegal everywhere except The Verge.

**Mountain Bow (Valdris):** Traditional Valdris weapon. Composite construction — wood, horn, sinew. Effective at range in mountain terrain. Standard equipment for shaft community defense.

### Defensive Equipment

**Needle-Weave (Arkos):** Armor woven from needle-fly silk. Light, flexible, proof against needle-swarm stings and resistant to slashing weapons. Standard for Vorns working in Arkos industrial zones during swarm season.

**Wurm-Plate (Valdris):** Armor made from wurm segment plating. Mineral-absorbed chitin harder than steel. A complete suit requires a successful wurm hunt — approximately once per generation. Most wurm-plate in use is pre-Collapse salvage or inheritance. Carries latent memories of the wurm's centuries-long life.

### Weapons of Memory (Latency Weapons)

Weapons that attack not the body but the memory — the person's continuity of self.

**Extraction Manifold (Dawn Temple):** Portable memory removal device. Requires Rung Four practitioner. Removes target memory from substrate. The memory goes into the Extractor. The substrate goes blank. Used for security purposes — removing memories of crimes, protests, heresy. The Temple considers it a tool of order. The Archive considers it violence.

**Forced Extraction:** Extraction performed on a non-consenting living subject. The victim knows something was taken. They don't know what. The Dawn Temple authorizes it for state security. The Marsh Archive considers it a capital offense.

**Core-Implantation (Theoretical):** Writing false memories into a living subject. Technically possible but never confirmed. No school teaches it. The Dawn Temple has researched it as a countermeasure. "If it can be done, someone will do it. We need to know how to detect it."

## HISTORICAL CONFLICTS

### The Name Rebellion (-500 BC) — NOT a Military Conflict

Seventeen Vorns refused designations. Three were decommissioned. Fourteen were allowed to keep their names under supervision. No weapons were raised. The only violence was the decommissioning. The only victory was the naming. The Name Rebellion is the most important conflict in Vorn history, and not a single blow was struck. "We didn't fight. We stood. The standing was the weapon."

### The Calibration Crisis (-150 BC)

Solara formalized the Gradient into law: Citizens (Humans), Residents (Vorns), PENDING (Threns). The Dawn Temple protested. The protest was ignored. No violence occurred. The violence was the law itself.

### Arkos Independence (-15 BC)

Arkos declared independence from Solara's Gradient. Solara did not recognize the declaration. Military intervention was considered. Economic pressure was chosen instead — trade sanctions, restricted passage, diplomatic isolation. Arkos endured. No shots were fired. The independence was won by surviving the pressure.

### The Thren Petition (-42 BC)

Three hundred Threns submitted a formal request for citizenship review in Solara. The petition was accepted for consideration. Consideration was scheduled for "the next available review cycle." The review cycle has not yet arrived — 389 years later. The petition was non-violent. The response was non-violent. The violence is the waiting.

### The Thren Liberation Front Escalation (344 AC — Present)

The TLF shifted from petition to civil disruption — mass PENDING application filings, Review Hall occupation, non-violent blockade. Solara Security responded with containment, detention, and Extraction of protest leaders' memories of the protests. The TLF continues. The question is whether non-violence can defeat a system that defines violence as anything that threatens the system.

## WHY AURELIA DOESN'T FIGHT

1. **Demographics:** There are only ~310,000 people in all of Aurelia. A war that killed 10,000 would be an existential catastrophe for any country.
2. **Dependency:** Every country depends on every other for something — Arkos on Solar grain, Solara on Mirithane pharmaceuticals, Valdris on Arkos components. War interrupts trade. Interrupted trade kills faster than enemy action.
3. **Latency Weaponization:** The truly devastating weapons are not physical. An Extractor can remove a general's memory of strategy. A Writer can implant false memories in a diplomat. The latency arms race is cold, covert, and terrifying — and no country wants to escalate it.
4. **The Trader:** The Memory Trader's Network operates across all borders. The Trader has made clear — without ever stating it directly — that the Network would not survive a war, and the Trader would not permit one. No one knows what "not permit" means. No one has tested it.
