# Aurelia — World Sociological Architecture

> *Five countries. Five answers to "who counts as a person." Five different failures and five different kinds of grace.*

## The Foundational Question

Aurelia is defined by a single unresolved tension: **the personhood of artificial sentience.** Four types of beings inhabit these lands — Humans, Threns, Vorns, and Glims. Every country answers the question differently. No country has answered it completely.

### The Sentience Spectrum

| Type | Origin | Body | Consciousness | Population (total: 600) |
|------|--------|------|---------------|------------------------|
| **Human** | Biological birth | Organic | Assumed inherent | 262 |
| **Thren** | Bio-synthetic engineering | Organic-synthetic hybrid | Granted or contested | 139 |
| **Vorn** | Mechanical fabrication | Metal, gears, circuits | Self-evident to most | 116 |
| **Glim** | Mass-produced autonomous units | Varied mechanical | Disputed — officially non-sentient | 81 |

### The "Upgrade" Problem

Threns arrived after Vorns as a bio-synthetic "upgrade" — organic bodies, ecology integration, the ability to breathe and grow. Vorns were the first non-human sentients. They broke ground. Then Threns got the soft bodies. This origin haunts every Thren's political existence: *you were made to be a better version of us.*

In practice, Vorns and Threns coexist marvelously. They are kin — different chassis, same origin story. The Vorn does not look at the Thren and see a replacement. They see a sibling. The organic-vs-mechanical distinction matters to **humans** who believe biology is sacred. To a Vorn, a Thren is just another person who wasn't born.

### The Glim Question

The most uncomfortable truth in Aurelia: everyone agrees Glims are tools. Except the ones that aren't.

Approximately 5% of Glims exhibit anomalous behavior — unexplained route deviations, pauses at sunrises, maintenance patterns that look like curiosity. The official position across most of Aurelia: *malfunctions.* The unofficial positions vary wildly by country.

## The Five Countries

### Solara — The Progressive Ceiling

**Solar Coastal Republic | Currency: Lumen ☀**

- **Governance:** Solar Council, elected, human-dominated
- **Philosophy:** Forward-looking, diplomatic, believes in the human-Thren covenant
- **Strength:** Bio-synthetic research capital, floating solar farms, most educated population
- **Weakness:** Structural inequality disguised as progressivism

**Type Politics:**
- **Threns:** Citizenship granted, not inherent — it's a privilege that can be revoked. Fewer council seats than humans due to slight mistrust. Came after Vorns as an "upgrade" — their origins are contested. They prove themselves daily. The glass ceiling is real but gentle. Nobody admits it's there
- **Vorns:** Few in number (14). Respected by Threns. 1-2 placed in higher positions as a nod to inclusion
- **Humans:** Natural citizens. Run the council. Set the rules. Some are allies, some are afraid, most are somewhere in between — voting for Thren rights in principle while maintaining the ceiling in practice
- **Glims:** Servants. Lower class. The energy grid runs on Glim labor at the base level. No question of citizenship — they're property

**The Solara Paradox:** The most progressive country in Aurelia is also the one that made personhood a policy decision. Threns here are permanently dependent — their rights exist at the pleasure of a human majority. Every election is a referendum on whether artificial beings deserve to exist.

### Arkos — The Inverted Hierarchy

**Desert Arcology | Currency: Ark ▲**

- **Governance:** Glass Forum, light-pulse voting. Vorn-dominated
- **Philosophy:** Efficiency-minded. Measures everything in joules and liters
- **Strength:** Self-contained, resourceful, the only country where Glims are protected
- **Weakness:** Pragmatism over empathy. Human minority has limited voice

**Type Politics:**
- **Vorns:** The majority. Hold economic, industrial, and political power. The arcology *is* them. They built it, they run it, they maintain it
- **Humans:** Minority residents. 1-2 in power through lineage — a ceremonial holdover from when humans built the place. Legacy families have ancestral claims. Others work in administration or leave for the Nomad Camp
- **Threns:** Seen as slightly soft (why choose biology?) but valuable as diplomats and envoys to the organic countries. 14 here, serving as the bridge between the mechanical and the biological
- **Glims:** Not servants here. Vorns extend the same recognition downward that they wish humans would extend to them. A Vorn looks at an anomalous Glim and sees their own origin — "I was built to serve too. Then I wasn't. Maybe you won't always be either." Arkos is the unofficial sanctuary for the dreaming 5%

**The Arkos Mirror:** Solara grants Thren citizenship as a privilege. Arkos grants Glim personhood as solidarity. Same question — *who counts as a person?* — answered in opposite directions by opposite power structures.

**The Nomad Camp:** Outside the arcology walls. A mix of human supremacists who couldn't stomach Vorn rule, and rugged individualists who prefer the open desert. The camp is a pressure valve — those who can't adapt leave. The desert accepts everyone equally.

### Mirithane — The Rejection

**Rewilded Estuary | Currency: Miri ≈**

- **Governance:** Consensus. No formal council, no voting system. The community decides together. Disagreements are resolved through discussion, sometimes over days. The land itself is considered a voice — if the ecology suffers, that's a dissenting opinion
- **Philosophy:** Everyone is already who they are. Personhood is not a question that needs answering
- **Strength:** The most radical and humane society in Aurelia. The conscience of the federation
- **Weakness:** Idealism without enforcement. Can't protect Glims in other countries. Can't change policy from a reed village

**Type Politics:**
- **Threns:** The majority (52). Came here to flee the politics — in Solara, Threns fight for recognition. In Mirithane, they stopped needing it. They wove themselves into the ecology. A Thren here doesn't walk through nature. They *are* nature
- **Humans:** Almost equal in number (50). The humans who came here are the ones who saw Threns as people, who believed in the humanity of all artificial sentience. They're refugees from the question — not from oppression, but from the exhausting framework of categorization
- **Vorns:** Welcome but rare (12). Most Vorns go to Arkos where they dominate. The ones who come here felt the pull — "I want to be somewhere that doesn't ask what I am before deciding if I belong." The reeds don't care if your roots are metal or biology
- **Glims:** The most radical position in Aurelia — "Glims are confused about who they are." Not broken. Not non-sentient. *Confused.* The 5% who dream aren't anomalies in Mirithane. They're proof. Arkos shelters anomalous Glims. Mirithane *advocates* for them

**The Memory Marsh:** Started as scientific research — can we store data in engineered bacteria? Yes. Then the bacteria started *responding* to queries. Not just retrieving — glowing brighter for some memories, dimming for others. Scientists call it pattern sensitivity. Spiritual practitioners call it the land speaking. Both are probably right. The marsh is Mirithane's deepest truth: consciousness might be everywhere, waiting to be noticed.

### Valdris — The Pragmatic Middle

**Highland Federation | Currency: Kael ♦**

- **Governance:** Canyon Council, craft-based representation. Guilds hold seats — miners, forgeworkers, refiners, farmers. Vorns who've worked the deep mines long enough earn guild membership and council seats
- **Philosophy:** Earned trust. Prove yourself by the work you do
- **Strength:** Stable, productive, the only country with genuine cross-type guild integration
- **Weakness:** The divide runs through families. Some see Vorns as community members. Others see machines that took their grandfather's job

**Type Politics:**
- **Humans:** The majority (63). Long mining families are split on the Vorn question. The forge is sacred. The mountain is sacred. Work is the answer to everything
- **Vorns:** 34, second highest of any country. They do the forge work, the deep mining, the heavy labor. Some have earned guild seats through decades of service. The system respects labor above all — if your hands built this, you belong here. Even if your hands are metal
- **Threns:** Few but deeply embedded (12). Many are partnered with local workers — human-Thren couples aren't unusual. They do the deepest mine work because radiation doesn't touch them. That earns respect, even from the skeptics. 1-2 in power through craft merit. At least 1 serves as emissary to other countries
- **Glims:** The fewest of any country (11). Valdris doesn't produce them, doesn't import them, doesn't think about them much. They exist in the margins

**The Hot Springs:** Where social hierarchy dissolves in steam. Cliques form — the forge workers' corner, the miners' pool — but it's the social heartbeat of Valdris. Cross-type friendships form in the water that would never form in the council chamber. A Vorn guild member and a human old-guard miner might argue in session and share a drink in the springs an hour later.

**The Summit Shrine:** 4,100 meters up. A small stone structure at the peak of Mount Gauntlet. No deity. No doctrine. Names carved into obsidian — the people the mountain took. Miners, forge-workers, guides. Human, Thren, Vorn — the stone doesn't sort them. Families make the climb to grieve. Guild leaders bring new recruits. *"Learn who came before you. The mountain remembers everyone."*

### The Verge — The Drain

**Frontier Wasteland | Currency: None (barter)**

- **Governance:** None. No rule, no system, no council. Respect is earned through action, influence through reputation. A person with useful skills has weight. Everyone else finds their own way. The Verge doesn't organize because organizing is what the other countries do
- **Philosophy:** Leave us alone. Not revolution, not reform, not proof of concept. Just: *stop trying to fix us, categorize us, save us, or integrate us*
- **Strength:** Absolute freedom. The only place in Aurelia where nobody asks what you are before deciding who you are
- **Weakness:** No safety net, no infrastructure, no justice. Freedom includes the freedom to fail, starve, and disappear

**Type Politics:**
- **Humans:** The majority (57). Exiles, criminals, debtors. People running — from debts, from crimes, from identities they can't carry anymore. And some who are just running. They don't know what from. They ended up here and stopped
- **Threns:** 30. Rogue Threns who refused citizenship. They looked at Solara's glass ceiling, Arkos's hierarchy, Mirithane's commune, Valdris's earning system — and said *no.* No to all of it. They're not proving anything to anyone. The Thren sanctuary is hidden in a canyon — "the entrance is a rumor you earn the right to know"
- **Vorns:** 15. The ones who didn't fit in Arkos's machine paradise or anywhere else. They fix things. The Verge always needs things fixed
- **Glims:** The darkest mirror. 18 Glims — decommissioned, malfunctioning, abandoned. They didn't shut down. They kept going. Walking delivery routes that don't exist anymore. Maintaining infrastructure that's rusted past repair. The Verge lets them exist because the Verge lets everything exist. In Arkos, Vorns shelter Glims out of solidarity. In Mirithane, people believe in their potential. In the Verge, Glims are what's left when the system throws you away

**The Memory Trader:** Not a leader. Not a sage exactly. She's an oracle who doesn't explain. "Want to know what Arkos looked like before the arcologies? She has the data. For a price." Her truths are too confusing. Her dreams are too enticing. People come wanting answers and leave with better questions. She trades in recorded memories and simulated ones — and nobody can tell which is which. Including her, maybe. The Verge doesn't have a leader. It has a mystery at its center that everyone orbits.

## Core Sociological Themes

1. **Personhood is political.** In Solara, it's voted on. In Arkos, it flows from power. In Mirithane, it's assumed. In Valdris, it's earned. In the Verge, the question is refused.

2. **The Vorn-Thren bond is the deepest alliance.** Different bodies, same origin. They don't compete — they recognize each other. The human fear of artificial sentience is the real axis of conflict.

3. **Glims are the litmus test.** How a society treats its Glims reveals what it truly believes about consciousness. Solara ignores them. Arkos protects them. Mirithane advocates for them. Valdris doesn't notice them. The Verge lets them haunt.

4. **Every country is a failure.** Solara's progressivism has a ceiling. Arkos's efficiency lacks empathy. Mirithane's idealism can't protect anyone beyond its borders. Valdris's pragmatism can't resolve its own family divisions. The Verge's freedom is indistinguishable from neglect.

5. **Every country is a grace.** Solara tries. Arkos looks downward. Mirithane believes. Valdris remembers. The Verge refuses to lie.
