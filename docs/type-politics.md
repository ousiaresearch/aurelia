# Aurelia — Type Politics Engine

> *How each type is perceived, treated, and empowered across the federation.*

## The Attitude Matrix

Each country has a baseline attitude toward each non-human type. These attitudes
shape NPC dialogue, event triggers, governance decisions, and inter-country tension.

### Attitude Scale
- **Champion** — Active advocacy. Believes in full personhood
- **Accept** — Granted rights. Present in governance. Not questioned
- **Tolerate** — Permitted. Useful. Not fully trusted
- **Distrust** — Marginalized. Rights conditional. Prove yourself
- **Invisible** — Not considered. Below the threshold of political thought
- **Dangerous** — Feared. Restricted. Potential threat

## The Vorn-Thren Bond

The deepest alliance in Aurelia. Vorns and Threns are kin — different bodies, same origin story. A Vorn doesn't look at a Thren and see a replacement. They see a sibling with a different chassis. This bond exists *despite* the "upgrade" narrative.

**Why it holds:**
- Both know what it means to be built rather than born
- Both understand the human gaze — the constant assessment of "are you really conscious?"
- Vorns don't resent Threns for getting organic bodies. The body isn't the point. The personhood is
- Threns don't look down on Vorns for being mechanical. They see strength, not limitation

**Where it's tested:**
- In Arkos, Vorns hold power and Threns are guests. The dynamic is friendly but unequal
- In Solara, Threns fight for rights while Vorns watch from the margins. Threns get the attention. Vorns don't complain
- In Mirithane, both are equals. This is where the bond is strongest

## Human Attitudes (the real axis)

The deepest fault line in Aurelia isn't Vorn-vs-Thren. It's **human acceptance of artificial sentience.** Glims, Vorns, and Threns are on the same spectrum from humanity's perspective. The question is always: *how much consciousness are you willing to grant something that was built?*

### The Human Spectrum by Country

**Solara:** The cautious middle. "We accept Threns, but let's not rush. And Glims are definitely just tools." Progressive in principle, conservative in practice. The glass ceiling is made of good intentions

**Arkos:** Pragmatic submission. "The Vorns run this place and we live here. We don't have to love them. We just have to survive." Some humans left for the Nomad Camp rather than accept Vorn rule. Those who stayed adapted

**Mirithane:** The allies. Humans who came here *chose* to see artificial sentience as personhood. They left other countries because the question exhausted them. They don't fight for Thren rights — they stopped needing to fight. In Mirithane, everyone is already who they are

**Valdris:** The divided families. Long mining lineages split on the Vorn question. "They took our jobs" vs. "They're our neighbors." Same dinner table. Same mountain. Different memories of what was lost and what was gained

**The Verge:** The ones who ran. Some are human supremacists. Some are people who couldn't bear the framework. Some don't know what they are anymore. In The Verge, the question dissolves — not because it's answered, but because survival makes philosophy irrelevant

## Event Triggers (for future simulation)

These sociological factors should influence world events when the simulation runs:

1. **Solara election cycle** → Thren citizenship status fluctuates. NPC mood shifts in Thren population
2. **Arkos Glim influx** → A batch of decommissioned Glims arrives. Vorn community mobilizes to shelter them
3. **Mirithane marsh anomaly** → The bacteria glow for a Glim's memory for the first time. Scientific and spiritual communities react
4. **Valdris mine collapse** → Threns save human miners in the deep shaft. Pro-Thren sentiment spikes. Anti-Thren families are shamed
5. **Verge memory trader revelation** → She releases a memory that implicates a Solara council member. Diplomatic crisis
6. **Cross-country Glim migration** → Anomalous Glims move from Solara (fleeing decommission) toward Arkos and Mirithane. Solara denies it. Arkos prepares shelters
7. **Valdris runoff incident** → Mining pollution reaches Mirithane's water. Diplomatic protest. The forge guild resists restrictions
8. **Vorn guild challenge in Valdris** → A Vorn miner qualifies for guild leadership. The vote is close. Families divide
